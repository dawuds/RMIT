## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Purpose

*State why this document exists, what regulatory obligation it fulfils, and what outcome it is intended to achieve.*

This document serves the following purposes:

- To formally appoint a **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** for [Organization Name] in compliance with BNM RMiT Clause 8.6, which requires financial institutions to designate a senior officer responsible for overseeing technology risk and information security.

- To establish clear **organisational positioning** of the CISO function, including independence requirements and escalation paths.

## 4. Mandate and Authority

### 4.1 CISO Mandate Statement

*Articulate the overarching mandate conferred upon the CISO, framing the role's strategic importance and the breadth of authority granted.*

The CISO is mandated by the Board of Directors / Senior Management of [Organization Name] to **lead, govern, and be accountable for** the Institution's information security strategy, technology risk management framework, and cyber resilience posture. The CISO operates with the authority necessary to fulfil this mandate across all business units, subsidiaries, and functions of the Institution.

The CISO's mandate encompasses the following domains:

### 4.2 Strategic Responsibilities

*List the strategic-level responsibilities within the CISO's mandate.*

- **Information Security Strategy:** Develop, maintain, and execute an Institution-wide information security strategy aligned to business objectives, regulatory requirements, and the evolving threat landscape.
- **Technology Risk Framework:** Own and govern the Institution's technology risk management framework, ensuring it is integrated with enterprise risk management practices.
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Ensure the Institution's information security and technology risk practices meet all applicable BNM, PDPA, and other regulatory requirements. Serve as primary liaison with BNM on technology risk and cybersecurity matters.
- **Cyber Resilience:** Lead the development and maintenance of the Institution's cyber resilience and incident response capabilities, including business continuity provisions related to technology.
- **Security Culture:** Champion a culture of information security awareness and accountability across the Institution.

### 4.3 Operational Responsibilities

*List the operational responsibilities within the CISO's mandate.*

- Oversee the day-to-day operation of the information security function, including Security Operations Centre (SOC), vulnerability management, identity and access management, and data loss prevention.
- Review, approve, and enforce information security policies, standards, and guidelines.
- Direct and oversee security assessments, penetration testing, and technology risk assessments.
- Manage information security incidents, including escalation to Senior Management, the Board, and BNM as required.
- Oversee third-party and vendor security risk management, including outsourced technology arrangements.
- Monitor and report on key technology risk and security metrics, key risk indicators (KRIs), and key performance indicators (KPIs).

### 4.4 Authority and Decision Rights

*Define the specific authorities and decision-making powers granted to the CISO.*

The CISO is granted the following authorities:

| Authority | Scope | Limit / Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Policy approval authority | Information security policies, standards, and procedures | Subject to Board / Risk Committee endorsement for material changes |
| Budget authority | Information security budget allocation and expenditure | Within approved budget envelope as per institutional delegation of authority |
| Technology risk veto | Right to escalate or recommend suspension of technology initiatives that pose unacceptable security risk | Escalation to CEO / Board Risk Committee where business override is sought |
| Vendor security assessment | Authority to approve or reject vendor security assessments and impose security requirements on third parties | — |
| Incident authority | Declare and manage information security incidents; invoke incident response plans | Notify CEO / Board within [X hours] for material incidents |
| Access and investigation | Right of access to all systems, logs, data, and personnel necessary to fulfil security oversight mandate | Subject to applicable privacy, legal, and HR protocols |
| Reporting | Direct reporting access to the Board Risk Committee / Audit Committee on security matters | — |

### 4.5 Limitations of Authority

*Document any limits or boundaries on the CISO's mandate to ensure appropriate checks and balances.*

The CISO's authority does not extend to:

- Unilateral decisions that result in the suspension of core business operations without prior CEO approval (except in declared security incidents where imminent risk requires immediate action).
- Commitments of expenditure beyond the approved delegation of authority limit.
- Decisions that fall within the exclusive authority of the Board, Audit Committee, or other governance bodies as defined in the Institution's governance framework.
