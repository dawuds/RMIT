## 1. Purpose and Authority

*This section establishes the formal basis for the charter, articulating why it exists and the authority under which it operates. Authors should describe the institution's commitment to technology risk governance and the regulatory mandate that necessitates this charter.*

### 1.1 Purpose

This Board Technology Risk Charter ("Charter") establishes the formal framework under which the Board of Directors of [Organization Name] ("the Board") discharges its responsibilities for the oversight, governance, and strategic direction of technology risk management across the institution.

The Charter formalises the Board's commitment to maintaining a robust technology risk governance structure that safeguards the operational resilience, cybersecurity posture, data integrity, and digital continuity of [Organization Name] and its customers.

This Charter is issued pursuant to the requirements of Bank Negara Malaysia's **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document** and shall serve as the primary governance instrument defining the Board's role in technology risk oversight.

### 1.2 Authority

The Board of Directors derives its authority to establish and maintain this Charter from the following instruments:

- The **Companies Act 2016** and the constituent documents of [Organization Name]
- **Bank Negara Malaysia's Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, effective [RMiT Effective Date], particularly **Clause 8.1**
- The **Financial Services Act 2013** / **Islamic Financial Services Act 2013** [*delete as appropriate*]
- The Board's own **Terms of Reference** and corporate governance framework
- Any other applicable BNM policy documents, guidelines, and circulars as amended from time to time

The Board hereby authorises the establishment, operation, and periodic review of the technology risk governance structure as described in this Charter and delegates specific functions as set out herein, while retaining ultimate accountability for technology risk oversight.

### 1.3 Objectives

The primary objectives of this Charter are to:

- Define the Board's responsibilities and accountability for technology risk governance in accordance with RMiT Clause 8.1
- Establish clear lines of authority, delegation, and escalation for technology risk matters
- Ensure that technology risk is integrated into the institution's overall risk appetite and enterprise risk management framework
- Provide a structured basis for the Board to receive, assess, and act upon technology risk information
- Demonstrate to Bank Negara Malaysia and other regulators the institution's commitment to sound technology risk governance
- Promote a culture of technology risk awareness at the highest level of the institution

## 4. Board Technology Risk Oversight Responsibilities

*This section is the substantive core of the charter. Authors should describe each oversight responsibility with sufficient specificity to enable effective execution and to satisfy regulatory scrutiny. Cross-reference to relevant policies and frameworks where applicable.*

### 4.1 Strategic Oversight

The Board shall exercise strategic oversight of technology risk by:

- **Approving the IT Strategic Plan** of [Organization Name] on an annual basis, ensuring it is aligned with the institution's corporate strategy and risk appetite
- **Setting the Technology Risk Appetite** by approving quantitative and qualitative thresholds for acceptable technology risk exposure, including cybersecurity, operational resilience, and data risk
- **Endorsing digital transformation strategies** that introduce material changes to the institution's technology risk profile
- **Challenging management assumptions** underpinning technology investment, risk mitigation, and resilience strategies
- **Ensuring alignment** between technology risk governance and the institution's overall Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) framework

### 4.2 Risk Appetite and Tolerance

The Board is responsible for:

- Approving and periodically reviewing the **Technology Risk Appetite Statement**, which shall define the institution's willingness to accept technology-related risks in pursuit of its strategic objectives
- Setting specific **risk tolerance thresholds** for key technology risk categories, including but not limited to:

| Risk Category | Appetite Descriptor | Key Metric | Tolerance Threshold |
|--------------|-------------------|------------|---------------------|
| Cybersecurity Risk | Low | Number of critical incidents per annum | [Threshold] |
| System Availability | Very Low | Core system uptime (%) | [e.g., ≥ 99.9%] |
| Data Breach / PDPA Risk | Zero Tolerance | Number of reportable personal data breaches | [Threshold] |
| Third-Party / Outsourcing Risk | Low | Number of critical vendor incidents | [Threshold] |
| Technology Change Risk | Medium | Failed change rate (%) | [Threshold] |

*Authors should populate thresholds in consultation with the Chief Risk Officer and Chief Information Security Officer, ensuring alignment with BNM supervisory expectations.*

### 4.3 Governance and Policy Oversight

The Board shall:

- **Approve** the IT Risk Management Framework and all material associated policies, including the Cybersecurity Policy, Business Continuity Plan (Technology), and Data Management Policy
- **Ensure** that technology risk policies are reviewed at least annually and updated to reflect changes in the regulatory environment, threat landscape, and institutional strategy
- **Oversee** the implementation of the IT governance structure, including the roles and responsibilities of the Chief Information Officer (CIO), Chief Information Security Officer (CISO), and technology risk management function
- **Ensure** that technology risk management is integrated into the institution's three lines of defence model

### 4.4 Cybersecurity Oversight

In accordance with RMiT Clause 19, the Board shall:

- Approve the institution's **Cybersecurity Framework** and be briefed on the institution's cybersecurity posture no less than quarterly
- Be notified of **material cybersecurity incidents** in a timely manner, in accordance with the institution's incident escalation procedures and BNM notification requirements
- Ensure that **cybersecurity stress tests and threat-led penetration testing (TLPT)** are conducted at appropriate intervals and that findings are remediated on a risk-prioritised basis
- Oversee the institution's participation in **financial sector threat intelligence sharing** arrangements, including those facilitated by the Financial Intelligence Network (FinTech) or equivalent bodies

### 4.5 Operational Resilience Oversight

The Board shall:

- Approve the institution's **Business Continuity Plan (BCP)** and **Disaster Recovery (DR) Plan** for technology systems and ensure these are tested at least annually
- Review the outcomes of BCP/DR exercises and ensure that material gaps are remediated within agreed timeframes
- Set and approve **Recovery Time Objectives (RTO)** and **Recovery Point Objectives (RPO)** for critical systems, with reference to BNM's requirements for financial institutions
- Oversee the institution's readiness to respond to significant technology disruptions, including those caused by third parties

### 4.6 Third-Party and Outsourcing Risk Oversight

The Board shall:

- Approve the institution's **Outsourcing and Third-Party Risk Management Policy**
- Ensure that **material technology outsourcing arrangements**, including cloud services, are subject to appropriate due diligence, contractual safeguards, and ongoing monitoring prior to and during the arrangement
- Receive reports on the performance and risk profile of material third-party technology providers at least annually
- Ensure that the institution retains adequate oversight and control over outsourced technology functions in accordance with BNM outsourcing guidelines

## 10. Review and Amendment Process

*This section governs how this Charter is kept current. Authors should ensure the review process is practical and that it assigns clear ownership.*

### 10.1 Review Frequency

This Charter shall be reviewed:

- **At minimum, annually**, at the annual technology risk strategy review meeting
- **Upon any material change** to the regulatory framework governing technology risk (including amendments to the RMiT Policy Document or related BNM guidelines)
- **Upon any material change** to the institution's technology strategy, risk profile, or corporate structure
- **Following a significant technology incident** that reveals gaps in the Board's oversight framework
- **At any time** at the request of the Board Chairperson, the Company Secretary, or the Head of Compliance

### 10.2 Amendment Process

| Step | Action | Responsible Party | Timeline |
|------|--------|------------------|---------|
| 1 | Identify the need for amendment and prepare a summary of proposed changes | Company Secretary / Head of Compliance | As required |
| 2 | Circulate proposed amendments to relevant management stakeholders for comment | Company Secretary | [X] business days for comment |
| 3 | Present proposed amendments to the Board (or BTRC) for review and discussion | Company Secretary / Head of Compliance | At next scheduled meeting, or ad hoc if urgent |
| 4 | Board approves or rejects proposed amendments by resolution | Board | At meeting |
| 5 | Approved amendments incorporated into updated Charter; version number incremented | Company Secretary | Within [X] business days of approval |
| 6 | Updated Charter distributed to all members and relevant officers | Company Secretary | Within [X] business days of approval |
| 7 | Updated Charter filed in the official document management system | Company Secretary | Upon distribution |

### 10.3 Interim Amendments

In urgent circumstances where an amendment is required before the next scheduled Board meeting, the Chairperson of the Board (or BTRC) may approve an interim amendment by written resolution, subject to formal ratification at the next scheduled meeting.

### 10.4 Document Ownership

The **Company Secretary** is the designated owner of this Charter and is responsible for:

- Maintaining the master copy of the document
- Initiating and coordinating the annual review process
- Ensuring that all approved amendments are properly documented and filed
- Notifying relevant stakeholders of updates to the Charter

### Appendix C — Glossary of Terms

*This appendix defines key technical and regulatory terms used in this Charter to ensure consistent interpretation by Board members.*

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Board Technology Risk Committee (BTRC)** | A sub-committee of the Board of Directors established to discharge dedicated oversight of technology risk, where applicable. |
| **Business Continuity Plan (BCP)** | A documented set of procedures and resources enabling [Organization Name] to continue critical operations during and following a disruptive event. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | The senior executive responsible for establishing and maintaining the enterprise vision, strategy, and programme to ensure information assets and technologies are adequately protected. |
| **Cybersecurity** | The collection of tools, policies, security concepts, security safeguards, guidelines, risk management approaches, actions, training, best practices, assurance, and technologies used to protect the cyber environment and organisation assets. |
| **Disaster Recovery (DR)** | The process, policies, and procedures related to preparing for recovery or continuation of technology infrastructure critical to an organisation following a natural or human-induced disaster. |
| **Key Risk Indicator (KRI)** | A metric used to monitor the level of risk exposure within a specific risk category, providing early warning signals to management and the Board. |
| **PDPA** | Personal Data Protection Act 2010 — the primary Malaysian legislation governing the processing of personal data in commercial transactions. |
| **RACI** | Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed — a responsibility assignment framework used to clarify roles in governance processes. |
| **Recovery Point Objective (RPO)** | The maximum targeted period in which data might be lost from an IT service due to a major incident. |
| **Recovery Time Objective (RTO)** | The targeted duration of time within which a business process must be restored after a disaster or disruption. |
| **RMiT** | Risk Management in Technology — the BNM Policy Document setting out requirements for the management of technology risk by financial institutions. |
| **Technology Risk** | The risk of loss resulting from inadequate or failed internal processes, people, and systems, or from external events, arising from the use of information technology. |
| **Third-Party Technology Risk** | Technology risk arising from the use of external service providers, vendors, or partners in delivering technology services or capabilities. |
