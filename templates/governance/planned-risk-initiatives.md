## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section states the intent of the document, its regulatory basis, and what it is designed to achieve within the organization's technology risk management framework.*

This document establishes the **Planned Risk Initiatives** for [Organization Name] for the period **[Initiative Period Start Date]** to **[Initiative Period End Date]**. It defines the formal programme of technology risk management activities that the organization commits to undertaking in fulfilment of its obligations under the Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)** Policy Document.

The document provides a structured and auditable record of planned initiatives, including their objectives, milestones, resource allocations, ownership, and progress tracking mechanisms. It serves as the primary governance instrument for the Chief Risk Officer (CRO) and Board Risk Committee to oversee the maturity and adequacy of the organization's technology risk posture.

### 1.2 Scope

*Describe the organizational, technological, and geographic scope covered by these initiatives. Specify which systems, business units, and risk domains are in scope.*

This document applies to:

- **Organizational Scope:** All business units, technology departments, and support functions of [Organization Name] and its Malaysian subsidiaries, as applicable.
- **Technology Scope:** All information systems, technology infrastructure, digital services, outsourced technology arrangements, and cloud services that support the organization's financial services operations.
- **Risk Domains:** Technology risk, cyber risk, operational resilience risk, third-party technology risk, and data management risk.
- **Geographic Scope:** All operations of [Organization Name] conducted within Malaysia and subject to BNM regulatory oversight.

### 7.2 Date Extension and Deferral Policy

*Describe the governance process that must be followed when a target completion date cannot be met. This demonstrates the organization maintains disciplined oversight even when timelines slip.*

If an initiative cannot be completed by its target completion date, the following process must be followed:

1. **Notification:** The initiative owner must notify the CRO in writing no later than [30] calendar days before the target completion date if a delay is anticipated.
2. **Impact Assessment:** A brief impact assessment must be prepared, documenting the reason for the delay, the risk implications of the extension, and any compensating controls to be applied during the extended period.
3. **Revised Date Approval:** A revised target completion date must be formally approved by the CRO. Extensions exceeding [90] days require Board Risk Committee endorsement.
4. **Record Update:** The Risk Initiative Register must be updated to reflect the revised target date, reason for extension, and approving authority.
5. **Regulatory Notification:** Where the delayed initiative relates to a specific BNM regulatory commitment, the Compliance function must be notified to assess whether supervisory disclosure is required.
