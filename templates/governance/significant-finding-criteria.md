## 1. Executive Summary

*Provide a concise overview of this document's purpose, its regulatory basis, and the significance of maintaining a defined significant finding criteria framework. Summarise the key thresholds and obligations that apply to [Organization Name].*

[Organization Name] operates under the regulatory supervision of Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) and is required to maintain robust internal audit governance practices in accordance with the **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, particularly **Clause 16.5**, which mandates that financial institutions establish and maintain documented criteria for identifying audit findings that require escalation to BNM.

This document, the **Significant Finding Criteria**, defines the standards, thresholds, and procedures by which [Organization Name]'s Internal Audit function classifies audit findings arising from technology risk reviews and determines whether such findings constitute significant findings requiring mandatory notification to BNM.

The framework established herein is a cornerstone of [Organization Name]'s technology governance and supports the Board and Senior Management in fulfilling their oversight obligations. It ensures that material technology risks, control failures, and compliance gaps are identified, escalated, and remediated in a timely and structured manner consistent with BNM's supervisory expectations.

**Key Obligations Addressed:**

- Classification of internal audit findings across technology risk domains
- Criteria for elevating findings to "significant" status
- Mandatory notification thresholds and escalation pathways to BNM
- Timelines and procedures for regulatory reporting
- Accountability structures for finding management and remediation

This document is reviewed annually and is subject to approval by the Board Audit Committee (BAC) of [Organization Name].

## 6. Finding Severity Classification

### 6.1 Classification Framework Overview

*Describe the severity classification framework applied to all audit findings. This section is foundational to determining whether a finding meets the significant finding threshold in Section 7.*

[Organization Name] applies a four-tier finding severity classification framework to all technology audit findings. The classification is determined by assessing the finding against a combination of **impact** and **likelihood** dimensions, with specific escalation rules for regulatory significance.

### 6.2 Severity Tiers

| Severity Tier | Label | Colour Code | Definition |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Critical | Red | Finding represents an immediate and material risk to [Organization Name], its customers, or the financial system. Likely to trigger BNM notification. |
| **Tier 2** | High | Orange | Finding represents a significant control weakness with substantial potential impact if not remediated promptly. May trigger BNM notification. |
| **Tier 3** | Medium | Yellow | Finding represents a control weakness requiring remediation within a defined period. Generally does not trigger BNM notification unless systemic. |
| **Tier 4** | Low | Green | Finding represents a minor control gap or improvement opportunity. Does not trigger BNM notification. |

### 6.3 Impact Assessment Criteria

*For each finding, the auditor must assess the potential impact across the following dimensions.*

| Impact Dimension | Critical | High | Medium | Low |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Financial Impact** | Loss or exposure > RM [X] million | Loss or exposure RM [Y]–[X] million | Loss or exposure RM [Z]–[Y] million | Loss or exposure < RM [Z] million |
| **Customer Impact** | Material harm to a large number of customers; systemic disruption to customer services | Significant harm to identifiable customer group | Limited customer impact, remediable | Minimal or no direct customer impact |
| **Regulatory / Legal** | Breach of a specific BNM regulatory requirement; potential enforcement action | Material non-compliance with regulatory expectation; heightened supervisory scrutiny | Minor non-compliance or process gap | Best practice deviation; no direct regulatory implication |
| **Operational Impact** | Critical system outage or severe degradation of core services | Significant operational disruption | Moderate operational impact; workarounds available | Minimal operational disruption |
| **Reputational Impact** | Public disclosure likely; material damage to [Organization Name]'s reputation | Internal escalation required; potential for external attention | Limited reputational exposure | No reputational exposure |
| **Data / Privacy** | Breach of personal data of a material number of data subjects; notifiable under PDPA | Unauthorised access or disclosure of sensitive data | Minor data handling error; no breach of PDPA thresholds | Low-risk data handling deviation |
| **Cyber / Security** | Active exploitation of vulnerability; confirmed security incident | Known exploitable vulnerability in critical system | Theoretical exploitable weakness | Low-risk configuration deviation |

### 6.4 Likelihood Assessment

| Likelihood Level | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **4** | Almost Certain | The adverse outcome is expected to occur in most circumstances |
| **3** | Likely | The adverse outcome will probably occur in more circumstances than not |
| **2** | Possible | The adverse outcome could occur at some time |
| **1** | Unlikely | The adverse outcome may occur only in exceptional circumstances |

### 6.5 Risk Rating Matrix

| | **Unlikely (1)** | **Possible (2)** | **Likely (3)** | **Almost Certain (4)** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Critical Impact (4)** | High | Critical | Critical | Critical |
| **High Impact (3)** | Medium | High | Critical | Critical |
| **Medium Impact (2)** | Low | Medium | High | High |
| **Low Impact (1)** | Low | Low | Medium | Medium |

### 6.6 Systemic Finding Identification

Findings that individually rate as Medium may be elevated to High or Critical where the Head of Internal Audit determines that one or more of the following systemic indicators apply:

- The same or similar finding has recurred in two or more consecutive audit cycles
- The finding is identified across multiple business units, systems, or processes simultaneously (pervasive control failure)
- The finding is symptomatic of a deeper structural weakness in [Organization Name]'s technology risk management framework
- The finding is consistent with industry-wide vulnerabilities or threats identified in BNM circulars, threat intelligence reports, or peer institution incident disclosures

**Documentation Requirement:** Where a finding is elevated due to systemic indicators, the Head of Internal Audit must document the basis for elevation in the audit working papers and in the Significant Finding Register (Appendix A).

### 6.7 Overriding Indicators for Immediate Escalation

Regardless of the risk rating matrix outcome, the following findings are automatically classified as **Critical** and require immediate escalation:

| Override Indicator | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Confirmed or suspected fraud involving technology systems | Regulatory and legal reporting obligations |
| Active or suspected unauthorised access to customer data | PDPA notification obligations; BNM reporting requirements |
| Confirmed cyber incident affecting production systems | BNM Incident Reporting requirements under RMiT |
| Identified breach of a specific BNM regulatory prohibition | Direct regulatory non-compliance |
| Finding identified during a BNM-directed audit or special examination | Supervisory priority |
| Finding involving a politically exposed person (PEP) or sanctions-related matter | AML/CFT and legal obligations |

## 7. Notification Trigger Criteria

### 7.1 Overview

*This section defines the precise criteria that, when met, trigger the mandatory obligation to notify BNM of a significant finding. Reference RMiT Clause 16.5 directly.*

In accordance with **BNM RMiT Policy Document, Clause 16.5**, [Organization Name] is required to notify BNM of significant findings arising from internal audit activities related to technology risk. This section defines the criteria that constitute a significant finding for the purposes of BNM notification.

A finding is classified as a **Significant Finding** requiring BNM notification when **one or more** of the following trigger criteria are met:

### 7.2 Primary Notification Triggers

| Trigger ID | Trigger Category | Trigger Condition | Severity Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| **T-01** | Regulatory Breach | The finding evidences a direct breach of a specific provision of the BNM RMiT Policy Document, BNM Cybersecurity Policy, or other applicable BNM policy | Automatic — Critical |
| **T-02** | Critical System Failure | The finding relates to a failure or near-failure of a critical system as defined in [Organization Name]'s Technology Risk Management Policy | Critical |
| **T-03** | Cyber Incident | The finding arises from or is associated with a confirmed cyber incident affecting production systems or customer data | Automatic — Critical |
| **T-04** | Data Breach | The finding relates to a confirmed or suspected breach of personal data of customers as defined under the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA) | Automatic — Critical |
| **T-05** | Recurrence | A High-severity finding has recurred without adequate remediation after two or more consecutive audit cycles | High (Escalated) |
| **T-06** | Systemic Failure | Two or more High-severity findings in the same audit cycle evidence a systemic failure of technology risk controls | High (Systemic) |
| **T-07** | Outsourcing Risk | A Critical or High-severity finding relates to a material outsourced technology service affecting [Organization Name]'s ability to meet regulatory obligations | Critical / High |
| **T-08** | Financial Integrity | The finding involves a material risk to the integrity of financial transactions or financial reporting through technology systems | Critical / High |
| **T-09** | Operational Resilience | The finding indicates a material weakness in [Organization Name]'s ability to achieve its Recovery Time Objective (RTO) or Recovery Point Objective (RPO) for critical systems | Critical / High |
| **T-10** | Governance Failure | The finding relates to a fundamental failure of IT governance structures, including the absence of a required policy, committee, or oversight mechanism mandated by BNM | Critical |

### 7.3 Secondary (Supporting) Criteria

In addition to the primary triggers above, the Head of Internal Audit shall exercise professional judgement to notify BNM where, in their assessment:

- The aggregate of multiple Medium-severity findings creates a material technology risk profile that warrants supervisory awareness, even where no individual finding meets a primary trigger
- An external auditor, regulatory examiner, or qualified independent third party has identified a finding that [Organization Name]'s internal audit function had not previously identified, suggesting a potential gap in audit coverage
- [Organization Name] has received a BNM supervisory communication directing it to enhance controls in a specific area, and internal audit findings confirm that such enhancements have not been implemented

### 7.4 Exclusions from Notification

The following do not constitute significant findings requiring BNM notification under this framework:

- Findings rated as Medium or Low that do not meet any primary or secondary trigger criterion
- Audit observations or improvement recommendations that do not constitute findings (i.e., no control failure has been identified)
- Findings that have been fully remediated prior to the finalisation of the audit report, with documented evidence of remediation, unless a trigger criterion has been independently met

### 7.5 Significant Finding Classification Decision Process

*Document the decision process the Head of Internal Audit follows when determining whether to notify BNM.*

```
STEP 1: Does the finding rate as Critical or High per Section 6?
  → YES: Proceed to Step 2
  → NO (Medium/Low): Assess secondary criteria in Section 7.3 → If not met, no BNM notification required

STEP 2: Does the finding meet one or more Primary Triggers (T-01 to T-10)?
  → YES: Finding is classified as Significant → Proceed to Section 8 (Notification)
  → NO: Review secondary criteria in Section 7.3

STEP 3 (if secondary criteria apply): Does the Head of Internal Audit determine BNM notification is appropriate under professional judgement?
  → YES: Finding is classified as Significant → Proceed to Section 8 (Notification)
  → NO: Document rationale for non-notification in Significant Finding Register (Appendix A)

STEP 4: For all significant findings, complete:
  - Significant Finding Register entry (Appendix A)
  - Significant Finding Notification Form (Appendix B)
  - BAC notification per Section 8
```

### 7.6 Significant Finding Register

All findings assessed under this framework, whether classified as significant or not, are recorded in the Significant Finding Register maintained by the Internal Audit function. The Register includes findings that were assessed against the criteria but determined not to meet the notification threshold, with documented rationale.

The Register is reviewed by the BAC at each scheduled meeting and is made available to BNM upon request.

### 8.5 Escalation for Timeline Breach

If [Organization Name] determines that it will not be able to meet a prescribed BNM notification timeline (e.g., due to complexity of the finding or ongoing remediation), the following escalation procedure applies:

- The finding remains open in the Significant Finding Register until the Head of Internal Audit formally certifies closure based on validated evidence

### 9.1 Specific Role Descriptions

#### Head of Internal Audit

The Head of Internal Audit is the **Owner** of this document and holds primary accountability for:

- Ensuring all audit findings are assessed against the significant finding criteria in a consistent and documented manner
- Determining whether BNM notification is required and, where so, ensuring notification is submitted within the prescribed timelines
- Maintaining the Significant Finding Register with complete and accurate records
- Presenting significant finding status reports to the BAC at each scheduled meeting
- Conducting the annual review of this document and presenting proposed amendments to the BAC for approval

#### Chief Compliance Officer

The Chief Compliance Officer supports the Head of Internal Audit by:

- Managing the formal communication interface with BNM for significant finding notifications
- Ensuring that notification content meets BNM's requirements and is legally reviewed prior to submission
- Advising on regulatory developments that may affect the significant finding criteria or notification thresholds
- Maintaining records of all BNM correspondence related to significant findings

#### Board Audit Committee (BAC)

The BAC provides oversight by:

- Approving this document annually
- Receiving timely notification of all significant findings
- Challenging management on the adequacy of remediation plans and timelines
- Approving the closure of significant findings based on the Head of Internal Audit's validation assessment

#### CIO / CISO

The CIO and CISO support the remediation process by:

- Owning and executing remediation plans for significant findings within their areas of responsibility
- Providing management responses and remediation plans within the timelines specified by the Head of Internal Audit
- Reporting remediation progress to the Head of Internal Audit on a [monthly] basis until closure
