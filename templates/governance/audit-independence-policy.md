## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*Describe the overarching objective of this policy. Explain why audit independence is essential to the governance framework of the organization and how it aligns with BNM RMiT obligations.*

This policy establishes the principles, requirements, and responsibilities for maintaining the independence of the Internal Audit function at [Organization Name] with respect to information technology (IT) audit activities. Audit independence is a foundational element of effective governance, ensuring that the Internal Audit function provides objective, unbiased assessments of the organization's IT risk management, control environment, and compliance posture.

The policy is issued pursuant to the requirements of Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, specifically Clause 13.2, which mandates that financial institutions maintain an independent IT audit function that is organizationally and functionally separate from the business units and technology functions it audits.

This policy supports the Board and Senior Management in discharging their oversight responsibilities by ensuring that audit findings, opinions, and recommendations are free from undue influence, bias, or conflict of interest.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the boundaries of this policy — which entities, systems, functions, personnel, and processes are covered. Include exclusions where appropriate.*

This policy applies to:

- **Organizational Entities:** [Organization Name] and all its subsidiaries, branches, and affiliated entities that are subject to BNM RMiT requirements.
- **Personnel:** All members of the Internal Audit department, co-sourced audit service providers, guest auditors, and any individual performing IT audit-related activities on behalf of [Organization Name].
- **IT Audit Activities:** All audit engagements covering information technology systems, cybersecurity controls, data governance, technology risk management, digital services, outsourced technology arrangements, and related IT governance processes.
- **Technology Domains:** Core banking systems, payment infrastructure, digital banking platforms, cloud environments, network and security infrastructure, data centers, end-user computing, and all systems classified within the IT audit universe.

**Exclusions:**

This policy does not apply to:
- External audit engagements conducted by the appointed external auditor, except where joint or reliance engagements are conducted with Internal Audit.
- Quality assurance reviews commissioned by the Internal Audit department itself, which are governed by the Quality Assurance and Improvement Program (QAIP).

*Describe how the audit independence governance structure is organized, including Board-level and management-level oversight arrangements.*

### 4.3 Detailed Role Descriptions

#### 4.3.1 Board Audit Committee (BAC)

*Specify the BAC's responsibilities with respect to audit independence oversight.*

The Board Audit Committee is responsible for:

- Approving the Audit Independence Policy and any material amendments thereto.
- Providing functional oversight of the Internal Audit function to ensure organizational independence is maintained at all times.
- Approving the appointment, performance assessment, remuneration, and where necessary, the removal of the Head of Internal Audit.
- Reviewing and approving the annual Risk-Based Audit Plan and any significant in-year modifications.
- Receiving and reviewing all IT audit reports, findings, and management responses.
- Reviewing and adjudicating on any independence impairments or conflicts of interest declared by the Head of Internal Audit.
- Commissioning external quality assurance reviews of the Internal Audit function at least once every five (5) years.

#### 4.3.2 Head of Internal Audit (HIA)

*Specify the HIA's responsibilities for upholding and administering audit independence.*

The Head of Internal Audit is responsible for:

- Maintaining and demonstrating the independence and objectivity of the Internal Audit function.
- Ensuring all IT audit personnel comply with this policy, the Internal Audit Charter, and applicable professional standards.
- Declaring any actual or potential conflicts of interest to the BAC immediately upon becoming aware of them.
- Establishing and maintaining an annual declaration process for all audit team members.
- Reviewing and approving auditor assignment decisions with due consideration for independence requirements.
- Implementing safeguards where independence impairments are identified.
- Escalating material independence threats to the BAC without delay.
- Maintaining oversight of co-sourced audit service providers to ensure equivalent independence standards are applied.

#### 4.3.3 IT Audit Team Members

*Specify the obligations of individual IT auditors with respect to independence and objectivity.*

Individual IT audit team members are responsible for:

- Completing the annual independence declaration and promptly declaring any changes throughout the year.
- Identifying and disclosing any actual, potential, or perceived conflicts of interest before commencement of any audit engagement.
- Maintaining professional objectivity throughout all audit engagements.
- Refraining from participating in any audit engagement where their independence or objectivity may be compromised.
- Adhering to applicable professional standards (IIA, ISACA) regarding independence.
- Not assuming operational or advisory responsibilities in areas subject to audit.

#### 4.3.4 Chief Information Officer / Technology Management

*Specify the obligations of technology management in supporting audit independence.*

The CIO and technology management are responsible for:

- Providing timely and complete access to systems, data, documentation, and personnel required for IT audit engagements.
- Refraining from actions that could compromise the independence or objectivity of the Internal Audit function.
- Responding to audit findings within agreed timeframes.
- Not interfering in audit scope, resource allocation, or findings.

#### 4.3.5 Human Resources and Compliance

Human Resources and Compliance are responsible for:

- Maintaining records of auditor role history to support post-employment cooling-off assessments.
- Administering the conflict of interest declaration process in coordination with the Head of Internal Audit.
- Ensuring that staff movements into or out of the Internal Audit function are assessed for independence implications.

## 5. Policy Statements and Requirements

### 5.1 Core Independence Principles

*State the fundamental independence principles that govern the Internal Audit function. These should be unambiguous policy mandates.*

[Organization Name] mandates that the Internal Audit function shall at all times maintain:

**5.1.1 Organizational Independence**

The Internal Audit function shall be organizationally positioned to enable it to fulfill its responsibilities free from interference. Specifically:

- The Head of Internal Audit shall report **functionally** to the Board Audit Committee and **administratively** to the [Chief Executive Officer / Managing Director] or equivalent.
- The Internal Audit function shall have no operational responsibilities in respect of activities subject to audit.
- The Head of Internal Audit shall have direct and unrestricted access to the Chairman of the Board Audit Committee and the Board of Directors.
- No member of the Internal Audit function shall be subjected to scope limitations, budget constraints, or resource restrictions imposed by management that would impair the ability to discharge audit responsibilities.

**5.1.2 Individual Objectivity**

All internal auditors shall:

- Maintain an objective state of mind in all audit activities.
- Not allow personal relationships, prior work history, financial interests, or organizational pressures to influence audit judgments.
- Disclose any actual, potential, or perceived impairment to objectivity before commencing an engagement.
- Be reassigned from any engagement where objectivity cannot be maintained.

**5.1.3 Prohibition on Self-Review**

- No internal auditor shall audit or provide assurance over systems, processes, or controls for which they held operational responsibility within the preceding **[24 / 36] months** (cooling-off period).
- This prohibition extends to systems or processes designed, implemented, or maintained by the auditor in a prior role.

**5.1.4 Prohibition on Assuming Operational Responsibilities**

- The Internal Audit function shall not assume any operational or managerial responsibility for activities within the audit universe.
- IT audit personnel shall not design, install, configure, or maintain any technology system or security control that falls within the audit universe.
- Advisory or consulting activities undertaken by Internal Audit shall be subject to independence safeguards as defined in Section 6.4.

### 5.2 Independence in Practice

*Provide specific operational requirements that translate the above principles into day-to-day practice.*

| Requirement | Standard | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Annual Independence Declaration | All IT audit staff | Annual (January) |
| Engagement-Level Independence Assessment | Audit team assigned to engagement | Before each engagement |
| Post-Employment Cooling-Off Assessment | Newly hired auditors with prior industry roles | On appointment |
| Conflict of Interest Disclosure | All staff | Ongoing / event-triggered |
| BAC Independence Reporting | Head of Internal Audit | At each BAC meeting |
| External Quality Assessment | Independent external assessor | Every 5 years (or upon request) |

### 5.3 Independence in Co-sourced Arrangements

*Define independence requirements that apply when engaging external audit service providers.*

When co-sourcing arrangements are used to supplement IT audit resources:

- The selected co-source provider shall not have a current or recent (within [2] years) consulting or advisory engagement with [Organization Name] in areas to be audited.
- Co-source providers shall complete an independence declaration before engagement commencement.
- The Head of Internal Audit retains accountability for the independence and quality of all co-sourced audit work.
- Rotation of co-source providers shall be considered at least every **[X] years** to preserve independence.
- The BAC shall be informed of any co-sourcing arrangements that exceed **[X]%** of the annual IT audit plan.

## 6. Audit Independence Requirements

### 6.1 Structural Independence Safeguards

*Detail the structural mechanisms that protect organizational independence.*

The following structural safeguards are in place to protect the independence of the IT audit function:

- **Direct Board Access:** The Head of Internal Audit has the right to meet privately with the BAC Chairman at any time without management present.
- **Budget Autonomy:** The Internal Audit budget is proposed by the Head of Internal Audit and approved directly by the BAC, without modification by executive management.
- **Staffing Authority:** Decisions on IT audit staffing, including hiring, promotion, and termination, are approved or endorsed by the BAC.
- **Protected Reporting Lines:** The Internal Audit function's reporting line to the BAC is enshrined in the Board-approved Internal Audit Charter and cannot be altered without BAC approval.

### 6.2 Independence Threat Categories

*Enumerate and define the categories of independence threats applicable to IT audit activities.*

The following threat categories shall be assessed by auditors and the Head of Internal Audit:

| Threat Category | Description | Example in IT Audit Context |
|---|---|---|
| **Self-Interest Threat** | Financial or other interests that could unduly influence judgment | Auditor holds shares in an IT vendor being assessed |
| **Self-Review Threat** | Auditing one's own prior work | Auditor reviews a system they previously implemented |
| **Advocacy Threat** | Promoting an auditee's position to a point that objectivity is compromised | Auditor previously championed a system that is now under review |
| **Familiarity Threat** | Long association with auditees leading to excessive sympathy | Auditor has audited the same team for multiple consecutive years |
| **Intimidation Threat** | Actual or perceived pressure from auditee management | Technology management threatens resource reduction if findings are reported |

### 6.3 Independence Assessment Process

*Define the process for identifying, evaluating, and addressing independence threats.*

**Step 1 — Identify:** Before each engagement, the assigned auditor(s) shall complete an **Engagement Independence Assessment Form** (refer Appendix A) to identify any actual or potential independence threats.

**Step 2 — Evaluate:** The Head of Internal Audit shall evaluate the significance of the identified threat using the following criteria:

- Nature and materiality of the threat
- Duration and frequency of the relationship
- Likelihood of undue influence
- Perception of independence by stakeholders

**Step 3 — Address:** Based on the evaluation, one of the following actions shall be taken:

| Threat Level | Action Required |
|---|---|
| **Immaterial / Remote** | Document assessment, proceed with engagement, note in audit file |
| **Significant but Mitigable** | Apply safeguard(s) as defined in Section 6.4, obtain HIA approval, document |
| **Material / Unmitigable** | Remove auditor from engagement, reassign, report to BAC if systemic |

**Step 4 — Document:** All independence assessments, regardless of outcome, shall be documented in the audit management system and retained for a minimum of **[X] years**.

### 6.4 Safeguards

*Define the safeguards available to address independence threats.*

Where independence threats are identified but are capable of being reduced to an acceptable level, the following safeguards may be applied:

| Safeguard | Description | Applicable Threat Types |
|---|---|---|
| Reassignment | Replace affected auditor with an unaffected team member | All threats |
| Supervision Enhancement | Increase supervisory review by an independent senior auditor | Self-review, familiarity |
| Peer Review | Require independent review of audit findings and conclusions | Self-review, advocacy |
| Disclosure | Disclose the relationship and safeguards applied in the audit report | Familiarity |
| Rotation | Rotate auditor off recurring engagements | Familiarity |
| BAC Notification | Inform the BAC of the threat and safeguards applied | Intimidation, systemic threats |
| External Validation | Engage an external party to validate findings | Self-review, high-risk engagements |

### 8.2 Audit Engagement Lifecycle

*Outline the standard phases of an IT audit engagement.*

Each IT audit engagement shall follow the structured lifecycle below:

#### Phase 1 — Planning

- Issue engagement notification to auditee
- Complete independence assessment (refer Section 6.3)
- Conduct preliminary risk assessment and define audit scope and objectives
- Develop engagement-level audit program
- Obtain BAC/HIA approval for scope

#### Phase 2 — Fieldwork

- Conduct opening meeting with auditee
- Execute audit program: interviews, walkthroughs, control testing, data analytics
- Document working papers in accordance with documentation standards
- Identify potential findings and discuss with auditee management

#### Phase 3 — Reporting

- Draft audit report with findings, root cause analysis, and recommendations
- Conduct closing meeting with auditee to discuss draft findings
- Obtain management responses and agreed action plans
- Finalize and issue audit report with HIA sign-off
- Distribute report to BAC and relevant stakeholders

#### Phase 4 — Follow-Up

- Track management's implementation of agreed action plans
- Conduct validation testing to confirm remediation
- Report outstanding items to BAC at each quarterly meeting
- Escalate overdue or inadequately remediated findings per Section 11

## 9. Finding Severity Classification

### 9.1 Severity Classification Framework

*Define how audit findings are classified by severity to prioritize management response and remediation.*

All IT audit findings shall be assigned a severity rating based on the potential impact of the identified deficiency on the organization's risk profile, regulatory compliance posture, and operational resilience.

### 9.2 Severity Rating Definitions

| Severity Rating | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | A control deficiency or non-compliance that poses an immediate and material risk of significant financial loss, regulatory sanction, reputational damage, or systemic failure. Requires immediate escalation and remediation. | Unmitigated critical vulnerability in a public-facing system; complete absence of key regulatory control; evidence of fraud or data breach |
| **High** | A significant control gap or weakness that substantially increases the likelihood of a material adverse event if left unaddressed. Requires urgent remediation within a defined short timeframe. | Privileged access not reviewed for 12+ months; critical patches unapplied; significant PDPA control failure |
| **Medium** | A control deficiency that, while not immediately critical, represents a meaningful gap in the control environment that could escalate over time or contribute to cumulative risk. | Inadequate change management controls; inconsistent security configuration standards; gaps in audit logging |
| **Low** | A minor control weakness or process improvement opportunity that presents limited immediate risk but should be addressed to strengthen the overall control environment. | Incomplete documentation; minor policy deviations; process inefficiencies with limited risk impact |
| **Advisory / Observation** | A recommendation for best practice improvement where no specific control failure has been identified. No mandatory management response is required. | Suggestions to align with emerging best practices or enhance maturity beyond minimum requirements |

### 9.3 Remediation Timelines

*Specify the maximum permitted timeframes for management to remediate findings by severity.*

| Severity Rating | Required Management Response | Maximum Remediation Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Within **[5] business days** | **[30] calendar days** (or immediate if operational risk) |
| **High** | Within **[10] business days** | **[90] calendar days** |
| **Medium** | Within **[15] business days** | **[180] calendar days** |
| **Low** | Within **[20] business days** | **[12] months** |
| **Advisory** | Discretionary | Discretionary |

### 9.4 Escalation for Overdue Findings

- Findings that are not remediated within the agreed timeframe shall be flagged as **overdue** in the audit management system.
- Overdue **Critical** and **High** findings shall be escalated to the BAC immediately.
- Overdue **Medium** findings shall be reported to the BAC at the next quarterly BAC meeting.
- Where management disputes a finding, the matter shall be escalated to the HIA for adjudication. Unresolved disputes shall be reported to the BAC.

### 9.5 Repeat Findings

*Define how findings that recur across audit cycles are treated.*

A finding is classified as a **Repeat Finding** if a substantially similar finding was raised in a prior audit engagement and the agreed remediation action has not been sustainably implemented.

Repeat findings shall:

- Be clearly identified as repeat findings in the audit report, with reference to the original finding.
- Automatically be rated at least one severity level higher than the original finding.
- Result in an escalation report to the BAC with an analysis of root cause and management's remediation failure.

## 10. Conflict of Interest and Safeguards

### 10.1 Conflict of Interest Policy Requirements

*Detail the specific conflict of interest requirements applicable to IT audit personnel.*

All members of the Internal Audit function shall be subject to the organization's Conflict of Interest Policy and the following IT audit-specific requirements.

### 10.2 Prohibited Activities and Relationships

*List activities and relationships that are prohibited for IT audit personnel to protect independence.*

IT audit personnel are prohibited from:

- Holding a financial interest (shares, bonds, options, or other instruments) in any technology vendor, service provider, or external party that is subject to audit, where such interest could influence audit judgment.
- Accepting gifts, hospitality, or other benefits from auditees, technology vendors, or parties whose activities are within the audit universe, beyond the limits permitted by the [Organization Name] Code of Conduct.
- Engaging in outside employment or consultancy with technology vendors, financial institutions, or parties that create a conflict with audit responsibilities, without prior HIA and BAC approval.
- Participating in audit engagements covering systems, processes, or units for which they previously held operational or advisory responsibility within the cooling-off period defined in Section 5.1.3.
- Engaging in personal relationships with key personnel in audited units where such relationships could impair objectivity.

### 10.3 Annual Independence Declaration

*Specify the contents and process for the annual independence declaration.*

All IT audit personnel shall complete and submit an **Annual Independence Declaration** by **[31 January]** each year. The declaration shall cover:

- Confirmation of no prohibited financial interests
- Disclosure of any outside employment or consultancy
- Disclosure of any personal relationships with personnel in audited units
- Disclosure of any prior operational roles in areas within the audit universe
- Confirmation of compliance with this policy and the Code of Conduct
- Any other matters that could impair independence or objectivity

Declarations shall be reviewed by the Head of Internal Audit and any disclosures assessed for materiality. Material disclosures shall be escalated to the BAC.

### 10.4 Cooling-Off Period Requirements

| Scenario | Cooling-Off Period |
|---|---|
| IT auditor previously held an operational role in a technology unit | **[24] months** from departure before auditing that unit |
| Technology unit staff seconded to Internal Audit | **[12] months** cooling-in period before auditing former unit |
| HIA previously held a senior technology management role | **[36] months** before assuming responsibility for audits of that domain |
| External auditor joins Internal Audit | **[12] months** before auditing entities covered in their external audit role |

## 11. Reporting and Escalation

### 11.1 Audit Reporting Requirements

*Define the required elements of IT audit reports and the reporting hierarchy.*

#### 11.1.1 Audit Report Contents

All IT audit reports shall include, at minimum:

- Audit title, reference number, and date
- Executive summary with overall audit opinion
- Audit scope, objectives, and methodology
- List of findings with severity classification, root cause, and management response
- Summary of prior findings followed up during the engagement
- Auditor's independence statement
- Distribution list

#### 11.1.2 Audit Opinion Scale

| Opinion | Description |
|---|---|
| **Satisfactory** | The control environment is adequate and effective. No material deficiencies identified. |
| **Needs Improvement** | The control environment is generally adequate but contains specific weaknesses requiring attention. |
| **Unsatisfactory** | The control environment contains significant deficiencies that pose material risk to the organization. Urgent management action is required. |

### 11.2 Reporting to the Board Audit Committee

*Define the format and frequency of IT audit reporting to the BAC.*

| Report | Frequency | Key Contents |
|---|---|---|
| **Audit Plan Execution Status Report** | Quarterly | Engagements completed, in progress, deferred; significant findings summary |
| **Individual Audit Reports** | Per engagement | Full audit report per Section 11.1.1 |
| **Annual IT Audit Summary Report** | Annual | Year-end plan completion, overall audit opinion, themes and trends, RBAP for coming year |
| **Independence Report** | Annual | Summary of independence declarations, any conflicts identified and resolved, co-sourcing overview |
| **Ad Hoc Escalation Reports** | As required | Critical findings, independence breaches, significant unresolved disputes |

### 11.3 Escalation Procedures

*Define the escalation path for critical findings, independence breaches, and unresolved issues.*

| Trigger | Escalation Path | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Critical finding identified | HIA → BAC Chairman (immediate notification) | Within **[24] hours** |
| Independence breach identified | Affected auditor → HIA → BAC Chairman | Within **[2] business days** |

| Overdue Critical finding | HIA → BAC (standing agenda item) | At next BAC meeting or sooner |
| Suspected fraud or irregularity | HIA → BAC Chairman and Board Chairman | Immediately |

*Define the process for approving temporary deviations from this policy.*

- Subject to compensating controls to mitigate the risk arising from the exception.

### Appendix F — Glossary of IT Audit Terms

*Supplementary reference glossary for readers unfamiliar with IT audit terminology.*

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Access Control Review** | An audit procedure assessing whether user access rights to systems are appropriate, authorized, and regularly reviewed. |
| **Application Control** | Automated controls embedded within an application system to ensure completeness, accuracy, and validity of data processing. |
| **General IT Control (GITC)** | Controls that apply broadly across the IT environment, including access management, change management, and IT operations. |
| **IT General Control (ITGC)** | Synonymous with General IT Control; foundational controls over the IT environment that underpin the reliability of application controls. |
| **Penetration Testing** | Authorized simulated cyberattack against an organization's IT systems to identify exploitable security vulnerabilities. |
| **Privileged Access** | Access rights that grant elevated capabilities within a system, typically held by system administrators, database administrators, and security personnel. |
| **Vulnerability Assessment** | A systematic review of security weaknesses in an information system, evaluating the system's susceptibility to known vulnerabilities. |
