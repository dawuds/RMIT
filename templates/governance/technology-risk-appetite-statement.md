## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section articulates why this document exists and what it seeks to achieve within the organisation's overall risk governance framework.*

This Technology Risk Appetite Statement ("Statement") defines the nature and quantum of technology risk that [Organization Name] ("the Organisation") is willing to accept, tolerate, or avoid in pursuit of its strategic business objectives. It establishes a common language and framework for technology risk decision-making across all levels of the Organisation, from the Board of Directors to operational technology teams.

This Statement is issued under the authority of the Board of Directors and constitutes a board-approved governance instrument. It forms a foundational component of [Organization Name]'s Enterprise Risk Management ("ERM") framework and Technology Risk Management ("TRM") programme.

The objectives of this Statement are to:

- Establish explicit boundaries within which technology risk-taking is permitted and encouraged;
- Provide measurable thresholds and qualitative guidance for identifying, assessing, and responding to technology risk;
- Ensure alignment between technology risk exposure and the Organisation's overall corporate risk appetite;
- Fulfil regulatory obligations under Bank Negara Malaysia's Risk Management in Technology ("RMiT") Policy Document; and
- Enable consistent and defensible risk decision-making across all technology domains.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the organisational, geographic, and technology boundaries to which this Statement applies.*

This Statement applies to:

**Organisational Scope:**

- All business units, departments, subsidiaries, and branches of [Organization Name];
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and third-party service providers who operate, maintain, or have access to the Organisation's technology assets and information systems;
- All members of the Board, Board Committees, and Senior Management with oversight responsibilities for technology risk.

**Technology Scope:**

- All information systems, applications, and platforms operated or outsourced by [Organization Name];
- All technology infrastructure, including networks, servers, endpoints, cloud environments, and operational technology;
- All data assets — whether structured or unstructured — processed, stored, or transmitted by the Organisation's technology estate;
- Third-party technology service providers and managed service arrangements;
- Digital products and services offered to customers of [Organization Name].

**Geographic Scope:**

- All domestic operations within Malaysia;
- All overseas operations, branches, and representative offices of [Organization Name] where applicable local regulations permit adoption of this Statement.

### 1.3 Effective Date and Supersession

This Statement is effective from [Effective Date] and supersedes all prior versions of the Technology Risk Appetite Statement issued by [Organization Name]. Where conflict arises between this Statement and subordinate policy documents, this Statement shall prevail unless a more restrictive control has been approved by the appropriate governing body.

## 3. Technology Risk Appetite Framework

### 3.1 Risk Appetite Philosophy

*Describe the organisation's overall philosophy and attitude toward technology risk-taking — this sets the tone for all subsequent thresholds and statements.*

[Organization Name] adopts a **conservative-to-moderate** technology risk appetite that reflects:

- Our obligations as a regulated financial institution to safeguard customer assets and data;
- The systemic importance of reliable, secure, and resilient technology in delivering financial services;
- Our commitment to continuous investment in technology risk management capabilities; and
- Our strategic ambition to adopt innovative technologies while maintaining prudent risk controls.

The Organisation's risk appetite is not static. It evolves in response to changes in the threat landscape, business strategy, regulatory expectations, and operational capability.

### 3.2 Risk Appetite Dimensions

Technology risk appetite is expressed across the following dimensions:

| Dimension | Description | Appetite Position |
|---|---|---|
| **Availability** | Tolerance for system downtime and service disruption | Low — critical system outages are not tolerated |
| **Confidentiality** | Tolerance for unauthorised disclosure of information | Very Low — data breaches are not tolerated |
| **Integrity** | Tolerance for unauthorised modification of data or systems | Very Low — data integrity failures are not tolerated |
| **Cyber Resilience** | Tolerance for successful cyber attacks causing material impact | Very Low — material cyber incidents are not tolerated |
| **Technology Change** | Tolerance for risk introduced by technology change | Moderate — managed change with defined controls |
| **Third-Party Technology** | Tolerance for risk arising from outsourced technology | Low — stringent due diligence and contractual controls required |
| **Regulatory Compliance** | Tolerance for non-compliance with technology regulations | Zero — regulatory breaches are not tolerated |
| **Innovation / Emerging Technology** | Tolerance for risk from adoption of new technologies | Moderate — sandboxed pilots with defined risk guardrails |

### 3.3 Risk Appetite versus Risk Tolerance versus Risk Limit

For clarity, the following definitions apply throughout this Statement:

- **Risk Appetite:** The aggregate level and type of risk [Organization Name] is willing to accept in pursuit of its strategic objectives, before controls are applied.
- **Risk Tolerance:** The acceptable deviation from the risk appetite, representing the boundary within which the Organisation will operate. Tolerance levels define the range of acceptable outcomes.
- **Risk Limit:** The specific quantitative threshold that, when breached, triggers mandatory escalation and remediation action. Limits operate within the bounds of tolerance.
- **Risk Capacity:** The maximum level of risk [Organization Name] is able to absorb given its capital, resources, and capabilities — this represents the outer boundary and must never be approached.

### 5.2 Risk Classification Matrix

Technology risks shall be classified using the following likelihood-impact matrix. All identified risks must be assigned a risk rating prior to reporting.

**Likelihood Scale:**

| Rating | Descriptor | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Almost Certain | Expected to occur in most circumstances (>90% probability within 12 months) |
| 4 | Likely | Will probably occur in most circumstances (50–90% probability within 12 months) |
| 3 | Possible | Might occur at some time (20–50% probability within 12 months) |
| 2 | Unlikely | Could occur at some time (5–20% probability within 12 months) |
| 1 | Rare | May only occur in exceptional circumstances (<5% probability within 12 months) |

**Impact Scale:**

| Rating | Descriptor | Financial Impact | Operational Impact | Reputational Impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | Catastrophic | >RM [Amount] | Extended outage >72 hours of critical system | Systemic public confidence failure; regulatory sanction |
| 4 | Major | RM [Amount] – RM [Amount] | Outage 24–72 hours of critical system | Significant adverse media; regulatory inquiry |
| 3 | Moderate | RM [Amount] – RM [Amount] | Outage 4–24 hours of critical system | Limited adverse media; internal escalation |
| 2 | Minor | RM [Amount] – RM [Amount] | Outage <4 hours; degraded service | Isolated customer complaints |
| 1 | Insignificant | <RM [Amount] | Minor disruption; no customer impact | No external visibility |

**Risk Rating Matrix:**

| | **Impact 1** | **Impact 2** | **Impact 3** | **Impact 4** | **Impact 5** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Likelihood 5** | Medium (5) | High (10) | High (15) | Critical (20) | Critical (25) |
| **Likelihood 4** | Low (4) | Medium (8) | High (12) | High (16) | Critical (20) |
| **Likelihood 3** | Low (3) | Medium (6) | Medium (9) | High (12) | High (15) |
| **Likelihood 2** | Low (2) | Low (4) | Medium (6) | Medium (8) | High (10) |
| **Likelihood 1** | Low (1) | Low (2) | Low (3) | Low (4) | Medium (5) |

| Rating | Score Range | Risk Appetite Response |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | 20–25 | Outside appetite — immediate escalation to Board; mandatory remediation |
| **High** | 10–16 | Approaches tolerance limit — escalation to Risk Committee; remediation plan required |
| **Medium** | 5–9 | Within tolerance — Senior Management monitoring; mitigation plan required |
| **Low** | 1–4 | Within appetite — operational management; routine monitoring |

## 6. Ownership and Custodianship

*This section defines the accountability framework for technology assets and risk exposures. Clear ownership is essential for effective risk appetite governance and ensures every risk has an identified accountable party.*

### 6.1 Ownership Principles

[Organization Name] applies the following principles to technology asset and risk ownership:

- Every technology asset and every identified technology risk must have a designated **Owner**;
- Asset Owners bear ultimate accountability for the risk associated with their assets;
- **Custodians** are responsible for day-to-day management, control operation, and monitoring on behalf of Owners;
- Ownership cannot be delegated below Head of Department level for Tier 1 critical assets;
- Ownership assignments must be reviewed at least annually and upon any material organisational change.

### 6.2 Ownership Definitions

| Role | Definition | Accountability Level |

| **Asset Owner** | The business or technology executive accountable for the strategic use, risk, and value realisation of a technology asset | Accountable |

## 8. Qualitative Risk Tolerance Statements

*This section sets out the Organisation's position on technology risk in plain language. These statements guide risk decision-making where quantitative metrics are absent or insufficient, and provide authoritative guidance to management and staff.*

### 8.1 Overarching Risk Tolerance Position

[Organization Name] has **zero tolerance** for technology risk events that result in:

- Material harm to customers, including financial loss, identity theft, or prolonged service denial;
- Regulatory sanction, enforcement action, or loss of operating licence;
- Confirmed and material breaches of customer personal data; or
- Fundamental compromise of the integrity of the Organisation's financial records.

In all other technology risk domains, [Organization Name] applies a **conservative** risk tolerance, accepting only residual risk that remains after the application of cost-effective, proportionate controls.

## 9. Breach Escalation Triggers and Response

*This section establishes the mandatory actions, escalation pathways, and communication requirements that are triggered when technology risk appetite thresholds are approached or breached. Timely escalation is a regulatory obligation under BNM RMiT.*

### 9.1 Escalation Principles

[Organization Name] applies the following principles to risk appetite breach escalation:

- **Speed:** Escalation must occur promptly. Delays in escalation are themselves a governance failure and will be treated as a breach of this Statement;
- **Transparency:** All escalations must be factual, complete, and free from bias or concealment;
- **Proportionality:** The speed and level of escalation is commensurate with the severity of the breach;
- **Accountability:** The individual who identifies a potential breach is responsible for initiating escalation, regardless of seniority.

### 9.2 Escalation Trigger Events

The following events constitute mandatory escalation triggers regardless of the current KRI status:

| Trigger Category | Trigger Event | Minimum Escalation Level |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical System Outage** | Unplanned outage of any Tier 1 system exceeding [X] minutes | CIO → CRO → CEO within [X] hours |
| **Confirmed Data Breach** | Any confirmed unauthorised access to or exfiltration of customer or confidential data | CISO → CRO → CEO → Board Chair within [X] hours |
| **Ransomware or Destructive Attack** | Activation of ransomware or confirmed destructive malware on any production system | CISO → CRO → CEO → Board within [X] hours |
| **Regulatory Notification Threshold** | Any incident meeting BNM incident reporting thresholds | CRO → CEO → Board → BNM notification within prescribed timeline |
| **KRI Red Threshold Breach** | Any KRI moving from Amber to Red status | Relevant Risk Owner → CRO → Board Risk Committee at next scheduled meeting |
| **Sustained KRI Amber Status** | Any KRI remaining in Amber status for more than [X] consecutive reporting periods | Risk Owner → CRO escalation and formal remediation plan |
| **Financial Loss Threshold** | Technology-related loss exceeding the Amber financial threshold | CRO → CEO → Board Risk Committee |
| **Third-Party Critical Failure** | Material failure of a critical technology service provider | CIO → CRO → CEO |
| **Failed DR Test** | Disaster recovery test failure on any Tier 1 or Tier 2 system | CIO → CRO with remediation plan within [X] business days |

### 9.3 Escalation Pathway and Timelines

| Severity | Definition | Initial Escalation | Time to Escalate | Board Notification |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **P1 — Critical** | Immediate, material impact on customer services, financial integrity, or regulatory obligations | Relevant Head → CRO + CEO simultaneous | Within [X] hours | Within [X] hours; Board Chair immediately |
| **P2 — High** | Significant risk of material impact; KRI at Red; major incident in progress | Relevant Head → CRO | Within [X] hours | At next Board Risk Committee; extraordinary if >48 hours |
| **P3 — Medium** | Elevated risk; KRI at sustained Amber; control failures identified | Risk Owner → CRO | Within [X] business days | Monthly risk report to Board Risk Committee |
| **P4 — Low** | Routine monitoring flags; KRI drifting toward Amber | Risk Owner → Risk Management team | Within [X] business days | Quarterly risk dashboard to Board |

### 9.5 Post-Breach Requirements

Upon confirmation of a risk appetite tolerance breach, the following actions are mandatory:

1. **Immediate Containment:** The relevant Custodian and Control Owner must initiate containment actions within [X] hours of breach confirmation;
2. **Root Cause Analysis:** A formal root cause analysis must be completed within [X] business days using the [Organisation's RCA Methodology];
3. **Remediation Plan:** A documented remediation plan with defined milestones must be submitted to the CRO within [X] business days;
4. **Board Risk Committee Update:** A breach report must be presented at the next scheduled Board Risk Committee meeting, or as an out-of-cycle paper if severity warrants;
5. **Lessons Learned:** Lessons learned must be documented and shared with relevant stakeholders within [X] days of remediation completion;
6. **Appetite Review:** The CRO shall assess whether the breach indicates a need to revise the relevant KRI thresholds or qualitative tolerance statements; and
7. **Regulatory Obligations:** All applicable regulatory notification requirements must be met within prescribed timelines.

### Appendix A — Glossary of Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Business Impact Analysis (BIA)** | A process to determine the impact of disrupting business functions and activities, used to inform recovery priority and resource allocation. |
| **Control** | A measure that modifies risk; includes policies, procedures, practices, and organisational structures that can be of administrative, technical, management, or legal nature. |
| **Critical Information Infrastructure (CII)** | Technology assets and systems so essential to national security, public health, safety, or economic wellbeing that their disruption or destruction would have a debilitating impact. |
| **Cyber Incident** | A breach or attempted breach of an organisation's information security, including unauthorised access, disruption, modification, or destruction of information assets. |
| **Data Breach** | A security incident resulting in the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to personal data or confidential information. |
| **Disaster Recovery (DR)** | The process, policies, and procedures related to preparing for recovery of technology infrastructure after a natural or human-induced disaster. |
| **Key Risk Indicator (KRI)** | A metric used to measure the likelihood that the probability and/or impact of a given risk will exceed the defined risk appetite. |
| **Recovery Point Objective (RPO)** | The maximum targeted period in which data might be lost due to an incident, measured in time. |
| **Recovery Time Objective (RTO)** | The targeted duration within which a business process must be restored after a disaster in order to avoid unacceptable consequences. |
| **Residual Risk** | The level of risk remaining after risk treatment has been applied. |
| **Risk Appetite** | The amount and type of risk an organisation is willing to accept in pursuit of its objectives. |
| **Risk Capacity** | The maximum amount of risk an organisation is able to absorb given its available capital, resources, and capabilities. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | The acceptable deviation from the risk appetite; the boundary within which the organisation will operate. |
| **Single Point of Failure (SPOF)** | Any part of a system that, if it fails, will stop the entire system from working. |
| **Tier 1 — Critical Asset** | A technology asset whose failure would cause immediate and material disruption to core business functions, customer services, or regulatory obligations. |
| **Vulnerability** | A weakness in an information system, system security procedures, internal controls, or implementation that could be exploited by a threat source. |
