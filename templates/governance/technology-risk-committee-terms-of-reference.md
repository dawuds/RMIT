## 1. Purpose and Authority

*This section establishes the legal and regulatory basis for the Technology Risk Committee, articulates why the Committee exists, and describes the delegated authority granted to it by the Board of Directors. Authors should ensure this section directly references the applicable BNM RMiT clauses and aligns with the institution's Board-approved governance framework.*

### 1.1 Purpose

The Technology Risk Committee (hereinafter referred to as "the Committee" or "TRC") of [Organization Name] (hereinafter referred to as "the Institution") is established pursuant to the requirements of Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically **Clause 8.2**, and the Institution's Board-approved governance framework.

The Committee exists to provide focused, Board-level oversight of technology risk across the Institution, ensuring that risks arising from the use, adoption, and management of technology are identified, assessed, monitored, and controlled within the Institution's approved risk appetite.

The Committee serves as the primary governance body responsible for:

- Providing strategic direction and oversight of the Institution's technology risk management framework.
- Ensuring alignment between technology strategy, business objectives, and risk tolerance.
- Overseeing compliance with all applicable regulatory requirements pertaining to technology risk, including but not limited to the BNM RMiT Policy Document.
- Escalating material technology risk matters to the Board of Directors for decision or notation.
- Providing independent challenge and assurance on technology risk reporting submitted by Management.

### 1.2 Authority

The Committee derives its authority from a formal delegation by the **Board of Directors of [Organization Name]**, as documented in the Board resolution dated **[Board Resolution Date]**, Reference No. **[Board Resolution Reference]**.

The Committee is authorized to:

- **Direct** Management to implement, modify, or discontinue technology-related activities where unacceptable risk exposure is identified.
- **Approve** technology risk policies, frameworks, and tolerance thresholds within the limits delegated by the Board.
- **Commission** independent reviews, assessments, or audits of technology risk matters.
- **Escalate** issues to the full Board where risks exceed the Committee's delegated approval authority or require Board-level decision.
- **Request** any information, report, or presentation from Management, Internal Audit, or external parties necessary to discharge its oversight responsibilities.

The Committee shall not assume Management's responsibility for day-to-day technology risk management but shall hold Management accountable for executing the approved technology risk framework effectively.

## 3. Responsibilities

*This section details the specific functions and duties the Committee must discharge. Authors should align these responsibilities directly to BNM RMiT Clause 8.2 obligations and ensure they are specific enough to be measurable at the annual Board effectiveness review.*

### 3.1 Strategic Oversight

The Committee shall:

- Review and recommend to the Board for approval the Institution's **Technology Risk Strategy**, ensuring it supports the overall business strategy and remains within the Board-approved risk appetite.
- Oversee the development and maintenance of the **Technology Risk Management Framework (TRMF)**, including associated policies, standards, and procedures.
- Provide oversight of the Institution's **technology roadmap** and significant technology transformation initiatives to ensure technology risk is adequately considered.
- Evaluate technology risk implications of material strategic decisions, mergers, acquisitions, and new product launches.

### 3.2 Risk Governance and Oversight

The Committee shall:

- Review and approve the Institution's **technology risk appetite statement** and key risk indicators (KRIs) for technology risk, recommending to the Board for final approval.
- Monitor the Institution's technology risk profile against approved appetite thresholds and direct Management to take corrective action where breaches or near-breaches are identified.
- Oversee the identification, assessment, treatment, and monitoring of **material technology risks**, including cybersecurity risks, IT operational risks, and third-party technology risks.
- Review and approve significant changes to the Institution's **cybersecurity framework**, including cybersecurity strategy, architecture principles, and incident response plans.
- Oversee the Institution's **technology incident management** capabilities, including the review of post-incident reports for significant technology incidents.
- Review findings and Management responses arising from **technology risk assessments**, penetration tests, vulnerability assessments, and red team exercises.

### 3.3 Regulatory Compliance Oversight

The Committee shall:

- Oversee the Institution's compliance with BNM RMiT requirements and all other applicable technology-related regulatory obligations.
- Review regulatory examination findings, audit observations, and Management Action Plans (MAPs) relating to technology risk.
- Monitor the timely remediation of regulatory findings and report status to the Board.
- Ensure the Institution maintains adequate documentation and evidence to demonstrate regulatory compliance.

### 3.4 Third-Party and Outsourcing Risk

The Committee shall:

- Oversee the governance of **technology outsourcing arrangements**, including cloud services, critical service providers, and fintech partnerships.
- Review and approve material technology outsourcing arrangements prior to execution or renewal, in accordance with the Institution's Outsourcing Policy.
- Monitor the performance and risk profile of critical third-party technology service providers.

### 3.5 Technology Resilience and Business Continuity

The Committee shall:

- Oversee the Institution's **technology resilience** program, including disaster recovery (DR) and technology-related business continuity plans.
- Review the results of DR and business continuity tests and assess the adequacy of recovery capabilities.
- Monitor the Institution's ability to maintain critical technology services within approved Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs).

### 3.6 Data Governance and Privacy

The Committee shall:

- Oversee the Institution's **data governance framework** as it relates to technology risk, including data classification, data integrity, and data availability.
- Receive reports on data incidents and privacy breaches with technology risk dimensions.
- [Additional data governance responsibilities as applicable under PDPA 2010 or BNM data-related guidelines.]

## 6. Reporting Obligations

*This section defines what the Committee must report to the Board, what Management must report to the Committee, and what information flows to regulators. Authors should specify report formats, timing, escalation thresholds, and the regulatory submission requirements under BNM RMiT.*

### 6.1 Reporting to the Board of Directors

The Committee shall report to the full Board as follows:

| Report | Frequency | Content |
|---|---|---|
| **Technology Risk Committee Report** | After each quarterly meeting | Summary of deliberations, decisions made, material risks noted, and recommendations to the Board |
| **Annual Technology Risk Review** | Annually (Q4) | Comprehensive review of the Institution's technology risk profile, framework effectiveness, and forward-looking risk assessment |
| **Material Incident Escalation** | Ad hoc (immediately upon determination of materiality) | Summary of incident, impact assessment, regulatory notification status, and remediation plan |
| **Regulatory Examination Update** | As required | Status of BNM or other regulatory findings related to technology risk |

### 6.2 Management Reporting to the Committee

Management shall submit the following standing reports to each ordinary meeting of the Committee:

| Report | Owner | Frequency | Key Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Technology Risk Dashboard** | CRO | Quarterly | KRI status, risk appetite utilization, top risks, emerging risks |
| **Cybersecurity Threat Report** | CISO | Quarterly | Threat landscape, incidents, vulnerability status, security metrics |
| **Technology Incident Register** | CTO / CISO | Quarterly | All incidents above [Severity Threshold], RCA summaries, remediation status |
| **Third-Party Technology Risk Report** | CRO | Quarterly | Critical vendor performance, outsourcing risk status |
| **IT Audit Findings Tracker** | Head of Internal Audit | Quarterly | Open findings, overdue MAPs, newly raised observations |
| **Regulatory Compliance Tracker** | CCO | Quarterly | RMiT compliance status, upcoming regulatory deadlines |
| **DR / BCP Test Results** | CTO | Semi-annually | Test outcomes, gaps identified, remediation actions |
| **Technology Risk Appetite Report** | CRO | Quarterly | Breaches, near-breaches, trending metrics |

### 6.3 Escalation Thresholds

Management shall escalate the following to the Committee on an **immediate or ad hoc basis**, outside the normal quarterly reporting cycle:

| Escalation Trigger | Escalation Mechanism | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Technology incident classified as **Critical** (P1) | Emergency notification to Chairperson; extraordinary meeting if required | Immediately upon classification |
| Technology incident with **regulatory notification obligation** under RMiT | Notification to Chairperson and CCO | Within [e.g., 24 hours] of determination |
| Technology risk KRI breach exceeding **[e.g., 150% of appetite threshold]** | Written report to Chairperson | Within [e.g., 3 business days] |
| Discovery of a **material control failure** in technology risk management | Written report to Chairperson | Within [e.g., 5 business days] |
| Significant **cybersecurity event** with potential for data loss or service disruption | Emergency notification to Chairperson | Immediately upon determination |
| Receipt of a **BNM regulatory notice** related to technology risk | Notification to Chairperson | Within [e.g., 1 business day] |

### 6.4 Regulatory Reporting

*Authors should detail any direct regulatory reporting obligations the Committee or its members have to BNM or other regulators, including mandatory incident notifications under RMiT.*

The Committee shall ensure that the Institution discharges its regulatory reporting obligations under BNM RMiT, including:

- Notification to BNM of **significant technology incidents** as defined under the RMiT Policy Document within the stipulated timeframes.
- Submission of any **technology risk-related returns** required by BNM.
- Response to BNM supervisory inquiries related to technology risk governance.

The Committee shall receive confirmation from Management and the CCO that all regulatory notifications have been submitted on a timely basis.
