## 1. Purpose and Authority

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section defines why the Technology Risk Function exists, the regulatory imperative for its establishment, and the overarching intent of this Charter. Authors should articulate the strategic and compliance rationale clearly.*

This Charter establishes the mandate, authority, structure, and operating principles of the **Technology Risk Function** (hereafter referred to as "the Function") of **[Organization Name]** (hereafter referred to as "the Institution"). The Function is constituted as a dedicated, independent second-line-of-defence capability responsible for the identification, assessment, monitoring, and oversight of technology risks across the Institution's operations.

The Charter gives effect to the Institution's obligations under Bank Negara Malaysia's Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, ensuring that technology risk management is conducted with appropriate rigour, independence, and board-level visibility commensurate with the Institution's risk profile and systemic importance.

### 1.2 Authority

*This section articulates the source and extent of the Function's authority, including delegated powers from the Board, the CRO, and relevant governance committees. Describe enforcement powers and escalation rights.*

The Technology Risk Function derives its authority from:

- The **Board of Directors** of [Organization Name], through approval of this Charter and the Institution's Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) Framework;
- The **Risk Management Committee (RMC)** of the Board, which provides oversight of technology risk as part of its broader risk governance mandate;
- The **Chief Risk Officer (CRO)**, who is the accountable executive sponsor of the Function and to whom the Head of Technology Risk reports; and
- The **RMiT Policy Document (Clause 8.7)** issued by Bank Negara Malaysia, which mandates the existence and independence of a dedicated technology risk oversight function.

The Function is authorised to:

- **Access** all technology systems, infrastructure, data, processes, and personnel necessary to discharge its oversight responsibilities;
- **Commission** independent assessments, audits, or reviews of technology risk where deemed necessary;
- **Escalate** technology risk issues directly to the CRO, RMC, and Board without requiring the consent of business or technology line management;
- **Challenge** risk acceptance decisions made by the first line of defence and escalate unresolved disagreements to the CRO or RMC;
- **Issue** binding risk directives and remediation instructions for critical technology risk exposures, subject to CRO endorsement; and
- **Recommend** suspension or limitation of technology services or activities where unacceptable risk exposure is identified.

### 1.3 Guiding Principles

*List the core principles that govern how the Function operates — independence, proportionality, transparency, etc.*

The Technology Risk Function shall operate in accordance with the following principles:

- **Independence:** The Function maintains organisational and operational independence from the first line of defence (technology and business units) to ensure objective oversight.
- **Proportionality:** Risk oversight activities are calibrated to the materiality and nature of technology risk exposures facing the Institution.
- **Transparency:** Findings, assessments, and opinions are communicated clearly, accurately, and in a timely manner to relevant stakeholders.
- **Proactivity:** The Function anticipates emerging technology risks and informs the Institution's risk posture before adverse events materialise.
- **Accountability:** All personnel within the Function are held to professional standards and are accountable for the quality and integrity of their risk assessments.
- **Collaboration:** The Function works constructively with the first and third lines of defence while maintaining appropriate separation of responsibilities.

## 2. Scope and Responsibilities

### 2.1 Scope

*Define the boundaries of the Function's remit — which entities, systems, risk types, and activities are in scope. Be explicit about what is excluded and why.*

The Technology Risk Function's oversight remit encompasses the following:

#### 2.1.1 Entities in Scope

| Entity | Applicability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [Organization Name] (Parent) | Full scope | All technology systems and operations |
| [Subsidiary 1] | Full scope | [Note on applicability] |
| [Subsidiary 2] | Partial scope | [Describe boundaries] |
| Outsourced Service Providers | In scope for material outsourcing | RMiT Clause 10 applicability |
| Cloud Service Providers | In scope | Subject to cloud risk framework |
| Third-party technology vendors | In scope for critical dependencies | [Note criteria for criticality] |

#### 2.1.2 Technology Risk Domains in Scope

The Function provides oversight across the following technology risk domains:

- **Cybersecurity Risk** — including threat intelligence, vulnerability management, incident response, and security architecture;
- **IT Operational Risk** — including system availability, change management, incident and problem management, and capacity planning;
- **Data and Information Risk** — including data governance, data quality, data loss prevention, and compliance with the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA);
- **Technology Resilience Risk** — including business continuity, disaster recovery, recovery time objectives (RTO), and recovery point objectives (RPO);
- **Third-Party and Outsourcing Risk** — including vendor management, cloud service provider risk, and material outsourcing arrangements;
- **Emerging Technology Risk** — including risks arising from artificial intelligence, machine learning, distributed ledger technology, and quantum computing;
- **Technology Project and Change Risk** — including risks associated with major system implementations, migrations, and technology transformation programmes; and
- **Regulatory Technology (RegTech) Risk** — including risks in systems used for regulatory reporting and compliance.

#### 2.1.3 Out of Scope

The following matters are explicitly outside the Function's primary remit:

- Day-to-day technology operations and service delivery (first-line responsibility);
- Internal audit of technology controls (third-line responsibility, per [Organization Name]'s Internal Audit Charter);
- Commercial decisions on technology procurement (subject to procurement policy governance).

### 2.2 Core Responsibilities

*Detail the specific activities the Function must perform to fulfil its mandate. These should map directly to RMiT requirements and internal governance expectations.*

#### 2.2.1 Risk Identification and Assessment

- Maintain and continuously update the **Technology Risk Register** to reflect the current risk landscape;
- Conduct periodic **Technology Risk Assessments (TRA)** at the institutional, domain, and system levels;
- Perform **risk profiling** of critical systems, infrastructure, and third-party dependencies;
- Review and validate **first-line risk self-assessments** and challenge where appropriate; and
- Assess the technology risk implications of new products, services, and strategic initiatives prior to launch or approval.

#### 2.2.2 Risk Monitoring and Reporting

- Define, maintain, and monitor a suite of **Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)** and **Key Control Indicators (KCIs)** for technology risk;
- Prepare and submit **Technology Risk Reports** to the CRO, RMC, and Board as stipulated in this Charter;
- Track the status of **risk remediation actions** and escalate overdue or stalled items; and
- Maintain a **technology risk dashboard** providing real-time visibility of the Institution's technology risk posture.

#### 2.2.3 Policy and Standards Governance

- Develop, maintain, and periodically review technology risk policies, standards, and procedures;
- Ensure alignment of internal policies with BNM RMiT requirements, NACSA cybersecurity guidelines, PDPA obligations, and international standards (ISO/IEC 27001, NIST CSF, COBIT);
- Provide **second-line review and approval** of technology control frameworks proposed by the first line; and
- Issue risk guidance and advisories to assist business and technology units in managing technology risk.

#### 2.2.4 Incident and Crisis Oversight

- Provide **second-line oversight** of material technology incidents, including cybersecurity breaches and critical system outages;
- Review post-incident reports and verify adequacy of root cause analysis and corrective actions;
- Escalate **reportable incidents** to BNM in accordance with RMiT reporting obligations; and
- Participate in **crisis management exercises** and validate the Institution's technology resilience posture.

#### 2.2.5 Regulatory Engagement

- Act as the primary liaison with **Bank Negara Malaysia** on technology risk matters, examinations, and supervisory queries;
- Coordinate responses to **BNM Technology Risk Examinations** and prudential reviews;
- Monitor regulatory developments and assess their impact on the Institution's technology risk framework; and
- Maintain records of **regulatory submissions, responses, and commitments** related to technology risk.

## 3. Organisational Positioning and Independence

### 3.1 Organisational Structure

*Describe where the Technology Risk Function sits within the Institution's organisational hierarchy. Include a description of reporting lines. An organisational chart may be included as an appendix.*

The Technology Risk Function is positioned within the **Risk Management division** of [Organization Name] and reports to the **Chief Risk Officer (CRO)**. The Function operates as a second-line-of-defence unit, structurally and operationally independent from technology and business units that generate technology risk (first line of defence).

The organisational structure of the Function is as follows:

| Role | Reports To | Key Accountability |
|---|---|---|
| Head of Technology Risk | Chief Risk Officer (CRO) | Overall Function leadership and CRO engagement |
| Senior Technology Risk Manager | Head of Technology Risk | Cybersecurity and operational risk oversight |
| Technology Risk Manager | Head of Technology Risk | Risk assessment, monitoring, and reporting |
| Technology Risk Analyst(s) | Technology Risk Manager | Risk data, KRI tracking, and documentation |
| [Additional Role] | [Reporting Line] | [Accountability] |

*Refer to **Appendix A** for the organisational chart of the Technology Risk Function.*

### 3.2 Independence Safeguards

*Articulate the structural and operational safeguards that protect the Function's independence from undue influence by first-line technology and business units.*

To preserve the independence and objectivity of the Technology Risk Function, the following safeguards are in place:

- **Reporting line independence:** The Head of Technology Risk reports directly to the CRO, with a dotted-line reporting relationship to the Board Risk Management Committee;
- **Performance evaluation:** Performance appraisals for Function personnel are conducted by the CRO, without input from the Chief Information Officer (CIO), Chief Technology Officer (CTO), or business heads;
- **Remuneration independence:** Remuneration of Technology Risk Function staff is not linked to technology project delivery metrics or business revenue targets;
- **Escalation rights:** The Head of Technology Risk has the right to escalate matters directly to the RMC and Board without requiring CIO or business approval;
- **Resource control:** The Function controls its own budget and headcount, approved through the CRO and Board; and
- **Conflict of interest management:** Function personnel shall not hold concurrent responsibilities in first-line technology or business roles. Conflicts of interest shall be declared and managed per the Institution's Conflict of Interest Policy.

### 4.2 Quorum Requirements

*Define the minimum attendance required for the Committee to conduct business and make valid decisions.*

- **Quorum for ordinary business:** A quorum shall be constituted by the attendance of at least **[50% + 1] of voting members**, including the Chairperson or a designated alternate;
- **Quorum for extraordinary resolutions** (e.g., risk appetite adjustments, critical escalations): Attendance of at least **[two-thirds] of voting members**, including the CRO;
- **Alternate representation:** Standing members unable to attend may nominate a sufficiently senior alternate in writing, subject to Chairperson approval; and
- **Lapsed quorum:** Where quorum is not achieved, the meeting shall be rescheduled within **[5] business days**, and interim decisions may be taken via written resolution with appropriate documentation.

### 4.3 Decision-Making

- Decisions shall be reached by **simple majority** of voting members present;
- In the event of a tied vote, the **Chairperson holds the casting vote**;
- **Dissenting opinions** shall be recorded in the meeting minutes; and
- Decisions involving unresolved disagreements between first- and second-line views shall be escalated to the **Risk Management Committee (RMC)**.

| **Medium** | Risk exposure approaching appetite threshold; control weakness identified; minor incident closed | Head of Technology Risk | Within 24 hours |
| **Low** | Routine risk findings; control improvements recommended | Technology Risk Manager | Within 5 business days |

### 7.2 Key Role Descriptions

#### 7.2.1 Chief Risk Officer (CRO)

- Accountable to the Board for the effective functioning of the Technology Risk Function;
- Approves the Technology Risk Appetite Statement and escalates material breaches to the RMC;
- Champions an independent risk culture and ensures adequate resourcing of the Function; and
- Acts as the primary point of escalation for unresolved technology risk disputes.

#### 7.2.2 Head of Technology Risk

- Leads the Technology Risk Function on a day-to-day basis;
- Chairs or co-chairs relevant technology risk forums;
- Represents the Function in regulatory engagements with BNM;
- Prepares and presents technology risk reports to the CRO, RMC, and Board; and
- Manages the professional development and performance of Function staff.

#### 7.2.3 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

- Serves as the first-line subject matter expert on cybersecurity risk;
- Provides input to the Function's cybersecurity risk assessments and KRIs;
- Supports incident escalation and regulatory notification processes; and
- Collaborates with the Function on cybersecurity policy development and standards setting.

#### 7.2.4 Chief Information Officer / Chief Technology Officer

- Accountable for technology delivery and operational performance as first-line owner;
- Supports the Function's access to systems, data, and personnel for oversight purposes;
- Implements risk remediation actions as directed by the Function and CRO; and
- Provides transparent reporting on technology risk events and near-misses.

#### 7.2.5 Business Units

- Identify and report technology risks arising from business activities to the Function;
- Implement technology risk controls within their respective domains;
- Participate in technology risk assessments as required; and
- Escalate technology risk concerns to the Function where first-line resolution is insufficient.

## 8. Review and Amendment Process

### 8.1 Scheduled Review

*Define the mandatory review cycle for this Charter and the process by which reviews are conducted.*

This Charter is subject to the following review cadence:

| Review Type | Frequency | Triggered By | Approver |
|---|---|---|---|
| Routine Review | Annually (minimum) | Calendar schedule | CRO and Board |
| Triggered Review | As required | Material regulatory change, structural reorganisation, significant risk event, or BNM direction | CRO and Board |
| Interim Amendment | As required | Minor administrative updates | CRO (with notation to Board) |

The Head of Technology Risk is responsible for initiating and coordinating each review cycle. A review shall assess:

- Continued alignment with BNM RMiT requirements and any amendments thereto;
- Adequacy of the Function's mandate relative to the Institution's current risk profile;
- Effectiveness of governance structures, reporting lines, and escalation protocols;
- Feedback from the RMC, Board, and regulatory examinations; and
- Benchmarking against industry practice and regulatory guidance.

### 8.2 Amendment Process

Amendments to this Charter shall be processed as follows:

1. **Proposal:** The Head of Technology Risk or CRO proposes amendments, with a written rationale;
2. **Consultation:** Proposed amendments are shared with affected stakeholders (CIO, CISO, Compliance, Internal Audit) for comment within **[10] business days**;
3. **CRO Review:** The CRO reviews and endorses proposed amendments;
4. **RMC Review:** Material amendments are presented to the RMC for endorsement;
5. **Board Approval:** Final amendments are submitted to the Board of Directors for approval; and
6. **Publication:** The approved, version-controlled Charter is published on the Institution's internal document management system and communicated to all relevant parties.

### 8.3 Version Control

All versions of this Charter shall be maintained in the Institution's document management system. Superseded versions shall be archived and accessible for audit purposes for a minimum of **[7] years**.
