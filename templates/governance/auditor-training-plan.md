## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section should articulate the overarching purpose of this training plan, explaining why a structured IT audit training programme is required and what organisational objectives it supports.*

This Auditor Training Plan establishes the annual training and professional development framework for the IT audit function at [Organisation Name]. It is developed in accordance with BNM's Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, specifically Clause 13.3, which requires financial institutions to maintain adequate internal audit capabilities commensurate with the institution's technology risk profile.

This plan ensures that auditors possess the requisite competencies to independently assess technology-related risks, evaluate the effectiveness of IT controls, and provide assurance to senior management and the Board that technology risks are being managed appropriately.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the boundaries of this training plan, including which teams, systems, and audit domains are covered. Specify any exclusions and the rationale for them.*

This training plan applies to:

- **Organisational Scope:** All personnel within the [Department Name / Internal Audit Division] who are assigned IT audit responsibilities, including full-time auditors, co-sourced audit staff, and audit management.
- **Functional Scope:** Training covering all domains within the IT audit universe, including but not limited to cybersecurity, application controls, infrastructure, data management, and third-party technology risk.
- **Temporal Scope:** Financial year [FY Year], spanning [Start Date] to [End Date].
- **Regulatory Scope:** Requirements under BNM RMiT (Clause 13.3), Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA), and other applicable BNM policy documents as listed in Section 12.

### 1.3 Alignment to Regulatory Requirements

*Briefly explain how this document satisfies the relevant regulatory obligations. Reference specific clauses.*

## 7. Finding Severity Classification

### 7.1 Severity Classification Framework

*Define the finding severity classification system used within [Organisation Name]'s IT audit function. Auditors must be trained to apply this framework consistently and objectively.*

All IT audit findings are classified using the following four-tier severity framework. Training ensures consistent application of this classification across all audits and auditors.

| Severity Level | Label | Definition | Escalation Requirement | Expected Management Response Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | **Critical** | A control deficiency that exposes the organisation to an imminent threat of significant financial loss, regulatory sanction, reputational damage, or systemic failure. Immediate action is required. | Board Audit Committee; Chief Audit Executive; Senior Management | Immediate — Within 30 days |
| **Level 2** | **High** | A significant control weakness that, if not addressed, could result in material financial loss, regulatory non-compliance, or significant operational disruption. | Chief Audit Executive; Senior Management | Within 60 days |
| **Level 3** | **Medium** | A control deficiency that represents a moderate risk, which may result in limited financial loss or operational inefficiency if not remediated. | IT Audit Manager | Within 90 days |
| **Level 4** | **Low** | A minor control deficiency or observation representing a low risk. Remediation improves control effectiveness but is not urgent. | IT Audit Manager | Within 180 days |

### 7.2 Severity Classification Criteria

*Provide the specific criteria and factors auditors must consider when assigning a severity classification to an audit finding.*

When classifying finding severity, auditors must evaluate the following factors:

1. **Likelihood** — The probability that the control weakness will result in an adverse event.
2. **Impact** — The potential consequences if the risk materialises (financial, regulatory, reputational, operational).
3. **Velocity** — How quickly a risk can escalate if unaddressed.
4. **Regulatory Sensitivity** — Whether the finding relates to a BNM regulatory requirement, PDPA obligation, or other mandatory standard.
5. **Pervasiveness** — Whether the weakness is isolated or systemic across multiple processes or systems.
6. **Management Awareness** — Whether management was previously aware of the finding and had failed to remediate it.

**Classification Decision Matrix:**

| Likelihood | High Impact | Medium Impact | Low Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **High** | Critical | High | Medium |
| **Medium** | High | Medium | Low |
| **Low** | Medium | Low | Low |

### 7.3 Finding Classification Training Requirements

*Specify the training requirements to ensure auditors apply the severity classification framework consistently.*

- All new auditors must complete finding severity classification training within **30 days** of joining the IT audit function.
- Annual calibration exercises will be conducted to ensure consistent application across the team.
- Disagreements on classification between auditor and reviewer must be escalated to the IT Audit Manager.
- A sample of classified findings will be reviewed quarterly by the Head of Application Development for quality assurance.

## 9. Assessment Methodology and Pass Criteria

### 9.1 Assessment Framework

*Describe the overall approach used to assess auditor competency before, during, and after training interventions. Assessments should be objective, consistent, and documented.*

Auditor competency is assessed at three levels throughout the training programme:

1. **Pre-Training Assessment** — Establishes baseline competency and validates the training needs analysis.
2. **Post-Training Assessment** — Measures knowledge and skill acquisition immediately following training delivery.
3. **Annual Competency Assessment** — A comprehensive evaluation conducted at the end of the financial year to measure overall competency development and inform the next year's training plan.

### 9.2 Assessment Types

*Define the specific assessment instruments used for each type of evaluation.*

| Assessment Type | Description | Applicable Training | Administered By | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Written Knowledge Test | Multiple-choice and short-answer questions testing theoretical knowledge | All mandatory modules | Training coordinator / LMS | Post-training |
| Practical Case Study | Simulation of a real IT audit scenario requiring application of skills | Methodology, Classification, Domain training | IT Audit Manager | Post-training; Annual assessment |
| Structured Interview | Competency-based interview to assess depth of understanding | Senior Auditors and above | Head of Application Development | Annual assessment |
| Observed Audit Review | Review of audit work papers to assess practical application of training | All auditors | IT Audit Manager | Quarterly QA review |
| 360-Degree Feedback | Stakeholder feedback on auditor competency and professional conduct | All audit staff | HR / IT Audit Manager | Annual |
| External Certification Exam | CISA, CISSP, ISO 27001 Lead Auditor examinations | Designated staff | ISACA / External body | As per certification schedule |

### 9.3 Pass Criteria and Remediation

*Define the minimum pass criteria for each assessment type and the remediation process for auditors who do not meet the required standard.*

**Minimum Pass Criteria:**
