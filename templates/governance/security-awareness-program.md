## 1. Executive Summary

*Provide a concise overview of the Security Awareness Program, its strategic rationale, key objectives, and alignment with [Organization Name]'s overall information security posture and regulatory obligations. This section should be written for a senior management audience and should not exceed one page.*

[Organization Name] operates within a dynamic cyber threat landscape and is subject to the regulatory requirements of Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) under the Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document. A robust and continuously evolving Security Awareness Program is a foundational control in mitigating human-centric risks, which remain one of the most significant vectors for information security incidents in the financial services sector.

This Security Awareness Program ("the Program") establishes a structured, organization-wide approach to cultivating a security-conscious culture among all personnel with access to [Organization Name]'s information systems, data, and technology assets. The Program is aligned with the requirements of RMiT Clause 15.1 and reflects [Organization Name]'s commitment to maintaining a resilient technology risk management framework.

Key highlights of the Program include:

- **Mandatory participation** for all employees, contractors, and third-party personnel with system access
- **Role-based training modules** tailored to the specific risk exposure of different personnel categories
- **Annual review cycle** to ensure content remains current with the evolving threat environment and regulatory landscape
- **Measurable outcomes** through structured assessments with defined pass criteria and remediation pathways
- **Board and senior management visibility** through periodic reporting on program effectiveness and completion rates

This Program is owned jointly by the **Head of Human Resources** and the **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)**, with oversight provided by the **Technology Risk Management Committee** or equivalent governance body.

## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Purpose

*Describe the primary purpose of this document and the Program it establishes. Reference the specific regulatory obligation being addressed.*

This document establishes and governs the **Security Awareness Program** for [Organization Name]. Its purpose is to:

1. Define the framework, content, and delivery mechanisms for security awareness training across the organization
2. Ensure compliance with BNM RMiT Clause 15.1, which requires financial institutions to implement a documented security awareness program covering all personnel with access to technology systems
3. Cultivate a culture of security awareness that reduces the likelihood and impact of human-related information security incidents
4. Provide a structured basis for measuring, monitoring, and continuously improving security awareness maturity across the organization
5. Demonstrate to regulators and auditors that [Organization Name] maintains a systematic and documented approach to personnel security education

## 7. Assessment Methodology and Pass Criteria

*Define how personnel knowledge will be assessed, the minimum pass criteria, the remediation process for those who fail to meet the standard, and how assessment results will be recorded and reported.*

### 7.1 Assessment Approach

Each mandatory training module includes a formal knowledge assessment to verify comprehension and retention. The Program uses a combination of assessment types:

| Assessment Type | Description | Applicable Modules |
|---|---|---|
| **Post-Module Quiz** | Multiple-choice or scenario-based questions administered immediately after module completion | All e-learning modules |
| **Annual Knowledge Assessment** | Comprehensive assessment covering all core curriculum areas, administered once per program year | All Personnel |
| **Role-Specific Assessment** | Technical or scenario-based assessments for personnel in elevated-risk roles | Privileged Users, Developers, Finance |
| **Phishing Simulation** | Behavioural assessment via controlled phishing campaigns; click-through rate and reporting rate tracked | All Personnel |
| **Policy Acknowledgement** | Signed acknowledgement that the individual has read, understood, and agrees to comply with key security policies | All Personnel |

### 7.2 Pass Criteria

| Assessment | Minimum Pass Score | Attempts Permitted Before Remediation |
|---|---|---|
| Post-Module Quiz | [75]% | [2] attempts |
| Annual Knowledge Assessment | [80]% | [2] attempts |
| Role-Specific Assessment | [80]% | [2] attempts |
| Phishing Simulation (Organisational) | N/A (click rate target: < [X]%) | N/A — behavioural metric |

### 7.3 Remediation Process

*Describe the process for personnel who fail to meet the minimum assessment standard or who fail to complete mandatory training within the stipulated timeframe.*

The following remediation process applies to all cases of failed assessments or non-completion:

**Step 1 — Automated Reminder**
Upon failure or non-completion, the LMS automatically generates a reminder notification to the individual and their line manager.

**Step 2 — Remedial Training Assignment**
The individual is assigned a targeted remedial module addressing the topic areas in which they demonstrated insufficient knowledge. Remedial training must be completed within **[30] calendar days** of the failed assessment.

**Step 3 — Reassessment**
Following completion of remedial training, the individual must retake and pass the assessment at the minimum pass threshold.

**Step 4 — Escalation**
If an individual fails to complete remedial training and reassessment within the stipulated period, the case is escalated to:
- The individual's departmental head
- Head of Human Resources
- CISO (for notification purposes)

**Step 5 — HR Action**
Persistent non-compliance may result in HR action in accordance with [Organization Name]'s disciplinary procedures and the applicable employment contract provisions. System access restrictions may be applied as an interim control measure, subject to HR and line management approval.

### 7.4 Record Keeping

All assessment results, completion records, and remediation actions are recorded in the **Learning Management System (LMS)**. Records are retained for a minimum of **[3] years** in accordance with [Organization Name]'s Records Retention Policy and regulatory requirements.

| Record Type | Storage Location | Retention Period | Access |

## 11. Non-Compliance and Escalation

*Define the consequences of non-compliance with mandatory training requirements and the escalation pathway for persistent non-compliance.*

### 11.1 Individual Non-Compliance

Failure to complete mandatory security awareness training within stipulated deadlines is a breach of [Organization Name]'s Information Security Policy and may result in the following actions:

| Non-Compliance Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| Failure to complete mandatory module by deadline | Automated LMS reminder to individual and line manager |
| Failure to complete within [14] days of first reminder | Escalation to departmental head; second formal notification |
| Failure to complete within [30] days of deadline | Escalation to Head of HR; formal written notice; potential system access restrictions |
| Failure of assessment after maximum permitted attempts | Assignment of remedial training; reassessment within [30] days |
| Persistent non-compliance (> [60] days past deadline) | HR disciplinary process in accordance with [Employment Act 1955 / employment contract provisions] |

### 11.2 Departmental Non-Compliance

Where a department consistently falls below the 95% completion target, the following escalation applies:

1. The departmental head is formally notified by the Head of HR and CISO
2. A remediation plan with specific completion milestones is agreed with the departmental head
3. If the department fails to achieve the target within the agreed remediation period, the matter is escalated to the relevant business unit CEO or COO
4. Persistent departmental non-compliance is reported to the TRMC and may be included in the annual report to the BRC

### 11.3 Third-Party Non-Compliance

Third-party personnel who fail to complete mandatory awareness training may have their system access suspended pending completion. [Organization Name]'s vendor management team shall include awareness training completion as a contractual obligation within third-party agreements and may enforce this through applicable contract provisions.
