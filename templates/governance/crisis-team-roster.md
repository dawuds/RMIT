## 1. Executive Summary

*Provide a concise overview of the purpose of this Crisis Team Roster, the institution's approach to crisis management, and the strategic importance of maintaining a current and operational crisis team structure. This section should be suitable for senior management and board-level readers.*

[Organization Name] is committed to maintaining operational resilience and ensuring the continuity of critical technology services in accordance with the requirements set forth by Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) under the Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document. This Crisis Team Roster establishes a formally documented structure of individuals authorized, trained, and empowered to respond to technology-related crises, incidents, and disruptions affecting the institution's operations.

The Crisis Team Roster serves as the definitive reference document for crisis response personnel, outlining the composition of the crisis management team, individual roles and responsibilities, contact information, escalation pathways, and activation protocols. It ensures that [Organization Name] maintains a state of readiness to respond to technology crises in a coordinated, timely, and effective manner.

This document is reviewed annually, or following any material change to organizational structure, personnel, or risk profile, and is subject to the formal approval of the Chief Risk Officer (CRO) and Board Risk Committee.

## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Purpose

*Describe the specific regulatory obligation and institutional rationale for maintaining this document. Reference the relevant BNM RMiT clauses explicitly.*

This Crisis Team Roster is established in compliance with BNM RMiT Clause 11.5, which requires financial institutions to maintain documented crisis management arrangements, including the identification of personnel with defined responsibilities for managing technology-related crises and incidents. The purpose of this document is to:

- Maintain a current and auditable register of all personnel assigned to crisis management roles within [Organization Name];
- Define clear roles, responsibilities, and authority levels for each crisis team position;
- Provide verified contact information to enable rapid mobilization of crisis response resources;
- Support the effective activation of crisis response and business continuity procedures;
- Serve as an auditable record demonstrating compliance with BNM RMiT obligations.

### 2.2 Scope

*Define the boundaries of this document — which systems, entities, business units, and geographic locations are covered, and which are explicitly excluded.*

This document applies to:

|---|---|

| **Business Functions** | All technology operations, information security, business continuity, and senior management functions |
| **Geographic Coverage** | All operational locations including Head Office ([Location]) and branches ([Locations]) |
| **Technology Perimeter** | All critical technology systems, infrastructure, and third-party technology services as defined in the Technology Asset Inventory |
| **Crisis Categories** | Technology failures, cybersecurity incidents, data breaches, system outages, and third-party service disruptions |

**Exclusions:**

- Physical security incidents not involving technology systems (refer to [Physical Security Incident Response Plan])

## 4. Methodology and Approach

### 4.1 Crisis Classification Framework

*Describe the criteria used to classify technology crisis events by severity. This classification determines which team members are activated and the expected response timelines.*

[Organization Name] classifies technology crisis events using a three-tier severity model aligned with BNM RMiT requirements and the institution's Risk Appetite Framework:

| Severity Level | Classification | Criteria | RTO Target | Team Activation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — Critical** | Major Crisis | Systemic failure affecting core banking; regulatory breach; widespread data compromise; reputational risk at board level | [e.g., 4 hours] | Full CMC + Full TCRT + Board notification |
| **Tier 2 — High** | Significant Incident | Partial failure of critical systems; potential data loss; customer-facing service degradation exceeding [X] hours | [e.g., 8 hours] | CMC Chair + Relevant CMC Members + Full TCRT |
| **Tier 3 — Medium** | Operational Incident | Localized system failures; non-critical service degradation; contained security events | [e.g., 24 hours] | TCRT Lead + Relevant Sub-Teams |

### 4.2 Crisis Team Activation Protocol

*Describe how and when the crisis team is activated, including the trigger criteria, notification channels, and decision authority for escalation between tiers.*

**Activation Triggers:**

- Automatic alerting from Security Operations Centre (SOC) or Network Operations Centre (NOC)
- Escalation from Technology Operations following internal incident triage
- Notification from third-party service providers or BNM
- Detection by any employee of a qualifying technology disruption or security event

**Activation Steps:**

1. **Detection & Initial Triage** — Technology Operations identifies a potential crisis event and performs initial severity assessment (within [X] minutes of detection)
2. **TCRT Activation** — TCRT Lead is notified and convenes the relevant sub-teams
3. **Severity Confirmation** — TCRT Lead confirms severity classification and determines if CMC escalation is required
4. **CMC Activation** (Tier 1 & 2 only) — CRO is notified; CMC is convened within [X] hours
5. **Board Notification** (Tier 1 only) — Board Risk Committee Chair is notified within [X] hours
6. **Regulatory Notification** — BNM and other applicable regulators are notified per regulatory reporting timelines

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

### 6.1 Role Descriptions

*Provide detailed descriptions of each crisis team role, including the scope of authority, key responsibilities, and required competencies. This section ensures clarity of responsibilities during high-pressure crisis situations.*

#### 6.1.1 Chief Risk Officer (CRO) — Crisis Management Committee Chair

**Authority Level:** Full executive authority to direct crisis response, authorize expenditure, and communicate with the Board and regulators.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Declare and formally activate the crisis management framework
- Chair all Crisis Management Committee sessions during a crisis event
- Approve all material decisions including system shutdowns, public communications, and regulatory notifications
- Maintain communication with the Board Risk Committee and Board of Directors
- Authorize engagement of external advisors, forensic specialists, or legal counsel
- Sign off on post-crisis reports submitted to BNM and other regulators

**Required Competencies:**
- Senior executive authority with enterprise risk management expertise
- Familiarity with BNM regulatory requirements and reporting obligations
- Crisis leadership and stakeholder management experience

#### 6.1.2 Chief Information Officer (CIO) — Technology Crisis Lead

**Authority Level:** Full authority over technology operations and recovery decisions.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Lead the Technology Crisis Response Team (TCRT) and report to the CMC
- Direct technical remediation, recovery, and containment activities
- Authorize activation of Disaster Recovery (DR) sites and Business Continuity plans
- Coordinate with third-party technology vendors and managed service providers
- Provide technical briefings to the CMC at defined intervals
- Oversee RTO/RPO compliance during recovery operations

#### 6.1.3 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) — Security Crisis Lead

**Authority Level:** Full authority over cybersecurity response and forensic investigation activities.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Lead the Cybersecurity Response Sub-Team for security-related crisis events
- Direct threat containment, isolation, and eradication activities
- Authorize forensic investigations and evidence preservation procedures
- Manage communication with external security agencies and MyCERT/NACSA
- Oversee vulnerability assessment following incident containment
- Prepare cybersecurity incident reports for regulatory submission

#### 6.1.4 TCRT Lead / Incident Commander

**Authority Level:** Operational authority over all TCRT sub-teams during a crisis event.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Serve as the primary operational coordinator for technology crisis response
- Receive and assess initial crisis notifications and perform severity classification
- Activate relevant TCRT sub-teams based on crisis type and severity
- Maintain an incident log and provide status updates to the CIO and CMC
- Coordinate resource requirements and technical decision-making across sub-teams
- Declare incident containment and initiate recovery phase activities

#### 6.1.5 Crisis Management Coordinator

**Authority Level:** Administrative and coordination authority within the CMC.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Maintain the Crisis Team Roster and ensure contact information is current
- Coordinate meeting logistics for CMC activations and exercises
- Maintain crisis event logs, minutes, and action items
- Distribute crisis communications to relevant internal stakeholders
- Track post-crisis action items through to completion
- Coordinate annual roster reviews and crisis simulation exercises
