## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Annual IT Audit Plan establishes a risk-based framework for conducting internal technology audits across [Organization Name] for the audit period covering [Audit Period Start Date] to [Audit Period End Date]. The plan ensures systematic, independent, and objective assurance over the institution's technology risk landscape in alignment with Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, specifically Clause 13.1, which mandates that financial institutions maintain a robust IT audit function capable of providing independent assurance on the adequacy and effectiveness of technology controls.

This document is reviewed on a **continuous monitoring basis**, with a formal **quarterly review cycle** to incorporate emerging technology risks, regulatory developments, and changes to the institution's IT environment.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the boundaries of the IT audit programme for the current audit cycle, specifying the technology domains, systems, and processes covered. Note any exclusions and the rationale for them.*

The scope of this Annual IT Audit Plan encompasses all critical technology domains and supporting infrastructure operated by or on behalf of [Organization Name], including but not limited to:

- **Technology Governance** — IT strategy, policies, and oversight structures
- **Cybersecurity and Information Security** — Security controls, threat management, and incident response
- **IT Infrastructure and Operations** — Data centres, network, servers, and operational processes
- **Application Systems** — Core banking, internet banking, mobile banking, and ancillary systems
- **Data Management and Analytics** — Data governance, data quality, and analytics platforms
- **Cloud Services** — Public, private, and hybrid cloud deployments
- **Third-Party and Vendor Management** — Outsourced technology services and managed service providers
- **Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery** — BCP/DR plans, testing, and recovery capabilities
- **Change and Project Management** — IT change processes and significant technology projects
- **End-User Computing** — Spreadsheet controls, end-user applications, and access management

## 2. Objectives and Scope of IT Audit

### 2.1 Audit Objectives

*State the primary objectives of the IT audit programme for the current year. Objectives should be measurable, aligned to the institution's technology risk appetite, and traceable to regulatory requirements.*

5. **Emerging Technology Risk** — Evaluate the institution's readiness and controls for emerging technology risks, including [Cloud Migration / AI Adoption / Digital Payments / other relevant initiative].

6. **Continuous Monitoring** — Maintain ongoing oversight of high-risk technology areas through continuous auditing and monitoring activities between scheduled audit engagements.

### 3.2 Risk Assessment Methodology

*Describe the criteria and scoring approach used to prioritise audit entities. The risk assessment must be demonstrably objective and repeatable.*

| Risk Factor | Weight | Description |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| Inherent Risk | 30% | Likelihood and impact of technology failure absent controls |
| Control Environment | 25% | Maturity and effectiveness of existing controls |
| Regulatory Sensitivity | 20% | Degree of regulatory obligation and supervisory scrutiny |
| Prior Audit Findings | 15% | Outstanding findings and repeat findings from prior cycles |
| Business Criticality | 10% | Impact on business operations if the entity fails |

### 4.2 Audit Lifecycle

*Describe each phase of the standard IT audit engagement lifecycle applied by the institution.*

Each IT audit engagement follows a structured lifecycle comprising five phases:

**Phase 1 — Planning and Risk Assessment**

- Define audit objectives, scope, and criteria aligned to the risk profile of the audit entity
- Conduct preliminary risk assessment and identify key control objectives
- Issue **Audit Engagement Letter** to the auditee confirming scope and timeline
- Prepare **Audit Programme** with detailed test steps mapped to control objectives
- Confirm resource requirements and engagement schedule

**Phase 2 — Fieldwork and Evidence Collection**

- Execute audit procedures in accordance with the approved Audit Programme
- Collect audit evidence through interviews, observations, document review, and technical testing
- Perform control testing (design effectiveness and operating effectiveness)
- Document all working papers in the **Audit Management System** ([System Name])
- Apply data analytics and continuous auditing techniques where applicable

**Phase 3 — Analysis and Finding Development**

- Analyse evidence to identify control deficiencies, weaknesses, and non-compliance
- Classify findings using the severity framework defined in Section 7
- Conduct **Preliminary Finding Discussion** with the auditee to validate factual accuracy
- Obtain management responses and proposed remediation actions with target dates

**Phase 4 — Reporting**

- Prepare **Draft Audit Report** and issue to auditee for management response
- Incorporate management responses and finalise the **Final Audit Report**
- Present significant findings to the [Chief Risk Officer / Chief Information Officer] as appropriate
- Submit Final Audit Report to the **Board Audit Committee** for Critical and High rated findings

**Phase 5 — Follow-Up and Closure**

- Track all open findings against agreed remediation timelines
- Conduct **follow-up verification** of implemented remediation actions
- Report outstanding findings and overdue items to the Board Audit Committee quarterly
- Close findings upon satisfactory evidence of remediation

## 7. Finding Severity Classification

### 7.1 Severity Rating Framework

*The finding severity framework must be consistently applied across all IT audit engagements to ensure comparability of findings, appropriate escalation, and proportionate remediation timelines. This framework aligns to BNM RMiT expectations for risk-proportionate control environments.*

All IT audit findings are classified using the following four-tier severity framework:

| Severity | Rating | Definition | Examples |
|----------|--------|------------|---------|
| **Critical** | 1 | Control failure with immediate potential for significant financial loss, regulatory breach, reputational damage, or compromise of critical systems. Requires urgent escalation and remediation. | Unpatched critical CVE on internet-facing system; unauthorised privileged access to production; absence of DR capability for critical system; active data breach |
| **High** | 2 | Significant control weakness with high potential to result in material financial loss, regulatory non-compliance, or operational disruption if not addressed promptly. | Inadequate segregation of duties in critical processes; missing MFA for privileged accounts; incomplete BCP testing; material PDPA non-compliance |
| **Medium** | 3 | Moderate control gap that, while not immediately threatening, represents a meaningful risk that should be addressed within a defined period. | Outdated security policies; incomplete vendor risk assessments; inadequate patch management for internal systems; gaps in user access reviews |
| **Low** | 4 | Minor deficiency or observation that represents a best-practice gap or efficiency improvement. Remediation can be scheduled as part of routine operations. | Minor documentation gaps; low-risk configuration deviations; process improvement recommendations |

### 7.2 Escalation and Reporting Requirements

*Define reporting and escalation thresholds based on finding severity to ensure appropriate management visibility.*

| Severity | Escalation Path | Reporting Vehicle | Reporting Timeline |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|--------------------|
| **Critical** | Head of Internal Audit → CEO → Board Audit Committee | Immediate verbal escalation + written flash report | Within **24 hours** of identification |
| **High** | Head of Internal Audit → CIO / CISO → BAC | Audit Report + BAC Quarterly Report | Within **5 business days** of report finalisation |
| **Medium** | Head of Internal Audit → Relevant Management Owner | Audit Report | Within **10 business days** of report finalisation |
| **Low** | Audit Lead → Auditee | Audit Report | With final audit report |

### 7.3 Remediation Timelines

*Standard remediation timelines are set commensurate with finding severity. Extensions must be formally approved by the Head of Internal Audit with documented rationale.*

| Severity | Standard Remediation Deadline | Extension Approval Authority | Maximum Extension |
|----------|-------------------------------|------------------------------|-------------------|
| **Critical** | 30 calendar days | Head of Internal Audit + CRO | 30 days (one extension only) |
| **High** | 90 calendar days | Head of Internal Audit | 60 days |
| **Medium** | 180 calendar days | Senior IT Auditor | 90 days |
| **Low** | 365 calendar days | Audit Lead | 180 days |

### 7.4 Repeat Finding Policy

*Repeat findings indicate systemic control weaknesses or inadequate management remediation and are subject to enhanced scrutiny.*

A finding is classified as a **Repeat Finding** where the same or substantially similar control deficiency was identified in a prior audit cycle and was marked as remediated. Repeat Findings are subject to:

- Automatic **severity upgrade** (e.g., Medium → High; High → Critical)
- Mandatory escalation to the **Board Audit Committee**, regardless of original severity
- Root cause analysis requirement as part of the management response
- Enhanced follow-up frequency (monthly verification instead of standard quarterly)

### 8.2 Key Responsibilities by Role

**Board Audit Committee (BAC)**

- Approve and provide oversight of the Annual IT Audit Plan
- Receive and challenge IT audit findings and management responses
- Monitor the resolution of Critical and High-rated findings
- Assess the adequacy of IT audit resources and competency
- Provide independent reporting to the Board on the technology control environment

**Head of Internal Audit**

- Own and maintain the Annual IT Audit Plan
- Ensure the audit function maintains independence, objectivity, and professional competency
- Approve all Final Audit Reports before issuance
- Escalate Critical findings to BAC and Senior Management
- Report quarterly on plan progress, findings status, and emerging IT risks

**IT Auditors (Senior and Staff)**

- Execute audit engagements in accordance with the approved Audit Programme
- Maintain audit working papers to evidential standards required by IIA and ISACA
- Communicate preliminary findings clearly and factually to auditees
- Follow up on open findings within assigned engagements
- Maintain and develop technology audit competencies through CPE

**Chief Information Officer (CIO)**

- Facilitate timely access to systems, documentation, and personnel for audit engagements
- Provide management responses and remediation plans for audit findings
- Ensure technology teams cooperate fully with the audit function
- Escalate resource or priority conflicts to the Head of Internal Audit promptly

**Management Owners (Auditees)**

- Provide accurate and complete information to the audit team during fieldwork
- Review and agree or formally dispute draft findings within agreed timelines
- Deliver credible, risk-proportionate management responses with committed remediation dates
- Implement remediation actions within agreed timelines and provide evidence of closure
