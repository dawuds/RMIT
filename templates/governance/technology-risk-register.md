## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section describes the intent of the Technology Risk Register and its role within the organization's broader enterprise risk management framework.*

This Technology Risk Register ("the Register") serves as the authoritative, consolidated record of all identified technology risks within [Organization Name]. It is established and maintained in accordance with the requirements of Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, specifically Clause 9.5, which mandates financial institutions to identify, assess, monitor, and treat technology risks in a structured and documented manner.

The Register fulfills the following purposes:

- Provide a single, comprehensive view of the technology risk landscape facing [Organization Name].
- Enable risk-informed decision-making by senior management, the Board Risk Committee, and the Board of Directors.
- Facilitate continuous monitoring and periodic review of technology risks against established risk appetite and tolerance thresholds.
- Demonstrate regulatory compliance with BNM RMiT and related supervisory expectations.
- Support the identification and prioritization of technology risk treatment activities, resource allocation, and remediation milestones.
- Serve as an audit-ready record for internal audit, external audit, and BNM supervisory examination purposes.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the boundaries of what is included within this register — systems, entities, processes, and geographies covered.*

This Register applies to all technology risks arising from or associated with:

**In Scope:**

- All information technology (IT) systems, applications, platforms, and infrastructure owned, operated, or managed by [Organization Name].
- Technology systems operated by third-party service providers, vendors, and outsourced service providers where [Organization Name] bears material technology risk exposure.
- Cloud computing environments, whether public, private, or hybrid, utilized by [Organization Name].
- Technology supporting core banking operations, payment systems, digital channels, and financial services delivery.
- Operational technology (OT) and industrial control systems where applicable.
- Data and information assets classified under [Organization Name]'s information classification policy.
- Cybersecurity risks that manifest as technology risks.
- All business units, subsidiaries, and branches of [Organization Name] operating within Malaysia.

**Out of Scope:**

- Non-technology operational risks (unless they have a direct technology causation or impact).
- Strategic and reputational risks not originating from technology failures or incidents.
- Risks managed under separate, standalone regulatory registers unless cross-referenced herein.

### 1.3 Alignment with Enterprise Risk Management

*Describe how this register integrates with the broader ERM and risk governance framework.*

This Technology Risk Register operates as a component of [Organization Name]'s Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) framework. Technology risks identified herein are:

- Escalated to the Enterprise Risk Register where they meet the threshold for material or significant risk designation.
- Reported to the Risk Management Committee (RMC) and Board Risk Committee (BRC) in accordance with the reporting cadence defined in Section 14.
- Reviewed in conjunction with the organization's Risk Appetite Statement to ensure that residual risk levels remain within approved tolerance limits.
- Aligned with the Information Security Management System (ISMS) and Business Continuity Management (BCM) frameworks.

## 3. Risk Register Framework

### 3.1 Risk Register Structure

*Explain the overall structure of the register and how entries are organized.*

The Technology Risk Register is organized into the following components:

1. **Risk Identification Record** — Unique identifier, risk description, and risk category.
2. **Risk Assessment Record** — Inherent risk rating, control effectiveness assessment, and residual risk rating.
3. **Risk Treatment Record** — Treatment strategy, action plans, responsible owners, and target dates.
4. **Risk Monitoring Record** — Key Risk Indicators (KRIs), monitoring frequency, and current status.
5. **Risk History Record** — Audit trail of changes, reviews, and incidents linked to each risk entry.

### 3.2 Risk Rating Scale

*Define the risk rating scales used throughout this register.*

**Likelihood Scale:**

| Rating | Score | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| Rare | 1 | Event may occur only in exceptional circumstances (< once in 10 years) |
| Unlikely | 2 | Event could occur at some time (once in 5–10 years) |
| Possible | 3 | Event might occur at some time (once in 2–5 years) |
| Likely | 4 | Event will probably occur in most circumstances (once per year) |
| Almost Certain | 5 | Event is expected to occur in most circumstances (multiple times per year) |

**Impact Scale:**

| Rating | Score | Financial Impact | Operational Impact | Reputational Impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Negligible | 1 | < RM 100,000 | Minimal disruption, < 1 hour | No media coverage |
| Minor | 2 | RM 100,000 – RM 500,000 | Limited disruption, 1–4 hours | Minor internal concern |
| Moderate | 3 | RM 500,000 – RM 2M | Moderate disruption, 4–24 hours | Limited media attention |
| Major | 4 | RM 2M – RM 10M | Significant disruption, 1–3 days | Significant media coverage |
| Catastrophic | 5 | > RM 10M | Critical disruption, > 3 days | Major regulatory/media attention |

**Risk Heat Map — Inherent Risk Rating:**

| | **Rare (1)** | **Unlikely (2)** | **Possible (3)** | **Likely (4)** | **Almost Certain (5)** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Catastrophic (5)** | Medium (5) | High (10) | High (15) | Critical (20) | Critical (25) |
| **Major (4)** | Low (4) | Medium (8) | High (12) | High (16) | Critical (20) |
| **Moderate (3)** | Low (3) | Medium (6) | Medium (9) | High (12) | High (15) |
| **Minor (2)** | Low (2) | Low (4) | Medium (6) | Medium (8) | High (10) |
| **Negligible (1)** | Low (1) | Low (2) | Low (3) | Low (4) | Medium (5) |

**Risk Rating Definitions:**

| Risk Rating | Score Range | Action Required |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | 20–25 | Immediate escalation to Board. Treatment plan mandatory within 30 days. |
| **High** | 10–19 | Escalation to Senior Management. Treatment plan mandatory within 60 days. |
| **Medium** | 5–9 | Management attention required. Treatment plan within 90 days. |
| **Low** | 1–4 | Monitor and review. Treatment at discretion of risk owner. |

### 3.3 Control Effectiveness Rating

*Define how the effectiveness of existing controls is assessed.*

| Rating | Score | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| Ineffective | 1 | Controls are absent or not functioning as intended |
| Partially Effective | 2 | Controls exist but have significant gaps or weaknesses |
| Moderately Effective | 3 | Controls are adequate but require improvement |
| Effective | 4 | Controls are well-designed and operating effectively |
| Highly Effective | 5 | Controls are comprehensive, automated, and continuously monitored |

**Residual Risk Calculation:**

Residual Risk Score = Inherent Risk Score × (1 − Control Effectiveness Factor)

*Where Control Effectiveness Factor ranges from 0.1 (Ineffective) to 0.9 (Highly Effective)*

## 4. Objectives and Scope of Technology Risk Management

### 4.1 Technology Risk Management Objectives

*Articulate the specific objectives that the technology risk management program aims to achieve for this organization.*

[Organization Name]'s Technology Risk Management program is guided by the following objectives, established in alignment with BNM RMiT Clause 9.5 and the organization's overall risk appetite:

**Objective 1 — Comprehensive Risk Identification**
Systematically identify all material technology risks across the organization's technology landscape, including emerging risks arising from new technologies, threat intelligence, and changes to the operating environment.

**Objective 2 — Rigorous Risk Assessment**
Apply consistent, evidence-based assessment methodologies to evaluate the likelihood and impact of identified technology risks, both in their inherent state (before controls) and residual state (after controls).

**Objective 3 — Effective Risk Treatment**
Develop and implement proportionate, risk-based treatment plans that reduce technology risk exposure to within the organization's approved risk appetite and tolerance thresholds.

**Objective 4 — Continuous Monitoring**
Maintain ongoing monitoring of technology risk indicators, control effectiveness, and the threat environment to ensure timely identification of risk changes and emerging threats.

**Objective 5 — Governance and Accountability**
Establish clear ownership, accountability, and escalation pathways for technology risk management, ensuring that risk responsibilities are embedded within technology and business functions.

**Objective 6 — Regulatory Compliance**
Ensure that technology risk management activities, documentation, and reporting satisfy all applicable BNM RMiT requirements and support supervisory examination readiness.

**Objective 7 — Informed Decision-Making**
Provide senior management and the Board with accurate, timely, and actionable technology risk information to support strategic and operational decision-making.

### 5.2 Risk Entry Criteria

*Define the criteria for a risk to be included in the Technology Risk Register.*

A technology risk shall be entered into this Register when it meets the following criteria:

- The risk has a plausible likelihood of occurring within a 3-year horizon.
- The risk, if it were to materialize, would have a financial, operational, reputational, or compliance impact rated at **Minor (2) or above** on the impact scale.
- The risk is directly attributable to technology, information systems, or the management of technology.
- The risk has not been fully mitigated (i.e., residual risk is not rated at the lowest acceptable level with no further action required).

## 6. Risk Assessment Methodology

### 6.1 Assessment Process

*Describe the step-by-step process for assessing technology risks identified for inclusion in the register.*

The technology risk assessment process follows these steps:

**Step 1 — Risk Description and Contextualization**
The risk owner, in consultation with the Technology Risk function, documents a clear and specific risk statement using the format:
> *"There is a risk that [event/threat], arising from [cause/source], which may result in [consequence/impact]."*

**Step 2 — Inherent Risk Assessment**
Assess the likelihood and impact of the risk in the **absence of any controls**, using the scales defined in Section 3.2. The inherent risk score is calculated as: Likelihood × Impact.

**Step 3 — Control Identification and Assessment**
Identify all existing controls relevant to the risk. Assess the design adequacy and operating effectiveness of each control using the Control Effectiveness Rating in Section 3.3.

**Step 4 — Residual Risk Assessment**
Assess the likelihood and impact of the risk **after considering the effectiveness of existing controls**. Document the residual risk score and rating.

**Step 5 — Risk Appetite Comparison**
Compare the residual risk rating against the organization's risk appetite thresholds (Section 4.2). Identify whether the residual risk is within tolerance or requires treatment.

**Step 6 — Treatment Decision**
Where residual risk exceeds tolerance, define the risk treatment strategy (accept, avoid, transfer, or mitigate) and develop an action plan.

**Step 7 — Risk Owner Sign-Off**
The designated risk owner reviews and formally accepts the risk assessment and associated treatment plan.

### 6.2 Control Assessment Framework

*Document the controls assessment approach for evaluating the effectiveness of technology controls.*

For each risk entry, controls are assessed across the following dimensions:

| Dimension | Description |
|---|---|
| **Design Adequacy** | Is the control designed to address the risk? Is the control specification complete and appropriate? |
| **Implementation Completeness** | Has the control been fully implemented as designed? Are there gaps in coverage? |
| **Operating Effectiveness** | Is the control operating as intended on a consistent basis? Is there evidence of operation? |
| **Automation Level** | Is the control manual, partially automated, or fully automated? |
| **Testing Frequency** | How frequently is the control tested and validated? |

### 8.1 Treatment Strategy Selection

*Document the rationale for selecting each treatment strategy against the risk appetite and available options.*

For each risk where the residual rating exceeds the approved risk appetite, a risk treatment strategy must be selected from the following options:

| Treatment Strategy | Description | When to Apply |
|---|---|---|
| **Mitigate** | Implement additional controls to reduce likelihood and/or impact | When the cost of controls is justified relative to risk reduction achieved |
| **Transfer** | Transfer risk to a third party (e.g., via insurance, contractual allocation) | When the risk is more efficiently managed externally |
| **Avoid** | Discontinue the activity or system giving rise to the risk | When residual risk cannot be reduced to within appetite |
| **Accept** | Formally accept the risk without additional treatment | When residual risk is within appetite, or cost of treatment exceeds benefit (requires Board/senior management sign-off for High/Critical risks) |

## 12. Progress Tracking and Monitoring Mechanism

### 12.1 Monitoring Framework

*Describe the overall framework for continuously monitoring technology risks, controls, and treatment actions.*

[Organization Name] employs a layered monitoring approach to ensure technology risks are tracked effectively and changes in the risk environment are identified promptly:

**Layer 1 — Automated Monitoring (Continuous)**
Technology controls generate automated alerts, logs, and metrics that are monitored by the Security Operations Centre (SOC) and IT Operations team. KRI thresholds trigger automated escalation workflows.

**Layer 2 — Operational Reporting (Weekly / Monthly)**
Technology teams compile operational risk and performance metrics that are reviewed in weekly and monthly operational meetings. KRI status is updated in this Register.

**Layer 3 — Risk Register Review (Quarterly)**
The Head of Technology Risk chairs a quarterly Technology Risk Register Review, attended by all risk owners. The full Register is reviewed, risk ratings are revalidated, and treatment action progress is assessed.

**Layer 4 — Governance Committee Reporting (Quarterly)**
A consolidated Technology Risk Report is submitted to the Risk Management Committee (RMC) and Board Risk Committee (BRC) on a quarterly basis. Critical and High risks are reported to the Board annually or as triggered by material changes.

**Layer 5 — Annual Assessment (Annual)**
A comprehensive annual risk assessment is conducted to review the full technology risk universe, validate the risk taxonomy, update risk ratings, and set the treatment program for the coming year.

### Appendix B — Risk Statement Guidance and Examples

*Guidance for risk owners on how to write well-structured risk statements.*

A good risk statement should clearly identify three components:

**Format:** *"There is a risk that [EVENT/THREAT], arising from [CAUSE/SOURCE], which may result in [CONSEQUENCE/IMPACT]."*

**Examples of Well-Structured Risk Statements:**

| Component | Poor Example | Good Example |
|---|---|---|
| Event/Threat | "Cyber attack" | "Unauthorized access to core banking systems by external threat actors" |
| Cause/Source | "Bad security" | "Exploitable vulnerabilities in externally facing applications, arising from delayed patch management" |
| Consequence | "Data breach" | "Exfiltration of customer personal data affecting up to [X] customers, resulting in PDPA notification obligations, potential regulatory sanctions, and significant reputational damage" |

**Complete Example:**
> *"There is a risk that unauthorized access to [Organization Name]'s internet banking platform is obtained by external threat actors, arising from unpatched vulnerabilities in the application layer and insufficient web application firewall (WAF) rules, which may result in fraudulent customer transactions, exfiltration of customer personal data, PDPA violation, BNM supervisory action, and estimated financial loss of RM [X]M."*

### Appendix C — Control Assessment Guidance

*Guidance notes for assessing the design adequacy and operating effectiveness of technology controls.*

**Design Adequacy Assessment Questions:**
- Does the control specification clearly define what is to be controlled, how, and by whom?
- Is the control capable of preventing, detecting, or correcting the risk it is designed to address?
- Is the control scope appropriate — does it cover all relevant systems, users, and scenarios?
- Is the control documented in policy, procedure, or standard?

**Operating Effectiveness Assessment Questions:**
- Is there evidence that the control is being performed as designed?
- Is the control being performed consistently and at the required frequency?
- Are control outputs being reviewed and acted upon?
- Have any control failures been identified in the past 12 months?

**Control Testing Methods:**

| Method | Description | Suitable For |
|---|---|---|
| Inquiry | Interview control operators to confirm process | All controls |
| Observation | Directly observe the control being performed | Manual controls |
| Inspection | Review documentary evidence of control operation | Document-based controls |
| Re-performance | Independently re-execute the control | Data-driven controls |
| Automated testing | System-based validation of automated controls | Automated controls |

### Appendix F — Glossary of Terms

*Definitions of key terms used in this Technology Risk Register.*

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Inherent Risk** | The level of risk present before considering the effect of any controls or risk treatment measures. |
| **Residual Risk** | The level of risk remaining after existing controls and risk treatment measures have been taken into account. |
| **Risk Appetite** | The amount and type of risk that [Organization Name] is willing to accept in pursuit of its strategic objectives, as approved by the Board. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | The acceptable variation in outcomes relative to the risk appetite, representing the boundary of acceptable risk-taking. |
| **Key Risk Indicator (KRI)** | A metric used to provide early warning signals of increasing risk exposures that may require management attention. |
| **Control** | Any action, process, device, practice, or other measure designed to reduce or manage risk. |
| **Risk Treatment** | The process of selecting and implementing measures to modify the level of risk (mitigate, transfer, avoid, or accept). |
| **Risk Owner** | An individual within [Organization Name] who is accountable for the identification, assessment, treatment, and monitoring of a specific technology risk. |
| **RACI** | Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed — a framework for clarifying roles and responsibilities. |
| **BNM RMiT** | Bank Negara Malaysia's Risk Management in Technology policy document, which sets out requirements for technology risk management in Malaysian financial institutions. |
| **PDPA** | Personal Data Protection Act 2010 — Malaysian legislation governing the processing of personal data in commercial transactions. |
| **NACSA** | National Cyber Security Agency of Malaysia — the national authority on cyber security. |
| **CII** | Critical Information Infrastructure — systems and assets designated as critical to national security, public safety, or the economy under NACSA's framework. |
| **SOC** | Security Operations Centre — a team dedicated to monitoring, detecting, and responding to cybersecurity events. |
| **PAM** | Privileged Access Management — controls and tools used to manage and monitor privileged accounts and access rights. |
| **VAPT** | Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing — technical assessments used to identify and verify security vulnerabilities. |
| **RTO** | Recovery Time Objective — the maximum acceptable time to restore a system or process following a disruption. |
| **RPO** | Recovery Point Objective — the maximum acceptable data loss measured in time, defining the age of the most recent recoverable backup. |
| **Three Lines of Defence** | A risk governance model in which business units (first line), risk and compliance functions (second line), and internal audit (third line) each play distinct roles in risk management and assurance. |
