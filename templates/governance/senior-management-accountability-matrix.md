## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section articulates the intent of the document. Authors should clearly state why the Senior Management Accountability Matrix exists and what regulatory obligations it fulfils.*

This document establishes the **Senior Management Accountability Matrix** for [Organization Name] in accordance with Bank Negara Malaysia's **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)** Policy Document, specifically **Clause 8.6**, which mandates that financial institutions clearly define and assign accountability for technology risk management at the senior management level.

The purpose of this matrix is to:

- Formally define the roles and responsibilities of senior management in relation to technology risk governance.
- Establish clear lines of accountability for the identification, ownership, management, and oversight of technology assets and associated risks.
- Provide a structured reference for Board of Directors (BOD) oversight, internal audit, regulatory examination, and governance reporting.
- Support a culture of technology risk awareness and accountability within [Organization Name]'s leadership structure.
- Ensure compliance with BNM RMiT requirements as well as applicable provisions under the **Financial Services Act 2013 (FSA)**, **Islamic Financial Services Act 2013 (IFSA)**, and **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the organisational boundaries of this document, including which entities, systems, functions, and personnel it covers.*

This document applies to:

- **Entities in scope:** [Organization Name] and all subsidiaries, branches, and affiliated entities operating under its technology governance framework, including [List entities as applicable].
- **Personnel in scope:** All members of the Board of Directors, Executive Management Committee (EXCO), C-Suite officers, and designated senior technology risk owners within [Organization Name].
- **Technology domains in scope:**
  - Core banking systems and critical applications
  - Information technology (IT) infrastructure
  - Cybersecurity and information security functions
  - Digital services and e-channels

### 6.3 Lifecycle Transition Governance

*Describe the governance process for transitioning assets between lifecycle stages.*

Lifecycle stage transitions require the following governance actions:

- **Planning to Implementation:** Written approval from the Asset Owner and endorsement by the Technology Steering Committee (TSC).
- **Implementation to Operational:** Post-Implementation Review (PIR) sign-off by the Asset Owner; notification to the CRO; update of this register within **[5 business days]** of go-live.
- **Operational to Refresh/Upgrade:** Technology roadmap approval by EXCO; updated accountability assignments confirmed by the Asset Owner.
- **Operational to Retirement:** Board-level notification for Critical assets; regulatory notification to BNM where applicable per RMiT requirements; data disposition confirmation from the Data Protection Officer (DPO).
- **Retirement to Decommissioned:** Formal closure certificate signed by the Asset Owner; archived in the document management system.

## 9. Technology Risk Escalation Framework

### 9.1 Escalation Principles

*This section defines how technology risk events and accountability disputes are escalated through senior management. Authors should align this framework with the organisation's Incident Management Policy.*

All senior management owners identified in Section 8 are required to escalate technology risk events and governance concerns in accordance with the following principles:

- **Timely escalation:** Risk events must be escalated within the timeframes defined below; delays in escalation are themselves reportable governance failures.
- **Transparency:** Owners must not withhold material information from escalation recipients.
- **Non-retaliation:** Personnel escalating genuine risk concerns are protected under [Organization Name]'s Whistleblowing Policy.

### 9.2 Escalation Thresholds and Pathways

| Severity Level | Trigger Criteria | Initial Escalation Target | Escalation Timeframe | Board Notification |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Severity 1 — Critical** | System outage affecting critical payment or banking services; data breach affecting >1,000 customers; ransomware or cyber attack on core systems | CEO, CISO, CIO simultaneously | Within **1 hour** of detection | Within **2 hours**; BNM notification per RMiT requirements |
| **Severity 2 — High** | Significant system degradation; potential data breach under investigation; third-party vendor breach affecting [Organization Name] | CIO, CISO, and CRO; CEO informed | Within **4 hours** | Within **24 hours** if confirmed material |
| **Severity 3 — Medium** | Non-critical system outage; control failure without immediate customer impact; minor data integrity issues | Asset Owner; CRO informed | Within **24 hours** | Included in next quarterly BRMC report |
| **Severity 4 — Low** | Minor incidents; single-system anomalies with no customer impact; near-misses | Asset Custodian; Owner informed | Within **5 business days** | Included in next quarterly risk report |
