*Summarize the specific requirements of RMiT Clause 11.2 that this document fulfills.*

BNM RMiT Clause 11.2 requires financial institutions to:

- Develop and implement a **Board-approved cybersecurity strategy** that sets the organization's overall cybersecurity direction.
- Ensure the strategy is **reviewed at least annually** and updated to reflect changes in the threat landscape, business environment, and regulatory requirements.
- Align the cybersecurity strategy with the institution's **overall business strategy and risk appetite**.
- Define **clear cybersecurity objectives** with measurable outcomes.
- Establish **governance and accountability** mechanisms for cybersecurity.
- Include a **roadmap of cybersecurity initiatives** with defined timelines and resource requirements.

## 8. Methodology and Approach

*Describe the methodologies, frameworks, and principles that underpin the organization's approach to cybersecurity.*

### 8.1 Guiding Principles

*State the core principles that guide cybersecurity decision-making and investment.*

[Organization Name]'s cybersecurity approach is governed by the following principles:

| # | Principle | Description |
|---|---|---|
| P-1 | **Risk-Based Approach** | Cybersecurity investments and controls are prioritized based on risk, with higher investment in protecting critical and high-risk assets. |
| P-2 | **Defense in Depth** | Multiple layers of security controls are implemented across people, processes, and technology to reduce reliance on any single control. |
| P-3 | **Least Privilege** | Access rights are granted on a need-to-know and need-to-use basis, and are regularly reviewed. |
| P-4 | **Assume Breach** | Security architecture and operations assume that a breach may occur and focus on rapid detection, containment, and recovery. |
| P-5 | **Security by Design** | Cybersecurity requirements are embedded into the design of technology solutions, business processes, and products from inception. |
| P-6 | **Resilience Over Prevention** | While prevention is pursued, the organization also invests in detection, response, and recovery capabilities to maintain resilience. |
| P-7 | **Shared Accountability** | Cybersecurity is the responsibility of every employee, not solely the IT or Security function. |
| P-8 | **Continuous Improvement** | The cybersecurity program is regularly assessed, tested, and improved based on threat intelligence, incident learnings, and maturity targets. |
| P-9 | **Customer Trust** | Protecting customer data and maintaining trust is a non-negotiable commitment. |
| P-10 | **Regulatory Alignment** | All cybersecurity activities are aligned with BNM RMiT, PDPA, and applicable regulatory requirements. |

### 9.2 Risk Classification and Scoring

**Likelihood Scale:**

| Rating | Level | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Certain | Expected to occur — has occurred previously and current controls are insufficient |
| 4 | Likely | Will probably occur in most circumstances |
| 3 | Possible | Might occur at some time |
| 2 | Unlikely | Could occur at some time, but is not expected |
| 1 | Rare | May occur only in exceptional circumstances |

**Impact Scale:**

| Rating | Level | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Catastrophic | Critical business functions unavailable >24 hours; mass customer data breach; regulatory enforcement action; systemic financial loss |
| 4 | Major | Significant business disruption; material data breach; regulatory findings; high financial loss |
| 3 | Moderate | Moderate business disruption; limited data compromise; operational loss; regulatory concern |
| 2 | Minor | Minor disruption; no data breach; minimal financial impact |
| 1 | Insignificant | Negligible impact; no service disruption |

**Risk Rating Matrix:**

| Likelihood \ Impact | 1 — Insignificant | 2 — Minor | 3 — Moderate | 4 — Major | 5 — Catastrophic |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

### Appendix G: Glossary of Terms

*Define key terms used in this document.*

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **APT (Advanced Persistent Threat)** | A prolonged and targeted cyberattack in which an intruder gains access to a network and remains undetected for an extended period. |
| **CISO (Chief Information Security Officer)** | The senior executive responsible for the organization's information and cybersecurity strategy and operations. |
| **CIRP (Cyber Incident Response Plan)** | The documented plan governing the organization's response to cybersecurity incidents. |
| **CSF (Cybersecurity Framework)** | NIST's framework for improving cybersecurity through five core functions: Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover. |
| **DLP (Data Loss Prevention)** | Technology and processes designed to prevent unauthorized transmission or exposure of sensitive data. |
| **EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response)** | Security technology that continuously monitors endpoints to detect and respond to cyber threats. |
| **IAM (Identity and Access Management)** | Policies, technologies, and processes for managing digital identities and controlling access to resources. |
| **MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)** | An authentication method requiring two or more verification factors beyond a username and password. |
| **MTTD (Mean Time to Detect)** | The average time from when a cybersecurity incident begins to when it is detected. |
| **MTTR (Mean Time to Respond)** | The average time from detection of a cybersecurity incident to its containment or resolution. |
| **PAM (Privileged Access Management)** | Tools and processes for controlling, monitoring, and auditing access by privileged users. |
| **PDPA** | Personal Data Protection Act 2010 — Malaysian legislation governing the processing of personal data. |
| **RMiT** | Risk Management in Technology — BNM's policy document governing technology and cybersecurity risk management for financial institutions. |
| **RPO (Recovery Point Objective)** | The maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured in time in the event of a disruption. |
| **RTO (Recovery Time Objective)** | The maximum acceptable time for a system or process to be restored following a disruption. |
| **SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)** | A system that collects and analyzes security event data from across the IT environment. |
| **SOC (Security Operations Centre)** | A team responsible for monitoring, detecting, analyzing, and responding to cybersecurity incidents. |
| **SOAR (Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response)** | Technology that enables automated responses to cybersecurity incidents. |
| **Threat Intelligence** | Evidence-based knowledge about existing or emerging threats that can be used to inform cybersecurity decisions. |
| **Vulnerability** | A weakness in a system, application, process, or control that could be exploited by a threat actor. |
| **Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA)** | A security model based on the principle of "never trust, always verify," requiring strict identity verification for every user and device. |
