## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This document serves as the organizational record template for Data Loss Prevention (DLP) alerts and incidents detected across [Organization Name]'s information systems, endpoints, networks, and cloud environments. It provides a structured framework for capturing, investigating, escalating, and resolving incidents involving potential unauthorized access, transmission, or exfiltration of sensitive or protected data.

This artifact fulfills the requirements of **Bank Negara Malaysia's Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, Clause 11.10**, which mandates financial institutions to maintain adequate controls and audit trails for the protection of customer and institutional data. Records contained herein support regulatory examination readiness, internal audit functions, and forensic investigation requirements.

### 1.2 Scope

This template applies to:

- All DLP alerts generated by [Organization Name]'s deployed DLP tooling (endpoint, network, email, and cloud channels)
- All personnel, systems, third parties, and contractors operating within the organization's technology environment
- All data classifications as defined in [Organization Name]'s Data Classification Policy, including but not limited to Restricted, Confidential, Internal, and Public data
- Incidents occurring across on-premises infrastructure, cloud platforms, and hybrid environments

### 1.3 Regulatory Context

| Regulation | Clause / Section | Requirement Summary |
|---|---|---|
| BNM RMiT | Clause 11.10 | Data loss prevention controls and incident logging |
| BNM RMiT | Clause 11.1 – 11.9 | Broader data management and protection obligations |
| PDPA 2010 | Section 9, 10 | Security and retention principles for personal data |
| NACSA | Critical Information Infrastructure Protection | Incident reporting for CII operators (if applicable) |
| ISO/IEC 27001:2022 | Annex A 8.12, 5.26 | Data leakage prevention and information security incident management |

| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information — information that can be used to identify a specific individual |
| **SOC** | Security Operations Centre — the team responsible for monitoring and responding to security events |
