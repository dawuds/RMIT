### 6.2 Responsibility Narrative

#### 6.2.1 Board of Directors / Board Risk Committee

The Board of Directors retains ultimate accountability for approving and overseeing the Organisation's Cybersecurity Strategy. The Board Risk Committee (BRC) acts on behalf of the Board to:

- Review and endorse the annual Cybersecurity Strategy Review Report;
- Approve the cybersecurity risk appetite and tolerance thresholds;
- Receive regular updates on the Organisation's cybersecurity posture; and
- Ensure that adequate resources are allocated to the delivery of the Cybersecurity Strategy.

#### 6.2.2 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

The CISO is the primary functional owner of the cybersecurity programme and is responsible for:

- Developing, maintaining, and implementing the Cybersecurity Strategy;
- Leading the cybersecurity strategy review process;
- Providing authoritative guidance on cybersecurity risk to senior management and the Board; and
- Maintaining awareness of the evolving threat landscape and its implications for the Organisation.

#### 6.2.3 Head of Technology Risk

The Head of Technology Risk is the **document owner** of this report and is responsible for:

- Coordinating and facilitating the annual cybersecurity strategy review;
- Producing this Cybersecurity Strategy Review Report;
- Maintaining the Technology Risk Register and escalating material risks; and
- Supporting governance reporting to the Technology Risk Committee and Management Risk Committee.

#### 6.2.4 CIO / CTO

The CIO / CTO is the first-line owner of technology risk and is responsible for:

- Ensuring that technology operations support the delivery of cybersecurity controls;
- Providing input to the cybersecurity strategy on technology direction and roadmap; and
- Implementing technology-related strategic initiatives within approved budgets.

#### 6.2.5 Business Unit Heads

Business Unit Heads are responsible for:

- First-line ownership of technology risk within their respective business functions;
- Ensuring compliance with the Organisation's cybersecurity policies and standards; and
- Supporting the implementation of cybersecurity controls relevant to their business operations.

#### 6.2.6 Head of Internal Audit

The Head of Internal Audit provides independent, third-line assurance over the design and operational effectiveness of cybersecurity controls, including:

- Conducting cybersecurity audit reviews as part of the annual audit plan; and
- Reporting material cybersecurity audit findings to the Board Audit Committee.

### 15.1 Ongoing Monitoring Framework

*Describe the continuous monitoring processes that provide assurance over the effectiveness of the cybersecurity strategy between annual reviews.*

[Organization Name] maintains a continuous monitoring framework to ensure that the Cybersecurity Strategy remains effective and responsive to changes in the threat environment. The monitoring framework comprises:

1. **Operational Monitoring** — Real-time and near-real-time monitoring of the security environment by the SOC, including log analysis, alert triage, and anomaly detection.
2. **Management Reporting** — Regular reporting of cybersecurity KPIs and risk indicators to management committees and the Board (see Section 15.2).
3. **Control Testing** — Periodic testing of control effectiveness, including vulnerability assessments, penetration testing, and control self-assessments.
4. **Threat Intelligence** — Continuous consumption and integration of threat intelligence from internal, industry, and national sources (NACSA, FinancialCERT).
5. **Regulatory Monitoring** — Active monitoring of BNM and other regulatory issuances that may affect the cybersecurity strategy.
6. **Third-Party Monitoring** — Ongoing monitoring of critical vendors through security ratings platforms, periodic assessments, and contractual security reviews.
7. **Incident Review** — Post-incident reviews and root cause analysis to identify systemic control weaknesses.
