## 1. Purpose and Scope

*This section defines the purpose of the Post-Implementation Review (PIR) and delineates the boundaries of the review. It must reference the relevant regulatory requirements and articulate the intended outcomes of the review exercise.*

### 1.1 Purpose

This Post-Implementation Review (PIR) Report documents a structured assessment of [Project/System Name] following its deployment into the production environment of [Organization Name]. The report evaluates whether the project has achieved its stated objectives, identifies deviations from the original business case and technical specifications, and captures lessons learned to improve future technology initiatives.

This document is prepared in accordance with **Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically **Clause 10.3**, which requires financial institutions to conduct post-implementation reviews to assess the effectiveness of technology implementations and the adequacy of controls introduced.

## 3. Methodology

*This section describes the approach, techniques, and processes used to conduct the Post-Implementation Review. The methodology must be sufficiently rigorous to support audit scrutiny and provide a defensible basis for findings and ratings.*

### 3.1 Review Approach

This review was conducted using the following approach:

- **Document Review** — Examination of project artefacts including the Business Case, Project Charter, Technical Design Documents, Test Evidence, and Change Management records.
- **Interview and Walkthrough** — Structured interviews with project stakeholders, business owners, and technical teams to validate documented outcomes.
- **Data and Log Analysis** — Analysis of system performance metrics, incident logs, and monitoring dashboards for the post-implementation period.
- **Control Testing** — Verification that controls specified in the project's security and risk documentation have been implemented and are operating effectively.
- **Benchmarking** — Comparison of outcomes against the success criteria defined in Section 2.2 and industry best practices.
