## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Purpose

*State the purpose of this document clearly, referencing the specific BNM RMiT clause that mandates its production. This section establishes the regulatory basis for the report.*

This Annual Technology Risk Report ("the Report") has been prepared by [Organization Name] ("the Institution") in fulfilment of its obligations under **Bank Negara Malaysia's Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically **Clause 16.7**, which requires financial institutions to submit a comprehensive annual technology risk report to BNM.

The Report provides a consolidated view of the Institution's technology risk landscape, risk governance arrangements, material technology incidents, risk exposures, and forward-looking risk management strategies for the reporting period **[1 January YYYY to 31 December YYYY]**.

The primary objectives of this Report are to:

- Document and communicate the Institution's technology risk profile to BNM and senior management;
- Demonstrate compliance with BNM RMiT requirements across all applicable technology risk domains;
- Provide transparency on material technology incidents, including those submitted via BNM LINK;

## 6. Classification and Categorisation of Technology Risks

*This section documents the institution's risk classification and categorisation methodology. It demonstrates to BNM that the institution applies a consistent, structured approach to understanding and prioritising technology risks.*

### 6.1 Risk Classification Framework

*Describe the institution's technology risk taxonomy and classification methodology, including how risks are identified, assessed, and categorised.*

[Organization Name] applies the following technology risk taxonomy, aligned with BNM RMiT requirements and industry best practice (e.g., NIST Cybersecurity Framework, ISO 27001, COBIT):

#### 6.1.1 Primary Risk Categories

| Risk Category | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Cybersecurity Risk** | Risk of unauthorised access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction of information or information systems | Phishing attacks, ransomware, data breaches, DDoS attacks, insider threats |
| **IT Infrastructure Risk** | Risk arising from failures or degradation of hardware, software, networks, or data centre facilities | Server hardware failures, network outages, data centre power failures |
| **Application / System Risk** | Risk arising from vulnerabilities, errors, or failures in business applications and systems | Software bugs, application vulnerabilities, system integration failures |
| **Data Management Risk** | Risk of data loss, corruption, unauthorised access, or non-compliance relating to data assets | Data quality failures, unauthorised data access, data loss incidents |
| **Technology Change Risk** | Risk arising from the implementation of technology changes, projects, or upgrades | Failed system migrations, inadequate change testing, project delivery failures |
| **Third-Party Technology Risk** | Risk arising from technology services provided by third parties, including cloud service providers | Cloud provider outages, vendor software vulnerabilities, outsourcing failures |
| **Business Continuity / Resilience Risk** | Risk that critical technology systems cannot be recovered within acceptable timeframes following a disruption | Inadequate BCP/DR arrangements, failed recovery tests |
| **Emerging Technology Risk** | Risk arising from the adoption or operation of new or rapidly evolving technologies | Artificial intelligence / ML model risk, quantum computing threats |

#### 6.1.2 Risk Severity Classification

*The following matrix is used to assess the severity of identified technology risks and incidents.*

| Severity Level | Impact Description | Likelihood Description | Response Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Catastrophic operational, financial, or reputational impact; potential systemic risk | Very likely to occur within the reporting period | Immediate escalation to Board and BNM; emergency response activated |
| **High** | Significant operational disruption; material financial loss or regulatory breach | Likely to occur within the reporting period | Escalation to Senior Management within 24 hours; urgent remediation |
| **Medium** | Moderate operational impact; limited financial loss; manageable with existing controls | Possible within the reporting period | Management-level review and remediation within 30 days |
| **Low** | Minor operational impact; negligible financial loss; within normal operating parameters | Unlikely to occur within the reporting period | Standard risk treatment process; resolution within 90 days |
