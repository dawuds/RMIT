## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Outsourcing Monitoring Report documents the oversight, governance, and continuous monitoring of all outsourced technology activities undertaken by [Organization Name]. It serves as the primary evidence artifact demonstrating compliance with Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, specifically **Clause 10.47**, which mandates that financial institutions maintain robust oversight mechanisms over technology service providers and outsourcing arrangements.

This report enables the Board of Directors, Senior Management, and relevant oversight committees to assess the operational health, risk posture, and regulatory compliance of all active outsourcing arrangements. It supports informed decision-making regarding vendor retention, remediation, or exit.

### 1.2 Scope

This report covers all outsourcing arrangements involving technology services, infrastructure, platforms, or processes that:

- Are material to the operations of [Organization Name];
- Involve the processing, storage, or transmission of customer data or critical operational data;
- Support regulated business functions subject to BNM oversight;
- Have been classified as outsourced technology activities pursuant to BNM RMiT definitions.

**In Scope:**
- All active Material Outsourcing Arrangements (MOA)
- All active Non-Material Outsourcing Arrangements (NMOA) involving technology
- Cloud service providers, managed service providers, and third-party technology vendors
- Sub-outsourcing arrangements where applicable

**Out of Scope:**
- Procurement of standard commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) software with no customisation or hosted delivery
- Arms-length market transactions as defined under BNM guidelines
- [List any other exclusions specific to the organization]

## 8. Exit Strategy Requirements

*This section documents the exit strategy provisions applicable to each outsourcing arrangement and records the current readiness status. A viable exit strategy must be maintained at all times and reviewed at each due diligence cycle or upon any material change to the arrangement or the vendor's risk profile.*

### 8.1 Exit Strategy Policy Requirements

Pursuant to BNM RMiT Clause 10.47 and [Organization Name]'s Outsourcing Policy, all outsourcing arrangements must maintain a documented and tested exit strategy that addresses the following:

- **Transition feasibility**: Availability of alternative service providers or in-house capability to absorb the function
- **Data portability**: Mechanisms to extract, migrate, and validate all data held by the vendor
- **Knowledge transfer**: Documentation of institutional knowledge, configurations, and operational procedures held by the vendor
- **Contractual exit provisions**: Notice periods, termination triggers, and wind-down obligations
- **Business continuity during transition**: Measures to maintain service continuity throughout the exit process
- **Regulatory notification**: BNM notification requirements for material outsourcing exits
- **Cost and timeline estimation**: Indicative cost and duration for orderly exit
