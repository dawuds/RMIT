## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Purpose

This Technology Risk Dashboard ("Dashboard") is a formal periodic reporting instrument prepared in accordance with **Bank Negara Malaysia's Policy Document on Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)**, specifically **Clause 8.1**, which requires financial institutions to maintain robust technology risk governance and provide the Board of Directors with sufficient, timely, and accurate information to discharge their oversight responsibilities.

The Dashboard is designed to:

- Provide the Board with a consolidated view of [Organization Name]'s technology risk posture across all material technology domains;
- Enable the Board to assess whether technology risks are being managed within the approved risk appetite and tolerance thresholds;
- Facilitate informed Board decision-making on material technology risk matters;
- Demonstrate regulatory compliance with BNM RMiT governance requirements; and
- Track the effectiveness of management's risk mitigation and remediation actions over time.

### 5.5 Risk Classification Methodology

*Briefly describe the methodology used to classify and rate technology risks, ensuring Board understanding of the basis for ratings.*

Technology risks presented in this Dashboard are assessed using the following methodology:

- **Inherent Risk:** Assessed based on the likelihood and potential impact of the risk in the absence of any controls, using the organisation's approved 5×5 risk matrix.
- **Control Effectiveness:** Evaluated against the design adequacy and operating effectiveness of controls mitigating each risk. Ratings are: *Strong*, *Adequate*, or *Weak*.
- **Residual Risk:** Determined by combining inherent risk rating with control effectiveness, representing the risk remaining after controls are applied.
- **Risk Ratings:** *Critical* (immediate escalation to Board), *High* (Board awareness, Management action plan required), *Medium* (Management monitoring), *Low* (Routine management).

### Appendix B: Technology Risk Definitions and Glossary

*Define key terms used in this Dashboard to ensure consistent understanding across Board members and management.*

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Asset Owner** | The individual or role with ultimate accountability for a technology asset, including its risk posture, compliance, and appropriate use. |
| **Asset Custodian** | The individual or role responsible for the day-to-day management, maintenance, and operation of a technology asset on behalf of the asset owner. |
| **Critical System** | A system whose failure would significantly impact regulated activities, customer service, financial integrity, or the organisation's ability to meet regulatory obligations. |
| **End-of-Life (EOL)** | A system or component for which the vendor has ceased providing software updates, security patches, or technical support. |
| **Inherent Risk** | The level of risk existing in the absence of any mitigating controls or management actions. |
| **Key Risk Indicator (KRI)** | A quantitative or qualitative metric used to monitor and signal the likelihood or magnitude of a risk event. |
| **MTTD** | Mean Time to Detect — the average elapsed time between the occurrence of a security event and its detection. |
| **MTTR** | Mean Time to Respond — the average elapsed time between detection of a security event and the initiation of a response action. |
| **Residual Risk** | The level of risk remaining after existing controls and mitigations have been applied. |
| **Risk Appetite** | The aggregate level and types of risk the organisation is willing to accept in pursuit of its business objectives, as approved by the Board. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | The acceptable deviation from the risk appetite within which the organisation is prepared to operate. |
| **RTO** | Recovery Time Objective — the maximum acceptable duration for restoring a system or service following a disruption. |
| **RPO** | Recovery Point Objective — the maximum acceptable period of data loss, measured in time, following a disruption. |
| **Threat Actor** | An individual or group capable of, and with intent to, exploit vulnerabilities in an organisation's technology environment. |
