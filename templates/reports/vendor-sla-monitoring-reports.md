## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Vendor SLA Monitoring Report documents the ongoing performance of third-party technology vendors engaged by **[Organization Name]** against contractually agreed Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs). It provides management with a consolidated view of vendor compliance, risk posture, and service continuity, in fulfilment of obligations under Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)** Policy Document, specifically **Clause 10.48**.

This report supports the Board and Senior Management in exercising effective oversight of technology outsourcing arrangements and ensures that vendor relationships are managed in a manner commensurate with the risk exposure they present to [Organization Name].

### 1.2 Scope

This report covers all technology vendors and third-party service providers engaged by [Organization Name] that are subject to formal SLA arrangements, including but not limited to:

- **Critical IT outsourcing arrangements** as defined under RMiT and the BNM Guidelines on Outsourcing
- Cloud service providers, managed security service providers (MSSPs), and infrastructure vendors
- Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) and Platform-as-a-Service (PaaS) providers supporting regulated business functions
- Data centre co-location and network connectivity providers

*Vendors categorised as non-critical or ad hoc service engagements below the defined materiality threshold may be excluded from this report at the discretion of the Head of IT Operations, provided that such exclusions are documented and approved.*

### 1.3 Regulatory Basis

| Regulation | Clause / Section | Requirement Summary |
|---|---|---|
| BNM RMiT Policy Document | Clause 10.48 | Financial institutions must monitor and review the performance of technology service providers against agreed SLAs on a continuous basis and conduct periodic formal reviews |
| BNM Guidelines on Outsourcing | Various | Ongoing monitoring, escalation, and exit planning obligations for outsourced functions |
| Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA) | Part III | Data processor obligations and accountability for vendor data handling |
| NACSA Critical Information Infrastructure (CII) Framework | Section 4 | Supply chain risk management for CII-designated entities |

## 4. SLA and KPI Definitions

*Define all SLA metrics and KPIs used in this report. Definitions must align with contractual definitions. Any deviation from contract language must be noted.*

### 4.1 Standard SLA Metrics

| Metric ID | Metric Name | Definition | Measurement Method | Calculation Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SLA-01 | System Availability | Percentage of time the service is operational and accessible within agreed parameters | Automated uptime monitoring | (Agreed Hours − Downtime) / Agreed Hours × 100% |
| SLA-02 | Incident Response Time (P1) | Time elapsed from incident detection to vendor acknowledgement for Priority 1 incidents | ITSM timestamp correlation | Clock starts from incident raised in ITSM |
| SLA-03 | Incident Resolution Time (P1) | Time elapsed from vendor acknowledgement to full service restoration for Priority 1 incidents | ITSM timestamp correlation | Clock stops upon confirmed service restoration |
| SLA-04 | Mean Time to Recover (MTTR) | Average time to restore service following an unplanned outage | Calculated from incident logs | Sum of all restoration times / number of incidents |
| SLA-05 | Change Success Rate | Percentage of approved changes implemented without causing a service degradation or incident | Change management records | Successful changes / Total changes × 100% |
| SLA-06 | Security Patch Compliance | Percentage of critical and high-severity patches applied within contracted timeframes | Patch management reports | Compliant patches / Total required patches × 100% |
| SLA-07 | Data Backup Success Rate | Percentage of scheduled backups completed successfully | Backup management console | Successful backups / Scheduled backups × 100% |
| SLA-08 | Disaster Recovery RTO | Time to restore service to operational state following a declared disaster | DR test results and actual events | Measured against contractual RTO commitment |

| SLA-03 | ≤ [4] hours | [4]–[8] hours | > [8] hours |

## 5. Vendor Risk Tiering Criteria

*Document the criteria used to classify vendors by risk tier. Tiering determines the intensity of monitoring, due diligence, and contractual requirements applied to each vendor.*

### 5.1 Risk Tier Classification Framework

| Risk Tier | Label | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Critical | Vendors providing services that are essential to the operation of critical business functions; substitution would require significant time and cost; access to sensitive customer or financial data | Core banking platform, cloud infrastructure, primary data centre |
| **Tier 2** | Important | Vendors providing significant but non-critical services; alternatives exist in the market; limited or controlled data access | Secondary applications, network connectivity, MSSP |
| **Tier 3** | Standard | Vendors providing commodity or low-risk services; readily substitutable; no access to sensitive data | Hardware maintenance, generic software licences, courier services |
