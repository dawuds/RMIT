## 3. Objectives and Scope of Remediation

### 3.1 Remediation Objectives

*State the specific, measurable objectives that the remediation programme aims to achieve.*

The remediation programme established by [Organization Name] in response to BNM's examination findings is governed by the following objectives:

1. **Full Closure of All Findings:** Achieve 100% closure of all [#] examination findings within the agreed remediation timeframes communicated to BNM.
2. **Root Cause Elimination:** Address and eliminate the underlying root causes of identified deficiencies, rather than implementing superficial or temporary corrective measures.
3. **Control Enhancement:** Strengthen the technology risk and information security control environment to prevent recurrence of similar findings in future examinations.
4. **Regulatory Transparency:** Maintain open, accurate, and timely communication with BNM throughout the remediation lifecycle, including prompt escalation of delays or material developments.
5. **Governance Integration:** Embed remediation progress tracking within the institution's existing technology risk governance framework, including Board and Senior Management reporting.
6. **Sustainability of Remediation:** Ensure that all remediated controls are operationally embedded, documented, tested, and subject to ongoing monitoring to sustain improvements beyond initial closure.

### 3.2 Scope of Remediation Programme

*Define what is and is not included in the remediation programme.*

**In Scope:**

- All findings listed in BNM's examination findings letter dated [Date] with reference [Reference Number]
- Associated control gaps, process deficiencies, and documentation shortfalls identified during examination fieldwork
- Supporting technology systems, infrastructure components, and third-party services referenced in findings
- Policies, procedures, and standards requiring update or creation as part of remediation

**Out of Scope:**

- Enhancement initiatives not directly linked to examination findings (tracked separately under [System/Register Name])
- Findings from other BNM examinations or regulatory bodies (reported separately)
- Business-as-usual technology change projects unless directly required for finding closure

## 7. Notification Trigger Criteria

### 7.1 Overview

*Explain the circumstances under which [Organization Name] is required to notify BNM outside of the regular quarterly reporting cycle.*

In addition to quarterly progress reports, [Organization Name] is obligated under **RMiT Clause 16.6** and internal governance policies to provide interim or ad hoc notifications to BNM when specific trigger events occur that materially affect the remediation programme. This section defines the criteria that activate such notification obligations.

### 7.2 Mandatory Notification Triggers

The following events or conditions constitute mandatory notification triggers requiring immediate or expedited communication to BNM:

| Trigger ID | Trigger Category | Trigger Description | Threshold / Condition | Notification Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| T-01 | Material Delay | A Critical or High severity finding will not be remediated by its agreed closure date | Delay of more than [X] business days confirmed or projected | Immediate written notification |
| T-02 | Escalation of Severity | A finding is re-assessed as higher severity than originally classified | Upgrade from Medium to High, or High to Critical | Within [X] business days of re-assessment |
| T-03 | New Finding Identified | A material technology risk or control deficiency related to the original examination scope is newly discovered | Any finding of High or Critical severity | Immediate written notification |
| T-04 | Significant Incident | A technology or cybersecurity incident affects systems or controls that are the subject of open examination findings | Incidents classified as Significant under BNM incident reporting requirements | Concurrent with incident notification |
| T-05 | Programme Cancellation or Suspension | Any remediation workstream is halted, cancelled, or indefinitely deferred | Workstream suspension exceeding [X] business days | Within [X] business days |
| T-06 | Change in Control or Structure | A material change to the institution's ownership, structure, or senior leadership affects remediation accountability | Board/C-suite change affecting programme sponsor or owners | Within [X] business days |
| T-07 | Regulatory or Legal Development | A new regulatory requirement or legal constraint prevents or materially alters a committed remediation action | Any binding legal or regulatory constraint | Within [X] business days of becoming aware |
| T-08 | Repeated Failure | A previously closed finding is identified as recurring or not sustainably remediated | Recurrence within [X] months of closure | Within [X] business days of identification |
| T-09 | Third-Party Failure | A critical vendor or service provider fails to deliver remediation-dependent services | Vendor default or material service failure | Within [X] business days |
| T-10 | Board Escalation | Board of Directors or senior governance body makes a decision materially affecting remediation commitments | Any Board resolution impacting programme scope, timeline, or resources | Within [X] business days of Board decision |

### 7.3 Discretionary Notification Triggers

*The following conditions may warrant notification to BNM at management's discretion, taking into account the institution's regulatory risk appetite and relationship management approach.*

| Trigger | Description | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Minor Delays — Low Severity Findings | Delays of [X] or fewer business days for Low severity findings | Include in next quarterly report with explanation |
| Resource Constraints | Temporary resource shortfalls that may affect timelines but are being actively managed | Notify if risk of delay exceeds [X] business days |
| Positive Material Progress | Significant acceleration of programme completion ahead of schedule | Notify as positive update; may support favourable supervisory assessment |
| Industry-Wide Issue | A systemic technology issue affecting the industry is also affecting [Organization Name]'s remediation | Notify if BNM has issued industry guidance on the matter |

### 7.4 Trigger Assessment Process

*Describe the internal process for identifying, assessing, and escalating potential notification trigger events.*

1. **Identification:** Any member of the Remediation Working Group, Finding Owner, or Action Owner who identifies a potential trigger event must immediately notify the Programme Manager (Head of Regulatory Affairs).
2. **Assessment:** The Programme Manager, in consultation with the Technology Risk Lead and Legal/Compliance, assesses whether the event meets the threshold for a mandatory or discretionary trigger.
3. **Escalation:** If a mandatory trigger is confirmed, the Programme Director (CIO) and Programme Sponsor (CEO/CRO) are notified within **[X] hours**.
4. **Documentation:** The trigger event is documented in the Remediation Issues Log (Appendix B) and the BNM Notification Register (Appendix C).
5. **Notification:** The Head of Regulatory Affairs prepares and submits the BNM notification in accordance with Section 8 (Notification Timeline Requirements).

### 10.2 Key Stakeholder Responsibilities (Narrative)

**Board Risk Committee / Board Information Technology Committee**
Provides ultimate oversight and accountability for the institution's examination remediation programme. Reviews remediation progress reports and approves material decisions, including timeline extensions and scope changes that require Board-level notification to BNM.

**Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**
Bears executive accountability for the institution's regulatory relationships and the success of the remediation programme. Approves Critical finding delay notifications and significant ad hoc communications to BNM.

**Chief Risk Officer (CRO)**
Responsible for the overall risk governance framework within which the remediation programme operates. Approves quarterly progress reports before BNM submission and chairs the Management Risk Committee's oversight of remediation.

**Chief Information Officer (CIO)**
Accountable for the operational delivery of technology-related remediation actions. Chairs the Technology Risk Steering Committee and serves as Programme Director for the remediation programme.

**Head of Regulatory Affairs**
Serves as Programme Manager for the remediation programme. Responsible for the end-to-end preparation, coordination, review, and submission of progress reports and notifications to BNM. Serves as the primary point of contact with BNM for examination-related matters.

**Head of Technology Risk**
Provides subject matter expertise on technology risk and control frameworks. Leads detailed tracking of milestone progress and coordinates with Finding Owners on remediation quality and evidence sufficiency.

**Chief Internal Auditor**
Provides independent assurance of finding closure, conducting targeted reviews or validations of completed remediation actions as requested by the programme governance bodies. Reports assurance outcomes to the Board Audit Committee.

**Finding Owners**
Each Finding Owner is accountable for the overall closure of their assigned finding(s), including management of Action Owners, escalation of risks and issues, and sign-off on evidence packages submitted to BNM.

**Action Owners**
Responsible for the day-to-day execution of specific remediation tasks and milestones. Provide status updates to Finding Owners and the Remediation Working Group on an agreed frequency.
