## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This document establishes the structured action plan framework for [Organization Name] to address all findings identified during regulatory examinations conducted by Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) or other competent authorities. It provides a systematic approach to tracking, remediating, and reporting on examination findings in compliance with BNM's **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)** Policy Document, specifically **Clause 16.6**, which mandates that financial institutions maintain formalised processes for addressing regulatory examination findings within prescribed timelines.

This template ensures that [Organization Name] demonstrates accountability, transparency, and continuous improvement in its technology risk management posture in response to regulatory scrutiny.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the scope of applicability, including the specific examination, covered systems, business units, and geographic coverage. Update this section for each new examination cycle.*

This document applies to:

- All technology risk and cybersecurity examination findings issued by BNM following on-site or off-site supervisory examinations of [Organization Name]
- All business units, subsidiaries, and outsourced functions within the scope defined in the examination letter reference **[BNM Examination Reference Number]**
- All technology systems, infrastructure, and processes classified under the RMiT policy framework
- Third-party service providers and critical outsourcing partners where findings relate to outsourced functions

**Out of Scope:**

- Audit findings from internal audit exercises (governed separately under [Reference Internal Audit Policy])
- Non-technology-related examination findings (governed under [Reference Operational Risk Policy])

## 2. Objectives and Scope of Examination Findings

### 2.1 Objectives of This Action Plan

*State the primary objectives this action plan is designed to achieve. These should directly correspond to the regulatory expectations articulated in the examination findings letter.*

This Examination Finding Action Plan is established with the following objectives:

1. **Remediation** — Systematically address each finding raised by BNM through targeted corrective and preventive actions
2. **Accountability** — Assign clear ownership and governance responsibility for each remediation workstream
3. **Traceability** — Maintain an auditable record of progress against committed timelines for regulatory review
4. **Recurrence Prevention** — Implement root-cause-driven controls to prevent recurrence of identified deficiencies
5. **Regulatory Reporting** — Satisfy the submission requirements under RMiT Clause 16.6 via BNM LINK within prescribed deadlines
6. **Board Oversight** — Provide the Board Technology Risk Committee (BTRC) and Senior Management with consolidated visibility of regulatory remediation status

### 2.2 Finding Classification Framework

*This section defines how findings are classified to prioritise remediation effort and escalation urgency.*

| Classification | Definition | Typical Remediation Period |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Finding poses an immediate and significant threat to the institution's technology resilience, cybersecurity posture, or regulatory compliance. Potential for material harm to customers or financial system stability. | ≤ 30 days |
| **Major** | Finding indicates a material control gap or systemic weakness requiring substantive remediation. Non-compliance with specific RMiT requirements. | ≤ 90 days |
| **Minor** | Finding highlights areas of improvement or partial compliance where the risk exposure is limited. | ≤ 180 days |
| **Observation** | Supervisory observation without formal finding status. Management response expected. | ≤ 180 days |

## 4. Accountable Owners

### 4.1 Ownership Framework

*This section defines how accountability is assigned for each examination finding. Ownership assignment must reflect genuine decision-making authority over the affected systems, processes, and resources.*

Ownership operates at three levels:

- **Executive Sponsor** — A member of Senior Management (C-suite or equivalent) who holds ultimate accountability for the finding's remediation and closure. The Executive Sponsor is responsible for escalation and resource allocation.
- **Finding Owner** — The Head of Function or Business Unit directly responsible for the control gap. The Finding Owner is accountable for day-to-day management of the remediation workstream and progress reporting.
- **Action Owner** — The individual(s) responsible for executing specific remediation actions within the finding. Action Owners report progress to the Finding Owner on an agreed frequency.

## 5. Notification Trigger Criteria

### 5.1 Internal Notification Triggers

*This section defines the events or conditions that require escalation or notification within [Organization Name]. Timely internal notification ensures that appropriate governance bodies are engaged before regulatory deadlines are missed.*

The following conditions **must** trigger immediate escalation to the relevant governance forum:

| Trigger Event | Escalation Level | Notified Party | Maximum Response Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Action target date will be missed by > 14 days | Level 1 | Finding Owner → Executive Sponsor | 24 hours of identification |
| Action target date will be missed by > 30 days | Level 2 | Executive Sponsor → CRO / Head of Regulatory Affairs | 24 hours of identification |
| New or worsening risk identified during remediation | Level 2 | Finding Owner → CRO / CISO | 24 hours of identification |
| Critical finding with no completed actions at 50% elapsed time | Level 3 | CRO → Board ITRC Chair | 48 hours of identification |
| Evidence of recurrence of a previously closed finding | Level 3 | Head of Regulatory Affairs → Board ITRC | 24 hours of identification |
| Material change in scope or complexity of finding | Level 2 | Finding Owner → Head of Regulatory Affairs | 48 hours of identification |
| BNM follow-up enquiry received | Level 3 | Head of Regulatory Affairs → CRO → CEO | Immediately upon receipt |

### 5.2 External (Regulatory) Notification Triggers

*Define the conditions under which [Organization Name] is obligated to notify BNM proactively, prior to the next scheduled submission.*

In accordance with RMiT Clause 16.6 and BNM LINK submission protocols, [Organization Name] **must** notify BNM proactively when:

1. A **Critical** finding cannot be remediated within the committed timeline due to circumstances outside management's control (e.g., third-party dependencies, system complexity)
2. A remediation action uncovers a **new material risk or control failure** not identified in the original examination
3. An **extension request** is required for any finding beyond the original committed timeline
4. A finding previously reported as **Closed** is determined to have **recurred or been inadequately remediated**
5. A **material incident** occurs that is directly related to an open examination finding

### 5.3 Escalation Matrix

```
Action-Level Delay (< 14 days)
        ↓
    Action Owner → Finding Owner [24 hrs]

Action-Level Delay (14–30 days) OR Risk Escalation
        ↓
    Finding Owner → Executive Sponsor → Head of Regulatory Affairs [24 hrs]

Action-Level Delay (> 30 days) OR Critical Finding at Risk
        ↓
    Executive Sponsor → CRO → Board ITRC Chair [48 hrs]

External Notification Required
        ↓
    Head of Regulatory Affairs → CRO → CEO → BNM LINK Submission
```

### 6.3 Timeline Breach Protocol

*Define the steps to be taken if a regulatory submission deadline is at risk of being missed.*

If a submission deadline is at risk, the following protocol must be followed:

1. **T-14 days:** Head of Regulatory Affairs flags the risk to CRO and relevant Finding Owners
2. **T-7 days:** CRO convenes emergency remediation review; determines if extension request to BNM is required
3. **T-3 days:** If extension is required, draft extension request is prepared and submitted via BNM LINK with: (a) justification for delay, (b) revised timeline with commitment dates, (c) evidence of interim controls in place
4. **T-0 (deadline):** If submission cannot be made, CRO notifies CEO immediately; CEO escalates to Board Chair and BNM Relationship Manager

## 7. BNM LINK Submission Process

### 7.1 BNM LINK Overview

*This section documents the end-to-end process for preparing, reviewing, approving, and submitting examination-related reports via BNM's LINK portal. It ensures consistency, accuracy, and audit traceability for all regulatory submissions.*

BNM LINK is the primary digital platform through which [Organization Name] submits regulatory reports, action plans, and correspondence to Bank Negara Malaysia. All examination-related submissions must be made exclusively via BNM LINK unless otherwise directed in writing by BNM.

### 7.2 Submission Preparation Process

*Follow this process for every scheduled or triggered submission.*

**Step 1 — Draft Preparation (T-21 days)**

- Head of Regulatory Affairs or designated Regulatory Affairs Officer initiates the submission draft in the BNM LINK portal or in the approved internal template
- Finding Owners provide written status updates for each open finding, including: percentage of actions completed, evidence artefacts available, projected completion dates, and blockers
- All supporting evidence documents are collated and indexed per Appendix B (Evidence Index Template)

**Step 2 — Internal Quality Review (T-14 days)**

- Technology Risk function reviews accuracy of technical remediation descriptions and evidence adequacy
- Compliance function confirms alignment with RMiT clause references and regulatory language
- Legal Counsel reviews submission language for any statements that may carry legal implications

**Step 3 — Senior Management Review (T-10 days)**

- CRO reviews the consolidated submission for completeness and accuracy
- Executive Sponsors confirm accuracy of the sections pertaining to their respective findings
- Any material changes to committed timelines must be escalated to the CEO at this stage

**Step 4 — Board / Delegated Authority Approval (T-7 days)**

- BTRC Chair (or delegated authority per the Approval Authority Matrix) provides written approval for the submission
- Approval is documented in the Version History (Section 9.1) of this document
- A signed copy of the Approval Sign-Off (Section 9.2) is retained in the regulatory records management system

**Step 5 — BNM LINK Submission (On or Before Deadline)**

| Submission Element | Responsible | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Log in to BNM LINK portal | Head of Regulatory Affairs | Access via [BNM LINK URL] using authorised institutional credentials |
| Navigate to Examination Responses module | Head of Regulatory Affairs | Select the relevant examination reference number |
| Upload Action Plan document | Head of Regulatory Affairs | Upload the signed PDF version of this document |
| Upload supporting evidence | Regulatory Affairs Officer | Upload all items listed in the Evidence Index (Appendix B) |
| Complete submission form fields | Head of Regulatory Affairs | Ensure all mandatory fields are populated accurately |
| Submit and retain confirmation | Head of Regulatory Affairs | Download and file the BNM LINK submission confirmation receipt |

**Step 6 — Post-Submission (Within 3 business days)**

- BNM LINK submission confirmation is filed in the Regulatory Correspondence Register
- Head of Regulatory Affairs notifies CRO and relevant Executive Sponsors of submission completion
- Submission date and BNM acknowledgement are recorded in the Submission Schedule (Section 6.2)
