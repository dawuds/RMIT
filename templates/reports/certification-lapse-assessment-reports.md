## 2. Regulatory Context

### 2.1 BNM RMiT Clause 14.2 — Technology Service Provider Oversight

*Summarize the regulatory obligations imposed by BNM RMiT Clause 14.2 as they pertain to vendor certification and ongoing assurance. This section establishes the legal and regulatory basis for conducting this assessment.*

Pursuant to BNM's Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, Clause 14.2 requires that financial institutions maintain continuous oversight of technology service providers, including the monitoring of third-party certifications and assurance reports. Where a vendor's certification has lapsed or been suspended, the institution must:

- Conduct a formal risk reassessment to determine the continued adequacy of controls;
- Document findings and remediation actions;
- Escalate material risks to appropriate governance bodies; and
- Maintain records of the reassessment for supervisory review.

## 3. Assessment Scope and Criteria

### 3.1 Assessment Objectives

*State the specific objectives of this risk reassessment. These should be measurable and directly linked to the certification lapse event.*

The primary objectives of this assessment are:

1. To determine the nature and materiality of the certification lapse and its implications for [Organization Name]'s risk posture.
2. To evaluate whether compensating controls are in place and adequate to maintain an acceptable level of residual risk during the lapse period.
3. To assess the vendor's remediation plan and timeline for recertification.
4. To recommend risk treatment actions, including continued engagement, enhanced monitoring, or vendor exit proceedings.

## 4. Methodology

### 4.1 Assessment Approach

*Describe the overall methodology used to conduct this assessment. Include data collection methods, review techniques, and how conclusions were reached.*

This assessment was conducted using a structured risk-based approach comprising the following steps:

1. **Documentation Review** — Review of the vendor's current certification status, previous audit reports, and contractual obligations.
2. **Vendor Inquiry** — Formal written request to the vendor for information regarding the lapse, root cause, and remediation plan.

4. **Stakeholder Interviews** — Interviews with internal business units relying on the affected service.
5. **Risk Rating** — Application of [Organization Name]'s standard risk rating methodology to quantify residual risk.
