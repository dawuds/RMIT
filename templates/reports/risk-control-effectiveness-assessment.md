## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section states why this assessment exists and its regulatory basis. Describe the objective of the assessment and how it fulfils the institution's obligations under RMiT Clause 10.3.*

This Risk Control Effectiveness Assessment (RCEA) documents the structured evaluation of technology risk controls implemented by [Organization Name] to determine whether those controls are operating as intended, are commensurate with the institution's risk appetite, and remain effective in managing identified risks within acceptable thresholds.

This assessment is conducted in compliance with Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) Policy Document on **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)**, specifically **Clause 10.3**, which requires financial institutions to periodically assess the effectiveness of controls implemented to manage technology risks. The findings of this assessment inform the institution's risk posture, support Board and Senior Management oversight, and feed into the Technology Risk Register and risk reporting cycle.

### 2.2 Effectiveness Rating Criteria

*Define the rating scale used to evaluate each control. Ratings must be consistently applied across all assessors and agreed prior to fieldwork commencing.*

The following effectiveness rating scale is applied uniformly across all controls assessed:

| Rating | Label | Definition | Required Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Effective | Control is operating as designed with no identified gaps. Evidence is complete and consistent. | No action required. Continue monitoring. |
| **2** | Largely Effective | Control is operating substantially as designed. Minor gaps exist but do not materially impair the control objective. | Monitor; address minor gaps within [90] days. |
| **3** | Partially Effective | Control is operational but significant gaps or inconsistencies exist. Risk exposure is elevated. | Remediation plan required within [60] days. |
| **4** | Ineffective | Control is not operating as intended or key control elements are absent. Risk is not being adequately managed. | Immediate escalation. Compensating controls required. |
| **5** | Not Assessed | Control was not assessed in this cycle. | Reason documented; schedule for next cycle. |

### 2.3 Assessment Criteria

*State the specific criteria against which controls are evaluated. These criteria form the basis of the assessment judgement.*

Each control is evaluated against the following criteria:

- **Design Adequacy:** Is the control designed appropriately to address the identified risk?
- **Operational Consistency:** Is the control applied consistently across all in-scope systems, processes, and personnel?
- **Timeliness:** Is the control executed within required timeframes and frequencies?
- **Evidence Quality:** Is sufficient, reliable evidence available to substantiate the control's operation?
- **Exception Management:** Are exceptions to the control identified, logged, escalated, and resolved appropriately?
- **Residual Risk Alignment:** Does the residual risk, post-control, remain within the institution's approved risk appetite?

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Assessment Approach

*Describe how the assessment was conducted, including the techniques and methods used to gather evidence and evaluate control effectiveness.*

This assessment was conducted using a risk-based approach, with assessment intensity calibrated to the criticality of the risk domain and control. The following assessment techniques were employed:

| Technique | Description | Controls Applicable |
|---|---|---|
| **Inquiry** | Interviews and discussions held with control owners and process participants to understand control design and operation. | All controls |
| **Observation** | Direct observation of control activities being performed (e.g., access reviews, patch deployment processes). | [Control IDs] |
| **Inspection** | Review of documentation, policies, logs, reports, and records to verify control operation and consistency. | All controls |
| **Re-performance** | Independent re-execution of control procedures to validate accuracy and completeness of outputs. | [Control IDs] |
| **Data Analytics** | Analysis of system-generated data (e.g., access logs, vulnerability scan results, alert metrics) to identify anomalies or control failures. | [Control IDs] |
