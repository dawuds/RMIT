## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section explains the intent of the document and the regulatory obligation it fulfils. Authors should clearly articulate why this assessment is conducted and what it is designed to achieve.*

This Board Competency Assessment Report documents the annual evaluation of the technology knowledge, skills, and capability levels of the Board of Directors of [Organization Name]. The assessment is conducted in accordance with Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, specifically **Paragraph 8.3**, which requires financial institutions to ensure that board members possess adequate technology-related competencies to fulfil their oversight responsibilities in managing technology and cyber risks.

The report serves the following purposes:

- To provide a structured, evidence-based assessment of each board member's technology and cybersecurity literacy.
- To identify individual and collective competency gaps within the Board.
- To inform targeted training, development, and succession planning.
- To demonstrate to BNM and other regulators that the Board exercises informed oversight of technology risk.
- To serve as a continuous monitoring record updated at a minimum on a **quarterly** basis, with a comprehensive annual review.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the boundaries of this assessment — who is covered, which competency domains are assessed, and which reporting periods apply.*

This report applies to:

**In Scope:**

- All members of the Board of Directors of [Organization Name], including:
  - Executive Directors
  - Non-Executive Directors (NED)
  - Independent Non-Executive Directors (INED)
  - Board Committee members with technology or risk oversight mandates (e.g., Board Risk Committee, Board Audit Committee, Board Technology Committee)

**Competency Domains Assessed:**

- Technology governance and strategy
- Cybersecurity risk awareness and oversight
- Digital transformation and emerging technology
- Data governance and data privacy (including PDPA obligations)
- IT risk management frameworks (including RMiT)
- Business continuity and technology resilience
- Regulatory technology (RegTech) and supervisory technology (SupTech)
- Cloud computing and third-party technology risk
