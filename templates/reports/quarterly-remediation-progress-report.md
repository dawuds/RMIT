## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Purpose

This Quarterly Remediation Progress Report is prepared in accordance with Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically **Clause 18.2**, which mandates that financial institutions maintain and report on the status of technology risk remediation activities to the Board of Directors on a regular basis.

The purpose of this report is to:

- Provide the Board of Directors with a transparent and comprehensive view of the status of all open technology risk remediation activities;
- Track progress against agreed remediation plans, milestones, and target completion dates;
- Identify risks, impediments, and resource constraints that may affect the timely closure of remediation items;
- Demonstrate [Organization Name]'s commitment to maintaining an effective technology risk management framework in compliance with BNM RMiT requirements; and
- Enable informed Board oversight and decision-making in relation to technology risk remediation.

### 2.2 Scope

This report covers all technology risk remediation activities arising from the following sources during the period **[Start Date]** to **[End Date]**:

- BNM regulatory examination findings and directives;
- Internal technology risk assessments and audits;
- External/independent technology audits and penetration testing exercises;
- Vulnerability assessments and threat intelligence findings;
- Incidents and near-misses with identified root causes requiring remediation;
- Self-identified control deficiencies reported by technology and business units.

## 8. Progress Tracking Mechanism

*Describe the methodology, tools, and governance processes used to track and report on remediation progress. This section provides the Board with assurance that a robust tracking framework is in place.*

### 8.1 Tracking Framework Overview

The remediation progress tracking framework for [Organization Name] operates on the following principles:

- **Single Source of Truth:** All remediation items are maintained in [Name of System, e.g., ServiceNow / Archer GRC / SharePoint Remediation Register], which serves as the authoritative record for all findings, actions, milestones, and evidence.
- **Evidence-Based Closure:** No remediation item may be marked as closed without documented evidence of completion, as defined in the Evidence Requirements section below.
- **Independent Validation:** All Critical and High findings are subject to independent validation by [Internal Audit / Second Line Risk / External Auditor] prior to closure.
- **Escalation Triggers:** Automated and manual escalation protocols are in place to ensure timely action on at-risk or overdue items.

### 8.2 Reporting Cadence and Governance

| Ad Hoc Reporting | As required | [Relevant forum] | [Report Owner] | Material incidents; BNM requests |

### 8.3 Status Definitions

*The following standardized status definitions are used consistently across all remediation reporting.*

| Status | Definition | Visual Indicator |
|---|---|---|
| **Completed** | Remediation action fully implemented; evidence gathered; independently validated where required | Green |
| **On Track** | Remediation action in progress; all milestones achieved to date; no risk to target completion date | Green |
| **At Risk** | Remediation action in progress; one or more milestones delayed or at risk of delay; target date may be missed without intervention | Amber |
| **Off Track** | Remediation action significantly delayed; target date will be missed; escalation and revised plan required | Red |
| **Not Started** | Remediation action not yet commenced; target date is in the future and no concerns identified | Blue |
| **Overdue** | Target completion date has passed and remediation is not complete; escalation required | Red / Bold |
| **Closed** | Remediation action completed and independently verified; finding formally closed in the register | Grey |

### 8.5 Closure Validation Process

The following process governs the formal closure of remediation items:

1. **Accountable Owner submits closure request** to the Remediation Programme Office with supporting evidence, via [System Name / Process].

6. **Closed findings are reported** to the Board in the subsequent Quarterly Remediation Progress Report.

### 10.2 Key Role Descriptions

**Board of Directors**
- Provides ultimate oversight of the technology risk remediation programme.
- Reviews and approves the Quarterly Remediation Progress Report.
- Approves significant resource requests and target date extensions for Critical and High findings.
- Ensures the organization's technology risk exposure remains within the Board-approved risk appetite.

**Board Risk Committee (BRC)**
- Reviews remediation progress on behalf of the full Board between quarterly Board meetings.
- Provides detailed scrutiny of Critical and High priority findings.
- Recommends Board action on material escalations.

**Chief Executive Officer (CEO) / Executive Committee (EXCO)**
- Accountable for ensuring adequate resources are allocated to the remediation programme.
- Approves management-level remediation reports prior to Board submission.
- Resolves cross-functional escalations and organizational blockers.

**Chief Risk Officer (CRO)**
- Owns the overall technology risk management framework.
- Accountable for the accuracy and completeness of the Quarterly Remediation Progress Report.
- Escalates material risk items to EXCO and Board as appropriate.

**Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) / Chief Information Officer (CIO)**
- Accountable for the execution of technology and cybersecurity remediation activities.
- Provides technical direction and oversight for all IT-related remediation workstreams.
- Manages third-party and vendor relationships in support of remediation delivery.

**Remediation Programme Office**
- Maintains the centralized remediation register as the single source of truth.
- Coordinates milestone tracking, evidence collection, and closure validation processes.
- Prepares remediation status reports for all governance forums.
- Identifies and escalates at-risk and overdue items.

**Accountable Owners (Business and IT)**
- Responsible for the day-to-day execution of assigned remediation actions.
- Ensures milestones are achieved on schedule and evidence is documented.
- Escalates resource constraints and impediments to the Remediation Programme Office and CRO.

**Internal Audit**
- Independently validates the closure of Critical and High priority findings.
- Provides assurance to the Board on the effectiveness of the remediation programme.
- Reports findings to the Audit Committee.
