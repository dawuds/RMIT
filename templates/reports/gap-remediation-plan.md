## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Purpose

*Describe the purpose of this document, its regulatory basis, and how it will be used within the organization's compliance governance framework.*

This Gap Remediation Plan has been prepared in accordance with **Section 18.2 of the Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document** (BNM/RH/PD 028-107). The plan documents all identified compliance gaps arising from the RMiT self-assessment or regulatory examination, establishes a structured remediation framework, assigns clear accountability, and sets measurable milestones to track progress toward full compliance.

This document serves as the primary governance instrument for managing, monitoring, and reporting on RMiT remediation activities. It shall be reviewed quarterly by the Chief Compliance Officer and presented to the Board Risk Committee (or equivalent governance body) for oversight.

### 2.2 Regulatory Basis

This plan responds to the following regulatory obligations:

- **BNM RMiT Policy Document** — Issued [Effective Date of RMiT], applicable to all licensed financial institutions, development financial institutions, insurance and takaful operators, and payment system operators supervised by BNM.

## 9. Progress Tracking Mechanism

*Define the tools, processes, and reporting structures used to track remediation progress, manage evidence of closure, and provide assurance to senior management and regulators.*

### 9.1 Tracking Framework

Progress against this remediation plan will be tracked through the following mechanisms:

1. **Gap Register (Living Document)** — The consolidated gap register in Section 4.2 shall be maintained as a living document, updated by Gap Owners on a weekly basis to reflect current status, percentage completion, and any changes to target dates.
2. **Remediation Dashboard** — A real-time dashboard summarizing gap status by priority, domain, and phase shall be maintained in [GRC Tool / SharePoint / Other Platform] and accessible to all governance bodies.
3. **Evidence Repository** — All remediation evidence (policies, audit reports, training records, test results, system screenshots, etc.) shall be stored in a centralized, access-controlled evidence repository at [Location / Platform]. Evidence shall be tagged by Gap ID for ease of retrieval during regulatory review.
4. **Milestone Tracking Log** — The master milestone schedule in Section 5.3 shall be updated following each governance meeting to reflect actual completion dates versus targets.
5. **Quarterly Remediation Report** — A formal written report shall be prepared by the Chief Compliance Officer each quarter and presented to the Board Risk Committee. The report shall include overall progress, gaps closed, gaps at risk, resource issues, and updated forecasts.

### 9.2 Gap Status Definitions

| Status | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Open** | Remediation has not yet commenced. |
| **In Progress** | Remediation actions are underway; target date is current. |
| **At Risk** | Remediation is in progress but there is a risk of missing the target date. Escalation may be required. |
| **Overdue** | Target date has passed and the gap is not closed. Escalation is mandatory. |
| **Pending Verification** | Remediation actions are complete; awaiting independent verification by Internal Audit or a designated verifier. |
| **Closed** | Gap is fully remediated; evidence has been reviewed and accepted; closure approved by the CCO. |
| **Closed — Accepted Risk** | Gap cannot be fully remediated within the plan period; residual risk has been formally accepted by Senior Management / Board and reported to BNM where required. |

### 9.3 Closure and Verification Process

*A gap shall only be considered "Closed" after completing all steps in the closure process below.*

**Step 1 — Gap Owner Confirmation**
The Gap Owner confirms that all remediation actions are complete and submits the evidence package to the Chief Compliance Officer for review.

**Step 2 — Evidence Review**
The Chief Compliance Officer reviews the evidence package to confirm that the evidence is complete, accurate, and sufficient to demonstrate that the underlying control is now in place.

**Step 3 — Independent Verification**
For all P1 and P2 gaps, Internal Audit (or an appointed external verifier) independently validates the evidence and confirms that the gap has been remediated to the required standard. For P3 and P4 gaps, the CCO may act as the verifier.

**Step 4 — Formal Closure**
The Chief Compliance Officer formally closes the gap in the Gap Register and updates the Remediation Dashboard. A closure note is prepared, signed, and retained in the evidence repository.

**Step 5 — Reporting**
Closed gaps are reported in the next quarterly remediation report to the Board Risk Committee.

### 11.2 Key Roles — Detailed Responsibilities

**Chief Compliance Officer**
- Owns the Gap Remediation Plan and is accountable for its execution.
- Coordinates the Remediation Steering Committee.
- Presents quarterly progress reports to the Board Risk Committee.
- Acts as the primary point of contact for BNM on remediation matters.
- Approves gap closures (P3, P4) and countersigns P1/P2 closures after Internal Audit verification.

**Head of Technology Risk**
- Responsible for the remediation of all gaps within the Technology Risk Governance and Technology Operations domains.
- Ensures Gap Owners within their domain are adequately resourced and supported.
- Provides domain-specific technical input to the remediation plan.

**Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)**
- Responsible for the remediation of all gaps within the Cybersecurity Management domain.
- Oversees the implementation of technical cybersecurity controls.
- Validates the adequacy of cybersecurity remediation evidence.

**Gap Owners**
- Personally accountable for executing remediation actions for their assigned gaps.
- Provide weekly status updates to the Remediation Steering Committee.
- Prepare and submit evidence packages upon completion of remediation.
- Escalate blockers promptly through the defined escalation path.

**Internal Audit**
- Independently verifies the closure of all P1 and P2 gaps.
- Provides assurance to the Board Risk Committee on the effectiveness of remediation.
- Reports any concerns about the adequacy of remediation to the Audit Committee.
