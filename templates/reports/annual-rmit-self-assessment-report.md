### 2.2 Assessment Criteria and Rating Scale

*Define the compliance rating scale used throughout this report. Consistency in rating criteria is essential for year-on-year comparability and for audit defensibility.*

The following four-point compliance rating scale is used throughout this report:

| Rating | Definition | Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| **Fully Compliant (FC)** | All RMiT requirements for the clause are met with documented evidence. Controls are effective and operating as intended. | ≥90% of sub-requirements met; no significant gaps; evidence is comprehensive and current |
| **Substantially Compliant (SC)** | Most RMiT requirements are met. Minor gaps exist but do not materially impair the effectiveness of the control. A remediation plan is in place. | 75–89% of sub-requirements met; gaps are low-risk and time-bound remediation plans exist |
| **Partially Compliant (PC)** | Some RMiT requirements are met, but significant gaps exist that reduce control effectiveness. Immediate remediation is required. | 50–74% of sub-requirements met; gaps are medium-to-high risk; management attention required |
| **Non-Compliant (NC)** | RMiT requirements are not met or controls are fundamentally absent or ineffective. Urgent escalation and remediation required. | <50% of sub-requirements met; critical gaps; risk to institution and/or customers |

| Control Rating | Definition |

### 3.4 Quality Assurance Process

*Describe the checks performed to ensure assessment accuracy, consistency, and independence.*

The following quality assurance measures were applied:

1. **Dual-reviewer sign-off:** All domain ratings were reviewed by a second assessor before finalisation.
2. **Evidence sufficiency check:** Each rating was validated against documented evidence. Ratings unsupported by evidence were downgraded or marked as not assessed.
3. **Internal Audit independent review:** Internal Audit conducted an independent review of the draft report and provided written commentary before final sign-off.
4. **Management validation:** Preliminary findings were shared with relevant business unit heads for factual accuracy confirmation before the report was finalised.
5. **Escalation protocol:** Any disagreements between assessors and management on ratings were escalated to the Assessment Sponsor (CCO) for resolution. Unresolved disagreements are documented in Appendix [X].
