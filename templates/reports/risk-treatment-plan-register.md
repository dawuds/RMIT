## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Risk Treatment Plan Register has been established in accordance with **Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically **Clause 9.3**, which requires financial institutions to maintain a structured and documented approach to the treatment of identified technology risks. This register serves as the authoritative record of all approved risk treatment plans, providing [Organization Name] with a consolidated view of risk treatment activities, ownership, timelines, and current status.

The primary purpose of this register is to:

- Provide a centralized, auditable record of all technology risk treatment activities undertaken by [Organization Name];
- Ensure accountability by assigning clear ownership to each risk treatment action;
- Enable structured monitoring and reporting of risk treatment progress to senior management and the Board;
- Demonstrate ongoing regulatory compliance with BNM RMiT requirements through documented evidence of risk management activity;
- Support the Technology Risk Management Committee and Board Risk Committee in discharging their governance obligations.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the organizational and technological boundaries to which this register applies. Include all systems, entities, and functions in scope.*

This register covers all technology risks identified within [Organization Name] that have been assessed, rated, and assigned a treatment action other than **Risk Acceptance** (accepted risks are recorded separately in the Risk Acceptance Register). The scope encompasses:

- **Organizational Scope:** All business units, departments, and subsidiaries of [Organization Name] that consume, manage, or are supported by technology systems and infrastructure;
- **Technology Scope:** All information technology (IT) assets, operational technology (OT) assets, cloud services, outsourced technology services, and third-party managed systems used in the conduct of regulated financial services;
- **Risk Categories in Scope:** Technology risks arising from cybersecurity threats, IT infrastructure, system availability, data integrity, change management, vendor management, and technology project delivery;
- **Geographic Scope:** [Specify jurisdictions, e.g., all Malaysian operations / head office and all domestic branches];
- **Regulatory Perimeter:** Risks subject to treatment under BNM RMiT, PDPA 2010, and any other applicable BNM policy documents as determined by [Organization Name]'s risk taxonomy.

## 2. Objectives and Scope of Risk Treatment

### 2.1 Risk Treatment Objectives

*Articulate the overarching goals of the risk treatment program for the current review period. Update this section each quarterly review cycle.*

[Organization Name]'s risk treatment program for the period **[Review Period Start Date]** to **[Review Period End Date]** is governed by the following objectives:

1. **Reduce Residual Risk to Acceptable Levels:** Implement controls and mitigating actions to bring all technology risks rated **High** and **Critical** within [Organization Name]'s approved risk appetite thresholds as defined in the Technology Risk Appetite Statement;
2. **Prioritize Treatment of Critical Risks:** Ensure that all risks rated **Critical** have an active treatment plan with a target completion date not exceeding **[X] months** from the risk identification date;
3. **Demonstrate Regulatory Compliance:** Maintain documentation sufficient to satisfy BNM examination requirements, including evidence of treatment activity, owner attestation, and Board/Management oversight;
4. **Integrate Treatment with Business Planning:** Align risk treatment resource requirements with [Organization Name]'s annual operating and capital budgeting processes;
5. **Achieve Treatment Milestones:** Complete at least **[X]%** of scheduled risk treatment milestones within the current quarterly review period.

### 2.2 Risk Treatment Strategy Framework

*Describe the treatment options available and the criteria for selecting each. This section should align with [Organization Name]'s Technology Risk Policy.*

[Organization Name] applies the following risk treatment strategies, consistent with internationally recognized frameworks (ISO/IEC 27005, NIST RMF) and BNM RMiT requirements:

| Treatment Strategy | Definition | Applicable Conditions | BNM RMiT Alignment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Risk Mitigation (Reduce)** | Implementing controls to reduce likelihood and/or impact of a risk | Residual risk exceeds appetite; cost of control is proportionate | Clause 9.3 — primary treatment approach |
| **Risk Transfer** | Transferring risk to a third party (e.g., cyber insurance, contractual SLAs) | Risk cannot be fully mitigated internally; third party better positioned to absorb | Clause 9.3; Clause 10 (Outsourcing) |
| **Risk Avoidance** | Ceasing the activity that gives rise to the risk | Risk level is unacceptable; no viable mitigation exists | Clause 9.3 |
| **Risk Acceptance** | Formal acceptance of residual risk at current level | Risk falls within appetite; cost of treatment exceeds benefit | Clause 9.3; documented in Risk Acceptance Register |

### 2.3 Scope Exclusions

*Document any risks or domains explicitly excluded from this register and the rationale for exclusion.*

The following are excluded from this Risk Treatment Plan Register:

- Risks formally accepted and recorded in the **Risk Acceptance Register** ([Document ID]);
- Operational risk incidents that are managed through the **Incident Management Register** ([Document ID]);
- Strategic risks that do not have a technology risk component;
- Risks associated with [Excluded System/Entity] as these are managed by [Entity Name] and reported separately.

### 4.1 Ownership Framework

*This section defines the ownership model applied to all risk treatment plans within this register. Owners are personally accountable for the delivery of assigned treatment plans within agreed timelines.*

[Organization Name] assigns ownership at two levels for each risk treatment plan:

- **Primary Owner:** The individual with direct accountability for executing the treatment plan, managing milestones, and reporting progress. The Primary Owner must have sufficient authority over the resources and processes required to implement the treatment.
- **Sponsor:** A senior executive (typically at the C-suite or Head of Department level) who provides organizational authority, removes escalation barriers, and is responsible for treatment plan outcomes at the management governance level.

1. Formally acknowledge their ownership of assigned treatment plans by signing the **Owner Attestation** in Appendix A;

## 5. Target Completion Dates and Scheduling

### 5.1 Scheduling Principles

*Describe the methodology used to set and maintain target completion dates for risk treatment plans.*

Target completion dates for all risk treatment plans are established in accordance with the following principles:

1. **Risk Rating Drives Timeline:** The inherent risk rating of the underlying risk determines the maximum allowable time to complete treatment, as defined in [Organization Name]'s Technology Risk Policy:

| Risk Rating | Maximum Treatment Period from Risk Identification |
|---|---|
| **Critical** | [X] months |
| **High** | [X] months |
| **Medium** | [X] months |
| **Low** | [X] months or next annual review cycle |

2. **Regulatory Deadlines Take Precedence:** Where BNM or other regulators have imposed specific remediation timelines, these supersede internal scheduling standards;
3. **Resource Feasibility:** Target dates are set in consultation with Primary Owners to ensure they are achievable given available resources and organizational capacity;
4. **Milestone Granularity:** For treatment plans with a total duration exceeding **[X] months**, intermediate milestones must be set at intervals not exceeding **[X] months** to enable meaningful progress tracking;
5. **Date Revision Protocol:** Target completion dates may only be revised with documented justification and formal approval as specified in Section 5.2.

### 5.2 Date Revision and Extension Protocol

*Document all approved extensions to treatment plan target completion dates.*

Any revision to a target completion date must be supported by:

- Written justification from the Primary Owner explaining the reason for delay;
- Assessment of the risk exposure during the extended period;
- Approval by the Head of Technology Risk and, for **Critical** or **High** rated risks, endorsement by the Chief Risk Officer (CRO) or equivalent;
- Notification to the Technology Risk Management Committee at the next scheduled meeting.

## 7. Progress Tracking Mechanism

### 7.1 Tracking Framework

*Describe the mechanisms by which [Organization Name] monitors the progress of risk treatment plans. This section should reference the tools, reporting cadence, and governance forums involved.*

[Organization Name] employs a structured, multi-layer approach to tracking the progress of risk treatment plans, ensuring that status information flows appropriately from operational owners through to senior management and Board-level governance:

**Layer 1 — Operational Tracking (Monthly)**
- Primary Owners complete the **Monthly Treatment Plan Status Update Form** (Appendix B) and submit to the Technology Risk Management team by the **[Xth] business day** of each month;
- The Head of Technology Risk reviews all updates and flags materially delayed plans for escalation.

**Layer 2 — Management Reporting (Quarterly)**
- The Head of Technology Risk consolidates treatment plan progress into the **Quarterly Technology Risk Report** for submission to the Technology Risk Management Committee (TRMC);
- The TRMC reviews aggregate treatment plan status, approves date extensions where justified, and escalates Board-reportable items.

**Layer 3 — Board Reporting (Semi-Annually / As Required)**
- A summary of treatment plan status for **Critical** and **High** rated risks is included in the **Board Risk Committee (BRC) Technology Risk Report**;
- Material deterioration in treatment plan progress (e.g., plans exceeding **[X] months** delay) triggers an ad hoc report to the BRC.

### 7.2 Status Definitions

*Standardized status definitions ensure consistent reporting across all treatment plans.*

| Status | Definition | Required Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Not Started** | Treatment plan approved but implementation activities have not commenced | Confirm start date with Primary Owner; escalate if start is overdue |
| **In Progress** | Treatment activities have commenced; milestones are being worked on | Continue monitoring; ensure next milestone date is confirmed |
| **On Track** | Treatment is progressing per the approved schedule; no material delays anticipated | No immediate action required; maintain quarterly review |
| **Delayed** | One or more milestones have been missed; completion is behind schedule by **[X] days or less** | Primary Owner to provide written explanation; revised schedule to be agreed within **[X] business days** |
| **Critically Delayed** | Treatment completion is behind schedule by more than **[X] days** OR a Critical/High rated risk has missed two consecutive milestones | Escalate to CRO; consider interim compensating controls; report to TRMC at next meeting |
| **Completed** | All treatment actions and milestones have been completed; residual risk has been re-assessed and documented | Update risk register with revised residual risk rating; close treatment plan; notify TRMC |
| **Cancelled** | Treatment plan has been formally cancelled with documented justification | Document reason; ensure alternative treatment or formal acceptance is in place; report to TRMC |

### 7.4 Treatment Plan Completion and Closure Process

*Describe the process for formally closing a treatment plan upon completion of all actions.*

A risk treatment plan is eligible for closure when the following conditions are satisfied:

1. All milestones listed in the Treatment Plan Card (Section 3.2) have been completed and evidence of completion is documented;
2. The Primary Owner has submitted a **Closure Request** to the Head of Technology Risk, including a post-implementation assessment of the residual risk rating;
3. The Head of Technology Risk has reviewed the Closure Request and validated that the treatment actions are adequate to achieve the target residual risk rating;
4. The revised residual risk rating has been recorded in [Organization Name]'s **Technology Risk Register** ([Document ID]);
5. The closure has been noted by the TRMC at the next scheduled meeting.
