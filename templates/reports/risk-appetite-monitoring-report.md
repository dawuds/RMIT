## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Purpose

This Risk Appetite Monitoring Report documents the periodic assessment of [Organization Name]'s actual technology risk levels against the approved risk appetite thresholds and tolerance limits established in the organization's Technology Risk Appetite Statement. The report is a mandatory governance artifact under Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically **Section 8.4**, which requires financial institutions to actively monitor technology risks and maintain documented evidence of risk appetite compliance.

This report serves the following objectives:

- Provide the Board Risk Committee (BRC), Board of Directors, and senior management with timely, accurate, and comprehensive visibility into the organization's technology risk posture.
- Demonstrate compliance with BNM RMiT requirements for ongoing risk appetite monitoring and escalation.
- Enable informed risk-based decision-making by identifying emerging risk concentrations and deviations from appetite before they escalate.
- Support the continuous improvement of the organization's technology risk management framework.

### 3.2 Measurement and Scoring

*Describe how risk levels are quantified and compared against appetite thresholds. Reference the approved scoring methodology and any models used.*

Risk levels are measured against approved thresholds using the following approach:

1. **Quantitative KRIs** — Numerical metrics (e.g., number of critical vulnerabilities, system uptime percentage) are compared directly against defined red, amber, and green (RAG) thresholds.
2. **Qualitative Assessments** — Risk areas without readily quantifiable metrics are assessed by the relevant risk owner using a defined [1–5] likelihood and impact matrix, as documented in the Technology Risk Assessment Methodology (Document ID: [Reference Document ID]).
3. **Aggregation** — Individual KRI statuses are aggregated to the risk domain level using a [worst-case / weighted average] approach as defined in the Risk Appetite Framework (Document ID: [Reference Document ID]).

### Appendix E — Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **BCP** | Business Continuity Plan — a documented set of procedures to ensure the continuation of critical business functions during and after a disruptive event. |
| **BNM** | Bank Negara Malaysia — the Central Bank of Malaysia and primary prudential regulator for financial institutions. |
| **BRC** | Board Risk Committee — a committee of the Board of Directors responsible for oversight of the organization's risk management framework. |
| **CRO** | Chief Risk Officer — the senior executive accountable for the organization's enterprise-wide risk management. |
| **DR** | Disaster Recovery — the process and set of procedures to recover and protect IT infrastructure following a disaster or major incident. |
| **KRI** | Key Risk Indicator — a metric used to signal increasing risk exposure against a defined threshold, providing early warning of potential risk appetite breaches. |
| **MTTD** | Mean Time to Detect — the average time elapsed between the occurrence of a security event and its detection by monitoring systems or personnel. |
| **MTTR** | Mean Time to Respond — the average time elapsed between the detection of a security incident and the completion of initial containment or resolution actions. |
| **NACSA** | National Cyber Security Agency of Malaysia — the lead agency responsible for the nation's cybersecurity. |
| **PDPA** | Personal Data Protection Act 2010 — Malaysian legislation governing the processing of personal data in commercial transactions. |
| **RAG** | Red, Amber, Green — a traffic light status framework used to indicate risk levels relative to defined thresholds. |
| **RACI** | Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed — a matrix used to clarify roles and responsibilities in governance processes. |
| **RMiT** | Risk Management in Technology — BNM's Policy Document outlining technology risk management requirements for financial institutions. |
| **RPO** | Recovery Point Objective — the maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured in time that can result from a disaster or major incident. |
| **RTO** | Recovery Time Objective — the maximum acceptable length of time that a system can be offline following a disaster or major incident. |
| **SIEM** | Security Information and Event Management — a system that collects, aggregates, and analyzes security event data from across the IT environment. |
