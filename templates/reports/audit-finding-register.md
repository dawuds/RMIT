## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This IT Audit Finding Register serves as the authoritative record of all technology-related audit findings identified by the Internal Audit function of [Organization Name]. It documents audit observations, associated risks, remediation action plans, accountable owners, target completion dates, and current status for all open and closed findings.

This register is maintained in compliance with **Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically **Paragraph 13.4**, which mandates that financial institutions maintain a structured and auditable record of IT audit findings and ensure timely remediation with appropriate management accountability.

## 2. Objectives

*State the specific objectives this register is designed to achieve, aligned to RMiT expectations for audit oversight.*

The IT Audit Finding Register is designed to achieve the following objectives:

- **Track and monitor** all open IT audit findings to ensure timely and complete remediation in accordance with agreed action plans
- **Provide management oversight** of technology risk exposures identified through audit activities, enabling informed decision-making at the Board and Senior Management level
- **Support regulatory compliance** with BNM RMiT Paragraph 13.4 and demonstrate a controlled, risk-based approach to IT audit management
- **Facilitate accountability** by assigning clear ownership of each finding and associated remediation actions to named individuals and business units
- **Enable trend analysis** of recurring technology risk themes to inform the IT audit universe and risk-based audit plan
- **Ensure escalation** of overdue, high-severity, or unmitigated findings to appropriate governance forums, including the Board Audit Committee (BAC)
- **Maintain an auditable trail** of all actions taken, decisions made, and approvals granted in relation to each audit finding

## 5. Finding Severity Classification

*Define the rating scale used to classify findings by severity, ensuring consistent application across all IT audit engagements.*

### 5.1 Severity Rating Definitions

[Organization Name] uses a four-tier severity classification framework for IT audit findings, aligned with the materiality thresholds and risk appetite approved by the Board Audit Committee.

| Severity Level | Label | Definition | Required Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| **S1** | **Critical** | Finding represents an immediate, significant threat to the organization's operations, financial position, regulatory standing, or data integrity. Exploitation or continuation of the condition could result in material financial loss, regulatory sanction, or severe reputational damage. | Immediate escalation to Board Audit Committee and Senior Management. Interim mitigating controls required within **5 business days**. Full remediation within **30 days** or as agreed with BAC. |
| **S2** | **High** | Finding represents a significant control weakness that exposes the organization to elevated risk of financial loss, regulatory non-compliance, or operational disruption. The control failure is not immediately exploited but represents a material gap. | Escalation to relevant C-Suite executive and Head of IT. Remediation plan required within **15 business days**. Full remediation within **90 days**. |
| **S3** | **Medium** | Finding represents a moderate control deficiency that, if unaddressed, could develop into a more significant risk. Current exposure is limited due to compensating controls or low likelihood of exploitation. | Management action plan required within **30 business days**. Full remediation within **180 days**. |
| **S4** | **Low** | Finding represents a minor control gap, process improvement opportunity, or best-practice deviation. Risk exposure is minimal with negligible likelihood of material impact. | Noted for management action. Remediation within **[12 months]** or next audit cycle. |

### 5.2 Severity Escalation Triggers

The following conditions trigger automatic severity escalation or immediate notification to the Board Audit Committee:

- Any **Critical (S1)** finding, regardless of remediation progress
- Any finding that remains **overdue beyond 30 days** from the agreed target date at High severity or above
- Any finding where management has **formally declined** to remediate without an accepted compensating control
- **Repeat findings** (same or substantially similar finding identified in two or more consecutive audits) at High severity or above
- Any finding with **regulatory examination origin** that remains open beyond the regulator's specified deadline

## 9. Roles and Responsibilities

*Define the roles involved in maintaining this register and their respective responsibilities across the audit finding lifecycle.*

### 9.1 RACI Matrix

| Annual review of audit universe | **A** | **R** | — | C | C | C | I |

**Key:** R = Responsible | A = Accountable | C = Consulted | I = Informed

### 9.2 Role Descriptions

**Head of Internal Audit**
- Accountable for the integrity, completeness, and accuracy of the IT Audit Finding Register
- Reviews and approves the quarterly finding status report prior to BAC submission
- Authorizes closure of all Critical and High severity findings
- Escalates unresolved Critical findings to the Board Audit Committee

**Senior IT Auditor / IT Audit Team**
- Maintains day-to-day updates to the register following each quarterly follow-up cycle
- Reviews closure evidence submitted by management and assesses adequacy
- Prepares the quarterly status report and escalation log for Head of Internal Audit review

**Finding Owner**
- Implements the agreed remediation action plan within the committed timeframe
- Provides progress updates to Internal Audit on a monthly basis for Critical and High findings
- Submits closure evidence to Internal Audit upon completion of all remediation milestones
- Notifies Internal Audit immediately if the target date cannot be met and proposes a revised plan

**Business Unit Head**
- Accountable for all findings raised within their business unit
- Endorses management responses and action plans before submission
- Ensures adequate resources are allocated for timely remediation

**Board Audit Committee**
- Receives and reviews the quarterly IT Audit Finding Register status report
- Approves formal risk acceptance for any finding where management elects not to remediate
- Provides oversight and challenge on the timeliness and adequacy of remediation efforts

### Appendix D — Glossary of Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Audit Finding** | An identified deficiency, gap, or weakness in an internal control, process, or system, documented during an audit engagement |
| **Audit Universe** | The complete inventory of all auditable entities — systems, processes, and domains — within the scope of the Internal Audit function |
| **BAC** | Board Audit Committee — the Board-level committee responsible for oversight of the internal audit function and audit findings |
| **BNM** | Bank Negara Malaysia — the Central Bank of Malaysia and primary financial services regulator |
| **COBIT** | Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies — an IT governance framework |
| **Finding Owner** | The individual accountable for implementing the remediation action plan for a specific audit finding |
| **Least Privilege** | The security principle that users should be granted only the minimum access rights required to perform their job function |
| **RACI** | Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed — a framework for defining roles in a process or activity |
| **Repeat Finding** | An audit finding that is substantially similar to a finding raised in a previous audit of the same entity or domain |
| **RMiT** | Risk Management in Technology — BNM's policy document governing technology risk management for financial institutions |
| **Risk-Based Audit Plan** | An audit plan that prioritizes audit engagements based on the assessed level of technology risk associated with each auditable entity |
