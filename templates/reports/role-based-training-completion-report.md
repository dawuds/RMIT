## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Purpose

This Role-Based Training Completion Report documents the outcomes of technology risk and cybersecurity training programmes conducted at [Organization Name] during the reporting period of [Quarter and Year]. The report provides evidence of the institution's commitment to maintaining a security-aware workforce in compliance with the Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)** Policy Document, specifically **Clause 15.3**, which mandates that financial institutions ensure personnel are equipped with the knowledge and skills necessary to manage technology risks commensurate with their roles and responsibilities.

This report is prepared on a **quarterly basis** and serves as:

- An internal governance record for senior management and the Board Risk Committee;
- An audit-ready artefact demonstrating regulatory compliance with BNM RMiT requirements;
- An operational tool to identify training gaps and drive continuous improvement in the institution's human capital risk posture.

## 5. Assessment Methodology and Pass Criteria

*Describe how training effectiveness is measured, including assessment design, pass mark thresholds, remediation procedures for failed attempts, and the governance process for validating assessment integrity.*

### 5.1 Assessment Design Principles

Training assessments at [Organization Name] are designed and reviewed in accordance with the following principles:

- **Alignment to Learning Objectives:** Each assessment question is mapped to a defined learning objective within the corresponding training module.
- **Risk Proportionality:** Assessment rigour is calibrated to the risk tier of the audience. Privileged user assessments carry higher pass thresholds and are subject to annual review.
- **Integrity:** Assessments are conducted on the [Learning Management System (LMS) Name] platform with individual login credentials. Question banks are randomised to mitigate answer sharing.
- **Accessibility:** Assessments are available in [Language(s), e.g., English and Bahasa Malaysia] and are designed to accommodate reasonable accessibility requirements.
