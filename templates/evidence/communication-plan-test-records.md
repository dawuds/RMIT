## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section defines the intent of this document and its role within the organization's business continuity and crisis management framework. Describe why crisis communication testing is conducted and what assurance it provides to regulators and senior management.*

This document serves as the official record of crisis communication testing conducted by [Organization Name] during business continuity and disaster recovery exercises. It provides structured, auditable evidence that the organization's crisis communication protocols have been exercised, validated, and refined in accordance with regulatory requirements.

Communication Plan Test Records demonstrate [Organization Name]'s readiness to maintain effective internal and external communications during disruptions, ensuring that stakeholders — including regulators, customers, counterparties, and staff — receive timely and accurate information during crisis events.

This document is maintained as a mandatory compliance artifact under **Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)** policy document, specifically in fulfilment of obligations under **RMiT Clause 10.35**, which requires financial institutions to conduct and document periodic testing of business continuity and crisis communication arrangements.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the organizational boundaries, systems, functions, and exercise types covered by these test records. Be specific about what is included and excluded to avoid ambiguity during audits.*

These test records apply to:

- **Organizational Scope:** All business units, technology functions, and support services of [Organization Name] that are designated as critical or important under the organization's Business Continuity Plan (BCP) and Technology Disaster Recovery (TDR) plan.
- **Communication Channels Covered:**
  - Internal staff notification systems (email, SMS blast, intranet alerts, PA systems)
  - Crisis Management Team (CMT) escalation trees
  - Senior management and Board notification protocols
  - External stakeholder communications (customers, regulators, media)
  - BNM regulatory notification procedures
  - Vendor and third-party partner notifications
  - Social media and public communications (where applicable)
- **Exercise Types Covered:**
  - Tabletop exercises
  - Functional drills
  - Full-scale simulation exercises
  - Unannounced call-tree activations
- **Exclusions:**
  - Day-to-day operational incident communications not classified as crisis-level events
  - Marketing or customer relationship communications unrelated to continuity events
  - [Any other exclusions specific to Organization Name]

## 2. Objectives and Scope of Testing

### 2.1 Testing Objectives

*State the specific, measurable objectives that each communication test exercise is designed to achieve. These objectives should align with the organization's BCP goals and the RMiT requirements.*

The primary objectives of crisis communication testing at [Organization Name] are:

1. **Validate Notification Timeliness:** Confirm that all designated recipients in the crisis communication chain can be reached within defined Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs) from the point of initial alert.
2. **Verify Contact Accuracy:** Ensure that all contact directories, escalation trees, and communication templates are current and operationally effective.
3. **Test Channel Redundancy:** Demonstrate that backup communication channels function correctly when primary channels are unavailable or degraded.
4. **Assess Human Readiness:** Evaluate whether designated personnel understand their communication roles, responsibilities, and authority levels during a crisis.
5. **Identify Gaps and Weaknesses:** Surface deficiencies in communication protocols, tools, or personnel preparedness before a real crisis occurs.
6. **Regulatory Assurance:** Generate auditable evidence for BNM examination and internal audit purposes demonstrating compliance with RMiT Clause 10.35.
7. **Continuous Improvement:** Capture lessons learned and action items to drive iterative improvements to the Communication Plan.
