## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document records the formal approval by the Audit Committee of [Organization Name] for the annual IT Audit Plan. It provides evidence of governance oversight over the internal audit function in accordance with **Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, Paragraph 13.1**, which requires institutions to maintain a risk-based IT audit programme subject to Board or Audit Committee approval.

**Scope:** This approval record covers the IT audit activities planned for the period **[Audit Plan Period, e.g., 1 January 2025 – 31 December 2025]**, encompassing all technology systems, platforms, and processes within [Organization Name] that are material to the delivery of financial services, data protection obligations under PDPA 2010, and cybersecurity requirements under NACSA guidelines.

### 5.2 Risk-Based Prioritisation Rationale

The following criteria were applied to score and rank auditable entities for inclusion in the current plan:

- **Regulatory materiality:** Areas subject to BNM RMiT mandatory requirements and recent regulatory focus.
- **Inherent risk level:** Systems with high concentration risk, transaction volumes, or data sensitivity.
- **Time since last audit:** Entities not reviewed within the past 12–24 months are prioritised.
- **Change activity:** Systems that have undergone significant change (upgrades, migrations, new implementations).
- **Audit findings history:** Areas with outstanding high or critical findings from prior cycles.
- **Management requests:** Specific areas flagged by the Board, Audit Committee, or senior management.

## 6. Audit Methodology and Standards

*Describe the methodology, standards, and frameworks that govern how IT audits within this plan will be planned, executed, and reported.*

### 6.1 Methodology Overview

All IT audits within this plan shall be conducted in accordance with:

- **International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing (IPPF)** — Institute of Internal Auditors (IIA)
- **ISACA IT Audit Framework (ITAF)** — including IS Audit Standards, Guidelines, and Procedures
- **COBIT 2019** — for IT governance and control evaluation
- **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)** — for cybersecurity-related assessments
- **BNM RMiT Policy Document** — as the primary regulatory standard for Malaysian financial institutions

### 6.2 Audit Phases

| Phase | Activities |
|---|---|
| **Planning** | Risk assessment, scope definition, audit programme preparation, preliminary data gathering |
| **Fieldwork** | Control testing, evidence collection, interviews, technical assessments, sampling |
| **Reporting** | Draft report preparation, management response, exit conference, final report issuance |
| **Follow-Up** | Tracking of management action plans, verification of remediation, closure of findings |

### 6.3 Quality Assurance

- All audit workpapers are subject to review by the **Head of Internal Audit** prior to report issuance.
- External Quality Assessment (EQA) of the Internal Audit function shall be conducted in accordance with IIA standards at least once every **[5 years / per Board-approved schedule]**.
- Co-sourcing arrangements with external specialists (if applicable) are governed by [Reference to Outsourcing Policy].

## 7. Finding Severity Classification

*Define the severity rating framework used to classify audit findings. Consistent classification ensures that management and the Audit Committee can prioritise remediation actions appropriately.*

### 7.1 Severity Rating Framework

| Severity Level | Criteria | Required Management Response Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Control failure exposing the institution to immediate regulatory breach, material financial loss, or significant data compromise. Direct impact on BNM RMiT compliance. | Immediate — within **7 calendar days** |
| **High** | Significant control weakness with high likelihood of adverse impact if not remediated. Material risk to operations, data, or regulatory standing. | Within **30 calendar days** |
| **Medium** | Moderate control deficiency. Impact is limited in scope or likelihood. Risk is manageable but requires timely remediation. | Within **60 calendar days** |
| **Low** | Minor control gap or process improvement opportunity. Negligible immediate impact. | Within **90 calendar days** |
| **Informational** | Observation or best-practice recommendation with no identified control failure. | At management's discretion |

### 7.2 Escalation Requirements

- **Critical** and **High** findings must be reported to the **Audit Committee** at the next scheduled meeting, or immediately if the finding poses an imminent risk.
- Overdue management action plans (past agreed remediation date) are escalated to the **Chief Executive Officer** and reported to the Audit Committee quarterly.
- Repeat findings (same finding raised in two or more consecutive audits) are automatically elevated by one severity level.
