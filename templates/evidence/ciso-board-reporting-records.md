## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*Describe the purpose of this document and why it is maintained. Explain what regulatory obligation it satisfies.*

This document serves as the official record of all presentations, briefings, and formal reports delivered by the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) — or equivalent designated officer — to the Board of Directors, Board Risk Committee (BRC), Board Audit Committee (BAC), or any other relevant board-level governance body at [Organization Name].

These records are maintained to demonstrate ongoing board oversight of technology and cybersecurity risk, as required under Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, particularly **Clause 8.6**, which mandates that the board and senior management maintain active governance over technology risk management.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the boundaries of this document — which entities, systems, reports, and time periods are covered.*

This record applies to:

- All CISO-level technology risk and cybersecurity reports presented to the Board of Directors of [Organization Name]
- Reports presented to any board-delegated committee with oversight responsibility for technology risk (e.g., Board Risk Committee, Board Audit Committee, IT Steering Committee at board level)
- Reports covering [Organization Name]'s operations within Malaysia, including any subsidiaries or branches where [Organization Name] exercises governance oversight
- Reporting periods from **[Start Date]** to **[End Date]** (updated on a rolling quarterly basis)

### 1.3 Out of Scope

*Identify what is explicitly excluded from this document's coverage.*

The following are out of scope for this record:

- Management-level (non-board) IT risk committee meeting minutes
- Operational incident reports not escalated to board level
- Vendor and third-party cybersecurity assessments unless explicitly tabled at a board meeting
- Internal audit reports unless presented by or co-presented with the CISO to the board

### 2.2 RMiT Clause 8.6 — Key Requirements Summary

*Summarise the specific obligations imposed by RMiT Clause 8.6 that this document satisfies.*

RMiT Clause 8.6 requires that:

1. The board and senior management receive **regular, comprehensive reporting** on the state of technology risk, cybersecurity posture, and technology-related incidents
2. Reports must be **adequate in frequency, content, and depth** to enable informed decision-making
3. The board must demonstrate **active engagement** with technology risk matters, evidenced by documented deliberations and follow-up actions
4. Technology risk reporting to the board must include, at minimum:
   - Current technology risk profile and risk appetite utilisation
   - Status of critical technology projects and initiatives
   - Cybersecurity threat landscape and incident summary
   - Key risk indicators (KRIs) and key performance indicators (KPIs) for technology
   - Compliance status with regulatory requirements

### 2.3 Regulatory Examination Context

*Explain how BNM examiners may use this artifact during a regulatory examination or audit.*

During a BNM technology risk examination or thematic review, examiners may request this record to:

- Verify that board-level oversight of technology risk is operative and not merely nominal
- Assess the **quality, completeness, and frequency** of CISO reporting to the board
- Confirm that the board has been **informed of material technology incidents**, emerging threats, and significant technology risk developments
- Evaluate the board's **response and follow-through** on CISO recommendations
- Validate that reporting cadence meets or exceeds the **quarterly minimum** implied by RMiT governance requirements

[Organization Name] must be able to produce this record, along with supporting meeting minutes and board resolutions, within **[X] business days** of an examiner's request.
