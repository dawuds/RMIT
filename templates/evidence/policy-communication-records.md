## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section describes the intent of this document and why it exists as a compliance artifact.*

This document serves as the formal record of communication activities undertaken by [Organization Name] to ensure that all staff, contractors, and third-party personnel with access to organizational systems and information assets are made aware of, and have acknowledged, the Access Control Policy. It demonstrates [Organization Name]'s commitment to compliance with Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, specifically Clause 10.51, which mandates that financial institutions establish and communicate robust access control policies to all relevant personnel.

This artifact provides auditable evidence that:

- The Access Control Policy has been formally communicated to all in-scope personnel.
- Staff have acknowledged receipt and understanding of the policy and their obligations thereunder.
- Communication activities are documented, tracked, and reviewed on a periodic basis.
- Gaps in policy communication are identified, escalated, and remediated in a timely manner.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the organizational and technical boundaries within which this policy communication applies.*

This document applies to all individuals who access, manage, or administer [Organization Name]'s information systems, applications, infrastructure, and data, including but not limited to:

**In-Scope Personnel:**

- All permanent employees of [Organization Name] across all business units and departments.
- Fixed-term contract staff and temporary employees engaged through [Organization Name] or third-party agencies.
- Third-party vendors, consultants, and managed service providers who are granted access to [Organization Name]'s information systems or data assets.
- Privileged users including system administrators, database administrators, network engineers, and security operations personnel.
- Senior management and Board-level personnel with access to information systems.

**In-Scope Systems and Assets:**

- Core banking systems and applications: [List Core Banking Systems, e.g., [Core Banking System Name]]
- Internet banking and digital channel platforms: [List Digital Platforms]
- Enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems: [List ERP Systems]
- Identity and Access Management (IAM) platforms: [IAM Platform Name]
- Network infrastructure, servers, and cloud environments.
- Data repositories, databases, and storage systems containing customer or sensitive organizational data.
- Third-party hosted applications and Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) platforms accessed by staff.

**Out-of-Scope:**

- Personnel whose employment or engagement has been fully terminated and whose access rights have been completely revoked.
- Systems classified as decommissioned with no active user access.

### 1.3 Document Objectives

*List the measurable objectives this document is designed to achieve.*

This document is designed to achieve the following objectives:

1. Establish a structured and repeatable process for communicating access control policies to all in-scope personnel.

## 2. Regulatory Context and Obligations

### 2.1 BNM RMiT Policy Framework Overview

*Briefly describe the regulatory framework and its relevance to access control policy communication.*

Bank Negara Malaysia issued the Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document (effective 1 January 2020, with subsequent updates) under the Financial Services Act 2013 (FSA), Islamic Financial Services Act 2013 (IFSA), and the Development Financial Institutions Act 2002 (DFIA). The RMiT Policy Document establishes minimum standards for technology risk management applicable to all financial institutions licensed under BNM's purview.

[Organization Name], as a [licensed financial institution / development financial institution / payment system operator] regulated by BNM, is required to comply in full with the requirements set out in the RMiT Policy Document. Non-compliance may result in regulatory supervisory action, including directions to remediate, financial penalties, or other enforcement measures.

### 2.2 Applicable RMiT Clauses

*List the specific regulatory clauses that this artifact addresses, with a brief description of each obligation.*

| Clause | Description | Obligation |
|---|---|---|
| **10.51** | Access Control – Policy Communication | Financial institutions shall ensure that an access control policy is established, communicated to all users, and acknowledged by them. Evidence of such communication shall be maintained. |
| **10.46** | Access Control – General Requirements | Financial institutions shall implement a formal access control policy to manage the issuance, management, and revocation of user access rights across all systems. |
| **10.47** | Access Rights – Least Privilege | Access rights shall be based on the principle of least privilege and need-to-know, ensuring users are granted only the minimum access necessary to perform their duties. |
| **10.48** | Access Provisioning | Formal procedures shall govern the provisioning of access rights, including appropriate authorization and approval prior to granting access. |
| **10.49** | Access Deprovisioning | Access rights shall be revoked promptly upon termination, transfer, or change in role of a user, following a defined deprovisioning procedure. |
| **10.50** | Privileged Access Management | Enhanced controls shall apply to the management of privileged accounts, including periodic review and strict approval workflows. |
| **10.52** | Access Review | Periodic reviews of user access rights shall be conducted to ensure access remains appropriate, and access rights shall be revoked for users who no longer require them. |

## 4. Policy Statements and Requirements

### 4.1 Access Control Policy Statement

*Summarize the key policy statements from the Access Control Policy that are communicated to staff. Reference the full policy document for complete detail.*

[Organization Name] is committed to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of its information systems and data assets through the rigorous management of user access rights. The following policy statements represent the core principles communicated to all in-scope personnel:

**4.1.1 Principle of Least Privilege**

All users shall be granted the minimum level of access necessary to perform their designated job functions. Access rights shall not be provisioned on the basis of convenience, seniority, or anticipated future requirements. Requests for access beyond minimum necessity shall require documented business justification and appropriate approval.

**4.1.2 Need-to-Know Basis**

Access to sensitive or confidential information, including customer data, financial data, and proprietary business information, shall be restricted to personnel with a legitimate business need to access such information in the performance of their duties.

**4.1.3 Individual Accountability**

Each user is assigned a unique user identifier (User ID). Sharing of credentials, passwords, or authentication tokens is strictly prohibited. Users are personally accountable for all actions performed under their assigned credentials.

**4.1.4 Formal Authorization**

All access provisioning requests must be formally submitted through [Organization Name]'s approved access request process and must receive prior authorization from the appropriate approving authority before access is granted. Retroactive approval is not permitted.

**4.1.5 Prompt Revocation**

Access rights shall be revoked immediately upon termination of employment or engagement, or adjusted promptly upon change of role or transfer, in accordance with the deprovisioning procedure defined in Section 5 of this document.

**4.1.6 Prohibition on Unauthorized Access**

Accessing systems, applications, data, or network resources for which a user does not have explicit authorization is strictly prohibited and constitutes a violation of [Organization Name]'s security policy and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination and referral to relevant authorities.

**4.1.7 Privileged Access Controls**

Privileged accounts (administrator, super-user, root, and equivalent accounts) are subject to enhanced controls including multi-factor authentication, restricted use to authorized administrative tasks only, logging of all privileged activity, and periodic certification. Privileged account credentials shall not be used for routine day-to-day activities.

**4.1.8 Remote Access**

Remote access to [Organization Name]'s systems shall only be permitted through [Organization Name]-approved secure remote access channels (e.g., VPN with multi-factor authentication). Unauthorized use of remote access tools or protocols is prohibited.

**4.1.9 Third-Party and Vendor Access**

Access granted to third-party vendors, consultants, and managed service providers shall be restricted to the minimum required, time-limited, subject to formal authorization, and subject to enhanced monitoring. Third-party access shall be revoked immediately upon conclusion of the engagement.

**4.1.10 Password and Authentication Requirements**

All users must adhere to [Organization Name]'s Password and Authentication Standard, including minimum password complexity, password history, expiry, and the use of multi-factor authentication for all critical systems and remote access as mandated by [Organization Name].

## 6. Segregation of Duties Matrix

*This section defines the Segregation of Duties (SoD) controls for access control activities. SoD ensures that no single individual holds conflicting access rights that could enable fraud, error, or policy circumvention without detection.*

Segregation of Duties (SoD) is a fundamental access control principle requiring that critical tasks or processes are divided among multiple individuals so that no single person can complete a high-risk transaction or process from initiation to completion without oversight. This section documents [Organization Name]'s SoD matrix for information systems and key business processes, in alignment with BNM RMiT Clause 10.51 and related access control requirements.

This process supports compliance with BNM RMiT Clause 10.52 and mitigates the risk of privilege accumulation, unauthorized access, and insider threat.

[Organization Name] conducts access certification reviews on the following schedule:

| Access to sensitive customer data | Quarterly | Data/System Owner | DPO / IAM Manager |

### Appendix E – Glossary of Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Access Certification** | The periodic process of reviewing and formally confirming or revoking user access rights across organizational systems. |
| **Access Control** | The process of managing who is authorized to access specific systems, applications, data, and network resources. |
| **Access Provisioning** | The process of granting a user access to systems, applications, or data following formal authorization. |
| **Access Deprovisioning** | The process of revoking a user's access rights upon termination, transfer, or change of role. |
| **BNM** | Bank Negara Malaysia – the central bank and primary financial services regulator of Malaysia. |
| **IAM** | Identity and Access Management – the framework and technology for managing digital identities and their access rights. |
| **Least Privilege** | The security principle that users should be granted the minimum level of access necessary to perform their job functions. |
| **Need-to-Know** | The principle that access to sensitive information should be restricted to those with a legitimate business requirement. |
| **Privileged Account** | An account with elevated permissions, such as administrator, super-user, or root accounts, that provide access beyond standard user rights. |
| **RACI** | A responsibility assignment matrix defining who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for a given activity. |
| **RMiT** | Risk Management in Technology – BNM's Policy Document governing technology risk management in Malaysian financial institutions. |
| **Segregation of Duties (SoD)** | The practice of distributing critical tasks among multiple individuals to prevent fraud and error. |
| **SIEM** | Security Information and Event Management – a system for real-time monitoring, correlation, and alerting on security events. |
| **User Access Review** | See Access Certification. |
| **VPN** | Virtual Private Network – a secure, encrypted connection for remote access to organizational systems. |
