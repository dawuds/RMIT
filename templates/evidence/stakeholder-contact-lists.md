## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document establishes and maintains the authoritative contact list for crisis notification of internal and external stakeholders in accordance with Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically **Clause 10.35**, which mandates that financial institutions maintain current and accessible stakeholder contact information to support timely crisis communication and regulatory notification obligations.

**Scope** covers all personnel, regulators, third-party service providers, and counterparties who must be notified during a technology-related incident, operational disruption, or material cybersecurity event affecting [Organization Name].

This document is classified as a **mandatory evidence artifact** and must be maintained current at all times. It is subject to review on a **per-event basis** (following activation) or **quarterly**, whichever occurs first.

### 5.1 Trigger Matrix

| Trigger Event | Category Notified | Severity Threshold | Triggering Condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| Technology system outage affecting customer services | CAT-01, CAT-02, CAT-03 | P1, P2 | System unavailable for > [X] minutes; customer-facing impact confirmed |
| Confirmed cybersecurity incident | CAT-01, CAT-02, CAT-03, CAT-04 | P1, P2 | Breach, ransomware, data exfiltration, or unauthorized access confirmed |
| Personal data breach | CAT-01, CAT-02, CAT-03 (PDPC) | P1, P2 | Personal data of customers or staff compromised or at risk |
| Third-party / vendor failure impacting operations | CAT-01, CAT-02, CAT-04 | P1, P2 | Critical vendor SLA breach or service failure with business impact |
| Fraudulent or financial crime activity | CAT-01, CAT-02, CAT-03 (BNM FIE) | P1, P2 | Confirmed fraud with financial or reputational impact |
| Business continuity plan activation | All categories (as applicable) | P1 | Declared by CRO or CEO |
| Regulatory inquiry or examination | CAT-01, CAT-02 (Compliance, Legal) | N/A | Receipt of regulatory notice or examination schedule |
| Media escalation or public disclosure | CAT-01, CAT-07 | P1, P2 | Media inquiry received or social media escalation detected |

*Document the end-to-end process for submitting incident notifications and reports to Bank Negara Malaysia via the BNM LINK portal. This section ensures that staff responsible for regulatory reporting can execute the process correctly and consistently under time pressure.*

### 7.2 Initial Incident Notification — Step-by-Step

*Complete these steps in sequence. Do not delay submission pending full investigation — initial notification must be made within the timeline specified in Section 6.1, even if information is incomplete.*

1. **Confirm incident severity** meets the BNM reporting threshold (refer to Section 6.1 and Appendix B — Reporting Decision Matrix).
2. **Log in** to BNM LINK at [URL] using authorized credentials.
3. **Navigate** to: `Submissions > Incident Reporting > Technology / Cyber Incident`.
4. **Select report type**: Initial Notification.
5. **Complete mandatory fields**:
   - Institution name and code
   - Incident date and time (MYT)
   - Incident description (factual summary — do not speculate on cause)
   - Systems or services affected
   - Customer / operational impact assessment
   - Immediate remediation steps taken
   - Estimated resolution timeline
6. **Attach supporting documentation** (if available): system logs, screenshots, initial impact assessment.
7. **Submit** and retain the BNM LINK **submission reference number** for records.
8. **Record submission** in the Notification Log (Section 6.3) and the organization's Incident Register.
9. **Notify** Head of Regulatory Affairs and CRO of submission completion.

### 7.3 Interim Update Submission

*Submit interim updates at the frequency specified in Section 6.1 or as directed by BNM, until the incident is fully resolved.*

1. Log in to BNM LINK.
2. Navigate to the existing incident record using the submission reference number.
3. Select: `Add Update / Interim Report`.
4. Provide factual updates on:
   - Current incident status
   - Revised scope or impact assessment
   - Progress on remediation actions
   - Any change to estimated resolution timeline
5. Submit and record the updated reference number.

### 7.4 Post-Incident / Final Report Submission

*Submit the final post-incident report within the deadline specified in Section 6.1. This report must be comprehensive and include root cause analysis.*

1. Log in to BNM LINK and locate the incident record.
2. Select: `Submit Final / Post-Incident Report`.
3. Complete all required fields including:
   - Confirmed root cause analysis
   - Full timeline of events
   - Total customer and operational impact
   - All remediation actions completed
   - Preventive measures and control enhancements implemented
   - Lessons learned summary
4. Attach the Post-Incident Review (PIR) report as a supporting document.
5. Submit and archive the final submission reference number in the Incident Register.
