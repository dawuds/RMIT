## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This document formally establishes and evidences the direct reporting line of the Internal Audit function to the Audit Committee of [Organization Name]. It serves as primary evidence of governance independence as required under Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, specifically Clause 13.2, which mandates that the audit function operate with organizational independence and report directly to the highest governance body responsible for oversight.

This documentation satisfies the following regulatory and governance obligations:

- **BNM RMiT Clause 13.2** — Independence and direct reporting of audit function to the Audit Committee
- **Bank Negara Malaysia Guidelines on Internal Audit Function** — Governance and independence requirements
- **Malaysian Code on Corporate Governance (MCCG)** — Board-level oversight of internal audit
- **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)** — Audit oversight of data governance and compliance

### 1.2 Scope

This document applies to:

- The Internal Audit function of [Organization Name]
- All technology audit activities conducted under the RMiT framework

## 6. Audit Methodology and Standards

*This section documents the standards, frameworks, and internal methodologies governing how the Internal Audit function conducts its work. This provides the Audit Committee with assurance that audit activities are conducted rigorously and consistently.*

### 6.1 Professional Standards Adopted

The Internal Audit function of [Organization Name] conducts all engagements in accordance with the following professional standards:

| Standard / Framework | Issued By | Applicability |
|---|---|---|
| International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing | Institute of Internal Auditors (IIA) | All internal audit activities |
| BNM Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) | Bank Negara Malaysia | Technology audit activities |
| BNM Guidelines on Internal Audit Function of Licensed Institutions | Bank Negara Malaysia | Governance and independence |
| ISACA IT Audit Framework (ITAF) | ISACA | Technology-specific engagements |
| ISO/IEC 27001:2022 | ISO/IEC | Information security audits |
| COBIT 2019 | ISACA | IT governance assessments |
| Personal Data Protection Act 2010 | Government of Malaysia | Data privacy audits |

### 6.2 Audit Engagement Lifecycle

Each audit engagement follows a standardized lifecycle:

1. **Planning** — Risk assessment, scoping, engagement letter issued to auditee
2. **Fieldwork** — Evidence gathering, walkthroughs, testing of controls, interviews
3. **Analysis** — Control gap analysis, root cause determination, severity rating
4. **Reporting** — Draft report issued, management response obtained, final report finalized
5. **Presentation** — Final report presented to Audit Committee
6. **Follow-Up** — Tracking of management action plan implementation to closure

### 6.3 Evidence Standards

All audit evidence must meet the following quality criteria:

| Criterion | Description |
|---|---|
| **Sufficient** | Adequate quantity of evidence to support the finding conclusion |
| **Reliable** | Evidence obtained from credible and authoritative sources |
| **Relevant** | Directly related to the audit objective and criteria |
| **Useful** | Supports audit conclusions and recommendations |

## 7. Finding Severity Classification

*This section defines the severity classification framework used by the Internal Audit function to rate all findings. Consistent and transparent severity ratings are essential for the Audit Committee to prioritize governance oversight and resource allocation.*

### 7.1 Severity Rating Definitions

| Severity Level | Definition | Required Resolution Timeframe | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Control failure that presents an immediate, material risk to the organization — including regulatory breach, significant financial loss, data breach, or systemic failure. Requires immediate escalation to the Audit Committee. | Immediate — within 30 days | Unauthorized access to production systems; failure to file mandatory BNM regulatory reports; major data breach affecting customer PII |
| **High** | Significant control weakness that, if unaddressed, could result in material risk exposure, regulatory non-compliance, or substantial operational impact. | 60 days | Inadequate privileged access management; outdated disaster recovery plans; third-party vendor with no security assessment |
| **Medium** | Control deficiency with moderate risk exposure. May not present immediate threat but requires timely remediation to prevent escalation. | 90 days | Incomplete user access reviews; inadequate change management documentation; gaps in security awareness training |
| **Low** | Minor control gaps or opportunities for improvement with limited risk impact. | 180 days | Documentation inconsistencies; minor process inefficiencies; advisory recommendations for best-practice alignment |
| **Advisory / Informational** | Observation noted for management awareness with no formal finding raised. No remediation SLA applies. | No SLA — management discretion | Emerging risk awareness; proactive recommendation for future planning |

### 7.2 Severity Rating Criteria Matrix

*Findings are rated using a combination of likelihood and impact dimensions. Auditors must document their rationale for severity assignments in the working papers.*

| | **High Impact** | **Medium Impact** | **Low Impact** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **High Likelihood** | Critical | High | Medium |
| **Medium Likelihood** | High | Medium | Low |
| **Low Likelihood** | Medium | Low | Advisory |

### 7.3 Escalation Protocol

| Scenario | Action Required | Escalation Target |
|---|---|---|
| Critical finding identified during fieldwork | Immediate verbal notification, followed by written escalation within 24 hours | Head of Internal Audit → Audit Committee Chair |
| High finding with no management response within SLA | Formal escalation memorandum | Head of Internal Audit → Audit Committee |
| Suspected fraud or misconduct identified | Immediate cessation of relevant audit work; separate investigation initiated | Head of Internal Audit → Board / Audit Committee (bypassing management) |
| Management disagrees with finding severity rating | Documented disagreement included in final report; Audit Committee notified | Head of Internal Audit presents both positions to Audit Committee |
| Overdue remediation (> 30 days past target date) | Overdue status reported in next Audit Committee update | Head of Internal Audit → Audit Committee |

### 7.4 Repeat Finding Policy

Findings that recur across consecutive audit cycles are automatically elevated by one severity level and flagged as **Repeat Findings** in all reports to the Audit Committee. Repeat findings indicate systemic root causes or inadequate management commitment to remediation.

| Finding Status | Description | Reporting Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| New | First occurrence in current audit cycle | Standard severity rating applies |
| Repeat | Same or substantially similar finding from prior cycle | Severity elevated by one level; flagged prominently in AC report |
| Persistent | Finding open for three or more consecutive cycles | Escalated to Audit Committee as a governance concern |
