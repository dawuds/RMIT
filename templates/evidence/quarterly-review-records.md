## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section defines the intent of this document and its function as a regulatory compliance artifact.*

This document constitutes the **Quarterly Review Record** for the Technology Risk Register maintained by [Organization Name] in accordance with the requirements of Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically **Clause 9.5**. It serves as formal evidence that the technology risk register has been systematically reviewed, updated, validated, and approved within the prescribed quarterly cycle.

This record fulfils the following objectives:

- Provide documented evidence of quarterly review activities as required by BNM RMiT Clause 9.5.
- Record all changes, additions, retirements, and status updates to technology risk items during the review period.
- Capture the current state of all technology risks, including their classification, ownership, treatment status, and residual risk ratings.
- Support internal audit, external examination, and regulatory inspection by maintaining a complete, timestamped, and version-controlled audit trail.
- Enable the Board Risk Committee (BRC) and Senior Management to assess the ongoing adequacy of technology risk management practices.

### 1.2 Scope

*This section delineates the boundaries of what is and is not covered by this quarterly review.*

This Quarterly Review Record applies to:

- All technology assets, systems, and infrastructure owned or operated by [Organization Name] that are subject to BNM RMiT obligations.
- All technology risk items currently registered in [Organization Name]'s Technology Risk Register as at [Review Period End Date].
- Information technology (IT) and operational technology (OT) risks, including but not limited to:
  - Cybersecurity and information security risks
  - IT infrastructure and availability risks
  - Application and software development risks
  - Third-party and vendor technology risks
  - Data management and data governance risks
  - Technology change and project delivery risks
  - Technology resilience and business continuity risks
  - Regulatory and compliance technology risks

**Out of Scope:**

- Non-technology operational risks managed under the Operational Risk Management framework (unless they have a direct technology dependency)
- Strategic and market risks unless driven by technology failure
- Risks specific to subsidiaries or related entities not consolidated under [Organization Name]'s RMiT scope (unless explicitly noted)

### 2.2 BNM RMiT Clause 9.5 — Specific Requirements

*This section extracts and interprets the specific BNM RMiT obligations fulfilled by this document.*

BNM RMiT Clause 9.5 requires financial institutions to:

1. **Maintain a technology risk register** that captures all identified technology risks in a structured and consistent manner.
2. **Review and update the risk register at least quarterly** to ensure it remains current, accurate, and reflective of the institution's evolving technology risk profile.
3. **Ensure completeness** of the register, including newly identified risks, changes to existing risks, and risks that have been retired or resolved.
4. **Document evidence** of each review cycle, including who performed the review, what changes were made, and who approved the updated register.
5. **Escalate material risks** to the appropriate governance bodies in a timely manner.

This Quarterly Review Record directly satisfies requirements (2), (3), (4), and (5) above and is to be read in conjunction with the Technology Risk Register itself (Document ID: [Risk Register Document ID]).

## 5. Classification and Categorisation

### 5.1 Risk Classification Framework

*This section documents the classification and categorisation structure applied to all risks in the Technology Risk Register. All risks must be classified consistently using the framework defined in the Technology Risk Management Policy.*

[Organization Name] classifies technology risks using the following dimensions, consistent with BNM RMiT requirements and [Organization Name]'s Technology Risk Management Policy (Document ID: [Policy ID]):

#### 5.1.1 Risk Category Definitions

| Risk Category | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Cybersecurity Risk** | Risks arising from threats to the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information systems and data through malicious or accidental cyber events. | Ransomware, phishing, DDoS, insider threats, data exfiltration |
| **IT Infrastructure Risk** | Risks arising from failures, degradation, or inadequacy of hardware, network, and platform infrastructure. | Server failure, network outage, storage degradation, capacity constraints |
| **Application Risk** | Risks arising from software defects, vulnerabilities, or inadequate design in business applications and systems. | Software vulnerabilities, coding errors, API failures, integration failures |
| **Data Management Risk** | Risks arising from inadequate data governance, data quality issues, or unauthorised data access. | Data corruption, data loss, unauthorised access, poor data lineage |
| **Third-Party / Vendor Risk** | Risks arising from reliance on external technology vendors, service providers, and outsourced arrangements. | Vendor service failure, concentration risk, vendor insolvency, SLA breaches |
| **IT Change Risk** | Risks arising from poorly managed technology changes, upgrades, or implementations. | Failed deployments, rollback failures, change-related outages |
| **Technology Resilience Risk** | Risks arising from inadequate business continuity, disaster recovery, or resilience capabilities. | RTO/RPO failures, backup failures, untested recovery plans |
| **Regulatory / Compliance Risk** | Risks arising from non-compliance with technology-related regulatory requirements. | RMiT non-compliance, data breach notification failures |
| **Emerging Technology Risk** | Risks arising from the adoption of new or evolving technologies before governance frameworks are mature. | AI/ML risks, cloud migration risks, open-source risks |
| **People and Process Risk** | Risks arising from inadequate skills, awareness, or processes in the management of technology. | Skill gaps, process failures, inadequate training |

#### 5.1.2 Risk Likelihood Scale

| Rating | Score | Definition | Indicative Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rare** | 1 | The event is highly unlikely to occur. | Less than once in 10 years |
| **Unlikely** | 2 | The event could occur but is not expected. | Once in 5–10 years |
| **Possible** | 3 | The event might occur at some point. | Once in 2–5 years |
| **Likely** | 4 | The event will probably occur in most circumstances. | Once per year |
| **Almost Certain** | 5 | The event is expected to occur regularly. | More than once per year |

#### 5.1.3 Risk Impact Scale

| Rating | Score | Financial Impact | Operational Impact | Reputational Impact | Regulatory Impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Negligible** | 1 | < MYR [Amount] | Minimal disruption, resolved within hours | Minimal, internal only | Minor query from regulator |
| **Minor** | 2 | MYR [Amount] – [Amount] | Limited disruption, resolved within 1 day | Limited, contained to customers | Regulatory observation |
| **Moderate** | 3 | MYR [Amount] – [Amount] | Significant disruption, resolved within 1 week | Public awareness, reputational damage | Regulatory direction |
| **Major** | 4 | MYR [Amount] – [Amount] | Critical disruption, resolved within 1 month | Significant media coverage | Regulatory enforcement action |
| **Catastrophic** | 5 | > MYR [Amount] | Existential disruption | Severe, long-term damage | License risk / major enforcement |

### 6.1 Ownership Framework

*This section documents the ownership and custodianship structure for all risks in the Technology Risk Register. Clear ownership is a fundamental requirement of BNM RMiT and [Organization Name]'s Technology Risk Management Policy.*

[Organization Name] applies the following ownership model for technology risks:

- **Risk Owner:** The senior individual accountable for managing the risk to an acceptable level. The Risk Owner has authority over the systems, processes, and resources required to implement risk treatment. Risk Owners are typically at Head of Department level or above.
- **Risk Custodian:** The individual responsible for the day-to-day management and monitoring of the risk and its associated controls. The Risk Custodian reports to the Risk Owner and provides regular updates on risk status.
- **Control Owner:** The individual responsible for implementing and maintaining specific controls that mitigate the risk. A single risk may have multiple Control Owners.
