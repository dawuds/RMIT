## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section states the intent of the document and its role in satisfying regulatory and operational requirements. Describe why these records are maintained and what they demonstrate to auditors.*

This document serves as the official record-keeping framework for emergency response training conducted at **[Organization Name]** in relation to fire emergency response procedures. It establishes the processes, standards, and evidence requirements for ensuring all personnel are adequately trained to respond to fire and related physical emergency events at organizational premises.

These records exist to:

- Demonstrate compliance with **Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)** policy, specifically **Clause 10.27**, which mandates that financial institutions maintain evidence of staff preparedness for technology and operational disruptions, including physical threats such as fire emergencies.
- Provide auditable evidence that [Organization Name] maintains a proactive and structured approach to emergency preparedness.
- Support business continuity and operational resilience objectives by ensuring personnel are equipped to act decisively during fire emergencies.
- Fulfil obligations under the **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)**, **Occupational Safety and Health Act 1994 (OSHA)**, and **Fire Services Act 1988** as applicable to organizational safety training requirements.

### 1.2 Document Scope

*Specify the boundaries of what this document covers — which premises, systems, processes, and personnel fall within its scope. Clearly state any exclusions.*

This document applies to:

- All **permanent, contract, and temporary employees** of [Organization Name].
- All **third-party contractors and vendors** with regular or long-term physical access to [Organization Name] premises.
- All **[Organization Name] occupied premises**, including:
  - [Headquarters Address]
  - [Branch Office 1 Address]
  - [Data Centre / Colocation Facility Address]
  - [Other Sites as Applicable]

**Out of Scope:**

- Remote-only employees who do not regularly attend any [Organization Name] premises are exempt from practical drill participation but must complete the online awareness module.
- Third-party data centre operators managing their own facilities independently are subject to their own emergency response frameworks; however, contractual evidence of their training must be obtained and retained by [Organization Name].


## 7. Assessment Methodology and Pass Criteria

*Document the assessment instruments used to evaluate training effectiveness and the standards participants must meet to be considered competent. This section must demonstrate that training is substantively evaluated, not merely attended.*

### 7.1 Assessment Framework

*Describe the multi-component assessment approach, including both knowledge and practical competency evaluation.*

The assessment framework for emergency response training at [Organization Name] comprises two components:

1. **Theoretical Knowledge Assessment** — evaluates understanding of fire emergency procedures, evacuation protocols, and responsibilities.
2. **Practical Competency Observation** — evaluates demonstration of correct behaviour during evacuation drills and practical exercises.

Both components must be completed. Failure in either component constitutes an overall failure for the training session.

### 7.2 Theoretical Knowledge Assessment

| Assessment Element | Description | Format | Duration | Weighting |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Multiple Choice Questions | Fire safety theory, evacuation procedures, alarm response | Written / Online | 30 minutes | 50% |
| Scenario-Based Questions | Decision-making in simulated emergency scenarios | Written | 20 minutes | 30% |
| Policy and Procedure Quiz | Knowledge of [Organization Name]'s specific emergency response policy | Written / Online | 10 minutes | 20% |

**Pass Criteria — Theoretical Assessment:**

- Minimum overall score: **70%**
- Minimum score on Scenario-Based Questions: **60%** (critical section — minimum threshold applies independently)
- Participants scoring below 70% overall are required to complete a remediation session and re-assessment within **30 calendar days**.
- Participants failing the re-assessment must undergo a structured remediation programme supervised by the HR Training Coordinator and Certified Trainer.

### 7.3 Practical Competency Observation

*The practical component is assessed by the Certified Trainer and designated Fire Wardens using a structured observation checklist during the live evacuation drill.*

| Competency Criteria | Assessed By | Pass Standard |
|---|---|---|
| Responds promptly to fire alarm within required time | Fire Warden / Trainer | Within 60 seconds of alarm activation |
| Follows designated evacuation route correctly | Fire Warden | No deviation from posted route |
| Proceeds calmly without endangering others | Fire Warden | No observed unsafe behaviour |
| Reports to correct assembly point | Fire Warden | Correct assembly point reached |
| Demonstrates knowledge of Fire Warden contact | Trainer (verbal check) | Correctly identifies on-duty Fire Warden |
| Does not use lifts during evacuation | Fire Warden / Trainer | Stairwell used exclusively |
| Follows Fire Warden instructions | Fire Warden | Full compliance observed |
| Logs attendance at assembly point (headcount) | Fire Warden | Participates in headcount process |

**Pass Criteria — Practical Assessment:**

- Participants must satisfy **all 8 criteria** to pass the practical component.
- Failure on any single criterion results in a practical fail and requires a supervised make-up drill within **14 calendar days**.

### 7.4 Overall Competency Determination

| Result | Criteria | Action Required |
|---|---|---|
| **Competent** | Pass on both theoretical and practical | Certificate issued; record updated |
| **Remediation Required — Theory** | Fail on theoretical; pass on practical | Re-assessment within 30 days |
| **Remediation Required — Practical** | Pass on theoretical; fail on practical | Make-up drill within 14 days |
| **Full Remediation Required** | Fail on both components | Structured remediation programme; full re-assessment within 30 days |
| **Non-Compliant** | Failed re-assessment or missed remediation | Escalation to Department Head and HR; formal HR process initiated |

### 7.5 Assessment Records Retention

All completed assessment papers, observation checklists, and results shall be retained for a minimum of **5 years** in accordance with [Organization Name]'s Records Retention Policy **[POL-RRM-XXX]** and BNM record-keeping requirements.

## 10. Incident-Triggered Training

*Document the process for conducting unscheduled or emergency training sessions triggered by actual incidents, near-misses, or significant regulatory findings.*

### 10.1 Triggers for Incident-Driven Training

| Trigger | Description | Training Required | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| Actual fire incident at any [Organization Name] premises | Fire occurs and evacuation is executed (partial or full) | Full debrief session + refresher training for all affected site personnel | Within 30 days of incident |
| Fire near-miss (e.g., uncontrolled smoke, unreported fire hazard) | Incident reported but fire does not fully develop | Targeted awareness session for affected area | Within 14 days of incident |
| Evacuation drill failure (completion rate <80% or safety breaches observed) | Drill reveals material gaps in staff preparedness | Mandatory re-drill for all staff at affected site | Within 30 days of failed drill |
| BNM regulatory finding related to emergency preparedness | BNM examiner identifies gap in training records or effectiveness | Remediation training programme as directed by corrective action plan | As stipulated in corrective action plan |
