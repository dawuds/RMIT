## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This document establishes the framework and content structure for role-specific technology risk and cybersecurity training materials developed and maintained by [Organization Name] in accordance with Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy, specifically Clause 15.3.

These training materials are designed to ensure that all personnel, commensurate with their roles and responsibilities, possess adequate knowledge and competency to identify, manage, and respond to technology risks and cybersecurity threats. The programme supports a culture of security awareness and informed risk stewardship across the institution.

### 1.2 Scope

This document applies to:

- All employees of [Organization Name], including permanent staff, contract staff, and third-party personnel with access to institutional systems and data
- Role-specific training tracks designated for the following functional groups:
  - Board of Directors and Senior Management
  - Technology and IT Operations
  - Information Security
  - Business Units and Front-Line Staff
  - Risk Management and Compliance
  - Vendors and Third Parties (where applicable)

### 1.3 Regulatory Context

| Regulatory Instrument | Clause / Section | Requirement Summary |
|---|---|---|
| BNM RMiT Policy | Clause 15.3 | Financial institutions shall develop and implement role-specific training programmes to build technology risk awareness and competency |
| BNM RMiT Policy | Clause 15 (General) | Ongoing security awareness and training obligations for all personnel |

## 2. Assessment Scope and Criteria

*Define the scope of competency assessment covered by these training materials, including which roles are assessed, the domains evaluated, and the criteria used to determine training adequacy.*

### 2.1 Assessment Scope

The assessment scope encompasses the following technology risk and cybersecurity domains:

| Domain | Description | Applicable Roles |
|---|---|---|
| Cybersecurity Fundamentals | Phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, clean desk | All Staff |
| Data Classification & Handling | PDPA obligations, data sensitivity, disposal | All Staff |
| Incident Reporting | Procedures to report suspected incidents | All Staff |
| Secure Development Lifecycle | OWASP Top 10, code review, vulnerability management | Technology / Development Teams |
| Access Management | Privilege management, MFA, PAM controls | IT Operations, Security |
| Third-Party Risk | Vendor onboarding security requirements | Procurement, Risk, IT |
| Technology Risk Governance | Risk appetite, board oversight obligations | Senior Management, Board |
| Threat Intelligence & SOC Operations | Threat detection, analysis, response | Information Security Team |

### 2.2 Assessment Criteria

*Specify the criteria used to evaluate whether the training programme and individual completions meet regulatory and institutional standards.*

- **Coverage**: All roles defined in Section 5 must have an assigned training track with documented learning objectives.
- **Relevance**: Training content must align with the technology risk profile of [Organization Name] as defined in the current Technology Risk Assessment.
- **Currency**: Content must reflect the latest threat landscape, regulatory updates, and internal policy changes.
- **Completion Rate Thresholds**: Defined per role in Section 5.3.
- **Competency Validation**: Completion of a post-training assessment with a minimum pass score as defined in Section 6.

## 6. Assessment Methodology and Pass Criteria

*Describe the method by which learner competency is evaluated for each training track, including question formats, scoring, remediation, and escalation procedures for repeated failures.*

### 6.1 Assessment Design Principles

- Assessments are designed to validate practical understanding, not merely factual recall.
- Question banks are reviewed and refreshed in alignment with the Content Update Schedule (Section 7).
- Questions are randomised per attempt to reduce answer-sharing risk.
- Assessment results are stored in the LMS with timestamps for audit purposes.

## 7. Content Update Schedule

*Define the process and schedule for reviewing, updating, and recertifying training content to ensure it remains accurate, relevant, and aligned with the current threat and regulatory environment.*

### 7.1 Update Triggers

Training content shall be reviewed and updated upon occurrence of any of the following triggers:

| Trigger Type | Description | Urgency |
|---|---|---|
| **Regulatory Change** | New or amended BNM guidelines, PDPA requirements, NACSA advisories, or related policy updates | High — update within [e.g., 30 days] of effective date |
| **Material Security Incident** | Significant internal or industry-wide security incident relevant to staff behaviour | High — update within [e.g., 14 days] |
| **Technology Change** | Introduction of new systems, platforms, or significant changes to existing infrastructure | Medium — update within [e.g., 60 days] |
| **Threat Landscape Shift** | Emergence of new attack vectors, threat actor techniques, or significant CVEs | Medium — update within [e.g., 30 days] |
| **Assessment Performance Trends** | Consistently low scores in a specific module domain across multiple review cycles | Medium — update within next review cycle |
| **Scheduled Quarterly Review** | Routine review regardless of triggers | Low — at each quarterly cycle |

### 7.2 Scheduled Review Calendar

| Review Cycle | Review Window | Content Owner | Approver |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | [01 Jan – 31 Mar] | [CISO Office / HR L&D] | [CISO / Head of HR] |
| Q2 | [01 Apr – 30 Jun] | [CISO Office / HR L&D] | [CISO / Head of HR] |
| Q3 | [01 Jul – 30 Sep] | [CISO Office / HR L&D] | [CISO / Head of HR] |
| Q4 | [01 Oct – 31 Dec] | [CISO Office / HR L&D] | [CISO / Head of HR] |

### 7.3 Content Update Process

1. **Identify Update Need** — Content owner identifies trigger event or scheduled review date.
2. **Gap Analysis** — Compare current content against updated regulatory text, threat intelligence, or internal policy.
3. **Content Revision** — SME drafts updated content and assessment questions.
4. **Peer Review** — Secondary SME reviews revised content for accuracy and completeness.
5. **Approver Sign-Off** — CISO and/or Head of HR approve revised materials.
6. **LMS Deployment** — Updated modules published to LMS with version control notation.
7. **Learner Notification** — Affected learner groups notified of updated content and any re-assessment requirements.
8. **Record Update** — Version history updated in this document (Section 9.1).
