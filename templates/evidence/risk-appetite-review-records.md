## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document constitutes the formal record of [Organisation Name]'s annual review of its Technology Risk Appetite, as required under **Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, Clause 8.4**. It provides documented evidence that the Board of Directors has reviewed, deliberated upon, and formally approved the organisation's risk appetite statements, quantitative thresholds, and qualitative tolerances governing the use and management of technology.

**Scope:** This record applies to all technology assets, systems, and processes operated by or on behalf of [Organisation Name], including but not limited to core banking systems, digital channels, third-party technology service providers, and critical shared infrastructure. It encompasses all risk domains addressed within the RMiT framework, including cybersecurity, operational technology risk, IT resilience, and third-party risk.

**Regulatory Basis:** Bank Negara Malaysia RMiT Policy Document (Ref: BNM/RH/PD 028-2), Clause 8.4 — *Risk Appetite*.

## 6. Qualitative Risk Tolerance Statements

*Document the Board-approved narrative statements that describe the organisation's acceptable risk posture for each major risk domain. These statements complement the quantitative thresholds and provide guidance for management judgement.*

### 6.1 Board-Approved Risk Tolerance Statements

**Cybersecurity Risk**

> *[Organisation Name] has **zero tolerance** for cybersecurity incidents resulting in the unauthorised access, exfiltration, or compromise of customer personal data or financial assets. The organisation maintains a **low appetite** for any disruption to critical systems arising from cyber threats, and will invest proactively in detection, prevention, and response capabilities to maintain residual risk within acceptable boundaries.*

---

**IT Resilience and Availability**

> *[Organisation Name] accepts **low** residual risk to the availability of critical technology systems and services. The organisation's risk tolerance for unplanned downtime of core banking, payment, and digital channel systems is defined by the Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs) set out in the Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Plans. Any breach of approved availability thresholds requires immediate escalation to Senior Management and the Board Risk Committee.*

---

**Third-Party and Outsourcing Risk**

> *[Organisation Name] maintains a **moderate appetite** for technology risks arising from the use of third-party service providers, subject to robust due diligence, contractual controls, and ongoing performance monitoring. The organisation has **zero tolerance** for material failures by critical technology service providers that result in prolonged customer impact or regulatory non-compliance.*

---

**Data Management and Privacy Risk**

> *[Organisation Name] has **zero tolerance** for non-compliance with the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA) and applicable BNM data management requirements. The organisation maintains a **low appetite** for data integrity failures and will enforce data governance controls proportionate to the criticality and sensitivity of data assets.*

---

**Technology Change and Innovation Risk**

> *[Organisation Name] accepts a **moderate-to-higher appetite** for risks associated with strategic technology transformation and innovation, provided that such initiatives are subject to structured risk assessment, robust change management controls, and do not compromise the integrity or availability of critical systems during transition.*

---

**Compliance and Regulatory Risk**

> *[Organisation Name] has **zero tolerance** for technology practices that result in material non-compliance with BNM RMiT requirements, PDPA obligations, or other applicable regulatory mandates. All technology risk management activities must meet or exceed the minimum standards prescribed by the relevant regulatory frameworks.*

## 7. Breach Escalation Triggers

*Define the conditions under which risk appetite breaches must be escalated, the escalation pathway, and the required management response. This section operationalises the organisation's breach management obligations under RMiT Clause 8.4.*

### 7.1 Escalation Trigger Matrix

| Trigger Level | Condition | Immediate Action Required | Escalation To | Timeframe for Escalation | Reporting Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 1 — Watch** | KRI enters Amber threshold | Notify Risk Custodian; initiate monitoring uplift | Technology Risk Manager | Within [X] business days | Include in next scheduled risk report |
| **Level 2 — Elevated** | KRI remains in Amber for [X] consecutive periods | Formal review of controls; prepare remediation plan | Chief Risk Officer / Technology Risk Committee | Within [X] business days of trigger | Submit formal risk event notification |
| **Level 3 — Breach** | KRI enters Red threshold / Risk Limit exceeded | Invoke incident response; initiate immediate containment | Board Risk Committee / Board of Directors | Within [24 / 48] hours | Board notification; assess BNM reporting obligation |
| **Level 4 — Critical Breach** | Zero-tolerance threshold violated; material regulatory breach | Activate crisis management protocol | Board of Directors / CEO | Immediately | BNM notification per Incident Reporting Guidelines; engage legal and compliance |

### 7.2 Escalation Pathway

```
Risk Custodian
    ↓ (Level 1 — Watch)
Technology Risk Manager
    ↓ (Level 2 — Elevated)
Chief Risk Officer / Technology Risk Committee
    ↓ (Level 3 — Breach)
Board Risk Committee
    ↓ (Level 4 — Critical / Zero-Tolerance Breach)
Board of Directors + BNM (if reportable)
```

### 7.3 Post-Breach Requirements

| Requirement | Description | Responsible Party | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| Root Cause Analysis (RCA) | Conduct formal RCA for all Level 3 and Level 4 breaches | Technology Risk Manager | Within [X] days of breach containment |
| Remediation Plan | Develop and submit a time-bound remediation plan | Risk Owner | Within [X] days of RCA completion |
| Board Reporting | Present breach summary, RCA findings, and remediation status to Board | Chief Risk Officer | At next Board Risk Committee meeting, or sooner if critical |
| Regulatory Notification | Assess obligation to notify BNM per incident reporting requirements | Chief Compliance Officer | Per BNM Incident Reporting Guidelines |
| Appetite Review | Assess whether risk appetite thresholds require adjustment post-breach | Chief Risk Officer | At next scheduled annual review, or as directed by Board |
