## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Lessons Learned Register serves as an evidence artifact under Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)** Policy Document, specifically addressing the requirements under **Clause 10.3** relating to technology project governance and continuous improvement. The register captures, categorises, and tracks lessons learned from completed technology projects to inform future project planning, risk management practices, and technology governance decisions within [Organization Name].

### 1.2 Scope

This register applies to:

- All technology projects concluded within the review period, including infrastructure upgrades, system implementations, and digital transformation initiatives
- Post-implementation reviews (PIRs) and post-incident reviews where technology was a contributing factor
- Technology change management activities that yield systemic insights applicable to future initiatives

This register **does not** cover:

- Ongoing or in-flight projects (unless a mid-project review has been formally sanctioned)
- Lessons documented under separate incident management or business continuity frameworks unless cross-referenced herein

### 1.3 Regulatory Basis

This document supports compliance with the following BNM regulatory instruments:

| Instrument | Clause / Section | Requirement Summary |
|---|---|---|
| BNM RMiT Policy Document | 10.3 | Technology project governance, post-implementation review, and continuous improvement |
| BNM RMiT Policy Document | 10.3(a) | Documentation of project outcomes and key learnings |
| BNM RMiT Policy Document | 10.3(b) | Escalation and remediation tracking for identified issues |

## 5. Status and Lifecycle Stage

*Each lesson must progress through a defined lifecycle from identification to closure. Active monitoring of status ensures that recommendations are acted upon and lessons are formally embedded into organisational practice.*

### 5.1 Lifecycle Stages

| Stage | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Open** | Lesson identified and entered into the register; recommendation not yet assigned |
| **In Progress** | Recommendation accepted; corrective or improvement actions underway |
| **Pending Validation** | Actions completed; awaiting verification that the improvement has been embedded |
| **Closed** | Lesson fully addressed; evidence of implementation confirmed and recorded |
| **Deferred** | Action postponed with documented rationale and revised target date |
| **Not Applicable** | Lesson noted for awareness only; no action required (with justification) |

## 7. Roles and Responsibilities

*The following RACI matrix defines accountability for the maintenance, review, and governance of the Lessons Learned Register in accordance with BNM RMiT Clause 10.3 requirements.*

### 7.1 RACI Matrix

**Legend:** R = Responsible | A = Accountable | C = Consulted | I = Informed

| Activity | Department Head | Project / Initiative Owner | IT Governance / Risk | Project Management Office (PMO) | Internal Audit | Chief Information Officer (CIO) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Initiate lessons learned capture post-project closure | A | R | C | R | I | I |
| Enter lesson into register | C | R | I | R | I | I |
| Assign classification and severity | C | R | R | C | I | I |
| Assign lesson owner and custodian | A | C | C | R | I | I |
| Develop and approve recommended actions | A | R | C | C | I | I |
| Implement corrective / improvement actions | C | A | C | I | I | I |
| Monitor action completion and update status | A | R | C | R | I | I |
| Conduct quarterly register review | A | C | R | C | C | I |
| Escalate overdue actions | A | C | R | I | I | A |
| Approve register for regulatory submission | I | I | C | I | C | A |
| Report lessons learned trends to Board / Senior Management | I | I | C | C | I | R |

### 7.2 Role Definitions

| Role | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Department Head** | The accountable owner of the register and the senior leader responsible for ensuring lessons are embedded into departmental practice |
| **Project / Initiative Owner** | The individual who led the project from which the lesson was drawn; responsible for identifying and documenting lessons |
| **IT Governance / Risk** | Provides oversight to ensure lessons are appropriately classified and that systemic risks are escalated |
| **Project Management Office (PMO)** | Coordinates lesson capture activities across projects and maintains the register as part of project closure procedures |
| **Internal Audit** | Reviews the register during audit cycles to assess governance effectiveness and regulatory compliance |
| **Chief Information Officer (CIO)** | Ultimate accountable officer for the organisation's technology governance posture; approves the register for formal regulatory evidence |

### Appendix C — Severity Classification Guide

*Use the following criteria to determine the appropriate severity rating for each lesson.*

| Severity | Criteria | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Lesson relates to an event that caused or could have caused significant regulatory breach, material financial loss, reputational damage, or systemic technology failure | Core banking system outage due to unpatched vulnerability; data breach affecting customer records |
| **High** | Lesson relates to a significant process failure, risk materialisation, or governance gap with moderate to high impact | Vendor delivering without contractual SLA met; insufficient UAT leading to post-go-live defects affecting operations |
| **Medium** | Lesson relates to a process inefficiency, missed risk indicator, or governance lapse with limited operational impact | Inadequate stakeholder communication causing minor project delays; documentation gaps identified during PIR |
| **Low** | Lesson is an observation or positive practice with minimal risk implication; primarily for awareness and knowledge sharing | Good practice in change management communication worth replicating; minor scheduling optimisation identified |
