## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This document serves as the official record of project approvals and stage-gate sign-offs within [Organization Name]. It provides an auditable evidence trail demonstrating that technology projects, changes, and initiatives have been reviewed, assessed, and formally authorised at each defined lifecycle stage in accordance with the Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, specifically Clause 10.1 governing technology project management and governance controls.

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the boundaries of what this record covers — include the types of projects, systems, or initiatives subject to this approval process, and any exclusions.*

This record applies to:

- All technology projects and system development initiatives undertaken by [Organization Name]
- Projects initiated by internal business units, IT department, or third-party vendors
- Changes to critical systems as defined under [Organization Name]'s Technology Risk Management Framework
- Stage-gate reviews for projects classified as [Minor / Significant / Critical] in accordance with [Organization Name]'s project classification policy

### Appendix D — Glossary of Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Stage Gate** | A formal review and approval checkpoint within the project lifecycle that must be passed before the project may proceed to the next phase. |
| **BNM RMiT** | Bank Negara Malaysia's Risk Management in Technology Policy Document, which sets out requirements for financial institutions to manage technology and cyber risks. |
| **Technology Risk Assessment (TRA)** | A structured evaluation of the technology risks associated with a project, system, or change, conducted in accordance with [Organization Name]'s risk management framework. |
| **RACI** | A responsibility assignment matrix defining who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for each activity. |
| **CAB** | Change Advisory Board — the governance body responsible for reviewing and approving changes to production environments. |
| **UAT** | User Acceptance Testing — formal testing conducted by business users to verify that a system meets defined requirements before production deployment. |
| **PDPA** | Personal Data Protection Act 2010 — Malaysian legislation governing the processing of personal data of individuals. |
| **NACSA** | National Cyber Security Agency of Malaysia. |
| **Post-Implementation Review (PIR)** | A formal review conducted after project deployment to assess whether objectives were met, lessons learned, and whether any outstanding actions are required. |
