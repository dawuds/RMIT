## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section defines the purpose of this document and the regulatory obligation it fulfils. Authors should describe why this record is maintained and what governance function it serves.*

This Board Acknowledgement Records document serves as formal evidence that the Board of Directors of [Organization Name] has received, reviewed, and deliberated on technology risk reports and related governance matters in accordance with the Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document. This record demonstrates the active oversight role of the Board in the management of technology risks and affirms the institution's commitment to sound technology governance.

This document is maintained as a primary compliance artifact to satisfy regulatory examination requirements and to provide an auditable trail of Board-level engagement with technology risk matters. It consolidates evidence from Board meetings, Board committee sessions, acknowledgement signatures, and related deliberation records into a single, coherent record set.

### 1.2 Scope

*Describe the organisational and temporal boundaries of this document. Specify which entities, business units, systems, and reporting periods are covered.*

This document applies to:

- **Organisational Scope:** All Board members, Board-level committees (including the Board Risk Committee, Board Audit Committee, and any Board Technology Committee), and the Company Secretary of [Organization Name] and its subsidiaries as applicable.
- **Functional Scope:** All technology risk reports, technology governance briefings, cybersecurity updates, IT audit findings, and significant technology incident reports presented to the Board or its committees.
- **Temporal Scope:** Records are maintained on a continuous basis with formal quarterly review cycles. This document covers the period from [Start Date] to [End Date / Present].
- **Regulatory Scope:** Obligations arising from BNM RMiT, specifically Clause 8.5 pertaining to Board oversight of technology risk management, and associated governance requirements under BNM's corporate governance standards.
- **Exclusions:** Operational-level technology risk records maintained below Board level are governed by separate management-tier documentation and are referenced herein only as source inputs to Board-level reporting.

### 1.3 Document Objectives

*List the specific objectives this document is designed to achieve from a compliance and governance perspective.*

- Provide verifiable, auditable evidence of the Board's receipt and acknowledgement of technology risk information.
- Document the nature and substance of Board deliberations on technology risk matters.
- Record Board resolutions, directives, and follow-up actions arising from technology risk discussions.
- Support regulatory examination and internal audit reviews of Board-level technology governance.
- Demonstrate alignment with BNM RMiT Clause 8.5 requirements for Board oversight of technology risk.
- Enable continuous monitoring and quarterly structured review of Board acknowledgement activities.

### 2.2 RMiT Clause 8.5 — Specific Requirements

*Summarise the specific requirements of RMiT Clause 8.5 as they apply to Board Acknowledgement Records. This section should be reviewed against the current version of the BNM RMiT policy to ensure accuracy.*

BNM RMiT Clause 8.5 establishes the following obligations relevant to this document:

- The Board shall receive and deliberate on comprehensive technology risk reports at regular intervals, and no less than quarterly.
- Reports presented to the Board must cover the institution's technology risk profile, significant technology incidents, cybersecurity posture, and technology-related audit findings.
- Evidence of the Board's receipt, review, and deliberation on technology risk information must be documented and retained to support regulatory examination.
- The Board is responsible for providing strategic direction and oversight of the institution's technology risk management framework.
- Escalation pathways from management to the Board must be clearly defined and evidenced.

### 12.2 Roles and Responsibilities Narrative

*Provide a brief narrative description of each key role's responsibilities in relation to Board Acknowledgement Records.*

**Board of Directors**
The Board holds ultimate accountability for the institution's technology risk governance. The Board is responsible for receiving and deliberating on technology risk reports, providing strategic direction, issuing directives, and ensuring that management actions are adequate to manage technology risks within the Board-approved risk appetite.

**Company Secretary**
The Company Secretary is the primary custodian of Board Acknowledgement Records. The Company Secretary is responsible for preparing and distributing Board papers, recording minutes of meeting, obtaining acknowledgement signatures, maintaining all registers contained in this document, conducting quarterly reviews, and ensuring secure retention of records in accordance with the Records Retention Policy.

**Chief Risk Officer (CRO)**
The CRO is responsible for the preparation and submission of quarterly technology risk reports and for presenting technology risk matters to the Board and Board committees. The CRO ensures that the Board is provided with accurate, timely, and comprehensive information on the institution's technology risk profile.

**Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)**
The CISO is responsible for preparing and presenting cybersecurity-related reports to the Board, including cybersecurity posture updates, threat landscape briefings, and significant cybersecurity incident reports.

**Chief Information Officer (CIO)**
The CIO is responsible for preparing and presenting technology operations, infrastructure, and project-related reports to the Board. The CIO is also responsible for the timely and accurate reporting of significant technology incidents.

**Head of Internal Audit**
The Head of Internal Audit is responsible for preparing and presenting technology audit findings and internal audit reports to the Board Audit Committee and, where required, to the Full Board.

**Head of Compliance**
The Head of Compliance is responsible for ensuring that the Board Acknowledgement Records process meets regulatory requirements and for advising the Company Secretary on compliance obligations. The Head of Compliance conducts independent review of the adequacy of Board acknowledgement evidence.

### Appendix C — Technology Risk Report Minimum Content Standards

*This appendix defines the minimum content requirements for each category of technology risk report submitted to the Board. These standards ensure that the Board receives comprehensive and consistent information to discharge its oversight responsibilities under BNM RMiT Clause 8.5.*

**C.1 Quarterly Technology Risk Report — Minimum Content**

- Executive summary of the technology risk profile for the quarter
- Technology risk dashboard / heat map showing current risk ratings across all risk categories
- Comparison against the Board-approved risk appetite and tolerance thresholds
- Summary of significant technology incidents during the quarter
- Status update on prior quarter's Board-directed actions
- Emerging technology risks and the threat landscape
- Key performance indicators (KPIs) and key risk indicators (KRIs) for technology risk
- Regulatory compliance status — BNM RMiT and other applicable requirements
- Management recommendations and proposed actions

**C.2 Cybersecurity Report — Minimum Content**

- Current cybersecurity threat landscape and threat intelligence summary
- Cybersecurity incident summary (including near-misses)
- Vulnerability management status
- Phishing and social engineering attack statistics
- Security Operations Centre (SOC) performance metrics
- Penetration testing and vulnerability assessment results (summary level)
- Cybersecurity awareness training completion rates
- Status of critical security patches and system updates
- Third-party cybersecurity risk summary
- Regulatory cybersecurity compliance status

**C.3 Technology Incident Report — Minimum Content**

- Incident description, timeline, and scope of impact
- Root cause analysis (preliminary or final)
- Immediate containment and recovery actions taken
- Customer / operational impact assessment
- Financial impact assessment (where applicable)
- Regulatory notification obligations and status
- Lessons learned and remediation actions
- Estimated timeline for full resolution and remediation

**C.4 Technology Audit Report — Minimum Content**

- Audit scope and objectives
- Summary of audit findings by severity (Critical, High, Medium, Low)
- Root causes of significant findings
- Management responses and remediation commitments
- Agreed remediation timelines
- Outstanding findings from prior audit cycles (aged findings)
- Overall audit opinion / rating
- Benchmarking observations (where applicable)
