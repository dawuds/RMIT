## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section defines the intent of this document and the regulatory context under which it is maintained. Authors should clearly articulate the remediation event this evidence package addresses and its relationship to BNM RMiT obligations.*

This Remediation Completion Evidence document provides a structured, auditable record confirming that all identified technology risk gaps assigned to [Organization Name] have been fully remediated in accordance with the requirements of Bank Negara Malaysia's **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically under **Clause 18.2** governing the closure of identified deficiencies.

This document serves as the primary evidentiary artifact to demonstrate to internal stakeholders, the Board Risk Committee, external auditors, and Bank Negara Malaysia that [Organization Name] has:

- Identified, prioritized, and assigned remediation actions to close technology risk gaps.
- Executed remediation plans within agreed timelines and resource allocations.
- Verified closure through independent confirmation and supporting evidence.
- Reported outcomes through the appropriate governance and escalation channels.

## 10. Escalation and Exception Management

*This section defines the escalation pathways and exception management procedures applicable to this remediation programme. All escalations and exceptions must be formally documented, approved, and recorded.*

### 10.1 Escalation Triggers

The following conditions must trigger an immediate formal escalation:

| Trigger | Escalation Level | Notified Party | Response Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| Critical-rated action overdue by > 7 days | Level 3 — Executive | CRO, CIO, CEO | Within 1 business day |
| High-rated action overdue by > 14 days | Level 2 — Senior Management | CRO, CISO | Within 2 business days |
| Medium-rated action overdue by > 30 days | Level 1 — Management | Head of Technology Risk | Within 5 business days |
| Critical action blocked with no mitigating control | Level 3 — Executive | CRO, CIO, CEO | Immediate (same day) |
| Regulatory notification threshold reached | Level 3 + BNM | CRO, CEO, Board Chair | Per BNM reporting requirements |
| Evidence of ineffective remediation identified post-verification | Level 2 | CISO, Head of Internal Audit | Within 2 business days |

### 10.2 Exception and Risk Acceptance Process

*Where a remediation action cannot be completed within the agreed timeline or the proposed remediation is not technically feasible, a formal exception or risk acceptance must be obtained.*

The following process applies:

1. **Identification:** The remediation owner identifies that an exception is required and documents the reason in writing.
2. **Assessment:** The Head of Technology Risk or CISO assesses the residual risk and proposed compensating controls.
3. **Compensating Controls:** The remediation owner must document interim compensating controls to be maintained for the duration of the exception.
4. **Approval:** Obtain formal written approval from the appropriate authority (as defined in Section 5.1).
5. **Monitoring:** The exception is tracked in the remediation register with a revised target date and reviewed at each governance meeting.
6. **Reporting:** Exceptions involving Critical or High-rated findings must be reported to the Board Risk Committee.
