## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section states the intent of this document and the regulatory obligation it fulfils. Authors should clearly articulate why this record exists and what risk decision it formalises.*

This Remediation Exception Record documents the formal risk acceptance decision made by [Organization Name] for one or more identified vulnerabilities or security deficiencies that could not be remediated within the prescribed Service Level Targets (SLTs) established under [Organization Name]'s Technology Risk Management Framework and the Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document.

This record serves as an authoritative audit trail demonstrating that:

- The vulnerability has been identified, assessed, and acknowledged by accountable management.
- A conscious, informed risk acceptance decision has been made in accordance with [Organization Name]'s risk appetite.
- Compensating controls have been evaluated and implemented where practicable.
- A structured remediation plan with defined milestones and target dates is in place.
- The residual risk is being actively monitored and managed until full remediation is achieved.

### 4.1 Objectives of This Exception Record

The objectives of raising this formal exception record are to:

1. Document the informed risk acceptance decision made by [Approving Authority] with respect to the identified vulnerability listed in Section 5.

## 14. Exception Escalation and Closure

### 14.1 Conditions for Escalation

*Any of the following conditions must trigger immediate escalation per the escalation chain defined in Section 9.2:*

- [ ] A remediation milestone is missed by more than [10] business days without an approved recovery plan.
- [ ] Active exploitation of the vulnerability is confirmed or suspected.
- [ ] A compensating control is found to be ineffective or has failed.
- [ ] The exception expiry date is within [30] calendar days and full remediation is not expected to be completed on time.
- [ ] A material change in the threat landscape significantly increases the likelihood or impact of exploitation.
- [ ] An internal audit or third-party assessment identifies the residual risk as unacceptable.
- [ ] A regulatory inquiry or BNM examination specifically references this vulnerability or exception.

### 14.2 Exception Extension

*Where remediation cannot be completed before the exception expiry date, a formal extension must be requested and approved prior to the expiry. Extensions are not automatic and require re-assessment.*

An exception extension request must include:

1. Explanation of why full remediation was not achieved within the original exception period.
2. Updated risk assessment reflecting any changes in the threat landscape or control environment.
3. Revised and re-confirmed remediation plan with updated milestones and target dates.
4. Confirmation that all compensating controls remain in place and effective.
5. Approval at an elevated authority level — [e.g., first extension: Department Head; second extension: CTO/CISO/CRO; third extension: Board Risk Committee].

| Extension | Approval Authority | Maximum Duration | Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| First Extension | [Department Head] | [e.g., 3 months] | [Extension request reference] |
| Second Extension | [CTO / CISO] | [e.g., 3 months] | [Extension request reference] |
| Third Extension (exceptional) | [Board Risk Committee] | [Subject to BRC decision] | [Extension request reference] |

### 14.3 Closure Criteria

*The exception record may only be formally closed when all of the following criteria are met:*

- [ ] All remediation milestones (M-001 through M-006) have been completed.
- [ ] Post-remediation vulnerability scan confirms the vulnerability is no longer present on all affected assets.
- [ ] Compensating controls have been reviewed and, where no longer required, decommissioned in a controlled manner.
- [ ] The asset register and vulnerability tracking system have been updated to reflect the remediated state.
- [ ] A closure report (Appendix D) has been prepared and approved by the Risk Owner.
