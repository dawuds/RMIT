## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section defines the purpose of the Risk Assessment Schedule and its role within the organization's broader technology risk governance framework. Describe why this document exists and what it enables.*

This Risk Assessment Schedule establishes the formal programme of planned and completed technology risk assessments undertaken by [Organization Name] in fulfilment of its obligations under Bank Negara Malaysia's (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, with particular reference to Clause 9.2 governing risk assessment requirements.

The schedule serves as the authoritative reference for:

- Planning and coordinating technology risk assessments across all in-scope domains
- Tracking the status, ownership, and outcomes of each assessment
- Demonstrating to regulators and internal auditors that a systematic, documented, and repeatable risk assessment process is in operation
- Supporting the Board and Senior Management in obtaining an accurate picture of the organization's technology risk posture
- Enabling timely identification, escalation, and treatment of technology risks that may impact service delivery, data integrity, customer trust, or regulatory compliance

### 1.2 Scope

*Define the organizational, systems, and geographic boundaries of this schedule. Be explicit about what is included and what is excluded.*

This schedule applies to all technology risk assessments conducted by or on behalf of [Organization Name], encompassing:

**In Scope:**

- All critical and non-critical IT systems, applications, and infrastructure components owned or operated by [Organization Name]
- Third-party and outsourced technology services where [Organization Name] retains accountability under BNM's outsourcing and RMiT requirements
- Cloud-hosted services, hybrid environments, and on-premises data centres
- Technology change initiatives, including major system upgrades, new product launches, and technology-enabled business process changes
- Cyber security assessments, vulnerability assessments, and penetration testing programmes
- Business continuity and disaster recovery risk assessments
- Data governance and data management risk assessments aligned with PDPA obligations
- Operational technology (OT) and internet of things (IoT) systems where applicable

**Out of Scope:**

- Non-technology operational risk assessments managed under the operational risk framework (unless these intersect with technology risk)
- Credit and market risk assessments
- [Any other explicitly excluded domains — list here]

**Geographic Coverage:** [Organization Name]'s operations within Malaysia, including all branches, subsidiaries, and affiliate entities that are subject to BNM supervision. [If applicable, note international entities that are included or excluded.]

### 2.2 RMiT Clause 9.2 Summary

*Provide a brief summary of what Clause 9.2 requires, to anchor the document in regulatory context.*

BNM RMiT Clause 9.2 requires financial institutions to establish and maintain a formal, structured risk assessment process to identify, assess, and manage technology risks. Key obligations under Clause 9.2 include:

- **Periodic risk assessments** conducted on a defined and documented schedule, covering all material technology systems and services
- **Event-driven risk assessments** triggered by significant changes to the technology environment, including new implementations, material upgrades, incidents, or changes in the threat landscape
- **Documented outputs** from each risk assessment, including risk ratings, risk treatment decisions, and accountable owners
- **Board and Senior Management reporting** on the aggregated technology risk profile derived from risk assessment outputs
- **Independent assurance** over the risk assessment process, including verification that assessments are conducted as planned and that findings are remediated within agreed timeframes

## 3. Objectives of the Risk Assessment Programme

### 3.1 Programme Objectives

*State the measurable objectives of the risk assessment programme. These should be SMART where possible and should be reviewed annually.*

The Technology Risk Assessment Programme of [Organization Name] is designed to achieve the following objectives:

**Objective 1 — Risk Identification:** Systematically identify all material technology risks across the organization's systems, processes, and third-party dependencies before they materialize as incidents or regulatory findings.

**Objective 2 — Risk Quantification:** Assess the likelihood and potential impact of identified technology risks using a consistent, documented methodology aligned with the organization's risk appetite as approved by the Board.

**Objective 3 — Regulatory Compliance:** Demonstrate to BNM, internal auditors, and the Board that risk assessments are conducted in accordance with RMiT Clause 9.2 and on a schedule that reflects the criticality and risk profile of assessed systems and services.

**Objective 4 — Risk Treatment:** Ensure that risk treatment actions — including risk acceptance, mitigation, transfer, or avoidance — are assigned to accountable owners with defined completion dates and are tracked to closure.

**Objective 5 — Continuous Improvement:** Use risk assessment findings and trends to drive improvements to technology controls, risk culture, and governance practices over time.

**Objective 6 — Board and Management Assurance:** Provide the Board Risk Committee and Senior Management with timely, accurate, and aggregated technology risk intelligence to support informed decision-making.

## 4. Scope of Risk Assessments

### 4.1 Assessment Categories

*Define the different types of risk assessments included in this schedule. Each category may have different scoping criteria, methodologies, and frequencies.*

The following categories of risk assessment are covered by this schedule:

#### 4.1.1 Periodic Technology Risk Assessments

Comprehensive risk assessments conducted on a defined recurring schedule, covering broad technology domains. Frequency is determined by the criticality and risk rating of the assessed system or domain.

| Criticality Tier | Assessment Frequency | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 — Critical Systems | Annually or upon material change | RMiT Clause 9.2; internal policy |
| Tier 2 — Important Systems | Every 18 months | Internal policy |
| Tier 3 — Supporting Systems | Every 2 years | Internal policy |

#### 4.1.2 Event-Driven Risk Assessments

Risk assessments triggered by specific events or changes, regardless of the periodic schedule. Trigger events include:

- New technology system implementations or major upgrades
- Significant changes to existing systems (architecture, infrastructure, access controls)
- Technology-related incidents (including near-misses) above defined severity thresholds
- New or materially changed outsourcing or cloud service arrangements
- Significant changes to the cyber threat landscape (e.g., emergence of new threat vectors relevant to the institution)
- Regulatory directives or supervisory feedback
- Material changes to business processes supported by technology
- Mergers, acquisitions, or significant organizational restructuring

#### 4.1.3 Cyber Security and Vulnerability Assessments

Specialized assessments focused on cyber security risks, including:

- Vulnerability assessments (internal and perimeter)
- Penetration testing (external and internal)
- Red team exercises (where applicable)
- Phishing simulation and social engineering assessments
- Dark web monitoring reviews

#### 4.1.4 Third-Party and Outsourcing Risk Assessments

Risk assessments covering technology services provided by third parties, including:

- Pre-engagement due diligence assessments for new technology vendors
- Periodic reviews of existing critical technology outsourcing arrangements
- Cloud service provider security assessments
- Assessments triggered by significant changes to vendor service terms or control environments

#### 4.1.5 Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Assessments

Risk assessments focused on the resilience of technology infrastructure, including:

- BCP/DR gap assessments
- Recovery Time Objective (RTO) and Recovery Point Objective (RPO) validation
- Crisis simulation and tabletop exercise reviews

## 7. Accountable Owners and Stakeholders

### 7.1 Assessment Ownership Framework

*Define how ownership is assigned for each risk assessment, and clarify the distinction between assessment ownership and system/domain ownership.*

Each risk assessment covered by this schedule has two primary ownership roles:

- **Assessment Owner:** The individual responsible for planning, executing, and reporting the risk assessment. The Assessment Owner is accountable for adherence to the assessment timeline, methodology, and quality of the assessment report.

- **System / Domain Owner:** The individual responsible for the system, process, or domain being assessed. The System / Domain Owner is accountable for providing access, documentation, and subject matter expertise during the assessment, and for accepting accountability for risk treatment actions arising from the assessment.

The **Head of Technology Risk** retains overall accountability for the integrity and completeness of this schedule and for escalating delays or overdue assessments to the appropriate governance forum.

### 13.2 Role Descriptions

*Provide a brief description of each key role in the context of this schedule.*

**Board Risk Committee**
Provides Board-level oversight of the technology risk management programme. Approves the technology risk appetite. Receives regular reporting on the technology risk profile and the status of the risk assessment programme. Accountable to shareholders and regulators for the adequacy of technology risk governance.

**Chief Risk Officer (CRO)**
Senior executive accountable for the enterprise risk management framework, within which technology risk sits. Receives and challenges aggregated technology risk reporting. Approves risk acceptances for Critical and High-rated risks arising from assessments.

**Chief Information Officer (CIO)**
Senior executive accountable for the IT function and technology strategy. Approves the annual Risk Assessment Schedule and exceptions thereto. Responsible for ensuring adequate resources are allocated to the risk assessment programme. Receives and acts on material findings from assessments.

**Head of Technology Risk**
Programme Owner of the Risk Assessment Schedule. Accountable for the integrity, completeness, and regulatory compliance of the risk assessment programme. Approves individual assessment plans and final reports. Escalates material findings and schedule deviations to the CIO, CRO, and Board Risk Committee as required.

**Assessment Owner**
Individual responsible for planning, executing, and reporting a specific risk assessment. May be a member of the Technology Risk team or a specialist (e.g., CISO for cyber assessments). Accountable for adherence to scope, methodology, timeline, and report quality.

**System / Domain Owner**
Individual accountable for the system, process, or domain being assessed. Responsible for providing access, documentation, and subject matter expertise during the assessment. Accountable for implementing agreed risk treatment actions arising from the assessment.

**Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)**
Assessment Owner for cyber security-focused assessments (penetration tests, vulnerability assessments). Subject matter expert for security control assessments across all assessment types. Maintains the vulnerability management programme.

**Internal Audit**
Provides independent assurance over the technology risk assessment programme, including reviewing whether assessments are conducted as scheduled, whether methodology is applied consistently, and whether findings are remediated within agreed timelines. Does not participate in assessments themselves to preserve independence.

**Data Protection Officer (DPO)**
Consulted on risk assessments involving the processing of personal data, to ensure PDPA implications are appropriately assessed and that data protection risks are captured in assessment outputs.

**Legal and Compliance**
Advises on regulatory requirements and implications arising from assessment findings. Supports regulatory reporting. Reviews assessment reports where legal or regulatory risks are identified.

### Appendix B — Assessment Report Template (Summary)

*Each completed risk assessment must produce a formal assessment report. The full report template is maintained separately at [Document Repository Path]. The summary structure below is provided for reference.*

A completed Technology Risk Assessment Report must contain at minimum the following sections:

1. **Executive Summary** — One-page summary of assessment scope, key findings, overall risk rating, and priority recommendations
2. **Assessment Background and Scope** — System/domain description; assessment objectives; methodology; period of assessment; assessment team
3. **Summary of Findings** — Risk register of all findings, rated by criticality; heat map visualization
4. **Detailed Findings** — One section per finding, containing: finding description; risk rating (inherent and residual); evidence; root cause analysis; management response; recommended risk treatment; agreed action; owner; due date
5. **Overall Risk Rating** — Aggregate risk rating for the assessed system/domain
6. **Risk Treatment Plan** — Summary of all agreed treatment actions, owners, and timelines
7. **Appendices** — Assessment documentation, interview records, evidence reviewed
