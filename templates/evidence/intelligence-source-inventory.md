## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This document serves as the authoritative inventory of all threat intelligence sources utilised by [Organization Name]'s Threat Intelligence Programme. It provides a structured record of each intelligence source, including its classification, ownership, operational status, and lifecycle stage, enabling governance, oversight, and audit traceability.

This inventory supports compliance with **Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)**, specifically **Clause 11.16**, which requires financial institutions to maintain a robust and documented threat intelligence capability, including the identification and management of intelligence sources that inform the institution's cyber threat detection and response activities.

### 1.2 Scope

This inventory applies to:

- All external threat intelligence feeds subscribed to or consumed by [Organization Name]
- All internal intelligence sources generated by operational security functions (e.g., SIEM, SOAR, EDR telemetry)
- Open-source intelligence (OSINT) sources actively monitored by the Threat Intelligence team
- Industry and sector-specific sharing communities and platforms (e.g., ISACs, FS-ISAC, CyberSecurity Malaysia feeds)
- Commercial threat intelligence platform subscriptions

This inventory covers sources used across all business units and technology environments within the scope of [Organization Name]'s Information Security Management System (ISMS).

## 3. Classification and Categorisation

*Each intelligence source must be classified according to the taxonomies below to enable prioritisation, access control, and appropriate handling procedures. Classification must be reviewed whenever a source's characteristics change materially.*

### 3.1 Source Type Taxonomy

| Type Code | Source Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **COM** | Commercial Feed | Paid subscription threat intelligence services (e.g., Recorded Future, CrowdStrike Falcon Intelligence, Mandiant) |
| **GOV** | Government / Regulatory | Advisories and feeds from national cybersecurity agencies (e.g., CyberSecurity Malaysia, BNM, NACSA, CISA, NCSC) |
| **ISAC** | Sector Sharing / ISAC | Intelligence shared within industry consortiums (e.g., FS-ISAC, Financial Services ISAC Malaysia) |
| **OSINT** | Open-Source Intelligence | Publicly available threat intelligence (e.g., AlienVault OTX, abuse.ch, Shodan, threat blogs) |
| **INT** | Internal Telemetry | Intelligence derived from [Organization Name]'s own security tooling (SIEM, EDR, SOAR, honeypots) |
| **PART** | Partner / Peer Sharing | Intelligence exchanged bilaterally with trusted peer organisations or technology partners |
| **ACAD** | Academic / Research | Threat research from academic institutions or independent security researchers |

### 3.2 Intelligence Category (by Threat Intelligence Type)

| Category Code | Category | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **STRAT** | Strategic | High-level threat landscape reporting; geopolitical, sector-wide trends |
| **TACT** | Tactical | TTPs (Tactics, Techniques, Procedures); threat actor profiles; MITRE ATT&CK mappings |
| **OPER** | Operational | Campaign-level intelligence; planned or active threat actor activity |
| **TECH** | Technical | Indicators of Compromise (IoCs): IPs, domains, hashes, URLs, CVEs |

## 6. Last Review Date

*Each intelligence source must be reviewed at least quarterly, or following a significant event (e.g., a major incident, a change in the threat landscape, a supplier notification, or a failed quality check). This section records the review history for each source and drives the review schedule.*

### 6.1 Review Triggers

Reviews of this inventory and individual source entries must be initiated upon:

- **Scheduled quarterly review** — calendar-driven, at least four times per year
- **Per-event trigger** — following a cybersecurity incident where the source's intelligence was relevant or absent
- **Supplier notification** — upon receipt of a material change notice from a commercial provider
- **Quality failure** — if a source is found to be consistently inaccurate, stale, or non-functional
- **Organisational change** — when the Source Owner or Custodian changes role or departs
- **New threat actor or campaign** — when a significant new threat is identified that warrants assessment of existing source coverage
- **Regulatory update** — upon issuance of new or revised BNM circulars, NACSA advisories, or relevant PDPA guidance

### Appendix D: Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **BNM** | Bank Negara Malaysia — the Central Bank of Malaysia |
| **RMiT** | Risk Management in Technology — BNM policy document governing technology risk in financial institutions |
| **Threat Intelligence** | Evidence-based knowledge about existing or emerging threats to information assets, used to inform security decisions |
| **IoC** | Indicator of Compromise — artefact observed on a network or in an operating system that indicates a computer intrusion |
| **ISAC** | Information Sharing and Analysis Centre — sector-specific organisation facilitating cyber threat intelligence sharing |
| **MISP** | Malware Information Sharing Platform — open-source threat intelligence platform |
| **OSINT** | Open-Source Intelligence — intelligence derived from publicly available sources |
| **SIEM** | Security Information and Event Management — platform aggregating security telemetry for monitoring and detection |
| **SOAR** | Security Orchestration, Automation and Response — platform automating security workflows and incident response |
| **STIX** | Structured Threat Information Expression — standardised language for representing cyber threat intelligence |
| **TAXII** | Trusted Automated Exchange of Intelligence Information — transport protocol for sharing cyber threat intelligence |
| **TLP** | Traffic Light Protocol — standardised system for classifying the sensitivity and shareability of security information |
| **TTP** | Tactics, Techniques and Procedures — the behaviour of a threat actor, as catalogued in frameworks such as MITRE ATT&CK |
| **NACSA** | National Cyber Security Agency — Malaysia's national cybersecurity authority |
