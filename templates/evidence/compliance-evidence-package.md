### 2.3 Compliance Rating Criteria

*Define the rating scale applied uniformly across all assessed clauses. This must be agreed upon and documented before the assessment commences.*

| Rating | Label | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Compliant | All controls are fully implemented, operating effectively, and substantiated by sufficient evidence. No gaps identified. |
| **2** | Substantially Compliant | Controls are largely in place with minor gaps. Remediation plans exist and timelines are within acceptable thresholds. |
| **3** | Partially Compliant | Controls are partially implemented or inconsistently applied. Material gaps identified with remediation in progress. |
| **4** | Non-Compliant | Controls are absent or ineffective. Significant gaps with no credible remediation plan in place. |

### 2.4 Evidence Sufficiency Criteria

*Describe the minimum standards that evidence must meet to be considered sufficient for substantiating a compliance rating.*

- Evidence must be **contemporaneous** — produced within the assessment period unless serving as baseline documentation.
- Evidence must be **attributable** — clearly linked to a named system, process owner, or organizational unit.
- Evidence must be **verifiable** — capable of independent verification by auditors or regulatory examiners.
- Evidence must be **complete** — sufficient to demonstrate the full operating effectiveness of the control, not merely its design.
