## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This document establishes and maintains official records of professional certifications, qualifications, and competency profiles for all IT auditors engaged in technology audit activities at [Organization Name]. It serves as authoritative evidence that personnel conducting IT audits hold the requisite qualifications commensurate with their assigned responsibilities.

This record supports [Organization Name]'s compliance with **Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically **Clause 13.3**, which mandates that internal audit functions possess adequate technical expertise and professional competency to independently assess technology risks and controls.

### 1.2 Scope

This document covers:

- All internal IT auditors employed by [Organization Name]
- External IT auditors and co-sourced audit specialists engaged under formal agreements
- Guest auditors, secondees, and regulatory examination support personnel
- IT audit management and oversight personnel responsible for sign-off on technology audit findings

**Out of Scope:** General (non-IT) audit staff who do not perform technology audit activities, and vendor assessors operating under separate third-party risk management frameworks.

### 1.3 Regulatory Context

| Regulation | Clause / Section | Requirement Summary |
|---|---|---|
| BNM RMiT Policy Document | 13.3 | Internal audit must have sufficient IT expertise; auditors must be suitably qualified and independent |
| BNM RMiT Policy Document | 13.1 – 13.2 | Audit function governance and independence requirements |
| BNM Guidelines on Internal Audit Function | General | Competency and continuing professional development obligations |

> *Note: This record is system-generated and automatically updated upon certification registration, renewal, or expiry events. Manual amendments require approval from the Head of Internal Audit.*

## 7. Finding Severity Classification Authority

*This section documents the authority and competency of IT auditors to classify audit findings by severity. Only auditors with confirmed qualifications and documented training in [Organization Name]'s finding classification framework are authorised to assign severity ratings to IT audit findings.*

### 7.1 Finding Severity Classification Framework

*[Organization Name] applies the following severity classification scale to all IT audit findings. This scale is aligned with BNM RMiT materiality thresholds and the IIA IPPF.*

| Severity Level | Label | Definition | Regulatory Materiality | Escalation Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1 – Critical** | Critical | A control failure that exposes the organization to immediate, severe regulatory, financial, or reputational risk; systemic breakdown | High (BNM reportable if applicable) | Board Audit Committee within 5 business days |
| **2 – High** | High | A significant control weakness with potential for material loss, regulatory breach, or material misstatement if not remediated | Medium-High | Audit Committee at next scheduled meeting |
| **3 – Medium** | Moderate | A control deficiency that, if exploited, could result in moderate impact; compensating controls partially mitigate | Medium | Head of Internal Audit; included in audit report |
| **4 – Low** | Low | A minor control gap with limited risk exposure; best-practice improvement recommended | Low | Included in audit report |
| **5 – Informational** | Advisory | Observation requiring no remediation action; noted for management awareness only | Nil | Included as advisory note in audit report |

### 7.2 Classification Authority Register

*Only the following designated individuals are authorised to assign or change a finding severity rating. Classification decisions must be documented in the audit management system audit trail.*

## 9. Review and Approval

### 9.1 Review Trigger Events

This document and its underlying records must be reviewed and updated upon the occurrence of any of the following events, in addition to the standard quarterly review cycle:

- A new IT auditor joins the function (onboarding)
- An existing auditor obtains a new professional certification
- An existing certification expires, lapses, or is revoked
- An auditor's role, responsibility, or audit assignment changes
- The approved risk-based audit plan is revised, creating new competency requirements
- A regulatory change affects required audit standards or qualifications
- An audit quality review or external quality assessment identifies competency-related observations
