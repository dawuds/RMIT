## 3. Assessment Scope and Criteria

*This section documents the scope of any periodic assessment or review conducted to evaluate the adequacy and effectiveness of existing threat sharing arrangements. Authors should define the criteria used to determine which arrangements are required and how their value is measured.*

### 3.1 Assessment Objectives

The assessment of threat sharing arrangements is conducted to determine whether:

- [ ] [Organization Name] maintains active and documented memberships in relevant threat sharing communities
- [ ] Intelligence received through these channels is being actioned and integrated into security operations
- [ ] Obligations under each agreement (e.g., reciprocal sharing, confidentiality, incident reporting timelines) are being fulfilled
- [ ] Agreements remain current, legally valid, and aligned with the organization's risk profile
- [ ] Data exchanged under each arrangement is handled in accordance with PDPA and internal data governance policies

## 4. Methodology

*This section describes the process and methods used to review and maintain threat sharing arrangements. Authors should document how assessments are conducted, what sources of information are used, and how findings are validated.*

### 4.1 Review Methodology Overview

The review of threat sharing arrangements follows a structured four-phase approach:

**Phase 1 – Inventory and Documentation Review**
- Compile a current register of all active and expired agreements and memberships
- Verify agreement validity, renewal dates, and signatory authority
- Review any amendments or addenda issued since the last assessment

**Phase 2 – Operational Effectiveness Assessment**
- Interview relevant stakeholders (SOC Manager, CISO, Threat Intelligence team)
- Review logs and records of threat intelligence received and actioned within the assessment period
- Assess whether intelligence inputs have influenced security controls, patching cycles, or incident response activities

**Phase 3 – Obligation Compliance Review**
- Confirm that reciprocal sharing obligations have been met within required timeframes
- Verify that submitted intelligence has been appropriately classified and sanitised before sharing
- Check compliance with confidentiality and data protection obligations in each agreement

**Phase 4 – Gap Analysis and Recommendations**
- Compare current arrangements against peer industry practices and BNM guidance
- Identify gaps in coverage (e.g., sectors, threat types, or geographies not covered)
- Produce recommendations for new memberships, agreement renewals, or process improvements
