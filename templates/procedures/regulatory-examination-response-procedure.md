## 1. Purpose

*Describe the overarching purpose of this procedure and the problem it addresses. Reference the specific regulatory obligation that necessitates its existence.*

This procedure establishes a structured, repeatable, and auditable process by which [Organization Name] responds to examination findings issued by Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) in connection with the Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document. It provides clear guidance to all relevant personnel on their obligations, actions, timelines, and communication requirements from the point of finding receipt through to formal remediation closure and regulatory submission.

This procedure ensures that [Organization Name]:

- Responds to BNM examination findings in a timely, accurate, and professional manner consistent with regulatory expectations;
- Meets all mandatory timeframes specified under BNM RMiT and related policy communications;
- Maintains a defensible audit trail of all actions taken in response to examination outcomes;
- Embeds continuous improvement into the regulatory response lifecycle to reduce repeat findings.

## 2. Scope and Applicability

*Define who and what this procedure applies to. Be explicit about business units, systems, and any exclusions.*

### 2.1 In-Scope Entities and Functions

This procedure applies to all of the following within [Organization Name]:

| Dimension | Scope |
|---|---|
| **Legal Entities** | [Organization Name] and its Malaysian-licensed subsidiaries subject to BNM oversight |
| **Business Functions** | Technology, Operations, Risk Management, Compliance, Internal Audit, Executive Management |
| **Examination Types** | On-site examinations, off-site reviews, thematic assessments, targeted inspections, and follow-up reviews conducted by BNM |
| **Regulatory Frameworks** | BNM RMiT Policy Document (Clause 16.6), and any BNM examination instructions issued under authority of the Financial Services Act 2013 (FSA) or Islamic Financial Services Act 2013 (IFSA) |
| **Personnel** | All employees, contractors, and third-party service providers engaged in examination response activities |

### 2.2 Out-of-Scope

The following are outside the scope of this procedure:

- Responses to examination findings issued by regulators other than BNM (e.g., Securities Commission Malaysia, LOFSA). Separate procedures govern those interactions.
- Internal audit findings that do not arise from or relate to BNM examination activities.
- Routine regulatory reporting and submissions (e.g., periodic statistical returns) not connected to examination findings.

### 2.3 Applicability Conditions

This procedure is triggered upon receipt of any of the following from BNM:

- A formal Examination Report or Letter of Findings;
- A BNM directive or supervisory letter referencing specific technology risk deficiencies;
- A BNM LINK notification identifying compliance gaps arising from an examination;
- Any informal communication from BNM examiners that identifies areas requiring remediation or management attention.

## 5. Examination Response Process Flow

*This section constitutes the core procedural guidance. Each sub-section addresses a distinct phase of the examination response lifecycle. Process owners must ensure each phase is executed in sequence and that all required outputs are documented and retained.*

### 5.1 Pre-Examination Preparation

*Describe the preparatory activities that should be completed before an examination commences to position [Organization Name] for an effective response.*

Pre-examination preparation is an ongoing responsibility of the Head of Regulatory Affairs and is not limited to periods when an examination has been announced. The following standing activities apply:

#### 5.1.1 Examination Readiness Register

[Organization Name] shall maintain an **Examination Readiness Register** that captures:

- Status of all outstanding findings from prior BNM examinations;
- Current state of all key technology risk controls that are subject to RMiT requirements;
- Availability and currency of all documentary evidence that BNM examiners are likely to request;
- Ownership and readiness of all subject-matter experts (SMEs) who may be called upon during examination.

*[Insert link or reference to the Examination Readiness Register template — Appendix A]*

#### 5.1.2 Upon Receipt of Examination Notice

Upon receiving formal notice of an upcoming BNM examination, the Head of Regulatory Affairs shall, within **two (2) business days**:

- [ ] Notify the CEO, CRO, CTO, and CISO of the upcoming examination
- [ ] Confirm the examination scope, dates, and BNM examination team contacts
- [ ] Designate the internal Point of Contact (PoC) for the examination
- [ ] Convene a pre-examination briefing with all relevant business and technology units
- [ ] Conduct a pre-examination readiness check against the Examination Readiness Register
- [ ] Ensure a secure document repository is established for examination-related materials
- [ ] Brief all personnel likely to interact with BNM examiners on protocols (see Section 5.1.3)

#### 5.1.3 Personnel Briefing Protocol

All personnel who may interact with BNM examiners shall be briefed on the following:

| Protocol | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Accuracy | Responses to examiner queries must be factually accurate. Personnel must not speculate or provide information outside their area of responsibility. |
| Escalation | Any examiner request for information outside pre-approved scope must be escalated to the PoC immediately. |
| Documentation | All materials provided to examiners must be logged in the examination document tracking register. |
| Approval | No document, data extract, or system access shall be provided to examiners without prior approval from the PoC or Head of Regulatory Affairs. |
| Communication | All written communications with BNM examiners must be routed through or copied to the Head of Regulatory Affairs. |

### 5.2 Examination Conduct Phase

*Describe the management of day-to-day examination activities while BNM examiners are on-site or actively engaged in an off-site review.*

During the active examination period, the following activities shall be managed by the designated PoC:

#### 5.2.1 Daily Examination Management

| Activity | Frequency | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| Internal examination status update to Head of Regulatory Affairs | Daily | PoC |
| Review and approval of documents/data to be provided to examiners | As requested | Head of Regulatory Affairs |
| Logging of all examiner requests and [Organization Name] responses | Continuous | PoC / Regulatory Affairs Team |
| Escalation of examiner observations that suggest potential findings | As arising | PoC |
| Weekly summary to EXCO on examination status | Weekly | Head of Regulatory Affairs |

#### 5.2.2 Preliminary Findings Management

Where BNM examiners provide **preliminary observations or draft findings** during the examination:

1. The PoC shall record each preliminary finding in the Examination Findings Tracker *(Appendix B)*.
2. The Head of Regulatory Affairs shall convene an internal review session within **one (1) business day** of receiving a preliminary finding.
3. Subject-matter experts shall prepare factual clarifications or supplementary evidence for any finding that [Organization Name] believes to be based on incomplete information.
4. Where a preliminary finding is contested, the Head of Regulatory Affairs shall raise the clarification with BNM through the designated examination PoC, in writing.

### 5.3 Findings Receipt and Initial Assessment

*Detail the activities triggered upon receipt of the formal Letter of Findings or Examination Report from BNM.*

#### 5.3.1 Findings Receipt

Upon receipt of the formal **Letter of Findings (LoF)** or Examination Report from BNM:

| Step | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Acknowledge receipt of the LoF to BNM (if required) | Per BNM instruction or within 2 business days | Head of Regulatory Affairs |
| 2 | Register the LoF in the Regulatory Engagement Register | Same day as receipt | Regulatory Affairs Team |
| 3 | Distribute the LoF to the CEO, CRO, CTO, and CISO | Same day as receipt | Head of Regulatory Affairs |
| 4 | Parse and log each individual finding in the Examination Findings Tracker | Within 1 business day | Regulatory Affairs Team |
| 5 | Assign a Finding Reference Number to each finding | Within 1 business day | Regulatory Affairs Team |
| 6 | Determine the response deadline for each finding | Within 1 business day | Head of Regulatory Affairs |

#### 5.3.2 Initial Severity Assessment

Upon logging each finding, the Head of Regulatory Affairs, in consultation with the CRO and CISO, shall assign an initial severity rating to each finding using the following classification:

| Severity | Definition | Initial Response Window |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Finding indicates a fundamental control failure or poses immediate risk to data, operations, or regulatory standing. BNM may have indicated supervisory concern. | Escalation to Board within 1 business day; RAP due within timeframe specified by BNM |
| **High** | Finding identifies a significant gap that requires prompt remediation. Likely to be a repeat finding or a finding with customer/systemic impact. | Escalation to EXCO within 2 business days; RAP due within 14 calendar days unless BNM specifies otherwise |
| **Medium** | Finding identifies a control weakness that requires remediation but does not present an immediate risk to operations or compliance standing. | Escalation to relevant business owner within 5 business days; RAP due within 30 calendar days unless BNM specifies otherwise |
| **Low** | Finding identifies a minor deficiency or opportunity for improvement. | Managed within normal compliance monitoring cycle; noted in next governance report |

### 5.4 Internal Escalation and Notification

*Detail the internal escalation chain that must be activated following receipt and severity assessment of examination findings.*

#### 5.4.1 Escalation Matrix

The following escalation steps are mandatory and must be executed within the timeframes specified:

| Finding Severity | Escalation Recipient | Escalation Method | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Critical | CEO, CRO, CTO, CISO, Board Risk Committee Chair | Direct verbal notification followed by written briefing note | Within 4 business hours of severity determination |
| High | CEO, CRO, CTO, CISO | Written briefing note via secure internal communication | Within 1 business day of severity determination |
| Medium | CRO, CTO, CISO, Head of relevant Business Unit | Written briefing note | Within 2 business days of severity determination |

#### 5.4.2 Board and Senior Management Reporting

| Forum | Reporting Trigger | Reporting Content | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Board Risk Committee | Any Critical finding; three or more High findings in a single examination | Executive summary of findings, severity assessment, proposed response approach, resource implications | Within the next scheduled meeting, or extraordinary session within 10 business days for Critical findings |
| Executive Committee (EXCO) | All High and Critical findings | Finding summary, root cause analysis, proposed RAP outline, ownership assignments | Within 5 business days of LoF receipt |

All internal escalation communications shall include, at minimum:

- Summary of each finding (one paragraph per finding)
- Assigned severity rating and rationale
- Preliminary root cause assessment
- Proposed response approach and target completion date
- Resource implications (budget, personnel, technology)
- Regulatory response deadline

### 5.5 Remediation Planning

*Describe the process for developing the Remediation Action Plan (RAP) that will form the basis of [Organization Name]'s formal response to BNM.*

#### 5.5.1 RAP Development Process

For each examination finding, a **Remediation Action Plan (RAP)** shall be developed as follows:

**Step 1 — Root Cause Analysis**

Prior to developing remediation actions, a structured root cause analysis shall be conducted by the relevant business and technology owners. The analysis shall:

- Identify the immediate cause of the finding;
- Identify the underlying systemic or process causes;
- Determine whether the finding is isolated or symptomatic of broader control weaknesses;
- Assess whether the finding constitutes a repeat of a prior BNM examination finding.

**Step 2 — Remediation Action Identification**

Based on the root cause analysis, remediation actions shall be identified that address:

- The immediate deficiency identified in the finding (corrective action);
- The underlying root cause (preventive action);
- Any related weaknesses identified during the root cause analysis (systemic improvement, where applicable).

**Step 3 — RAP Documentation**

Each finding's RAP shall be documented using the RAP template *(Appendix C)*, which captures:

| RAP Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Finding Reference Number | As assigned in the Examination Findings Tracker |
| BNM Finding Description | Verbatim from the LoF |
| Root Cause | Summary of root cause analysis outcome |
| Corrective Actions | Specific actions to be taken, with milestone dates |
| Preventive Actions | Actions to prevent recurrence |
| Accountable Owner | The individual accountable for RAP delivery |
| Target Completion Date | Realistic date for full remediation |
| Success Criteria | Measurable criteria by which remediation will be assessed as complete |
| Evidence of Completion | Documents or artefacts that will demonstrate remediation |
| Status | [Not Started / In Progress / Completed / Verified] |

**Step 4 — RAP Review and Approval**

| Review Level | Reviewer | Approval Required Before |
|---|---|---|
| Technical review | CTO / CISO (for technology findings) | Submission to Head of Regulatory Affairs |
| Risk assessment | CRO | Submission to Head of Regulatory Affairs |
| Regulatory alignment | Head of Regulatory Affairs | Submission for executive approval |
| Final approval | CEO or designated authority | Formal submission to BNM |

### 5.6 Formal Response Submission

*Describe the preparation and quality assurance process for the formal written response to BNM prior to submission.*

#### 5.6.1 Response Document Structure

The formal written response to BNM shall be structured as follows:

1. **Covering Letter** — Signed by the CEO (or authorised signatory), addressed to the relevant BNM examination team, acknowledging findings and confirming [Organization Name]'s commitment to remediation.
2. **Management Response Matrix** — A structured table cross-referencing each BNM finding with [Organization Name]'s acknowledgement, root cause summary, and RAP reference.
3. **Remediation Action Plans** — Individual RAPs for each finding (see Section 5.5).
4. **Supporting Evidence** — Where [Organization Name] has already completed corrective actions prior to formal submission, documentary evidence shall be included as annexures.
5. **Attestation** — A formal attestation signed by the CEO (and Board Chair for Critical findings) confirming the accuracy and completeness of the response.

#### 5.6.2 Pre-Submission Quality Assurance Checklist

Before submission, the Head of Regulatory Affairs shall verify the following:

- [ ] All findings from the LoF have been individually addressed in the response
- [ ] Each RAP has been reviewed and approved by the relevant executive owner
- [ ] Target completion dates are realistic and supported by resource commitments
- [ ] Supporting evidence is clearly referenced and annexed
- [ ] The response is factually accurate and consistent with information provided during the examination
- [ ] The covering letter is signed by the appropriate authorised signatory
- [ ] The response is being submitted via the correct channel (BNM LINK or as directed in the LoF)
- [ ] The submission is being made before the BNM-specified response deadline
- [ ] A complete copy of the submission has been retained in the Regulatory Document Repository

3. The Head of Regulatory Affairs shall contact BNM directly (via the examination team contact or BNM LINK helpdesk) to advise of the technical difficulty and request guidance on alternative submission.
4. If BNM confirms an alternative submission method (e.g., encrypted email), follow BNM's instruction and retain records of all communications.
5. Document the incident, the steps taken, and the resolution in the Regulatory Engagement Register.

### 5.8 Post-Submission Monitoring and Follow-Up

*Describe the ongoing monitoring obligations after the initial response has been submitted, including progress reporting to BNM and internal governance.*

#### 5.8.1 Remediation Progress Tracking

Following submission of the formal response, the Head of Regulatory Affairs shall:

- Maintain a current status record for each RAP in the Examination Findings Tracker;
- Obtain monthly status updates from each RAP owner;
- Escalate any RAP that is at risk of missing its target completion date to the CRO and the relevant executive owner within **five (5) business days** of the delay being identified.

#### 5.8.2 BNM Progress Reporting

Where BNM has requested periodic progress updates on examination finding remediation:

| Reporting Requirement | Frequency | Format | Submission Channel |
|---|---|---|---|
| Remediation progress update | [As specified by BNM in LoF] | [BNM-specified format or RAP status table] | BNM LINK |
| Interim evidence of completion | Upon milestone completion | Supporting documents | BNM LINK |
| Final remediation confirmation | Upon closure of all findings | Attestation letter + evidence package | BNM LINK |

#### 5.8.3 Managing BNM Queries Post-Submission

BNM may issue clarifying questions or requests for additional information following review of the submitted response. Upon receipt of such a query:

1. Log the query in the Regulatory Engagement Register with date received.
2. Determine the BNM-specified response timeline (or, if not specified, default to five (5) business days).
3. Assign the query to the relevant SME for factual response preparation.
4. Review and approve the draft response through the Head of Regulatory Affairs before submission.
5. Submit via BNM LINK and retain confirmation.

### 5.9 Closure and Lessons Learned

*Describe the activities required to formally close each examination finding and to embed institutional learning.*

#### 5.9.1 Finding Closure Criteria

A finding shall be considered **closed** only when all of the following conditions are met:

- [ ] All remediation actions identified in the RAP have been completed
- [ ] Evidence of completion has been compiled and quality-checked
- [ ] The evidence package has been submitted to BNM via BNM LINK
- [ ] BNM has confirmed in writing (via BNM LINK or formal letter) that the finding is accepted as closed, **or** the applicable regulatory timeframe for BNM confirmation has elapsed without BNM raising further concerns

*Note: Internal completion of remediation actions does not constitute regulatory closure. BNM confirmation of closure must be obtained and retained.*

#### 5.9.2 Lessons Learned Review

Within **30 calendar days** of the closure of all findings from an examination, the Head of Regulatory Affairs shall convene a **Post-Examination Review** with the CRO, CTO, CISO, and relevant business unit heads. The review shall cover:

| Review Topic | Questions to Address |
|---|---|
| Root Cause Patterns | Were there common root causes across multiple findings? Do they indicate systemic weaknesses? |
| Repeat Findings | Were any findings repeats of prior examination issues? Why did prior remediation not prevent recurrence? |
| Process Effectiveness | Did the examination response process work as intended? What caused delays or complications? |
| Regulatory Relationship | Was the engagement with BNM examiners professional and effective? Were there communication challenges? |
| Control Environment | Do the findings indicate gaps in the broader technology risk control framework that warrant proactive attention? |
| Procedure Updates | Does this procedure require updating based on lessons learned? |

The outcomes of the Post-Examination Review shall be:

- Documented in a **Post-Examination Review Report** *(Appendix D template)*;
- Presented to the Board Risk Committee at the next scheduled meeting;
- Used to update the Examination Readiness Register and, where applicable, this procedure.

## 6. Notification Trigger Criteria

*Specify the precise conditions that trigger mandatory internal and external notification obligations in connection with examination findings.*

### 6.1 Internal Notification Triggers

The following events trigger mandatory internal notifications under this procedure:

| Trigger Event | Mandatory Notification | Notification Method |
|---|---|---|
| Receipt of BNM Letter of Findings or Examination Report | CEO, CRO, CTO, CISO, Head of Regulatory Affairs | Immediate verbal + written briefing note |
| Any finding classified as Critical severity | Board Risk Committee Chair, CEO, CRO, CTO, CISO | Immediate verbal escalation + written briefing note within 4 business hours |
| Any finding classified as High severity | CEO, CRO, CTO, CISO | Written briefing note within 1 business day |
| Identification of a Repeat Finding | CEO, CRO, Internal Audit | Written notification within 1 business day of classification |
| RAP milestone at risk of being missed | CRO, CEO, relevant Business Unit Head | Written escalation within 5 business days of delay identification |
| BNM query received post-submission | Head of Regulatory Affairs, CRO | Written notification same day as receipt |
| BNM finding formally closed | CRO, relevant Business Unit Head, Internal Audit | Written notification within 2 business days of BNM confirmation |

### 6.2 External Notification Triggers

*[Organization Name] shall assess whether any examination finding triggers mandatory external reporting obligations. This assessment shall be conducted by the Head of Regulatory Affairs in consultation with Legal Counsel and the CRO.*

Potential external notification triggers include:

| Scenario | Potential External Obligation | Assessment Required |
|---|---|---|
| Finding indicates a material technology incident occurred | BNM technology incident reporting under RMiT Clause 11 | Assess whether the underlying incident met reporting thresholds at the time; if not already reported, assess current obligation |
| Finding indicates a data breach or personal data compromise | PDPA notification obligations (where applicable); BNM notification | Legal and Compliance assessment required within 24 hours |
| Finding discloses a material control failure | Assessment of obligation to report to relevant Board committee per governance framework | Head of Regulatory Affairs + Legal Counsel |
| [Organization Name] is a member of a financial group | Group holding company or parent notification obligations | Head of Regulatory Affairs to notify Group Compliance |

## 7. Notification and Response Timeline Requirements

*Summarise all mandatory timelines governing the examination response process. This section serves as a quick-reference timeline guide for all personnel.*

### 7.1 Mandatory Response Timelines

*The default timelines below apply unless BNM specifies alternative timelines in the Letter of Findings. BNM-specified timelines always take precedence.*

| Activity | Default Timeline | BNM-Specified Override |
|---|---|---|
| Acknowledgement of LoF receipt to BNM | 2 business days from receipt | [As specified in LoF] |
| Internal distribution of LoF | Same business day as receipt | N/A |
| Initial severity assessment of all findings | 1 business day from LoF receipt | N/A |
| Escalation of Critical findings to Board | 4 business hours from severity determination | N/A |

### 7.2 Timeline Escalation Triggers

Where any of the following conditions arise, the Head of Regulatory Affairs shall immediately escalate to the CEO and CRO:

- The formal BNM response deadline cannot be met due to internal delays in RAP preparation or approval;
- A BNM-specified progress reporting deadline is at risk;
- BNM has issued a reminder or follow-up on an overdue response;
- Internal resource constraints are preventing timely remediation of Critical or High findings.

In such circumstances, the Head of Regulatory Affairs shall proactively contact BNM in writing to advise of the situation, request any available deadline extension, and propose a revised timeline. This communication shall be submitted via BNM LINK and must be approved by the CEO before transmission.

## 11. Exceptions and Escalation

*Describe the process for managing exceptions to this procedure and how conflicts or unforeseen circumstances should be escalated.*

### 11.1 Exception Management

Where circumstances prevent strict adherence to any requirement of this procedure:

1. The exception shall be documented in writing by the Head of Regulatory Affairs, specifying the nature of the exception, the reason it is necessary, and the alternative approach being adopted.
2. The exception shall be approved by the CRO before alternative action is taken.
3. Approved exceptions shall be recorded in the Exceptions Register maintained by the Regulatory Affairs team.
4. Exceptions shall be reviewed at the next relevant governance forum (minimum quarterly at Board RC level).
5. Where an exception affects [Organization Name]'s ability to meet a BNM regulatory deadline, it shall be escalated to the CEO immediately and BNM shall be notified as described in Section 7.2.

### 11.2 Conflict Resolution

Where there is a conflict between this procedure and:

- **BNM instructions** — BNM instructions always take precedence. This procedure shall be updated at the next available opportunity to reflect BNM requirements.
- **Internal policies** — The Head of Regulatory Affairs shall escalate to the CRO and Legal Counsel for determination.
- **Other procedures** — The CRO shall be the deciding authority on which procedure takes precedence.

### Appendix F — Glossary of BNM Examination Terminology

*This appendix provides additional context on terminology specific to BNM examination communications to assist personnel unfamiliar with regulatory examination language.*

| Term | BNM Usage Context |
|---|---|
| **Supervisory Concern** | Indicates BNM has elevated concern about the finding; typically associated with Critical or High severity findings requiring priority attention |
| **Management Response Required** | BNM requires a formal written response from senior management acknowledging the finding and committing to remediation |
| **Corrective Action Plan** | BNM's terminology for what this procedure refers to as a Remediation Action Plan (RAP) |
| **Satisfactory Progress** | BNM confirmation language indicating that interim remediation progress is acceptable; does not constitute finding closure |
| **Finding Closed** | Formal BNM confirmation that a finding has been remediated to BNM's satisfaction |
| **Thematic Examination** | A BNM examination focused on a specific risk theme (e.g., cyber resilience) across multiple financial institutions, rather than a full-scope examination of [Organization Name] specifically |
| **Targeted Review** | A focused BNM examination of a specific control area or business function, typically following a prior examination finding or a reported incident |
| **Follow-Up Examination** | A BNM examination conducted to verify the adequacy of remediation undertaken in response to prior examination findings |
