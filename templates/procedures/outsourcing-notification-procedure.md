## 1. Purpose

*Describe the intent of this procedure, the problem it solves, and why it exists within the organisation's compliance framework.*

This procedure establishes the formal requirements, steps, and accountabilities for [Organization Name] when notifying Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) of material technology outsourcing arrangements, as required under paragraph 16.3 of BNM's Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) policy document. It ensures that all material outsourcing notifications are submitted accurately, completely, and within prescribed timelines to maintain regulatory compliance and safeguard the integrity of [Organization Name]'s technology and operational risk management framework.

This procedure supports the broader objectives of:

- Ensuring regulatory compliance with BNM RMiT and all associated supervisory expectations;
- Maintaining full visibility of the organisation's third-party technology dependencies;
- Enabling timely escalation and governance over outsourcing risk;
- Establishing a defensible audit trail for all outsourcing notification activities.

## 2. Scope and Applicability

*Define the boundaries of this procedure — which systems, functions, entities, and personnel it applies to. Be explicit about what is in scope and what is excluded.*

### 2.1 In Scope

This procedure applies to:

- All **material technology outsourcing arrangements** entered into, materially varied, renewed, or terminated by [Organization Name] and its subsidiaries operating under BNM's supervisory jurisdiction;
- All **outsourcing of critical or important functions** where the service involves technology systems, data processing, software-as-a-service (SaaS), cloud infrastructure, managed security services, or IT operations;
- **New arrangements, material amendments, and terminations** of existing outsourcing contracts that meet the materiality criteria defined in Section 5 of this procedure;
- All **business units, departments, and subsidiary entities** of [Organization Name] that engage or intend to engage third-party technology service providers.

### 2.2 Out of Scope

The following are **not** covered by this procedure:

- Procurement of off-the-shelf software licences that do not involve hosted data processing or operational dependencies;
- Intra-group arrangements where the service provider is a wholly owned entity within [Organization Name]'s group structure and subject to equivalent governance controls, unless otherwise determined by BNM;
- Non-technology outsourcing arrangements (e.g., facilities management, catering) unless such arrangements involve access to technology systems or regulated data;
- Engagements with consultants or contractors performing discrete advisory functions without ongoing system access or data hosting responsibilities.

### 2.3 Applicability

| Licensed insurer / licensed takaful operator | Yes – mandatory |
| Development financial institution (DFI) | Yes – as applicable under BNM direction |

| Subsidiary of [Organization Name] | Yes – subject to group policy alignment |

## 3. Regulatory Context

*Summarise the regulatory obligations underpinning this procedure. Reference the specific BNM policy document, version, and clauses. This section provides the "why" in a regulatory audit context.*

### 3.1 BNM RMiT Policy Reference

[Organization Name] is subject to BNM's **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)** policy document, which sets out minimum standards for technology risk governance, resilience, and outsourcing management for financial institutions operating in Malaysia.

**Key regulatory obligations under RMiT Paragraph 16.3:**

- A financial institution shall notify BNM of any **material outsourcing arrangement** prior to implementation or, where prior notification is not practicable, as soon as reasonably possible;
- The notification shall include prescribed information enabling BNM to assess the risk profile and operational implications of the arrangement;
- Financial institutions are required to maintain an up-to-date **register of all outsourcing arrangements** and make this available to BNM upon request;
- Material amendments to, or termination of, a notified outsourcing arrangement shall also be notified to BNM in accordance with prescribed timelines.

## 5. Notification Trigger Criteria

*Define the specific conditions and thresholds that require a BNM notification to be submitted. Triggers should be unambiguous and measurable to support consistent application across business units.*

### 5.1 Materiality Determination

An outsourcing arrangement is considered **material** and triggers a BNM notification obligation if one or more of the following criteria are met:

#### 5.1.1 Functional Criticality Triggers

- [ ] The outsourced function supports a **critical business process** including, but not limited to: core banking, payment processing, credit decisioning, customer data management, cybersecurity operations, or regulatory reporting;
- [ ] The outsourced function has **direct customer impact** — disruption would result in customers being unable to access products or services;
- [ ] The arrangement involves **hosting, processing, or transmission of regulated data**, including personally identifiable information (PII), financial transaction data, or supervisory reporting data;
- [ ] The service provider will have **privileged or persistent access** to [Organization Name]'s production systems, networks, or data repositories.

#### 5.1.2 Financial Materiality Triggers

| Threshold Type | Materiality Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Annual contract value (single vendor) | ≥ MYR [X] million | Or as defined in [Organization Name]'s materiality policy |
| Cumulative annual spend (category) | ≥ MYR [X] million | Aggregated across related services |
| Percentage of total technology budget | ≥ [X]% | As a proportion of annual IT spend |
| [Additional threshold] | [Value] | [Notes] |

#### 5.1.3 Risk-Based Triggers

- [ ] The arrangement has been classified as **Tier 1 (Critical)** under [Organization Name]'s vendor risk tiering framework (refer to Section 6);
- [ ] The service provider is subject to **foreign jurisdiction** — data or operations will be hosted or processed outside Malaysia;
- [ ] The arrangement involves **sub-outsourcing** to a fourth party that is itself critical to service delivery;
- [ ] The arrangement involves **cloud computing services** classified as critical infrastructure under RMiT;
- [ ] BNM has issued a **specific supervisory direction** requiring notification of a particular category of outsourcing.

#### 5.1.4 Change Triggers (Notification of Material Amendments)

A notification must also be submitted when any of the following changes occur to an existing notified arrangement:

- [ ] Change of service provider (full or partial novation of contract);
- [ ] Material expansion of scope — new functions or services added to an existing arrangement;
- [ ] Sub-outsourcing of a critical component to a new fourth party;
- [ ] Change in data hosting jurisdiction;
- [ ] Material changes to contractual SLAs, security obligations, or termination rights;
- [ ] Termination or expiry of the arrangement without renewal.

### 5.2 Notification Timeline Requirements

| Trigger Type | Notification Deadline | Responsible Party |
|---|---|---|
| New material outsourcing arrangement | At least **[X] business days** prior to implementation | Head of Vendor Management |
| Material amendment to existing arrangement | Within **[X] business days** of amendment execution | Head of Vendor Management |
| Termination / expiry of arrangement | Within **[X] business days** of termination effective date | Head of Vendor Management |
| Emergency/unplanned termination | As soon as practicable, not exceeding **[X] business days** | Head of Vendor Management |
| BNM-directed notification (ad hoc) | As specified in BNM direction | Head of Vendor Management |

> **Note:** Timeline requirements should be confirmed against the prevailing BNM RMiT policy and any subsequent supervisory guidance. [Organization Name]'s Legal and Compliance function should be consulted to confirm applicable deadlines.

## 6. Vendor Risk Tiering Criteria

*Establish a consistent and defensible methodology for classifying vendors according to risk level. Tiering drives the intensity of due diligence, monitoring, and governance applied to each arrangement.*

### 6.1 Risk Tiering Framework Overview

[Organization Name] applies a **three-tier vendor risk classification** to all outsourcing arrangements. The assigned tier determines the level of due diligence, contractual protections, ongoing monitoring, and BNM notification obligations applicable to each arrangement.

### 6.2 Risk Tier Definitions

| Tier | Label | Description | BNM Notification Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Critical | Outsourcing arrangements supporting critical or important functions; high financial value; involves regulated data; or could cause significant operational disruption if it fails | **Yes – mandatory** |
| **Tier 2** | Significant | Arrangements with moderate operational impact; involves sensitive data; financial value above threshold; or has identifiable systemic risk | **Yes – mandatory if materiality criteria met** |
| **Tier 3** | Standard | Low-criticality arrangements; no regulated data; limited financial exposure; easily substituted | **No – internal oversight only** |

### 6.3 Tier Assignment Criteria

*Complete this table to define specific scoring or threshold criteria for each tier. Criteria should be reviewed annually by the Head of Vendor Management and the Chief Risk Officer.*

| Assessment Dimension | Tier 1 (Critical) | Tier 2 (Significant) | Tier 3 (Standard) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Operational Impact** | Disruption halts critical business operations | Disruption causes significant but manageable impact | Disruption has minimal operational impact |
| **Data Sensitivity** | Regulated data (PII, financial, supervisory) | Internal confidential data | Non-sensitive / publicly available data |
| **Annual Contract Value** | ≥ MYR [X]M | MYR [Y]M – MYR [X]M | < MYR [Y]M |
| **Data Jurisdiction** | Data hosted offshore | Data hosted offshore with approved controls | Data hosted in Malaysia |
| **Substitutability** | Difficult to replace; long transition period | Replaceable with significant effort | Easily substituted |
| **Regulatory Exposure** | Directly impacts regulatory compliance | Indirectly impacts regulatory compliance | No regulatory exposure |
| **Sub-outsourcing** | Material sub-outsourcing to fourth parties | Limited sub-outsourcing | No sub-outsourcing |
| **Concentration Risk** | Single provider for critical function | One of few providers | Multiple alternatives available |

### 6.4 Tier Assignment Process

```
Step 1: Complete Vendor Risk Tiering Assessment Form (Appendix A)
         ↓
Step 2: Vendor Management team reviews completed assessment
         ↓
Step 3: Risk function validates tier assignment
         ↓
Step 4: Head of Vendor Management approves final tier
         ↓
Step 5: Tier recorded in Outsourcing Register (Appendix C)
         ↓
Step 6: If Tier 1 or Tier 2 with materiality criteria met → proceed to BNM Notification
```

### 6.5 Tier Review and Escalation

- Vendor risk tiers shall be reviewed **at least annually** or upon any material change to the outsourcing arrangement;
- Any vendor initially classified as Tier 3 that subsequently meets Tier 1 or Tier 2 criteria must be re-tiered within **[X] business days** of the change being identified;
- Disputes over tier assignment shall be escalated to the **Chief Risk Officer (CRO)** for final determination;
- The Board Risk Management Committee (BRMC) shall be informed of any changes to Tier 1 vendor classifications on a **quarterly** basis.

## 7. Step-by-Step Process Flow

*Document the end-to-end process for identifying, assessing, approving, notifying, and monitoring material outsourcing arrangements. Each step should be actionable and assign a responsible party.*

### 7.1 Process Overview

The outsourcing notification process comprises six phases:

| Phase | Description | Primary Owner |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1** | Identification and Triage | Business Unit / Procurement |
| **Phase 2** | Vendor Risk Assessment and Tiering | Vendor Management / Risk |
| **Phase 3** | Due Diligence | Vendor Management / IT Security |
| **Phase 4** | Internal Approval | Head of Vendor Management / CRO / Board |
| **Phase 5** | BNM Notification Submission | Head of Vendor Management / Compliance |
| **Phase 6** | Ongoing Monitoring and Periodic Review | Vendor Management |

### 7.2 Phase 1: Identification and Triage

**Objective:** Detect all new or materially amended outsourcing arrangements at the earliest opportunity.

| Step | Action | Responsible | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.1 | Business unit identifies requirement to engage a third-party technology service provider | Business Unit Head | Internal requisition / business case |
| 1.2 | Business unit submits **Outsourcing Intent Notification** to Vendor Management using [Form Ref] | Business Unit Head | Completed Outsourcing Intent Form |
| 1.3 | Vendor Management reviews submission and applies preliminary materiality screen against criteria in Section 5 | Vendor Management Analyst | Preliminary materiality determination |
| 1.4 | If preliminary determination is **material**: proceed to Phase 2. If **not material**: proceed via standard procurement process with Tier 3 monitoring | Vendor Management Analyst | Routing decision documented |
| 1.5 | All new engagements recorded in the Outsourcing Register regardless of materiality determination | Vendor Management Analyst | Updated Outsourcing Register |

> **Key Control:** No contract with a technology service provider may be signed until a completed Outsourcing Intent Form has been reviewed by Vendor Management. This gate is enforced by the Procurement function.

### 7.3 Phase 2: Vendor Risk Assessment and Tiering

**Objective:** Assign an appropriate risk tier to the proposed arrangement.

| Step | Action | Responsible | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2.1 | Vendor Management completes **Vendor Risk Tiering Assessment Form** (Appendix A) for the proposed arrangement | Vendor Management Analyst | Completed tiering assessment |
| 2.2 | Risk function reviews tiering assessment for consistency with risk appetite | Technology Risk Manager | Risk review sign-off |
| 2.3 | Head of Vendor Management approves final tier assignment | Head of Vendor Management | Approved tier on Outsourcing Register |
| 2.4 | Business unit and Procurement notified of tier assignment and corresponding requirements | Vendor Management Analyst | Notification to stakeholders |
| 2.5 | If Tier 1 or qualifying Tier 2: flag for BNM notification; initiate Phase 3 due diligence | Vendor Management Analyst | BNM notification flag set |

### 7.4 Phase 3: Due Diligence

**Objective:** Conduct structured pre-engagement assessment of the service provider.

| Step | Action | Responsible | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3.1 | Issue **Vendor Due Diligence Questionnaire** (Appendix B) to prospective service provider | Vendor Management Analyst | Completed DDQ received from vendor |
| 3.2 | Conduct financial soundness review of service provider | Finance / Vendor Management | Financial assessment report |
| 3.3 | Conduct information security assessment (VAPT, SOC 2, ISO 27001 review or equivalent) | IT Security | Security assessment report |
| 3.4 | Conduct legal and regulatory compliance review | Legal / Compliance | Legal review memorandum |
| 3.5 | Conduct operational resilience review (BCP/DR capabilities) | Technology Risk / IT | Resilience assessment |
| 3.6 | Conduct data protection and PDPA compliance review for data-handling arrangements | Data Protection Officer | PDPA compliance assessment |
| 3.7 | Review sub-outsourcing arrangements and assess fourth-party risk | Vendor Management Analyst | Sub-outsourcing risk assessment |
| 3.8 | Consolidate due diligence findings into **Vendor Assessment Report** | Head of Vendor Management | Vendor Assessment Report |
| 3.9 | Unacceptable findings escalated to CRO; decision: proceed with remediation conditions, defer, or reject | CRO | CRO decision memorandum |

### 7.5 Phase 4: Internal Approval

**Objective:** Obtain required governance approvals before contractual commitment and BNM notification.

| Step | Action | Responsible | Approval Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4.1 | Prepare **Outsourcing Approval Paper** summarising arrangement, risk assessment, and proposed controls | Head of Vendor Management | — |
| 4.2 | Technology Risk Committee (TRC) or equivalent reviews and approves | Head of Vendor Management | Technology Risk Committee |
| 4.3 | For Tier 1 arrangements: escalate for Board Risk Management Committee (BRMC) approval | CRO | BRMC |
| 4.4 | Legal finalises and executes outsourcing contract incorporating mandatory RMiT clauses | Legal | General Counsel |
| 4.5 | Approval documented and retained in Outsourcing Register | Vendor Management Analyst | Updated Outsourcing Register |

### 7.6 Phase 5: BNM Notification Submission

**Objective:** Submit a complete and accurate notification to BNM within prescribed timelines.

| Step | Action | Responsible | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5.1 | Compliance confirms notification timeline based on arrangement type (refer to Section 5.2) | Compliance Officer | Notification deadline confirmed |
| 5.2 | Head of Vendor Management prepares BNM notification package using prescribed BNM format (refer to Appendix D) | Head of Vendor Management | Draft notification package |
| 5.3 | Compliance reviews notification package for completeness and accuracy against RMiT requirements | Chief Compliance Officer | Reviewed notification package |
| 5.4 | CEO or authorised signatory approves notification for submission | CEO / [Authorised Signatory] | Signed notification |
| 5.5 | Compliance submits notification to BNM via [BNM submission portal / written submission] | Chief Compliance Officer | Submitted notification with timestamp |
| 5.6 | Acknowledgement receipt from BNM obtained and filed | Compliance Officer | BNM acknowledgement on file |
| 5.7 | Outsourcing Register updated to reflect notification submission and BNM acknowledgement | Vendor Management Analyst | Updated register |
| 5.8 | Arrange for vendor onboarding to proceed (if not already commenced) | Business Unit / IT | Vendor onboarding initiated |

> **Key Control:** No live production access shall be granted to a Tier 1 service provider until BNM notification has been submitted (or as otherwise approved by the CRO and Compliance where prior notification is not practicable).

### 7.7 Phase 6: Ongoing Monitoring and Periodic Review

**Objective:** Maintain continuous oversight of outsourcing arrangements post-onboarding.

| Step | Action | Responsible | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6.1 | Monitor service provider performance against SLAs and KPIs defined in Section 9 | Vendor Management Analyst | Monthly |
| 6.2 | Conduct formal vendor performance review meeting | Head of Vendor Management | Quarterly |
| 6.3 | Review and update vendor risk tier assignment | Technology Risk Manager | Annually / upon material change |
| 6.4 | Conduct periodic due diligence refresh for Tier 1 vendors | Vendor Management Analyst | Annually |
| 6.5 | Review exit strategy currency and test exit readiness for Tier 1 vendors | Head of Vendor Management | Annually |
| 6.6 | Report outsourcing risk summary to Technology Risk Committee | Head of Vendor Management | Quarterly |
| 6.7 | Report Tier 1 vendor changes to BRMC | CRO | Quarterly |
| 6.8 | Submit periodic outsourcing updates to BNM if required under supervisory direction | Chief Compliance Officer | As required |
| 6.9 | Upon contract renewal: reassess materiality, re-tier, and re-notify BNM if material changes exist | Head of Vendor Management | At renewal |

## 9. SLA and KPI Definitions

*Define the minimum contractual service level and performance measurement standards applicable to all material outsourcing arrangements. These shall be reflected in contracts with Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors.*

### 9.1 Minimum Contractual SLA Requirements

All material outsourcing contracts shall include the following minimum SLA provisions:

| SLA Category | Minimum Requirement | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **System Availability** | Agreed uptime target for critical services | ≥ 99.9% per month | ≥ 99.5% per month | As negotiated |
| **Incident Response – Critical** | Initial response to critical incidents | ≤ [X] minutes | ≤ [X] minutes | ≤ [X] hours |
| **Incident Resolution – Critical** | Full resolution of critical incidents | ≤ [X] hours | ≤ [X] hours | As negotiated |
| **Incident Response – High** | Initial response to high-severity incidents | ≤ [X] hours | ≤ [X] hours | As negotiated |
| **Scheduled Maintenance Notice** | Advance notice of planned outages | ≥ [X] business days | ≥ [X] business days | As negotiated |
| **Security Incident Notification** | Notification to [Organization Name] of confirmed security breach | ≤ [X] hours of discovery | ≤ [X] hours of discovery | ≤ [X] hours |
| **BCP Invocation Notice** | Notification upon invocation of vendor's BCP | ≤ [X] hours | ≤ [X] hours | As negotiated |
| **Recovery Time Objective (RTO)** | Maximum tolerable downtime for service recovery | ≤ [X] hours | ≤ [X] hours | As negotiated |
| **Recovery Point Objective (RPO)** | Maximum tolerable data loss | ≤ [X] hours | ≤ [X] hours | As negotiated |

### 9.2 Operational KPIs

The following KPIs shall be tracked and reported by all Tier 1 and Tier 2 service providers on a **monthly** basis:

| KPI | Description | Measurement Method | Target | Reporting Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Service Availability** | Actual system uptime as a percentage of total contracted hours | Automated monitoring / vendor report | [X]% | Monthly |

## 10. Exit Strategy Requirements

*An exit strategy must be documented for all material outsourcing arrangements prior to contract execution. This section defines the minimum requirements for exit planning and activation.*

### 10.1 Exit Strategy Policy

[Organization Name] shall maintain a documented and tested exit strategy for all Tier 1 outsourcing arrangements and, where practicable, Tier 2 arrangements. The exit strategy shall ensure that [Organization Name] can transition away from any service provider in an **orderly, timely, and controlled manner** that minimises operational disruption, protects customer interests, and maintains regulatory compliance.

Exit strategies shall be reviewed **annually** and updated within **[X] business days** of any material change to the outsourcing arrangement.

### 10.2 Exit Strategy Minimum Components

Each exit strategy document shall address the following:

| Component | Description | Required For |
|---|---|---|
| **Transition Timeline** | Estimated timeframe to fully transition services away from the incumbent provider, including key milestones | Tier 1, Tier 2 |
| **Successor Service Provider Options** | Identified alternative providers capable of delivering equivalent services | Tier 1 |
| **In-Sourcing Feasibility** | Assessment of [Organization Name]'s capability to re-insource the function, including resource and cost implications | Tier 1 |
| **Data Migration Plan** | Procedures for extracting, migrating, and validating data from the incumbent provider's systems | Tier 1, Tier 2 |
| **Knowledge Transfer Requirements** | Documentation and training requirements to transfer operational knowledge from vendor to [Organization Name] or successor | Tier 1, Tier 2 |
| **System Interdependency Mapping** | Map of all systems and interfaces dependent on the outsourced service | Tier 1 |
| **Customer Communication Plan** | Plan for communicating service transition to affected customers, where applicable | Tier 1 |
| **Regulatory Notification Plan** | Obligations to notify BNM of outsourcing termination (refer to Section 5.2) | Tier 1, Tier 2 |
| **Parallel Running Period** | Plans for running incumbent and successor services in parallel to validate transition | Tier 1 |
| **Contract Termination Requirements** | Summary of contractual notice periods, termination fees, and data deletion obligations | All tiers |
| **Exit Trigger Scenarios** | Defined scenarios that would trigger exit plan activation (refer to Section 10.3) | Tier 1, Tier 2 |

### 10.3 Exit Trigger Scenarios

The exit strategy shall be activated, or readiness assessed, upon any of the following triggers:

- [ ] Vendor insolvency, administration, or cessation of business;
- [ ] Sustained and unresolved failure to meet critical SLAs (Level 3 escalation per Section 9.3);
- [ ] Confirmed material security breach or data compromise attributable to vendor failure;
- [ ] Vendor acquired by an entity that presents a conflict of interest or unacceptable risk;
- [ ] Change in vendor's regulatory standing — loss of required licences, material sanctions imposed;
- [ ] BNM direction to terminate or transition the outsourcing arrangement;
- [ ] Contractual termination — expiry without renewal or contractual breach;
- [ ] [Organization Name]'s strategic decision to in-source or change the service provider;
- [ ] Material deterioration in vendor's financial soundness assessed as a going-concern risk.

### 10.4 Exit Readiness Testing

- Exit readiness shall be tested at least **annually** for all Tier 1 arrangements through tabletop exercises or, where operationally feasible, simulation exercises;
- Test results shall be documented and reviewed by the Head of Vendor Management and the CRO;
- Material gaps identified during exit readiness testing shall be tracked as action items with defined owners and resolution timelines;
- Test results shall be reported to the Technology Risk Committee.
