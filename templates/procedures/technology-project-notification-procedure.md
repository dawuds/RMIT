## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

*This section articulates the intent of the procedure. Describe why this document exists and what compliance obligation it fulfils.*

This procedure establishes the formal requirements and standardised process for [Organization Name] to notify Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) of major technology projects in accordance with the **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically **Clause 16.2**. The procedure ensures that all qualifying technology initiatives are identified, assessed, and reported to the regulator within the prescribed timelines and in the required format.

This document supports [Organization Name]'s obligation to maintain transparency with BNM regarding significant technology undertakings that may affect the institution's operational resilience, cybersecurity posture, or delivery of financial services to customers.

### 1.2 Objectives

*List the specific objectives this procedure is designed to achieve.*

The objectives of this procedure are to:

- Establish clear criteria for determining when a technology project constitutes a **major technology project** requiring BNM notification.
- Define the mandatory timeline and sequence of notification activities prior to and during project execution.
- Assign unambiguous ownership and accountability across all notification activities.
- Standardise the submission process via **BNM LINK** to ensure completeness, accuracy, and timeliness.
- Create an auditable record of all notifications submitted to BNM.
- Provide a mechanism for continuous monitoring and quarterly review of notification obligations.

### 1.3 Background

*Briefly describe the regulatory context that necessitates this procedure.*

BNM's RMiT Policy Document imposes obligations on financial institutions to proactively notify the regulator of major technology projects. This requirement reflects BNM's supervisory interest in ensuring that large-scale technology change programmes do not introduce undue risk to the financial system, customer data, or the continuity of critical financial services. [Organization Name] recognises that timely, accurate, and complete notification is a cornerstone of its regulatory relationship with BNM.

## 4. Scope and Applicability

*Define precisely which technology projects, business units, and project types fall within the scope of this procedure.*

### 4.1 In-Scope

This procedure applies to:

- **All technology projects** initiated, sponsored, or managed by [Organization Name] that meet one or more of the notification trigger criteria defined in Section 5.
- **All business divisions and subsidiaries** of [Organization Name] that initiate technology projects, regardless of whether those projects are managed internally or by a third party on behalf of the organisation.
- **Outsourced technology projects** where [Organization Name] retains material accountability for the technology outcome.
- **Joint technology initiatives** undertaken in partnership with other financial institutions, fintech entities, or infrastructure providers where [Organization Name] is a participant.
- **Technology projects initiated by Group entities** that have a direct or material impact on [Organization Name]'s operations, systems, or regulatory standing.

### 4.2 Out-of-Scope

The following are **not** subject to this procedure unless they independently meet a notification trigger criterion:

- Routine technology maintenance, patching, and minor system upgrades that do not materially alter system functionality or architecture.
- Hardware refresh activities that do not change system capacity, resilience, or capability in a material way.
- Technology projects with a total budget below [RM X million] and no impact on critical systems.
- Internal process improvements that do not involve changes to core technology systems.
- Proof-of-concept or pilot projects that are not intended for production deployment.

> **Note:** The determination of whether a project is in scope must be made through the formal screening process described in Section 5. Exclusions must be documented and retained for audit purposes.

## 5. Notification Trigger Criteria

*Define the specific criteria that, when met, obligate the organisation to notify BNM of a technology project. Criteria must be aligned with RMiT Clause 16.2 and any supplementary BNM guidance.*

### 5.1 Overview

A technology project is classified as a **major technology project** requiring BNM notification if it meets **one or more** of the criteria set out in this section. The assessment of trigger criteria must be performed at project inception and reviewed at key project milestones.

### 5.2 Primary Trigger Criteria

*Complete each criterion with reference to BNM's defined thresholds or [Organization Name]'s own thresholds where BNM does not prescribe a specific figure.*

| Criteria ID | Trigger Criterion | Threshold / Definition | Assessment Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| TC-01 | **Budget Threshold** | Total project budget exceeds [RM X million] | Confirmed project budget as approved by [Governance Body] |
| TC-02 | **Critical System Impact** | The project involves changes to or replacement of a system classified as critical by [Organization Name] | Review against Critical Systems Register |
| TC-03 | **New Technology Platform** | The project introduces a new core technology platform not previously deployed by [Organization Name] | Architecture review by CIO/CISO |
| TC-04 | **New Technology Service Provider** | The project involves engagement of a new third-party technology service provider for material technology services | Review against TTSP Register |
| TC-05 | **Cloud Adoption** | The project involves migration of material workloads or data to a cloud environment | Cloud Risk Assessment |
| TC-06 | **Customer-Facing System** | The project materially changes a customer-facing digital channel, product system, or payment infrastructure | Business impact assessment |
| TC-07 | **Systemic Risk** | The project is assessed as posing potential systemic risk to the financial system | CRO sign-off |
| TC-08 | **BNM Direction** | BNM has explicitly directed [Organization Name] to notify it of a specific project | Written directive from BNM |
| TC-09 | **Cross-Border Data** | The project involves the processing, storage, or transmission of material data across national borders | Data governance review |
| TC-10 | **Regulatory Programme** | The project is initiated in response to a regulatory requirement, direction, or enforcement action | Regulatory tracking register |

### 5.3 Screening Process

*Describe how projects are screened against trigger criteria at inception.*

All technology projects must undergo a **Notification Trigger Screening** at the project initiation stage. The screening must be:

1. Completed using the **BNM Notification Trigger Screening Form** (Appendix A).
2. Reviewed and signed off by the **Head of Technology Risk** and the **Head of Regulatory Affairs**.
3. Retained in the project record and the Regulatory Notifications Register.
4. Repeated at each major project milestone (see Section 6.4).

### 5.4 Escalation for Borderline Cases

Where a project does not clearly meet or fail a trigger criterion, the matter must be escalated to the **Chief Risk Officer (CRO)** for a determination. The CRO's decision must be documented and countersigned by the **Head of Regulatory Affairs**. Where there remains material uncertainty, [Organization Name] shall apply the **precautionary principle** and notify BNM.

## 7. Step-by-Step Process Flow

*This section provides the detailed, sequential steps for executing the BNM technology project notification process from identification through to closure.*

### 7.1 Process Overview

The notification process comprises four phases:

| Phase | Description |
|---|---|
| **Phase 1: Identification and Screening** | Identify whether a technology project meets the notification trigger criteria. |
| **Phase 2: Preparation** | Prepare the notification submission, including all required documentation. |
| **Phase 3: Submission and Confirmation** | Submit the notification via BNM LINK and confirm receipt. |
| **Phase 4: Post-Notification Management** | Manage BNM queries, material change notifications, and project closure notifications. |

---

### 7.2 Phase 1: Identification and Screening

**Step 1.1 — Project Registration**

*All technology projects must be registered in [Organization Name]'s project management system at inception.*

- The **Project Manager** registers the new project in [Project Management System].
- The registration must capture: project name, sponsor, estimated budget, target go-live date, and a brief description of scope.
- The Project Manager notifies the **Head of Regulatory Affairs** of the new project registration within **[X] business days**.

**Step 1.2 — Trigger Screening**

*The Head of Regulatory Affairs assesses each registered project against the notification trigger criteria.*

- The Head of Regulatory Affairs completes the **BNM Notification Trigger Screening Form** (Appendix A) for each new project.
- The screening must be completed within **[X] business days** of receiving the project registration notification.
- Where the project meets one or more trigger criteria, it is classified as a **Major Technology Project** and proceeds to Phase 2.
- Where the project does not meet any trigger criteria, the outcome is documented and the project is logged in the Regulatory Notifications Register (Appendix B) as **Not Applicable — No Notification Required**.

**Step 1.3 — Escalation of Borderline Cases**

- Where the trigger assessment is inconclusive, the Head of Regulatory Affairs escalates to the **CRO** per the process described in Section 5.4.
- The CRO's determination is documented and filed.

**Step 1.4 — Notification Calendar Update**

- Upon confirmation that a project is a Major Technology Project, the Head of Regulatory Affairs adds all mandatory notification deadlines to the **Regulatory Notifications Calendar**.
- The Project Manager is informed of notification deadlines and the information they must provide to support each submission.

---

### 7.3 Phase 2: Preparation

**Step 2.1 — Notification Package Assembly**

*Prior to each notification stage deadline, the Head of Regulatory Affairs assembles the notification package.*

- The Head of Regulatory Affairs initiates the notification package at least **[Y] business days** before the submission deadline.
- The required content for each notification stage is set out in Section 8.2 and Appendix C.
- The Project Manager provides updated project information, including revised scope, timeline, budget, risk assessment, and any TTSP details.

**Step 2.2 — Risk Assessment Review**

- The **Head of Technology Risk** reviews and signs off the technology risk assessment included in the notification package.
- Where the project involves a TTSP, the **TTSP risk assessment** must also be included and signed off.

**Step 2.3 — Internal Review and Approval**

*All notification packages must be reviewed and approved before submission to BNM.*

The notification package must be reviewed and approved in the following sequence:

| Step | Reviewer | Review Focus | Approval Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Head of Technology Risk | Technical accuracy; risk assessment completeness | Yes |
| 2 | CIO | Technology strategy alignment; architectural accuracy | Yes |
| 3 | CRO | Risk profile; enterprise risk implications | Yes |
| 4 | Head of Regulatory Affairs | Regulatory completeness; BNM LINK submission readiness | Yes |
| 5 | [CEO / MD or authorised delegate] | Final approval for submission | Yes |

**Step 2.4 — Pre-Submission Checklist**

- Before submission, the Head of Regulatory Affairs completes the **Pre-Submission Checklist** (Appendix D) to confirm all required fields, supporting documents, and approvals are in order.

---

### 7.4 Phase 3: Submission and Confirmation

**Step 3.1 — BNM LINK Submission**

*The formal notification is submitted via BNM LINK per the process described in Section 8.*

- The Head of Regulatory Affairs (or designated authorised submitter) logs into **BNM LINK** and completes the notification submission.
- The full BNM LINK submission process is described in Section 8.

**Step 3.2 — Submission Confirmation and Record-Keeping**

- Upon successful submission, the Head of Regulatory Affairs downloads and retains the **BNM LINK submission confirmation receipt**.
- The submission details (submission date, reference number, notification stage) are recorded in the **Regulatory Notifications Register** (Appendix B).
- The Project Manager and relevant stakeholders are notified of successful submission.

**Step 3.3 — BNM Acknowledgement**

- The Head of Regulatory Affairs monitors BNM LINK and [Organization Name]'s regulatory correspondence inbox for any acknowledgement, queries, or requests for additional information from BNM.
- Any BNM acknowledgement is filed in the project record and the Regulatory Notifications Register.

---

### 7.5 Phase 4: Post-Notification Management

**Step 4.1 — Responding to BNM Queries**

*BNM may request additional information or clarification following a notification submission.*

- Upon receipt of a BNM query, the Head of Regulatory Affairs acknowledges receipt within **[X] business days**.
- The Head of Regulatory Affairs coordinates the preparation of a response with the relevant project stakeholders.
- Responses to BNM queries must be reviewed and approved by the **CRO** before submission.
- All BNM queries and responses are documented in the Regulatory Notifications Register.

**Step 4.2 — Material Change Notifications**

- Where a Major Technology Project undergoes a material change (as defined in Section 5.2 or as otherwise communicated by BNM), the Head of Regulatory Affairs initiates a **Material Change Notification** submission within the required timeline (Section 6.2).
- The Material Change Notification follows the same preparation and approval process as the original notification.

**Step 4.3 — Project Closure Notification**

- Upon completion of the Post-Implementation Review (PIR), the Head of Regulatory Affairs submits the **Stage 5 Post-Implementation Notification** to BNM per Section 6.2.
- The PIR must confirm: go-live outcome, any residual risks, and evidence of controls implemented.

**Step 4.4 — Registry Closure**

- Upon submission of the Stage 5 notification and receipt of any final BNM correspondence, the Head of Regulatory Affairs marks the project as **Closed** in the Regulatory Notifications Register.
- All notification records are archived per [Organization Name]'s records retention policy for a minimum of **[X] years**.

## 10. Escalation and Exception Handling

### 10.1 Escalation Triggers

*Define the circumstances under which issues must be escalated and to whom.*

The following circumstances require immediate escalation:

| Trigger | Immediate Action | Escalation Path | Target Resolution Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Notification deadline will be missed or is at risk | Notify CRO immediately; assess options | Head of Regulatory Affairs → CRO → CEO/MD | Immediate |
| BNM has issued a query requiring urgent response | Mobilise response team | Head of Regulatory Affairs → CRO | Within [X] business days |
| Project trigger screening outcome is inconclusive | Formal escalation to CRO per Section 5.4 | Head of Regulatory Affairs → CRO | Within [X] business days |
| Material change not flagged by Project Manager in time | Assess impact; notify CRO | Head of Regulatory Affairs → CRO → CEO/MD | Immediate |
| BNM LINK is unavailable near a submission deadline | Notify BNM by email; document | Head of Regulatory Affairs → CRO | Immediate |
| BNM raises supervisory concerns arising from notification | Engage senior management and legal | Head of Regulatory Affairs → CRO → CEO/MD → Board Risk Committee | As directed |

### 10.2 Exception Process

*Describe the process for managing exceptions to this procedure.*

Where compliance with this procedure is not possible due to extraordinary circumstances, the following exception process applies:

1. The Head of Regulatory Affairs documents the nature and reason for the exception.
2. The exception request is approved by the **CRO**.
3. Where the exception relates to a missed or delayed BNM notification, the **CEO/MD** must be informed and BNM must be notified proactively.
4. All exceptions are logged in the **Exceptions Register** and reported to the **Board Risk Committee** in the next scheduled report.

### 10.3 Breach and Non-Compliance

- Any confirmed breach of notification obligations under RMiT Clause 16.2 must be escalated to the **CEO/MD** and **Board Risk Committee** immediately.

## 11. Monitoring and Compliance Assurance

### 11.1 Ongoing Monitoring

*Describe the continuous monitoring activities that ensure compliance with this procedure.*

The Head of Regulatory Affairs is responsible for continuous monitoring of notification obligations, including:

- **Regulatory Notifications Calendar:** Maintained and reviewed weekly to ensure all upcoming notification deadlines are identified and preparation activities are on track.
- **Project Pipeline Monitoring:** Regular review (at minimum monthly) of the active technology project pipeline with the CIO to identify new projects that may trigger notification obligations.
- **BNM Correspondence Monitoring:** Daily monitoring of BNM LINK and [Organization Name]'s designated regulatory correspondence inbox for any BNM communications relating to submitted notifications.
- **Material Change Monitoring:** Regular communication with Project Managers to identify any project scope, timeline, budget, or risk changes that may constitute a material change requiring notification.

### 11.2 Quarterly Review

This procedure is subject to a **formal quarterly review** by the Head of Regulatory Affairs, in consultation with the CRO and CIO. The quarterly review includes:

- Assessment of all notifications submitted during the quarter for completeness and timeliness.
- Review of any exceptions, escalations, or breaches during the quarter.
- Assessment of any changes to BNM guidance or supervisory expectations.
- Update of this procedure and associated templates where required.
- Preparation of a **Quarterly Compliance Report** for the **Board Risk Committee**.
