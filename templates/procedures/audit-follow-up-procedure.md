## 1. Purpose

*Describe the primary intent of this procedure and the problem it is designed to solve. Explain why tracking audit findings to closure is critical for the organization's governance and regulatory standing.*

This procedure establishes the formal process by which [Organization Name] tracks, monitors, and validates the remediation of all information technology (IT) audit findings from initial identification through to confirmed closure. It ensures that findings raised by the Internal Audit function, external auditors, and regulatory bodies — including Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) — are addressed in a timely, accountable, and documented manner in accordance with BNM's Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document, specifically Clause 13.4.

The procedure supports [Organization Name]'s commitment to:

- Maintaining a robust internal control environment over technology risk;
- Demonstrating compliance with BNM RMiT and associated regulatory requirements;
- Providing the Board and Senior Management with timely assurance over the status of audit remediation;
- Preventing recurrence of control weaknesses through systematic follow-up and root cause verification.

## 5. Audit Follow-Up Process Flow

*Document the end-to-end, step-by-step process for managing audit findings from identification through to closure. Each step should clearly indicate inputs, outputs, and responsible parties.*

### 5.1 Process Overview

The Audit Follow-Up lifecycle consists of six phases:

```
[Finding Issuance] → [CAP Agreement] → [Remediation Execution]
       → [Progress Monitoring] → [Closure Verification] → [Reporting & Archival]
```

### 5.2 Step-by-Step Procedure

#### Step 1 — Audit Finding Issuance

*This step covers how findings are formally communicated to process owners following the conclusion of audit fieldwork.*

1.1. Upon conclusion of audit fieldwork, the Audit Lead prepares a **Draft Audit Report** containing all identified findings, categorized by severity (refer to Section 6).

1.2. The Draft Report is issued to the relevant **Issue Owner(s)** and their line management for review and factual verification within **[X] business days** of fieldwork completion.

1.3. Issue Owners may submit written responses disputing factual inaccuracies. All disputes are adjudicated by the Head of Internal Audit. Disagreements on risk rating are escalated to the ARMC if unresolved.

1.4. A **Final Audit Report** is issued within **[X] business days** of the factual verification period closing. The Final Report is distributed to:

| Recipient | Distribution Method |
|---|---|
| Issue Owner(s) | Email / Audit Management System |
| Issue Owner's Line Manager | Email |
| Chief Information Officer / CISO | Email |
| Chief Risk Officer | Email |
| Head of Internal Audit | Email |
| ARMC (for Critical/High findings) | Board paper submission |

#### Step 2 — Corrective Action Plan (CAP) Agreement

*This step ensures each finding is matched with a documented, management-owned remediation plan before tracking begins.*

2.1. Issue Owners must submit a completed **Corrective Action Plan** for each finding within **[X] business days** of receiving the Final Audit Report.

2.2. Each CAP must include as a minimum:

- [ ] Root cause analysis (RCA) of the identified weakness;
- [ ] Description of the proposed corrective action;
- [ ] Identification of the accountable Issue Owner (name and role);
- [ ] Proposed target remediation date;
- [ ] Interim risk mitigation measures (where the remediation date exceeds [X] days).

2.3. The Audit Lead reviews each submitted CAP for:

- Adequacy of the proposed action relative to the identified root cause;
- Reasonableness of the proposed target date given finding severity;
- Whether interim controls are sufficient to manage risk in the interim period.

2.4. Agreed CAPs are formally acknowledged in writing. Disagreements on CAP adequacy are escalated to the Head of Internal Audit and, if necessary, to the ARMC.

2.5. All CAPs are logged in the **Audit Finding Tracking Register** (Appendix A).

#### Step 3 — Remediation Execution

*This step describes the responsibilities of the Issue Owner during the active remediation period.*

3.1. The Issue Owner is responsible for executing the agreed CAP within the committed timeframe.

3.2. The Issue Owner must:

- Maintain documented evidence of remediation progress;
- Notify the assigned Audit Follow-Up Coordinator of any material changes to scope, approach, or timeline;
- Submit a **Remediation Completion Notice** to the IAD upon believed completion of all agreed actions.

3.3. Where remediation requires involvement of third-party vendors or outsourced service providers, the Issue Owner retains full accountability and must maintain oversight of vendor delivery.

#### Step 4 — Progress Monitoring

*This step ensures that the IAD maintains continuous visibility over remediation status and intervenes when findings are at risk of becoming overdue.*

4.1. The **Audit Follow-Up Coordinator** (designated within IAD) maintains the Audit Finding Tracking Register and performs status reviews at the following intervals:

| Finding Severity | Monitoring Frequency |
|---|---|
| Critical | Fortnightly |
| High | Monthly |
| Medium | Quarterly |
| Low | Semi-annually or at next audit cycle |

4.2. Issue Owners must provide a written progress update to the Audit Follow-Up Coordinator at each monitoring interval, including:

- Current remediation status (Not Started / In Progress / Completed — Pending Verification);
- Summary of actions taken to date;
- Any risks to achieving the target date;
- Revised target date (if applicable, subject to approval — refer to Section 11).

4.3. The Audit Follow-Up Coordinator flags all **at-risk** findings (i.e., findings where remediation is less than 50% complete with less than [X] days to target date) to the Head of Internal Audit for review.

#### Step 5 — Closure Verification

*This step defines how Internal Audit independently verifies that a finding has been fully and effectively remediated before it is formally closed.*

5.1. Upon receiving a Remediation Completion Notice, the Audit Lead or assigned auditor conducts a **Closure Review** to independently verify that:

- All agreed corrective actions have been implemented;
- Evidence of implementation is sufficient, appropriate, and verifiable;
- The root cause of the finding has been addressed (not merely the symptom);
- The implemented control is operating effectively.

5.2. The Closure Review may include, but is not limited to:

- [ ] Review of documentary evidence submitted by the Issue Owner;
- [ ] Walkthrough of the remediated process or control;
- [ ] Sample testing of the implemented control;
- [ ] Technical testing (e.g., re-testing of a patched vulnerability);
- [ ] Review of system configurations or access control lists.

5.3. Closure Review outcomes:

| Outcome | Description | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Closed** | All actions implemented; root cause addressed; evidence sufficient | Finding marked Closed in tracking register; closure confirmed to Issue Owner in writing |
| **Partially Closed** | Some actions implemented; residual gap remains | Finding status updated to "In Progress"; outstanding items documented; revised target date agreed |
| **Not Closed** | Insufficient evidence; root cause not addressed; actions not implemented | Finding status reverted to "Open"; escalation initiated per Section 11 |

5.4. Closure verification must be completed within **[X] business days** of receiving the Remediation Completion Notice.

#### Step 6 — Reporting and Archival

*This step ensures findings and their closure are reported to governance bodies and retained in accordance with record retention requirements.*

6.1. The Head of Internal Audit presents a consolidated **Audit Follow-Up Status Report** to the ARMC at each scheduled meeting, covering:

- Total open findings by severity;
- Findings closed since last report;
- Overdue findings and escalation status;
- Repeat findings and trend analysis;
- Summary of approved extensions.

6.2. Upon confirmed closure, all supporting documentation is archived in the **Audit Management System** / [designated document management system] in accordance with Section 12.

## 6. Finding Severity Classification

*Define how audit findings are rated by severity. Severity classification drives monitoring frequency, escalation requirements, and reporting obligations. Ensure ratings are consistently applied across all audits.*

### 6.1 Severity Rating Framework

All audit findings raised under this procedure shall be assigned one of the following severity ratings at the time of the Draft Audit Report:

| Severity | Definition | Remediation SLA | Escalation Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | A finding that represents an immediate and material threat to the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of critical systems or data; a regulatory breach; or a control failure that has or could result in significant financial loss or reputational damage. | [X] calendar days | Immediate — Board/ARMC notification required |
| **High** | A finding that represents a significant control weakness with material risk potential; likely to attract regulatory attention if unaddressed; or where compensating controls are insufficient or absent. | [X] calendar days | ARMC notification at next scheduled meeting |
| **Medium** | A finding that represents a moderate control gap or process inefficiency where existing compensating controls partially mitigate the risk, but improvement is required. | [X] calendar days | Head of Internal Audit |
| **Low** | A finding that represents a minor control gap, best-practice deviation, or process improvement opportunity with limited risk impact. | [X] calendar days | Audit Lead |

### 6.2 Rating Criteria Matrix

*Use this matrix as a guide when assigning severity ratings. The final rating is determined by the Head of Internal Audit.*

| Impact \ Likelihood | Rare | Unlikely | Possible | Likely | Almost Certain |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Catastrophic** | High | High | Critical | Critical | Critical |
| **Major** | Medium | High | High | Critical | Critical |
| **Moderate** | Low | Medium | High | High | Critical |
| **Minor** | Low | Low | Medium | Medium | High |
| **Negligible** | Low | Low | Low | Medium | Medium |

### 6.3 Repeat Finding Escalation

A finding is classified as a **Repeat Finding** if it is substantively similar (by root cause or control domain) to a finding raised in the preceding [X] audit cycles.

- Repeat Findings are automatically escalated by one severity level (e.g., Medium becomes High);
- Three or more consecutive Repeat Findings in the same control area are escalated directly to the ARMC;
- The Head of Internal Audit shall prepare a dedicated Repeat Finding Report for ARMC consideration where repeat findings are identified.

### 7.2 Role Descriptions

#### 7.2.1 Board of Directors / Audit and Risk Management Committee (ARMC)

- Provides ultimate oversight of the internal audit function and remediation status;
- Reviews and approves the annual risk-based audit plan;
- Receives and challenges the Audit Follow-Up Status Report at each ARMC meeting;
- Holds Senior Management accountable for timely remediation of Critical and High findings.

#### 7.2.2 Head of Internal Audit

- Accountable for the design, implementation, and effectiveness of this procedure;
- Approves final severity ratings and material changes to CAPs;
- Approves extensions to remediation timelines for High and Critical findings;
- Presents the Audit Follow-Up Status Report to the ARMC;
- Escalates systemic or repeat control failures to the ARMC.

#### 7.2.3 Audit Lead / Audit Follow-Up Coordinator

- Maintains the Audit Finding Tracking Register;
- Issues Draft and Final Audit Reports;
- Reviews and challenges submitted CAPs for adequacy;
- Conducts periodic monitoring and closure verification activities;
- Prepares the Audit Follow-Up Status Report for the Head of Internal Audit's review;
- Manages day-to-day communication with Issue Owners.

#### 7.2.4 Issue Owner

- Accountable for implementing the agreed Corrective Action Plan within the committed timeframe;
- Submits timely progress updates to the Audit Follow-Up Coordinator;
- Provides evidence of remediation to support closure verification;
- Notifies the IAD immediately if the agreed target date cannot be met;
- Retains accountability for remediation even where execution is delegated to a team member or vendor.

#### 7.2.5 Issue Owner's Line Manager

- Provides organizational support and resources to enable timely remediation;
- Accountable for escalating resource constraints that may impact remediation timelines;
- Endorses extension requests submitted by Issue Owners.

#### 7.2.6 Chief Information Officer (CIO) / Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

- Consulted on technical remediation approaches for complex IT findings;
- Responsible for ensuring technology resources are made available for Critical and High finding remediation;
- Informed of all Critical findings upon issuance of the Final Audit Report.

#### 7.2.7 Chief Risk Officer

- Maintains alignment between audit findings and the enterprise risk register;
- Consulted on risk acceptance decisions where remediation is not feasible within the SLA;
- Informed of ARMC-escalated findings and associated risk decisions.

- Conduct closure verification;
- Report status to governance bodies;

## 11. Escalation and Exception Handling

*Define clear escalation paths for findings that are overdue, disputed, or where risk acceptance is sought. Escalation procedures ensure that governance bodies are not surprised by material control weaknesses.*

### 11.1 Overdue Finding Escalation

When a finding reaches its target remediation date without confirmed closure, the following escalation path applies:

| Days Overdue | Action | Escalation Level |
|---|---|---|
| **1–[X] days** | Audit Follow-Up Coordinator contacts Issue Owner for update | Audit Follow-Up Coordinator |
| **[X+1]–[X] days** | Formal written notice issued; Issue Owner's Line Manager notified | Head of Internal Audit |
| **[X+1]–[X] days** (High/Critical) | Escalation to CIO/CISO and CRO; ARMC notified at next meeting | Head of Internal Audit |
| **[X+1]+ days** (Critical) | Immediate ARMC notification; potential BNM notification assessed | Head of Internal Audit / Board |

### 11.2 Extension Requests

Issue Owners may submit a formal request for an extension to the target remediation date under the following conditions:

- The extension is requested **before** the original target date expires;
- A documented business justification is provided;
- Adequate interim risk mitigation controls are in place and documented;
- The extension is approved by the appropriate authority.

**Extension Approval Authority:**

| Finding Severity | Extension Approval Authority |
|---|---|
| Critical | ARMC |
| High | Head of Internal Audit (inform ARMC) |
| Medium | Head of Internal Audit |
| Low | Audit Lead |

No finding may be extended more than **[X] times** without ARMC review and approval.

### 11.3 Risk Acceptance

Where a finding cannot be remediated due to documented technical, operational, or financial constraints, the Issue Owner may apply for **formal risk acceptance**. Risk acceptance:

- Must be supported by a documented risk acceptance rationale;
- Must be reviewed by the Chief Risk Officer;
- Must be approved by the ARMC for Critical and High findings;
- Is time-limited (maximum [X] months) and subject to annual review;
- Does not constitute closure — the finding remains open in the tracking register with status "Risk Accepted."

Risk acceptance is not available for findings that constitute a direct regulatory breach.

### 11.4 Dispute Resolution

Where there is a substantive disagreement between the Issue Owner and the IAD regarding:

- The accuracy of a finding's factual basis;
- The appropriateness of the severity rating;
- The adequacy of the proposed corrective action;

The matter is escalated to the Head of Internal Audit for determination. If unresolved, the matter is referred to the ARMC for adjudication. The ARMC's decision is final.
