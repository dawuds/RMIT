## 1. Purpose

*This section states the primary objective of the procedure and the regulatory obligation it fulfils. It should clearly articulate why the document exists and what outcome it is designed to achieve.*

This procedure establishes the formal process by which [Organization Name] identifies, assesses, escalates, and reports significant technology audit findings to Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) in accordance with the requirements of the **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) Policy Document**, specifically Clause **16.5**.

The objectives of this procedure are to:

- Define a consistent, repeatable process for the identification, classification, and reporting of technology-related audit findings that meet the criteria for regulatory notification;
- Ensure timely and accurate communication of material technology risks and control deficiencies to BNM in the format and within the timelines mandated by the regulator;
- Provide clear accountability to personnel involved in the audit, escalation, and notification process;
- Support the Board and Senior Management of [Organization Name] in discharging their oversight obligations with respect to technology risk governance; and
- Maintain a complete and auditable record of all regulatory notifications and subsequent remediation activities.

This procedure complements the overarching **[Organization Name] Internal Audit Charter**, **Technology Risk Management Framework**, and **Incident Management Policy**, and should be read in conjunction with those documents.

## 6. Audit Methodology and Standards

*This section describes the professional standards, frameworks, and methodologies that govern how technology audits are conducted at [Organization Name]. It provides the basis for credibility of audit findings raised under this procedure.*

### 6.1 Professional Standards

All technology audits conducted by or on behalf of [Organization Name] shall be performed in accordance with the following standards:

| Standard | Issuing Body | Application |
|---|---|---|
| International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing | Institute of Internal Auditors (IIA) | All internal audit engagements |
| ISACA IS Audit and Assurance Standards | ISACA | Technology-specific audit engagements |
| COBIT 2019 Framework | ISACA | IT governance and control assessment |
| NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) | NIST | Cybersecurity audit benchmarking |
| ISO/IEC 27001:2022 | ISO | Information security control assessment |
| BNM RMiT Policy Document | BNM | Regulatory compliance audit |

### 6.2 Audit Engagement Lifecycle

Each technology audit engagement conducted under this procedure shall follow the standardized engagement lifecycle below:

**Phase 1 — Planning**
- Define audit objectives, scope, and criteria;
- Conduct preliminary risk assessment and develop audit program;
- Notify auditee and agree engagement timeline;
- Issue formal Engagement Notification Letter.

**Phase 2 — Fieldwork**
- Conduct walkthroughs, interviews, and control testing;
- Gather and evaluate audit evidence;
- Document working papers in accordance with IIA standards;
- Identify potential findings and discuss with auditee management.

**Phase 3 — Reporting**
- Draft audit observations and findings;
- Conduct exit conference with auditee;
- Issue Draft Audit Report for management response;
- Obtain Management Action Plans (MAPs) from auditee;
- Issue Final Audit Report.

**Phase 4 — Reporting and Escalation**
- Assess all findings against the significance threshold (Section 8);
- Escalate significant findings per the process defined in Section 10;
- Submit BNM notification where required (Section 9);
- Present findings to Board Audit Committee.

**Phase 5 — Follow-Up**
- Track MAP implementation against committed timelines;
- Conduct follow-up testing to validate remediation;
- Update Remediation Tracking Register;
- Report overdue or unresolved significant findings per Section 12.

### 6.3 Evidence Standards

All audit findings must be supported by sufficient, relevant, and reliable evidence. The following evidence types are considered acceptable:

| Evidence Type | Examples | Reliability |
|---|---|---|
| Physical | System configurations, architecture diagrams, physical access records | High |
| Documentary | Policies, procedures, audit logs, change records, contracts | High |
| Testimonial | Management interviews, process walkthroughs | Medium |
| Analytical | Data analysis, trend review, benchmarking | High (when validated) |

### 6.4 Working Paper Requirements

Audit working papers shall:
- Be prepared, reviewed, and approved within the [Audit Management System];
- Be retained for a minimum of [X] years in accordance with Section 14;
- Clearly reference the finding, evidence, and conclusion for each audit procedure;
- Be subject to quality review by a senior auditor not involved in fieldwork.

## 7. Finding Severity Classification

*This section defines the classification framework for rating audit findings. A consistent severity model ensures proportionate escalation and reporting, and supports BNM's assessment of the institution's risk and control environment.*

### 7.1 Severity Classification Framework

All audit findings identified during technology audit engagements shall be classified using the following four-tier severity model:

| Severity Level | Label | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | **Critical** | A finding that represents an immediate and material threat to the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of critical systems or data; a significant failure of regulatory compliance; or a control weakness that could result in financial loss, regulatory sanction, or reputational harm. | Absence of multi-factor authentication on privileged accounts; unpatched critical vulnerability actively exploited in the wild; material breach of RMiT requirements; complete failure of DR capability for a critical system. |
| **Level 2** | **High** | A finding that represents a significant control weakness with the potential to result in material adverse outcomes if not remediated within a defined period. The risk is elevated but an immediate crisis is not imminent. | Inadequate privileged access controls; absence of real-time security monitoring for critical systems; incomplete BCP testing; significant ITGC deficiencies. |
| **Level 3** | **Medium** | A finding that represents a control gap that, while not immediately threatening, could escalate to a more serious risk if not addressed. May include regulatory non-conformances that do not rise to the level of a material breach. | Outdated IT risk assessments; incomplete documentation of security baselines; minor deviations from change management procedures; isolated access control exceptions. |
| **Level 4** | **Low** | A finding that represents a minor deviation from best practice or internal standards, with limited risk exposure. Typically represents an improvement opportunity rather than a control failure. | Minor documentation gaps; process inefficiencies; non-critical policy non-conformances; observations on emerging risks not yet crystallized. |

### 7.2 Severity Rating Decision Matrix

*Use the matrix below to guide the classification decision. Severity is determined by the combination of likelihood and potential impact, considering both quantitative and qualitative factors.*

| | **Negligible Impact** | **Minor Impact** | **Moderate Impact** | **Major Impact** | **Critical Impact** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Almost Certain** | Medium | High | Critical | Critical | Critical |
| **Likely** | Low | Medium | High | Critical | Critical |
| **Possible** | Low | Medium | High | High | Critical |
| **Unlikely** | Low | Low | Medium | High | Critical |
| **Rare** | Low | Low | Low | Medium | High |

### 7.3 Impact Dimensions

When assessing impact, auditors shall consider the following dimensions:

| Dimension | Considerations |
|---|---|
| **Financial** | Potential for direct financial loss, fraud, or regulatory fine |
| **Operational** | Disruption to critical business processes or customer services |
| **Regulatory** | Non-compliance with BNM RMiT, FSA/IFSA, or other applicable regulations |
| **Reputational** | Potential for adverse public, media, or regulator perception |
| **Data/Privacy** | Risk of unauthorized disclosure of customer or sensitive data |
| **Systemic** | Potential contagion or cascading failure across interconnected systems |

### 7.4 Finding Classification Approval

| Severity Level | Classification Approved By |
|---|---|
| Critical (Level 1) | Head of Internal Audit |
| High (Level 2) | Head of Internal Audit |
| Medium (Level 3) | Senior Internal Auditor / Audit Manager |
| Low (Level 4) | Audit In-Charge |

## 8. Notification Trigger Criteria

*This section defines the specific conditions under which [Organization Name] is required to formally notify BNM of a technology audit finding under RMiT Clause 16.5. Clear criteria prevent both under-reporting (regulatory risk) and over-reporting (regulatory noise).*

### 8.1 Mandatory BNM Notification Triggers

A technology audit finding shall trigger mandatory notification to BNM under RMiT Clause 16.5 when **any one or more** of the following criteria are met:

| Trigger ID | Trigger Criterion | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **T-01** | The finding is classified as **Critical (Level 1)** under the severity framework defined in Section 7 | Materiality threshold |
| **T-02** | The finding relates to a **systemic failure** of a key technology control that is pervasive across multiple critical systems or business units | Systemic risk |
| **T-03** | The finding identifies a **material non-compliance** with a specific requirement of the BNM RMiT Policy Document | Regulatory breach |
| **T-04** | The finding relates to a **significant vulnerability** in systems that process, store, or transmit customer financial data, where exploitation could result in material harm | Data risk |
| **T-05** | The finding is a **repeat finding** — previously reported at Critical or High severity and not remediated within the committed MAP timeline — where the continued control weakness presents a material risk | Persistent risk |
| **T-06** | The finding relates to a **failure of the Business Continuity or Disaster Recovery** capability for a system classified as critical to the delivery of regulated financial services | Operational resilience |
| **T-07** | The finding involves **evidence of unauthorized access**, tampering, or compromise of critical technology systems, even if identified through audit procedures rather than the incident management process | Integrity risk |
| **T-08** | The finding is assessed by the Head of Internal Audit, in consultation with the CRO, as likely to be of **supervisory interest to BNM**, even if it does not strictly meet criteria T-01 through T-07 | Supervisory judgment |

### 8.2 Non-Triggering Conditions

The following conditions do **not** independently trigger mandatory BNM notification, unless combined with a trigger criterion above:

- Findings classified as Medium (Level 3) or Low (Level 4);
- Findings for which a validated and fully implemented Management Action Plan exists at the time of report finalization;
- Findings relating to processes or systems that are not material to the delivery of regulated financial services;
- Internal audit quality observations that do not relate to technology risk or control effectiveness.

### 8.3 Trigger Assessment Process

*Describe the process by which the notification trigger assessment is performed for each audit finding. This should be a formal, documented step in the audit reporting lifecycle.*

1. The Audit In-Charge completes the **Finding Trigger Assessment Form** (Appendix B) for each finding classified as High or Critical;
2. The completed form is reviewed and approved by the Head of Internal Audit;
3. Where trigger criteria are met, the finding is escalated per Section 10, Step 6;
4. Where trigger criteria are not met, the rationale for non-notification is documented and retained in the audit working papers;
5. All trigger assessment decisions are recorded in the **Regulatory Notification Register** (Appendix C), regardless of outcome.

### 11.2 Role Descriptions

| Role | Key Responsibilities Under This Procedure |
|---|---|

| **Audit In-Charge / Senior Auditor** | Executes audit fieldwork. Documents findings. Completes initial severity assessment. Conducts auditee discussions. Prepares draft reports and trigger assessment forms. |

| **CTO / CIO** | Auditee representative for technology domain findings. Ensures adequate resource is allocated to remediation. Escalates technical remediation issues to the CAE. |
| **CISO** | Consulted on findings relating to cybersecurity controls. Provides subject matter expertise on severity assessment and remediation adequacy. |
| **Compliance Function** | Coordinates BNM notification submission. Maintains the Regulatory Notification Register. Manages ongoing BNM communication in conjunction with the CAE. |
