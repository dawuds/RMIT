## 1. Purpose

*Describe the purpose of this document and the regulatory obligation it fulfills. Reference the specific BNM RMiT clause and any internal policy it supports.*

This document establishes the formal **Fraud Investigation Procedures** for [Organization Name] in accordance with the Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) **Risk Management in Technology (RMiT)** Policy Document, specifically **Clause 12.7**, which requires financial institutions to implement robust controls for identifying, investigating, and responding to suspected fraudulent activity in technology-enabled financial services.

The purpose of this document is to:

- Define a structured, repeatable methodology for investigating flagged transactions and suspected fraud cases;
- Ensure timely detection, containment, and resolution of fraud events to minimize financial and reputational harm to [Organization Name] and its customers;
- Establish clear accountability across all teams involved in the fraud investigation lifecycle;
- Ensure compliance with applicable Malaysian regulatory requirements, including BNM RMiT, the Financial Services Act 2013 (FSA), the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA), and applicable Anti-Money Laundering (AML) obligations;
- Support the continuous improvement of fraud controls through post-investigation review and lessons learned.

## 5. Fraud Investigation Process Flow

*Provide the end-to-end, step-by-step process flow for fraud investigations. This section should be supported by a process diagram (referenced as an appendix) and a detailed narrative for each stage.*

### 5.1 Overview

The fraud investigation lifecycle at [Organization Name] is structured into five sequential phases, each with defined inputs, outputs, timelines, and accountability:

```
[Detection & Alerting] → [Alert Triage] → [Case Investigation] → [Resolution & Recovery] → [Reporting & Closure]
```

*Note: A detailed process flow diagram is provided in **Appendix A**. All timelines below represent maximum permissible durations and are subject to escalation if not met.*

### 5.2 Phase 1: Detection and Alerting

*Describe how fraud alerts are generated, the sources of alerts, and the initial logging process.*

**Trigger Sources:**

| Source | Description | Alert Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Transaction Monitoring System (TMS) | Automated rule-based or ML-driven alerts from [TMS Name] | Per rule severity |
| Customer Complaint | Customer reports unauthorized transaction via branch, call centre, or digital channel | High |
| Internal Staff Referral | Employee identifies suspicious activity during normal operations | Medium / High |
| Law Enforcement Request | Police report or court order referencing an account or transaction | High |
| Industry Intelligence | Fraud typology alerts from ABM, PIDM, or BNM | Medium |
| Interbank Notification | Notification received from another financial institution | High |

**Steps:**

1. Automated alert or manual referral is generated and logged in [Case Management System Name];
2. Alert is assigned a unique **Alert ID** and timestamped;
3. Alert is classified by **Alert Type** (e.g., account takeover, unauthorized fund transfer, card fraud, phishing-related) and **Severity** (Critical / High / Medium / Low);
4. Fraud Operations team is notified via [notification channel, e.g., system dashboard, email, SMS];
5. Initial data capture is performed: account number, transaction details, customer profile snapshot, and device/session metadata (where available).

**Timelines:**

| Alert Severity | Maximum Time to Acknowledge | Maximum Time to Assign to Analyst |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | 15 minutes | 30 minutes |
| High | 30 minutes | 1 hour |
| Medium | 2 hours | 4 hours |
| Low | 1 business day | 2 business days |

### 5.3 Phase 2: Alert Triage

*Refer to Section 7 for detailed alert triage procedures.*

At this phase, analysts perform initial review to determine whether an alert should be:

- **Closed as False Positive** — No further action required; alert closed with documented rationale;
- **Closed with Monitoring** — Alert closed but account flagged for enhanced monitoring;
- **Escalated to Case** — Alert escalated for full investigation; a **Case ID** is created and linked to the Alert ID.

### 5.4 Phase 3: Case Investigation

*Refer to Section 8 for detailed investigation procedures.*

Full investigation is conducted by a designated **Case Investigator**, encompassing:

- Transaction analysis and reconstruction;
- Customer identity verification and KYC review;
- Device and access log analysis (with SOC support, where applicable);
- Liaison with receiving institutions (for fund recovery);
- Preparation of investigation report.

### 5.5 Phase 4: Resolution and Recovery

**Steps:**

1. Determination of fraud type and whether loss has been incurred;
2. Initiation of fund recall / reversal procedures (refer to [Fund Recovery Procedures, Doc ID]);
3. Account remediation: account freeze, password reset, card blocking, as appropriate;
4. Determination of customer liability in accordance with [Consumer Protection Policy, Doc ID] and BNM Guidelines on Financial Consumer Protection;
5. Customer notification (refer to Section 9);
6. Determination of whether STR is required under AMLA (refer to AML/CFT Policy);
7. Internal loss event reporting in accordance with operational risk framework.

### 5.6 Phase 5: Reporting and Closure

**Steps:**

1. Case investigation report finalized and reviewed by [Senior Investigator / Fraud Manager];
2. Case status updated in [Case Management System Name];
3. Regulatory reporting completed where applicable (STR, BNM incident reporting per RMiT);
4. Post-investigation review conducted for confirmed fraud cases;
5. Lessons learned and control enhancements submitted to Fraud Management Committee;
6. Case formally closed; records retained per Section 12.

## 6. Transaction Monitoring Rules

### 6.1 Rule Governance Framework

| Lifecycle Stage | Description | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|

| **Deployment** | Rule deployed in [TMS Name] production environment | [Technology / TMS Administrator] |

| **Rule Retirement** | Ineffective or superseded rules decommissioned and documented | Head of Fraud Management |

| Rule Category | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|

## 7. Alert Triage Procedures

*Define the step-by-step process for triaging fraud alerts from initial receipt through to disposition (closure or escalation). Include decision criteria and documentation requirements.*

### 7.1 Triage Objectives

The objective of the alert triage process is to:

- Rapidly assess the validity and risk of each alert;
- Prioritize investigative resources toward high-risk alerts;
- Minimize false positives to reduce operational burden;
- Ensure that genuine fraud cases are escalated promptly.

### 7.2 Triage Analyst Responsibilities

*Triage analysts are first-line reviewers responsible for the initial assessment of all alerts. They must complete triage within the timelines defined in Section 5.2.*

Triage analysts are required to:

1. Review the alert details in [Case Management System Name], including transaction data, account profile, and rule trigger rationale;
2. Conduct an initial **Customer Risk Assessment** by reviewing:
   - Customer KYC status and risk rating;
   - Account activity history (last [X] days);
   - Previous alerts and case history;
   - Device and session metadata (IP address, device fingerprint, geolocation);
3. Perform **Transaction Verification** by reviewing:
   - Transaction amount, recipient details, and channel;
   - Consistency with customer's historical behaviour;
   - Whether the customer has previously transacted with the same beneficiary;
4. Apply the **Triage Decision Matrix** (Section 7.3) to determine alert disposition;
5. Document the triage decision with rationale in [Case Management System Name];
6. Escalate or close the alert within the timelines defined in Section 5.2.

### 7.3 Triage Decision Matrix

*Use the following matrix to determine the appropriate disposition for each alert. All decisions must be documented with supporting rationale.*

| Scenario | Indicators | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| Clear false positive | Customer-initiated, within normal behaviour pattern, consistent device/location | Close — False Positive |
| Low-risk anomaly | Minor deviation from pattern, no corroborating indicators, no prior alerts | Close — No Action Required |
| Elevated risk — monitoring required | Moderate behavioural anomaly, no confirmed fraud indicators, first occurrence | Close — Enhanced Monitoring Applied |
| Potential fraud — unverified | Multiple corroborating fraud indicators, unusual transaction, no prior contact with beneficiary | Escalate to Case — Pending Verification |
| High-risk / active fraud suspected | Large amount, new device, new beneficiary, prior alerts on account, time-sensitive | Escalate to Case — Immediate Action Required; initiate account freeze |
| Confirmed fraud (customer-reported) | Customer confirms unauthorized transaction | Escalate to Case — Confirmed Fraud; initiate account freeze and recovery |

### 7.4 Account Freeze Authorization

*Define the thresholds and authorization levels for placing a hold or freeze on a customer account during triage.*

| Action | Trigger Condition | Authorization Level |
|---|---|---|
| Temporary Transaction Hold (< [X] hours) | Suspected active fraud; pending verification | Triage Analyst |
| Account Freeze (> [X] hours) | High-risk alert; customer-reported fraud | Senior Fraud Analyst / Fraud Manager |
| Account Suspension | Confirmed fraud; mule account identification | Head of Fraud Management |

> **Note:** Account freeze actions must be communicated to the customer within **[X] hours** in accordance with Section 9 (Customer Notification Process) and applicable consumer protection obligations.

### 7.5 Triage Documentation Requirements

For every alert reviewed, the triage analyst must record the following in [Case Management System Name]:

- Alert ID and timestamp of triage commencement and completion;
- Data sources reviewed (account history, device logs, TMS rule details);
- Summary of findings;
- Triage decision and supporting rationale;
- Actions taken (e.g., account freeze, enhanced monitoring flag);
- Analyst ID and timestamp of sign-off.

## 8. Investigation Procedures

*Provide detailed, step-by-step procedures for conducting fraud case investigations. This section covers the full investigation lifecycle from case opening to report finalisation.*

### 8.1 Case Opening

Upon escalation from triage, the **Case Investigator** must:

1. Accept assignment of the case in [Case Management System Name] and acknowledge within [X] hours;
2. Review the triage summary and all supporting documentation;
3. Confirm or update the case priority classification:

| Priority | Description | Resolution Target |
|---|---|---|
| P1 — Critical | Active fraud in progress; significant financial loss; systemic risk | [X] hours |
| P2 — High | Confirmed fraud; recovery action required; customer distress | [X] business days |
| P3 — Medium | Suspected fraud; investigation required; no immediate active threat | [X] business days |
| P4 — Low | Low-risk; routine investigation; monitoring case | [X] business days |

4. Notify the customer if not already done (refer to Section 9);
5. Identify and preserve all relevant evidence (refer to Section 12).

### 8.2 Transaction Analysis

*Investigators must conduct a thorough analysis of all transactions associated with the case to reconstruct the fraud event.*

**Steps:**

1. Obtain complete transaction records for the affected account for the period [X] days prior to and [X] days after the flagged transaction(s);
2. Reconstruct the **transaction chain**, including:
   - Source account and originating channel;
   - Intermediary accounts (if applicable);
   - Ultimate beneficiary account(s);
3. Identify the **point of compromise** (e.g., phishing, social engineering, SIM swap, malware, credential theft);
4. Determine the **total financial exposure** (confirmed loss + attempted but blocked amounts);
5. Identify any linked accounts, devices, or entities that may be part of the fraud network;
6. Document findings in the **Investigation Workbook** (Appendix C).

### 8.3 Digital Evidence Collection

*All digital evidence must be collected, preserved, and handled in accordance with the Evidence Management Policy (Section 12) to ensure admissibility.*

| Evidence Type | Source System | Responsible Party |
|---|---|---|
| Transaction logs | Core Banking System / Payment Switch | Case Investigator |
| Authentication logs (login, OTP) | IAM / Authentication System | Case Investigator / SOC |
| Device and session metadata | [Fraud Detection Platform / TMS] | Case Investigator |
| IP address and geolocation data | Network logs / CDN | SOC |
| Customer communication records | CRM / Call Centre System | Case Investigator |
| TMS alert data and rule triggers | [TMS Name] | Fraud Analytics Team |
| CCTV / ATM footage (if applicable) | Physical Security / Operations | [Physical Security Team] |

### 8.4 Interbank Coordination and Fund Recovery

*Where fraudulent funds have been transferred to accounts at other financial institutions, investigators must initiate fund recovery procedures as a matter of urgency.*

1. Identify the receiving institution(s) using the beneficiary account number;
2. Initiate a **fund recall request** to the receiving institution via [interbank communication channel, e.g., RENTAS, DuitNow dispute mechanism, ABM Fraud Hotline] within **[X] hours** of fraud confirmation;
3. Document all interbank communications in the case record;
4. Monitor the status of fund recall and escalate to HFM if no response is received within **[X] business days**;
5. If funds cannot be recovered through interbank mechanisms, assess whether:
   - A police report has been lodged by the customer;
   - Legal action / court order is warranted;
   - The matter should be referred to FIED (BNM) or PDRM.

### 8.5 Investigation Report

*Upon completion of the investigation, the Case Investigator must prepare a written Investigation Report. The report must be reviewed and approved by the Fraud Manager before case closure.*

The Investigation Report must include:

- **Executive Summary:** Brief description of the fraud event, total financial impact, and outcome;
- **Incident Timeline:** Chronological sequence of events from fraud occurrence to detection and response;
- **Fraud Type Classification:** Classification of the fraud typology in accordance with [Organization Name]'s fraud taxonomy;
- **Root Cause Analysis:** Identified root cause(s) and contributing factors;
- **Evidence Summary:** List of evidence collected and preserved;
- **Customer Impact Assessment:** Financial loss, whether funds were recovered, and determination of customer liability;
- **Regulatory Reporting:** Whether STR was filed; BNM incident report reference (if applicable);
- **Recommendations:** Control improvements and preventative measures to reduce recurrence;
- **Case Disposition:** Confirmed Fraud / Attempted Fraud / Inconclusive / False Positive.

**Investigation Report Approval:**

| Report Tier | Case Value / Complexity | Reviewer | Approver |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | < RM [X] | Senior Fraud Analyst | Fraud Manager |
| Tier 2 | RM [X] — RM [X] | Fraud Manager | Head of Fraud Management |
| Tier 3 | > RM [X] or systemic fraud | Head of Fraud Management | Chief Risk Officer / Board Risk Committee |

## 9. Customer Notification Process

*Define the process for notifying customers of suspected or confirmed fraud, account actions taken, and the outcome of investigations. Ensure alignment with BNM consumer protection obligations.*

### 9.1 Notification Obligations

[Organization Name] is obligated under BNM's **Guidelines on Financial Consumer Protection** and applicable service agreements to notify customers in a timely, clear, and transparent manner when:

- A fraud alert has been raised on their account and action has been taken (e.g., account freeze);
- A suspected or confirmed unauthorized transaction has been identified;
- An investigation has been concluded and a determination of liability has been made;
- Recovery of funds has been successful or unsuccessful.

### 9.2 Notification Channels

| Channel | Use Case | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| SMS / Push Notification | Immediate alert notification; account freeze notification | Automated via [Notification System] |
| Email | Formal written notification of investigation outcome | Sent to registered email address |
| Phone Call | High-value cases; elderly / vulnerable customers; customer in distress | By Fraud Operations / Customer Care |
| In-Branch | Customer prefers in-person communication; document signing required | Arranged by Customer Care |
| Registered Mail | Legal notification; formal dispute resolution | Sent to registered address |

### 9.3 Notification Timeline Requirements

*The following timelines represent maximum permissible durations. Earlier notification is encouraged where operationally feasible.*

| Notification Event | Maximum Timeline | Channel | Responsible Party |
|---|---|---|---|
| Account freeze / hold notification | Within **[X] hours** of account action | SMS + Email | Fraud Operations |
| Acknowledgement of fraud report | Within **[X] business days** of customer report | Email / Phone | Customer Care |
| Investigation status update | Within **[X] business days** of case opening | Email / Phone | Fraud Investigator |
| Investigation outcome notification | Within **[X] business days** of case closure | Email + Phone | Fraud Investigator |
| Fund recovery outcome | Within **[X] business days** of recovery determination | Email + Phone | Fraud Investigator |

### 9.4 Notification Content Requirements

All customer notifications must include:

- [Organization Name] letterhead / official branding and reference number;
- Clear, plain-language description of the action taken and the reason;
- Steps the customer needs to take (if any);
- Contact details for further enquiries;
- Relevant consumer protection information and recourse options (e.g., right to dispute, referral to BNMLINK / BNMTELELINK);
- Privacy statement confirming that personal data is handled in accordance with PDPA.

*Approved notification templates are maintained in **Appendix D**.*

### 9.5 Vulnerable Customer Considerations

*Where a customer is identified as elderly, disabled, or otherwise vulnerable, additional care measures must be applied.*

- Assign a dedicated **Customer Care representative** to manage all communications;
- Offer in-branch or phone-based communication as default;
- Ensure communications are in the customer's preferred language;
- Document vulnerability status in the case record;
- Apply heightened consideration when assessing customer liability.

### 9.6 Customer Dispute and Liability Determination

*Define the process for determining customer liability in accordance with applicable regulations and internal policy.*

| Scenario | Customer Liability Determination | Applicable Policy |

| Unauthorized transaction — customer not at fault | Zero liability; full restitution | [Consumer Protection Policy, Doc ID] |

| Customer-initiated fraud (first-party fraud) | No restitution; refer to AML/CFT Policy | [AML/CFT Policy, Doc ID] |
| Disputed determination | Refer to internal dispute resolution process | [Dispute Resolution Procedures, Doc ID] |

## 12. Evidence Management and Record Keeping

*Define the procedures for collecting, preserving, and retaining evidence in a manner that ensures integrity and regulatory compliance.*

### 12.1 Evidence Collection Principles

All evidence collected during fraud investigations must adhere to the following principles:

- **Integrity:** Evidence must not be altered, corrupted, or tampered with after collection. Investigators must use read-only forensic tools where applicable;
- **Chain of Custody:** A documented **Chain of Custody Record** must be maintained for all physical and digital evidence;
- **Proportionality:** Evidence collection must be proportionate to the nature of the investigation and must not exceed what is necessary;
- **Legal Admissibility:** Evidence must be collected and preserved in a manner consistent with requirements for potential use in civil or criminal proceedings;
- **Data Privacy:** Evidence collection involving personal data must comply with PDPA; any data sharing with external parties requires appropriate legal basis.

### 12.2 Chain of Custody Requirements

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Evidence ID | Unique identifier assigned to each piece of evidence |
| Description | Nature and format of the evidence |
| Source | System or location from which evidence was obtained |
| Collected By | Full name and employee ID of the collecting investigator |
| Collection Date/Time | Date and time of collection (UTC+8) |
| Collection Method | Tool or method used to collect the evidence |
| Storage Location | System or physical location where evidence is stored |
| Access Log | Record of all personnel who have accessed the evidence |
| Hash Value | Cryptographic hash (e.g., SHA-256) of digital evidence files for integrity verification |

## 13. Case Closure and Post-Investigation Review

*Define the criteria and process for formally closing a fraud case, and the requirements for post-investigation review.*

### 13.1 Case Closure Criteria

A fraud case may only be formally closed when all of the following conditions have been met:

- [ ] Investigation report has been completed and approved by the appropriate authority (per Section 8.5);
- [ ] All customer notifications have been issued (per Section 9);
- [ ] Customer liability determination has been made and communicated;
- [ ] Fund recovery outcome has been determined and documented;
- [ ] STR assessment completed; STR filed if required;
- [ ] All regulatory reporting obligations have been fulfilled;
- [ ] All evidence has been secured and the Chain of Custody record is complete;
- [ ] Case record in [Case Management System Name] is fully populated and signed off.

### 13.2 Post-Investigation Review

*A Post-Investigation Review must be conducted for all Tier 2 and Tier 3 cases and for any case that reveals a control gap or systemic issue. For Tier 1 cases, the review is conducted on a sample basis.*

**Review Objectives:**

- Identify root causes and contributing control failures;
- Assess the effectiveness of the fraud investigation process;
- Identify opportunities to update TMS rules, detection thresholds, or procedures;
- Capture lessons learned for training purposes.

**Review Output:**

| Output | Description | Recipient |
|---|---|---|
| Post-Investigation Review Report | Documented findings and recommendations | Fraud Management Committee |
| Control Enhancement Request | Formal request to update controls or TMS rules | Head of Fraud Management / IT / Risk |
| Training Needs Identification | Areas requiring staff awareness or training updates | HR / Training Team |
| Regulatory Notification | If review reveals a reportable control failure | CRO / Compliance |

### 13.3 Key Performance Indicators

*The following KPIs are used to measure the effectiveness of the fraud investigation function. Results are reported monthly to the Risk Committee.*

| KPI | Definition | Target | Measurement Frequency |
